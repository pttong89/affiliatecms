Frequently Asked Questions

What makes 2022 running shoes different from previous years?

2022 marked a significant evolution in running shoe technology with several key developments. First, supercritical foams like PWRRUN PB and FF Blast Max became more widely available across different price points, not just premium racing shoes. Second, plate technology diversified beyond just carbon fiber to include nylon and composite materials that work better for training. Third, brands focused heavily on versatility, creating shoes that could handle multiple training demands rather than being highly specialized. Finally, we saw major improvements in upper materials and fit, with brands using more sustainable materials without sacrificing performance. These changes made 2022 one of the most innovative years in recent running history.

Should beginners buy carbon-plated running shoes in 2022?

For beginners, I generally recommend starting with non-plated shoes to develop proper running mechanics and strength. However, 2022 introduced more beginner-friendly plated options like the Saucony Endorphin Speed 3 with its flexible nylon plate. If you're set on trying plated shoes as a beginner, look for models with nylon or composite plates rather than rigid carbon fiber. These provide some assistance without overwhelming your natural running form. Focus first on building weekly mileage safely in quality daily trainers like the ASICS Novablast 3, then gradually introduce speed-oriented shoes as your experience grows. Remember, the best shoe is one that keeps you healthy and enjoying your runs.

How many pairs of running shoes should I own in 2022?

The ideal number depends on your weekly mileage and goals. For casual runners (15-25 miles/week), one quality daily trainer like the ASICS Novablast 3 is sufficient. Intermediate runners (25-50 miles/week) benefit from a two-shoe rotation: one daily trainer and one speed/tempo shoe like the Endorphin Speed 3. Serious runners (50+ miles/week) should consider a three-shoe rotation adding a max cushion option like the New Balance SC Trainer for recovery runs. Competitive runners often maintain 4-5 pairs including specialized racing shoes. The key is rotating shoes to extend their lifespan and reduce repetitive stress on your legs. In 2022's market, quality shoes are more versatile than ever, so you can accomplish more with fewer pairs.

Are expensive running shoes worth it in 2022?

2022 saw both premium innovations and excellent value options, making this question more complex than ever. Expensive shoes ($200+) like the Saucony Endorphin Pro 3 offer cutting-edge foam and plate technology that can genuinely improve performance and reduce fatigue. However, many runners will find tremendous value in mid-range options ($120-160) like the ASICS Novablast 3 or Puma Deviate Nitro 2. The key is matching the shoe to your needs and budget. If you're training for a goal race and running high mileage, premium shoes often justify their cost through durability and performance benefits. For recreational runners, mid-range shoes offer 90% of the performance at 60% of the price. Consider your running goals, weekly mileage, and budget when making this decision.

What's the biggest running shoe trend from 2022?

The biggest trend in 2022 was the mainstreaming of "super shoe" technology into daily trainers. Previously, advanced foams and plates were reserved for racing shoes costing $250+. In 2022, we saw these technologies appear in versatile trainers like the Saucony Endorphin Speed 3 ($170) and even some daily trainers. This democratization means runners at all levels can access performance-enhancing technology. Additionally, there was a strong focus on sustainability, with brands using more recycled materials without sacrificing performance. The third major trend was the emphasis on versatility over specialization, with shoes like the Puma Deviate Nitro 2 capable of handling everything from easy runs to tempo efforts. This trend toward accessible, sustainable, versatile shoes defined 2022's market.
