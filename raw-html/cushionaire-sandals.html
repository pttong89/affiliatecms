Unlike athletic shoes where a snug fit is often preferred, cushionaire sandals work best with a relaxed fit that allows natural foot expansion throughout the day. Strap Adjustment and Customization Tips

Proper strap adjustment is essential for both comfort and foot health. The front strap should hold your foot securely without creating pressure points across the instep. The back strap, when present, should provide heel security without being overly tight. During my extensive wear testing, I developed specific adjustment techniques that maximize comfort. For the front buckle, I recommend starting with a looser setting and gradually tightening until you achieve secure foot placement without restricting circulation. The leather straps will stretch slightly during the break-in period, so initial adjustment should account for this change. Regular readjustment during the first few weeks ensures optimal fit as both the straps and footbed adapt to your feet. The back strap adjustment varies based on intended use. For casual wear, a looser setting allows easy slip-on functionality. For more active use, such as hiking or extended walking, a firmer adjustment provides better heel control and overall stability. Learning to quickly adjust straps based on activity level maximizes the versatility of your cushionaire sandals.
