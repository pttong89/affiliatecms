How do I choose the right size when shopping for black trainers online?

Online sizing requires careful attention to brand-specific characteristics and customer feedback patterns. Start by consulting the manufacturer's official sizing chart and measuring your feet in the evening when they're naturally slightly swollen. Read customer reviews focusing on sizing feedback - look for patterns like "runs small," "runs large," or "true to size." Different brands have distinct sizing philosophies: Nike typically runs narrow and small, New Balance runs true to size with width options, while Hoka often runs large. Consider ordering from retailers with generous return policies, allowing you to try multiple sizes if necessary. When in doubt between two sizes, consider your intended use - active purposes may benefit from slightly larger sizes to accommodate swelling and thicker socks.

How should I care for and maintain black trainers to extend their lifespan?

Proper maintenance significantly extends trainer lifespan while preserving appearance and performance. For canvas styles, regular brushing removes surface dirt and prevents embedding. Use specialized canvas cleaners or mild soap solutions for deeper cleaning, avoiding harsh chemicals that can damage materials. Leather trainers benefit from conditioning every few months to prevent cracking and maintain flexibility. Allow shoes to fully air dry between wears - rotating between two pairs prevents moisture buildup that leads to odor and material degradation. Store them with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture. Address stains immediately before they set permanently. For active use, consider waterproofing sprays to prevent damage from unexpected moisture. Replace insoles when they lose cushioning rather than replacing entire shoes, often extending usable life by months.
