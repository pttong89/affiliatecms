What Are On Cloudrock Boots?

On Cloudrock boots represent the Swiss brand's ambitious entry into the hiking footwear market, combining their signature running shoe technology with hiking-specific features. These boots blur the line between trail runners and traditional hiking boots, creating what On calls "speed hiking" footwear.

The defining feature of on cloudrock boots is the CloudTec cushioning system - hollow pods on the sole that compress on impact and spring back for energy return. This technology, borrowed from their running shoes, provides exceptional comfort on long hikes while maintaining a relatively lightweight profile.

What sets these boots apart from traditional hiking footwear is their hybrid design philosophy. They offer more ankle support than trail runners but remain lighter and more flexible than heavy-duty hiking boots. The waterproof membrane, MissionGrip outsole, and synthetic upper construction make them suitable for various weather conditions and terrain types.

The cloudrock hiking boots line includes several models: the original Cloudrock, the improved Cloudrock 2, and specialized variants like the Cloudrock 2 Waterproof. Each model targets different hiking styles and conditions, from day hiking to light backpacking.

Key Technologies:

  • CloudTec Cushioning: Hollow pods for comfort and energy return
  • MissionGrip Outsole: Multi-directional lugs for traction
  • Speedboard: Rigid plate for stability and propulsion
  • Waterproof Membrane: Proprietary weather protection

Performance on Different Terrains

Through extensive testing across diverse landscapes, I've evaluated how on cloudrock boots perform in real-world conditions. From the granite slabs of Yosemite's Half Dome approach to the muddy trails of the Pacific Northwest, these boots reveal distinct strengths and limitations.

Rocky and Technical Terrain

On rocky terrain, the cloudrock boots excel in providing comfort over long distances. The CloudTec cushioning absorbs impact effectively, reducing foot fatigue during extended boulder hopping. However, the relatively soft MissionGrip compound wears quickly on abrasive surfaces. After 150 miles on granite trails in the Sierra Nevada, I noticed significant lug wear.

Muddy and Wet Conditions

The waterproof membrane performs adequately for light rain and shallow puddles, but the tread pattern struggles with mud clearance. The widely spaced lugs don't shed mud effectively, leading to reduced traction on slippery surfaces. The On Cloudrock waterproof hiking boots work best in dry to moderately wet conditions.

Well-Maintained Trails

These boots truly shine on well-maintained trails where comfort becomes paramount. The lightweight design and energy-returning CloudTec system make mile-eating a pleasure. On packed dirt trails and moderate inclines, the On Cloudrock hiking boots outperform heavier traditional boots in terms of hiking efficiency.

Performance Limitations:

  • Limited ankle support for heavy backpack loads (30+ lbs)
  • Tread wear on abrasive rock surfaces
  • Poor mud shedding capabilities
  • Less protection than traditional hiking boots

Frequently Asked Questions

Are On Cloudrock boots good for hiking?

Yes, On Cloudrock boots are excellent for day hikes and light backpacking on well-maintained trails. They offer superior comfort with CloudTec cushioning and moderate ankle support. However, they're best suited for casual to moderate hiking rather than technical terrain or heavy backpacking loads exceeding 30 pounds. The lightweight design and energy return make them ideal for hikers who prioritize comfort over maximum protection and durability.

How waterproof are On Cloudrock boots?

On Cloudrock boots feature a proprietary waterproof membrane that performs well initially but may lose effectiveness after 150+ miles of use. The waterproofing works adequately for light rain and shallow puddles but isn't designed for prolonged water exposure or deep creek crossings like traditional hiking boots. Many users report water penetration through the ankle collar during heavy rain or when hiking through wet vegetation for extended periods.

What's the difference between Cloudrock and Cloudrock 2?

The Cloudrock 2 is lighter, more durable, and features improved materials compared to the original. Key upgrades include better lacing systems, enhanced MissionGrip outsole, reduced weight (about 3 ounces less), and improved collar fit. The Cloudrock 2 also has better protection for the CloudTec pods underneath, addressing one of the main durability concerns with the original model. The newer version also offers improved breathability while maintaining waterproof capabilities.

Do On Cloudrock boots run true to size?

On Cloudrock boots generally run true to size, but most hikers should size up half a size for proper toe room with hiking socks. The fit has become less roomy in recent years, so trying them on first is recommended. People with narrow feet typically find them more comfortable than those with wide feet. The boot's European sizing can also cause confusion, so checking On's size guide and reading user reviews for your specific foot width is advisable.

How long do On Cloudrock boots last?

On Cloudrock boots typically last 300-500 miles depending on terrain and usage. The CloudTec pods and MissionGrip outsole show wear after 200+ miles of rocky terrain. While comfortable, they don't match the durability of traditional leather hiking boots, making them better suited for occasional use rather than daily hiking. The synthetic construction and specialized cushioning system aren't designed for the same longevity as resoleable traditional hiking boots.
