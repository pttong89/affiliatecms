Frequently Asked Questions

Are Danner 600 boots true to size?

The danner 600 tends to run slightly large, and Danner officially recommends sizing down half a size for optimal fit. This recommendation aligns with my personal experience and feedback from hundreds of users. The DPDX last provides a roomier fit than many hiking boots, which allows for thicker socks and natural foot swelling during long hikes. However, if you're between sizes or have narrow feet, definitely consider the half-size reduction. Wide-footed hikers may find the standard width perfectly suitable without sizing adjustments.

How long do Danner 600 boots last?

With proper care, the danner 600 typically delivers 1,000-1,500 miles of hiking before requiring resoling, depending on terrain and hiking style. The full-grain leather upper and quality construction mean the boots often outlast their original outsoles. Thanks to Danner's recrafting service, you can extend the boots' lifespan to 3,000+ miles with one or two resoles. I've personally achieved over 2,000 miles on my first pair with one recrafting service, and they're still going strong. The key is regular leather conditioning and proper storage.

Can Danner 600 be used for winter hiking?

The danner 600 performs excellently in winter conditions, though they're not insulated boots. The waterproof construction and high-quality leather provide good insulation properties, and the boots accommodate thick wool socks comfortably. I've used them successfully in temperatures down to 15°F with appropriate sock layering. The Vibram outsole works well with microspikes for icy conditions. However, for extended winter camping or extreme cold conditions below 0°F, dedicated insulated winter boots would be more appropriate. What's the difference between Danner Dry and Gore-Tex versions?

Danner Dry is Danner's proprietary waterproof membrane, while Gore-Tex is a third-party waterproof/breathable technology. Both provide excellent waterproof protection, but Gore-Tex offers superior breathability, making it better for warm weather hiking or high-intensity activities. Danner Dry versions typically cost $20-40 less than Gore-Tex models. In my experience, Gore-Tex models stay more comfortable during summer hiking, while Danner Dry performs equally well in cool, wet conditions. The choice often comes down to budget and intended use – if you primarily hike in cool conditions, Danner Dry is perfectly adequate.

Are Danner 600 boots good for backpacking?

The danner 600 excels at day hiking and weekend backpacking but has limitations for extended backpacking trips. The 37-39 ounce weight can contribute to leg fatigue during long-distance hiking, and many thru-hikers prefer lighter alternatives. However, for weekend trips with moderate pack weights (25-35 lbs), they provide excellent comfort and protection. The boots' durability makes them ideal for frequent weekend warriors who log 50-100+ hiking days annually. If you prioritize foot comfort and protection over minimal weight, they're an excellent backpacking choice for trips under five days.

How should I care for my Danner 600 boots?

Proper care extends the life of your danner 600 significantly. Clean them after each use with a soft brush and mild soap to remove dirt and debris. Condition the leather every 40-50 hiking days using a quality leather conditioner – I recommend Danner's own conditioning products for best results. Allow boots to air dry completely before storage, and use cedar shoe trees to maintain shape. Avoid heat sources like direct sunlight or heaters, which can crack the leather. Store in a cool, dry place with good air circulation. Following this routine, along with periodic professional cleaning, will maximize your boots' lifespan and performance.
