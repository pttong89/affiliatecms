How do these shoes fit compared to other hiking boots?

The Moab 3 Waterproof runs slightly large and wide compared to athletic shoes and many European hiking boot brands. What's the difference between Moab 2 and Moab 3 waterproof models?

The Moab 3 Waterproof offers significant improvements over the Moab 2, including an upgraded Vibram TC5+ outsole with better traction and durability, enhanced heel support through improved midsole construction, and a more refined waterproof membrane system. The new version features recycled materials in construction while maintaining the comfort characteristics that made the Moab 2 popular. The upper materials blend remains similar, but the Moab 3 offers better long-term durability and improved performance on technical terrain.

Can these shoes handle winter hiking and snow conditions?

The Moab 3 Waterproof performs well in shoulder-season conditions and light snow, but they're not designed for serious winter mountaineering. The waterproof membrane effectively keeps snow out while the insulation provided by the upper materials offers moderate warmth in temperatures down to approximately 20°F. For serious winter hiking below 10°F or in deep snow conditions, dedicated winter boots with additional insulation would be more appropriate. However, for fall and spring hiking with occasional snow encounters, these shoes provide adequate protection and performance.
