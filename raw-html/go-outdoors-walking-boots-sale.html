How to Choose the Right Walking Boots from Go Outdoors Sales

Selecting the right walking boots during a go outdoors walking boots sale requires balancing price considerations with performance requirements. The excitement of significant discounts can lead to poor choices if you don't maintain focus on fit and intended use. Through my experience helping friends and family choose hiking boots, I've developed a systematic approach that ensures satisfaction regardless of the discount level.

Fit remains the paramount consideration, even during compelling sales. I always recommend trying boots in the afternoon when feet are slightly swollen from daily activity, mimicking conditions during long hikes. The boot should accommodate your thickest hiking socks with enough room for toe movement but prevent heel lifting during walking tests. Many hikers make the mistake of choosing boots that feel perfect in the store but become uncomfortable during extended use.

Construction Quality Checklist

When evaluating discounted boots, I examine the sole attachment method (stitched is superior to glued), upper material quality (full-grain leather or high-denier synthetic), and waterproof membrane integrity. These factors determine long-term durability and performance far more than initial purchase price.

Terrain compatibility should guide your selection process during sales. Lightweight men's walking shoes work excellently for well-maintained trails and day hikes, while full-height boots provide necessary ankle support for rough terrain and overnight backpacking. I've learned to resist the temptation of heavily discounted boots that don't match my actual hiking needs.

Seasonal considerations become particularly important when shopping clearance sales. End-of-season boots may be heavily discounted but unsuitable for immediate use. I've found success purchasing winter boots during spring sales for the following year, but this requires adequate storage space and confidence in long-term sizing needs.
