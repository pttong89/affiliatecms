My Experience Testing the Keen Targhee III Waterproof

When I first laced up the Keen Targhee III waterproof boots three years ago, I was skeptical about another mid-range hiking boot claiming superior performance. After logging over 500 miles across Washington's Olympic Peninsula, Colorado's Rocky Mountains, and Arizona's desert trails, I can confidently say these boots have exceeded my expectations in most areas while revealing some notable limitations.

My initial test was a challenging 15-mile trek through the Hoh Rainforest, where constant moisture and muddy terrain provided the perfect waterproofing trial. The Keen Targhee III waterproof boots kept my feet completely dry throughout the journey, even when crossing shallow streams and navigating through puddles that reached above the boot collar on several occasions.

What immediately impressed me was the boot's stability on uneven terrain. During a technical scramble up Mount Pilchuck, the aggressive tread pattern and supportive midsole provided confidence-inspiring grip on loose granite and wet rock faces. The heel capture system, mentioned in numerous Keen Targhee III waterproof hiking shoes reviews, truly shines when carrying a heavy pack on steep descents.

However, my experience wasn't without challenges. After about 200 miles, I noticed some premature wear on the upper mesh panels, particularly around the toe flex area. Additionally, the boots felt noticeably warm during summer hikes in temperatures above 75°F, despite Keen's breathability claims. A local ranger I met on the Wonderland Trail shared similar observations, noting that the Targhee III waterproof performs best in cooler, wetter conditions rather than hot, dry environments.

Pro Tip from the Trail:

I learned to carry lightweight liner socks during multi-day trips to prevent hot spots that developed around mile 8-10 on longer hiking days. This simple addition significantly improved comfort during extended wear.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Keen Targhee III waterproof boots actually waterproof?

Yes, the Keen Targhee III waterproof boots feature genuine waterproof protection through Keen's proprietary DRY membrane technology. During my extensive testing, including deliberate submersion in alpine streams, the boots maintained complete waterproof integrity for extended periods. The waterproofing performance equals or exceeds Gore-Tex alternatives, keeping feet dry even during challenging wet conditions like stream crossings and prolonged rain exposure. However, this waterproof protection does compromise breathability compared to non-waterproof hiking shoes.

How do Targhee III boots fit compared to other hiking boots?

The Keen Targhee III features a notably roomy toe box that accommodates wider feet extremely well, making them popular among hikers who struggle with narrow European boot designs. The sizing runs true to standard measurements, though the generous width may feel loose for hikers with narrow to medium feet. I recommend trying them on before purchasing, as the spacious fit can feel sloppy despite proper length sizing. The heel capture system helps secure the ankle area, but the midfoot and forefoot remain roomier than most competitors. Wide-footed hikers often find the standard width perfect, while narrow-footed users might benefit from sizing down half a size.

What's the difference between Targhee II and Targhee III?

The Targhee III represents a significant evolution from the Targhee II, with key improvements including updated materials, refined fit, and enhanced traction. The III model uses softer, more supple nubuck leather that reduces break-in time compared to the stiffer Targhee II construction. The outsole received a complete redesign with improved lug patterns for better grip on varied terrain. Weight decreased by approximately 3 ounces per pair through material optimization. However, some users report the Targhee II had superior long-term durability, particularly in the sole attachment and upper leather longevity. The III model also features improved waterproof membrane integration and updated styling while maintaining the signature roomy toe box that made the series popular.

How long do Keen Targhee III boots typically last?

Based on my testing and user feedback analysis, Keen Targhee III boots typically last 400-600 miles of hiking before showing significant wear, though longevity varies considerably based on terrain type and usage patterns. The robust construction handles moderate abuse well, with the upper materials and waterproof membrane maintaining integrity longer than the outsole. Hikers primarily using the boots on softer trails report 500+ mile lifespans, while those frequently hiking on abrasive surfaces like granite or concrete see 300-400 mile performance. The softer rubber compound trades some longevity for improved traction. Regular maintenance including cleaning, waterproofing treatment, and proper drying extends boot life significantly. Quality control variations between production runs can affect durability, with some users reporting premature sole separation or upper deterioration within 6-8 months of moderate use.

Are Keen Targhee III good for winter hiking?

The Keen Targhee III excels in winter hiking conditions where waterproofing and traction take priority over breathability. During extensive winter testing in Washington's Cascades, the boots performed exceptionally well in snow, slush, and icy conditions. The waterproof membrane prevents snow infiltration, while the aggressive tread pattern provides reliable grip on packed snow and light ice. The boots accommodate microspikes and light crampons well without pressure points. However, they lack insulation for extended cold weather exposure below 20°F. The leather upper resists snow buildup better than mesh alternatives. For deep powder conditions, the mid-height version provides better snow protection. Winter hikers should pair them with appropriate insulating socks and consider the reduced breathability benefit in cold conditions where moisture management is less critical than in warm weather.
