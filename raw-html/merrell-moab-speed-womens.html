Sizing, Fit & Width Guide

Proper sizing is crucial for optimal performance and comfort in any hiking shoe, and the Merrell Moab Speed womens presents some specific considerations that differ from standard athletic shoe fitting. Based on extensive testing with multiple foot types and comprehensive user feedback analysis, I've developed definitive sizing recommendations.

The general consensus among users and my personal experience suggests ordering a half size larger than your typical athletic shoe size. This accommodation accounts for natural foot swelling during long hikes and provides essential toe room for downhill sections where feet slide forward in the shoe.

The toe box design in the Merrell Moab Speed womens provides more generous room compared to many trail runners, but less than traditional hiking boots. This balanced approach works well for most foot shapes, though users with particularly wide or narrow feet may need special consideration.

Fit testing should always be conducted while wearing your preferred hiking socks, as sock thickness significantly impacts overall fit. I recommend testing the fit later in the day when feet are naturally more swollen to simulate hiking conditions. The shoe should feel secure without creating pressure points, with approximately thumb-width space between your longest toe and the shoe's front.

Pro Fitting Tips from 8 Years of Trail Experience:

  • Always fit shoes in the afternoon when feet are slightly swollen
  • Wear your actual hiking socks during fitting
  • Walk on inclined surfaces to test for heel slippage
  • Check for adequate wiggle room in the toe box
  • Consider how your feet behave during long activities

Width considerations are particularly important for the Moab Speed womens. While the standard width accommodates most foot types well, women with significantly wide feet should seriously consider the dedicated wide-width models that Merrell offers. The wide version provides additional room without compromising the shoe's performance characteristics.

For users requiring orthotic support, the removable footbed makes accommodation straightforward. However, adding aftermarket insoles may require going up an additional half size to maintain comfortable volume. This consideration is especially important for users with high arches or specific foot conditions requiring specialized support.
