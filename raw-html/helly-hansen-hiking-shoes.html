Frequently Asked Questions

How do Helly Hansen hiking shoes compare to Merrell or Salomon?

Are Helly Hansen hiking shoes good for wide feet?

The fit profile of Helly Hansen hiking shoes tends toward a medium width, which can feel narrow for hikers with genuinely wide feet. However, the upper materials do stretch somewhat during the break-in period, and the Norwegian last design provides good toe box volume. For wide feet, I recommend trying the Cascade Low model, as it offers the most accommodating fit. Consider going up a half size if you're between sizes, and always test with your preferred hiking sock system before committing to extended trail use.

What is the typical lifespan of Helly Hansen hiking shoes?

With proper care and rotation, quality Helly Hansen hiking shoes typically provide 600-1000 miles of hiking before requiring replacement. The HellyTech membrane maintains waterproof integrity longer than many competitors, often lasting the entire life of the shoe. Outsole wear varies significantly based on terrain type and hiking style, but the HellyGrip compound shows good durability on mixed surfaces. Can Helly Hansen hiking shoes handle winter conditions?

The insulation and construction quality of Helly Hansen hiking shoes makes them suitable for moderate winter hiking conditions, though not extreme cold weather mountaineering. The waterproof membrane prevents snow infiltration effectively, while the insulation systems provide warmth down to approximately 20°F with proper sock layering. The HellyGrip outsole offers good traction on packed snow and moderate ice conditions. For serious winter backpacking or mountaineering, consider supplementing with microspikes or crampons as conditions warrant.

How should I break in new Helly Hansen hiking shoes?

Proper break-in is crucial for optimal performance with Helly Hansen hiking shoes. Start with short walks around your neighborhood, gradually increasing distance over 20-30 miles of use. Pay attention to pressure points and adjust lacing techniques as needed. Use your intended hiking sock system during break-in to ensure proper fit assessment. The Norwegian leather and synthetic materials do soften with use, but rushing the process can create long-term comfort issues. For extended backpacking trips, ensure at least 50 miles of break-in hiking before departure to identify any potential problems.
