Care and Maintenance for Long-lasting Performance

Daily Cleaning Routine

After Each Use:

  1. Remove loose dirt with a soft brush
  2. Wipe down with damp cloth
  3. Remove insoles to air dry
  4. Stuff with newspaper if very wet
Pro Tip: Never place outdoor UGGs near direct heat sources like radiators or fireplaces, as this can crack leather and damage the adhesives.

Deep Cleaning & Protection

Monthly Deep Clean:

  1. Use specialized suede/leather cleaner
  2. Apply waterproofing treatment
  3. Condition leather components
  4. Replace worn insoles if needed
Recommended Products: UGG Cleaner & Conditioner, Scotchgard Heavy Duty Water Shield, cedar shoe trees for storage.

Seasonal Storage

Off-Season Storage:

  1. Clean thoroughly before storage
  2. Apply protective coating
  3. Insert cedar shoe trees
  4. Store in breathable dust bags
Avoid: Plastic bags, damp basements, extreme temperature locations, or storing when dirty or wet.

How do you care for outdoor UGGs?

Care for outdoor UGGs by cleaning after each use with a damp cloth, using specialized suede/leather cleaners, applying waterproof spray regularly, air-drying away from direct heat, and storing with cedar shoe trees. Avoid machine washing and always let them dry completely before storage. The key to longevity is establishing a routine: daily cleaning removes dirt and debris, weekly deep cleaning maintains appearance, monthly waterproofing treatments preserve protection, and seasonal conditioning keeps materials supple. Proper storage during off-seasons prevents damage from moisture, pests, and temperature extremes. Investment in quality care products specifically designed for premium leather and suede will pay dividends in extended boot life and maintained performance.

Can you wear UGGs in snow and wet conditions?

Yes, but only specific waterproof models. Outdoor UGGs like the Adirondack III feature waterproof leather and sealed seams, making them suitable for snow and wet conditions. Regular suede UGGs should be avoided in these conditions as they lack proper water resistance. The distinction is crucial â fashion UGGs will quickly absorb moisture, leading to staining, odor, and potential damage to both the boot and your feet. Waterproof outdoor models, however, are engineered specifically for wet conditions with treated leather, sealed seams, and moisture-wicking linings. Always verify waterproof ratings and consider applying additional protective treatments for extreme conditions.

What's the difference between indoor and outdoor UGGs?

Outdoor UGGs feature waterproof materials, rugged Vibram outsoles, reinforced construction, and weather-resistant treatments. Indoor UGGs use soft suede, lightweight soles, and focus on comfort rather than durability, making them unsuitable for challenging outdoor conditions. The construction differences are significant: outdoor models employ full-grain leather or treated materials that resist water and abrasion, while indoor models prioritize softness and breathability with untreated suede. Sole construction varies dramatically â outdoor UGGs feature aggressive tread patterns and durable rubber compounds designed for traction on various surfaces, whereas indoor models have minimal treads suitable only for smooth indoor surfaces. The lining and insulation systems also differ, with outdoor models incorporating moisture-wicking materials and enhanced thermal properties.
