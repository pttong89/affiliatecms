Tommy Mallet, the visionary founder who transformed a personal passion into a global luxury brand

When I first discovered mallet shoes during my London exploration in 2023, I was immediately intrigued by their unique story. Founded in 2015 by Tommy Mallet—a former reality TV star from "The Only Way is Essex"—and his partner Evren Ozka, Mallet London represents one of the most fascinating entrepreneurial journeys in modern footwear.

Tommy's inspiration came from a personal frustration that many sneaker enthusiasts can relate to. During a television shoot, he fell in love with a particular pair of shoes but couldn't afford them. This approach has resonated with over 150,000 customers worldwide, including celebrities like Conor McGregor and Olly Murs who regularly sport the brand.

What sets Mallet apart in the crowded sneaker market is their London-centric identity. Each design draws inspiration from iconic London neighborhoods, with styles named after famous streets and areas. This cultural connection reflects the brand's journey from humble London origins to becoming a recognized leader in modern luxury footwear. The brand has successfully transformed from a startup idea into a global phenomenon, proving that authentic storytelling and quality craftsmanship can disrupt even the most established markets.

Mallet Shoes Sizing Guide: Getting the Perfect Fit

Sizing Alert: Key Information Before You Buy

Based on my personal testing and extensive research, most mallet shoes run true to size. However, the Almorah model is a notable exception, running approximately half a size larger than standard measurements.

After trying multiple mallet shoes styles across different seasons and occasions, I've developed a comprehensive understanding of their sizing characteristics. The brand's commitment to consistent manufacturing results in reliable fit expectations, though understanding their unique design elements is crucial for optimal comfort.

My Personal Sizing Experience

GRFTR Series (Size UK 9): Perfect fit with normal socks, comfortable for 8+ hour wear during London walking tours
Popham Model (Size UK 9): True to size, premium leather construction provides excellent foot support
Almorah Exception (Size UK 8.5): Required half-size down due to generous fit, particularly in toe box area

The brand's signature 4.5cm elevated sole adds considerable height—more than traditional sneakers but requires adjustment time. During my first week wearing GRFTR models, I noticed the elevated position affects walking dynamics, particularly on uneven surfaces common in national park trails. However, this design feature provides excellent arch support for urban environments and long periods of standing.

Width considerations are equally important. Mallet shoes generally accommodate standard to slightly wider feet comfortably. The premium leather construction allows for natural stretching over time, improving fit with regular wear. For those with particularly narrow feet, consider going down half a size, while wider feet typically find the standard sizing accommodating.

Compared to mainstream brands, mallet shoes sizing aligns closely with Nike and Adidas standards, making transition easier for those familiar with these measurements. Unlike some Amiri shoes that run notoriously small, or Birdies shoes that vary significantly between styles, Mallet maintains consistent sizing across their entire range (Almorah excepted).
