Materials and Construction Quality

The foundation of any exceptional outdoor gear lies in its materials and construction methods. Ultra100: The High-Performance Fabric

The Fastpack 30 utilizes Ultra100 fabric from Challenge Sailcloth, a material originally developed for high-performance sailing applications. This UHMWPE (Ultra High Molecular Weight Polyethylene) fabric combines a woven face with a laminated film backing, creating extraordinary tear and tensile strength while maintaining minimal weight. At 100 denier, it represents the lightest option in the Ultra fabric line while still providing superior cut, abrasion, and wear resistance.

What makes Ultra100 particularly valuable for backpacking applications is its long-term waterproofing capabilities. Unlike traditional nylon fabrics that require periodic DWR treatment renewal, Ultra100 maintains its water resistance throughout its lifespan. This characteristic proved invaluable during my testing in wet Pacific Northwest conditions, where the fabric shed moisture effectively without adding weight from water absorption.

The UL 40/45 employs X-Pac VX07, a fabric that has become the gold standard in ultralight backpacking circles.
