How to Choose the Right ASICS Shoe for Your Foot Type

Foot Type Assessment Guide

The wet foot test remains the gold standard for initial foot type assessment. Step out of a shower onto a piece of cardboard or brown paper, then examine your footprint. A complete footprint indicates flat feet (low arches), while a narrow connection between heel and forefoot suggests high arches. A moderate connection indicates normal arch structure.

Flat-footed individuals typically benefit most from ASICS stability shoes like the Gel Kayano 31 or GT-2000 13. These shoes provide structured medial support to prevent excessive arch collapse, which often exacerbates plantar fasciitis. During my testing with flat-footed volunteers, stability models consistently outperformed neutral shoes in pain reduction and comfort ratings.

High-arched feet require maximum cushioning to compensate for reduced natural shock absorption. The Gel Nimbus 27 excels in this category, providing generous heel and forefoot padding that high arches desperately need. My testing revealed that high-arched individuals often experience relief within days of switching to maximum cushioned ASICS models, compared to weeks with stability-focused shoes.

For normal arch structure, the choice depends primarily on pronation patterns and activity levels. Neutral pronators typically thrive in Gel Nimbus or Cumulus models, while mild overpronators benefit from GT-series stability features. This flexibility makes ASICS particularly valuable for individuals with normal arch structure but varying activity demands.

Professional Fitting Recommendation

Visit a specialty running store for professional gait analysis when possible. Many locations offer free assessments that can identify pronation patterns invisible to casual observation. This analysis proves invaluable when choosing between ASICS stability and neutral models for optimal plantar fasciitis management.
