Complete Buying Guide & Sizing Recommendations

Sizing Considerations for Hunting Boots

Proper sizing represents the most critical factor in hunting boot satisfaction, based on my experience testing multiple Rocky Core models. These boots generally run true to size, but hunting-specific considerations require careful attention. I recommend sizing up half a size from your regular shoe size to accommodate thick hunting socks and account for foot swelling during long hunting days. The toe box design in rocky core hunting boots provides adequate room for toe movement without excess space that could cause blisters. During my fitting tests with various sock combinations, I found that medium-thickness merino wool hunting socks work best with true-to-size boots, while thicker insulation socks require the half-size increase. The width accommodates most foot shapes without feeling overly roomy. For hunters with wide feet, Rocky offers wide width options in most Core models. I've tested both regular and wide versions, finding the wide option provides extra room without compromising heel security. The lacing system allows fine-tuning of fit across different foot shapes and personal preferences.
