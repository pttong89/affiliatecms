Are Merrell Moab 2 Mid Gore Tex boots really waterproof?

Yes, the Gore-Tex membrane provides genuine waterproof protection. I've tested these boots extensively in stream crossings, puddles, and sustained precipitation. Regular maintenance and proper care extend the waterproof lifespan significantly. The breathable nature of Gore-Tex prevents moisture buildup from internal perspiration while maintaining external water resistance.

What's the typical lifespan of these boots with regular hiking use?

Do these boots run true to size or should I size up or down?

Sizing generally runs true to standard athletic shoe measurements, though individual fit preferences influence optimal selection. I recommend trying boots with your preferred hiking socks, as thickness affects internal volume significantly. The generous toe box accommodates natural foot swelling during long hikes, reducing blister risk. Some users with narrow feet report loose fits requiring thicker socks or aftermarket insoles. Wide foot compatibility represents a significant strength of this design. Afternoon fitting sessions provide better size accuracy, as feet naturally expand throughout the day. Consider sizing up half-size if frequently wearing thick socks or have broader feet. REI and similar retailers offer excellent return policies facilitating proper fit confirmation.

Can these boots handle winter hiking and snow conditions?

The men's moab 2 mid gore tex provides adequate winter performance in specific conditions but has limitations requiring consideration. Dry snow conditions up to 6 inches depth work well with appropriate sock layering systems. Temperatures above 20°F remain comfortable with merino wool socks. The Gore-Tex membrane prevents snow infiltration while maintaining breathability during exertion. However, deep snow, extreme cold below 15°F, or icy conditions exceed these boots' capabilities. Vibram TC5+ traction performs adequately on packed snow but struggles with ice without microspikes or crampons. Insulation levels remain insufficient for extended cold weather exposure. Consider dedicated winter boots or supplementary traction devices for challenging winter conditions.

What maintenance is required to keep the Gore-Tex waterproofing effective?

Proper maintenance significantly extends Gore-Tex performance and overall boot lifespan. Clean boots immediately after muddy or salty conditions using mild soap and lukewarm water. Allow complete air drying before storage, avoiding direct heat sources that can damage membrane integrity. Apply DWR (Durable Water Repellent) treatments every 20-30 hiking days or when water no longer beads on the surface. Nikwax and Grangers offer excellent Gore-Tex compatible treatments. Store boots in well-ventilated areas with cedar shoe trees maintaining shape. Avoid petroleum-based products that can compromise membrane function. Regular inspection for punctures or tears enables early repair before waterproofing failure. Professional reproofing services are available when home treatments prove insufficient.
