Complete REI Hiking Boots Buying Guide

Understanding Fit and Sizing

REI's fitting process is comprehensive, but understanding the fundamentals helps you make better decisions. Your hiking boot size should typically be 0.5-1 size larger than your street shoe to accommodate foot swelling and thick socks.

Professional Fitting Tips:

  • Visit REI in the afternoon when feet are naturally swollen
  • Bring the socks you'll actually wear hiking
  • Test boots on REI's inclined ramp to check downhill fit
  • Walk around the store for at least 10 minutes
  • Consider custom insoles if you have specific foot issues

Frequently Asked Questions

Are REI hiking boots worth the price?

REI hiking boots offer excellent value through their co-op member benefits, generous return policy, and quality construction. While initially more expensive than big-box retailers, the long-term value becomes apparent through member dividends (10% back annually), expert fitting services, and the ability to return boots after extensive use if unsatisfied. For serious hikers who value customer service and product expertise, the premium is worthwhile. Budget-conscious hikers should consider REI Co-op brand boots or seasonal sales to get REI's service benefits at lower prices.

What's the difference between REI hiking boots and other brands?

REI carries premium brands like Salomon, Merrell, and KEEN alongside their own co-op brand. The difference lies in REI's expert fitting services, comprehensive return policy, and member benefits. Their staff expertise in matching boots to specific hiking needs sets them apart from general sporting goods stores. REI also curates their selection, ensuring only quality brands make it to their shelves. The co-op model means profits return to members as annual dividends, making the higher initial prices more reasonable over time.

How long do REI hiking boots typically last?

REI hiking boots typically last 300-800 miles depending on terrain, hiking frequency, and boot construction. Leather boots generally outlast synthetic ones, while boots used on rocky terrain wear faster than those used on soft trails. From my experience, Merrell Moab boots average 400-500 miles, while premium leather boots from brands like LOWA can exceed 600 miles. REI's fitting expertise helps maximize boot lifespan by ensuring proper fit, reducing premature wear from poor alignment or pressure points.

Should I buy REI hiking boots online or in-store?

For your first REI hiking boot purchase, visiting a store is highly recommended. REI's fitting expertise—including gait analysis, proper sizing, and personalized recommendations—is invaluable for finding boots that truly fit your feet and hiking style. Once you know your preferred brands and sizes from different manufacturers, online purchases become more viable. REI's generous return policy applies to both online and in-store purchases, but getting the fit right initially saves time and shipping costs. The investment in professional fitting pays off in comfort and performance on the trail.

What's REI's return policy for hiking boots?

REI offers a 100% satisfaction guarantee within one year of purchase for co-op members, and 90 days for non-members. This means you can return hiking boots even after extensive trail use if you're unsatisfied with their performance. The policy covers defects, fit issues, and general dissatisfaction. I've personally returned boots after 6 months due to durability issues, and the process was hassle-free. This generous policy removes the risk from investing in quality hiking boots, making REI's higher prices more palatable knowing you're protected if the boots don't meet expectations.
