Assess Your Hiking Style and Terrain

The first consideration is honestly evaluating your hiking habits and preferred terrain. Day hikers on well-maintained trails have different needs than backpackers carrying heavy loads across technical terrain. LOWA's lighter models like the Axos series excel for casual hiking, while the Renegade and Zephyr series handle more demanding conditions with greater support and durability.

Consider the seasonal conditions you'll encounter most frequently. If you primarily hike in dry conditions, non-waterproof models offer better breathability and lower cost. However, if you encounter wet conditions regularly or need versatility across seasons, GORE-TEX models provide essential weather protection. I've found that most hikers benefit from waterproof boots given the unpredictability of weather and terrain conditions.

Understanding Fit and Sizing

LOWA boots follow European sizing standards, which can differ significantly from American athletic shoe sizing. I strongly recommend getting professionally fitted or carefully measuring your feet before ordering online. LOWA boots tend to run true to European size with a slightly wider last than many American brands, making them suitable for hikers with wider feet who struggle with narrow hiking boots.

The key fitting indicators include adequate toe room (about thumb-width of space), secure heel hold without slipping, and no pressure points along the sides or top of the foot. Remember that hiking boots should accommodate thicker socks and slight foot swelling during long hiking days. When trying on boots, wear the socks you'll actually use for hiking, not thin dress socks.
