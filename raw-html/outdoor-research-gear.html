What Makes Outdoor Research Gear Stand Out

Founded in 1981 by Ron Gregg after a near-fatal accident in the Cascades, Outdoor Research emerged from a genuine need for better outdoor gear. What sets OR apart isn't just their Seattle heritage—it's their unwavering commitment to solving real problems faced by outdoor enthusiasts. Having tested their gear extensively across the demanding Pacific Northwest environment, I can attest that their reputation for durability and functionality is well-earned.

The company's approach to gear development is refreshingly practical. Rather than chasing fashion trends, they focus on creating products that perform when conditions turn harsh. Their famous Infinite Guarantee isn't just a marketing gimmick—it's a testament to their confidence in their products. This lifetime warranty covers normal wear and manufacturing defects, reflecting their commitment to gear that lasts.

What truly distinguishes Outdoor Research is their fabric innovation. Technologies like AscentShell provide waterproof-breathable protection that rivals Gore-Tex at a fraction of the weight. Their Ferrosi fabric combines stretch, breathability, and weather resistance in ways that make it ideal for active pursuits. These aren't just technical specs—they translate into real-world performance that keeps you comfortable during long days in unpredictable conditions.

The brand's athlete network provides crucial field testing that ensures products meet the demands of serious outdoor pursuits. From alpinists tackling Denali to trail runners covering ultra-distances, OR gear is tested by individuals who push equipment to its limits. This real-world validation process results in gear that consistently performs when failure isn't an option, making it a trusted choice for both weekend warriors and professional guides.

Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right OR Gear

Understanding Fabric Technologies

Outdoor Research employs various fabric technologies, each optimized for specific conditions and activities. AscentShell represents their premier waterproof-breathable technology, offering performance comparable to Gore-Tex while maintaining superior stretch and breathability. Having tested AscentShell pieces extensively, I find them particularly effective for high-output activities where traditional waterproof fabrics become clammy.

Ferrosi fabric, used in OR's popular softshell line, combines nylon and spandex for remarkable stretch while maintaining wind resistance and water repellency. This fabric excels in dynamic conditions where movement is crucial—rock scrambling, alpine climbing, or active hiking. Understanding these fabric characteristics helps match gear to intended use rather than simply choosing based on weather ratings.

Sizing and Fit Considerations

OR generally runs true to size with a slightly athletic cut that accommodates layering without excess bulk. However, specific pieces may vary—the Echo Hoodie runs slightly large while technical shells maintain trim profiles. I recommend consulting individual product reviews and OR's detailed sizing charts, paying particular attention to chest, sleeve, and torso measurements for optimal fit.

Consider intended layering systems when selecting sizes. Shells designed for alpine use typically accommodate thicker midlayers, while pieces intended for active use are cut closer to the body. Personal preference plays a role—some prefer roomier fits for comfort while others prioritize the performance benefits of closer-fitting technical garments.

How do I care for and maintain Outdoor Research gear?

Proper care extends OR gear lifespan significantly. Wash technical fabrics with specialized detergents like Nikwax or Grangers, avoiding fabric softeners that compromise DWR coatings. Air dry when possible, and periodically reapply DWR treatment to maintain water repellency. Store gear loosely to preserve insulation loft in down items. Following these practices, my OR gear has maintained performance through years of Pacific Northwest adventures, validating their quality construction and the importance of proper maintenance for long-term performance.

What's covered under Outdoor Research's Infinite Guarantee?

The Infinite Guarantee covers manufacturing defects and normal wear throughout the product's usable life. This includes seam separation, zipper failure, and fabric degradation from regular use. However, it excludes damage from accidents, improper care, or intentional misuse. I've successfully used this warranty twice for worn-out gaiters and a jacket with failing DWR coating, receiving excellent customer service and prompt replacements both times. This warranty demonstrates OR's confidence in their products and commitment to customer satisfaction.
