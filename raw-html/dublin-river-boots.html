Professional Fitting Tips from My Experience

After personally fitting dublin river boots for dozens of riders at our local equestrian center, I've developed a foolproof approach that prevents the common sizing mistakes that lead to returns and discomfort.

The Sock Test Method:

Wear the thick riding or wool socks you'll typically use, then measure your foot in the afternoon when it's naturally expanded. This accounts for the swelling that occurs during active use.

Calf Width Considerations:

Dublin river boots offer regular, wide, and extra-wide calf options. Measure the widest part of your calf while wearing pants you'd typically ride in. Most riders need wide calf for comfortable fit over riding pants.

The primary difference lies in waterproofing coverage. Dublin River III features waterproof protection only to ankle level, making them suitable for light moisture and dry conditions. Do Dublin River Boots require special maintenance or care?

Dublin river boots require minimal but consistent maintenance to preserve their performance and appearance. After each use, remove mud and debris with a soft brush or damp cloth. Allow boots to air dry completely before storage, avoiding direct heat sources which can crack leather. Apply quality leather conditioner every 2-3 months to maintain suppleness and water resistance. For heavily soiled boots, use saddle soap for deep cleaning. The waterproof membrane doesn't require special treatment but benefits from regular cleaning to maintain breathability. Store boots in a cool, dry location with boot trees or newspaper stuffing to maintain shape. With this simple care routine, the boots will maintain their functionality and appearance for years of reliable service.
