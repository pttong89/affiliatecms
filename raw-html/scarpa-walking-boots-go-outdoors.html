Complete Buying Guide and Sizing Tips

Understanding SCARPA Sizing and Fit

SCARPA's Italian sizing system differs significantly from standard UK measurements, making proper fitting essential for optimal performance. Their unique last system accommodates European foot shapes, typically running slightly narrower than British equivalents. This characteristic means most hikers require half to full size larger than their normal shoe size.

The fitting process at GO Outdoors involves several critical steps that prevent future problems. First, measure feet during afternoon hours when natural swelling has occurred, mimicking conditions during actual hiking. Second, wear your preferred hiking socks during fitting, as sock thickness significantly affects internal volume. Third, ensure adequate toe room – approximately thumb-width clearance prevents black toenails during descents.

GO Outdoors staff utilize specialized measuring devices that assess not only length but also width and arch characteristics. This comprehensive approach identifies the optimal SCARPA model for your specific foot shape. Different models accommodate varying foot volumes, with the Terra series favoring medium to wide feet while Rush models suit narrower profiles.

Common Sizing Mistake: Never assume your regular shoe size applies to SCARPA boots. Always get professionally fitted, especially for your first SCARPA purchase.

Maintenance and Care for Maximum Longevity

Daily Care and Cleaning Procedures

Proper maintenance begins immediately after each hiking trip, determining whether your SCARPA walking boots go outdoors investment lasts two years or twenty. The golden rule involves never storing dirty boots, as accumulated debris accelerates material degradation and compromises waterproofing systems. Remove mud, vegetation, and salt deposits before they dry and become embedded in leather or fabric surfaces.

The cleaning process starts with removing laces and insoles, allowing thorough access to all boot components. Use lukewarm water and soft brushes to remove surface contamination, avoiding harsh detergents that strip natural oils from leather. For stubborn mud, allow boots to dry partially before brushing, as completely wet mud spreads rather than removes effectively.

Drying requires patience and proper technique to prevent damage. Never place boots near direct heat sources like radiators, fires, or heated towel rails, as rapid drying causes leather cracking and sole separation. Instead, stuff boots with newspaper or specialized boot dryers, changing paper every few hours until completely dry. This process typically requires 24-48 hours depending on conditions and boot construction.

Pro Maintenance Tip: Rotate between two pairs of boots when hiking regularly. This allows proper drying time between uses and extends the life of both pairs significantly.

Waterproofing and Treatment Schedule

Waterproofing maintenance varies between Gore-Tex lined and non-membrane boots, requiring different approaches for optimal results. Gore-Tex boots need external DWR (Durable Water Repellent) treatment renewal every 6-12 months depending on usage frequency. When water stops beading on the surface and begins soaking into the outer material, it's time for re-treatment.

Leather boots require more intensive care including cleaning, conditioning, and waterproofing in separate steps. Monthly treatment with appropriate leather conditioners maintains flexibility and prevents cracking, while waterproofing products should be applied every 2-3 months or after heavy use. The key is using products specifically designed for outdoor footwear rather than general leather care items.

Application technique matters as much as product selection. Clean boots thoroughly before applying any treatments, ensuring products can penetrate properly. Apply conditioners and waterproofing agents in thin, even coats, allowing complete absorption between applications. Excess product can clog membrane pores or attract dirt, compromising performance rather than enhancing it.

Long-term Storage and Seasonal Preparation

Proper storage during inactive periods prevents deterioration and ensures boots remain ready for immediate use. Clean and completely dry boots before storage, applying appropriate conditioners to prevent leather desiccation. Cedar shoe trees maintain shape while absorbing residual moisture and odors, particularly important for expensive boots like SCARPA models.

Storage location should be cool, dry, and well-ventilated, avoiding areas with temperature extremes or direct sunlight. Basements and garages often have humidity issues that promote mold growth, while attics can become too hot and dry. A bedroom closet or dedicated gear storage area typically provides ideal conditions for long-term boot preservation.
