Frequently Asked Questions

How does the wide version of Lowa Renegade GTX fit compared to regular width?

The Lowa Renegade GTX wide version provides significantly more room in the forefoot area, with approximately 2-3mm additional width in the toe box. However, many users find that even the wide version can feel snug initially due to the boot's construction. The wide last offers greater volume for people with bunions, flat feet, or naturally wider feet, but I recommend trying them on with your intended hiking socks for the best fit assessment. The mid-wide version specifically addresses the needs of hikers who need both ankle support and extra width.

Are Lowa Renegade GTX Wide boots worth the premium price?

At around $240-280, the Lowa Renegade GTX wide represents excellent value for serious hikers with wide feet. I've found these boots last 500-1000+ miles depending on terrain, making the cost-per-mile reasonable. What's the break-in period like for Lowa Renegade GTX Wide boots?

The break-in period for Lowa Renegade GTX wide typically takes 20-40 hours of wear, or about 2-3 weeks of regular use. Start with short walks around the house, then progress to light day hikes. The nubuck leather will gradually soften and conform to your foot shape. I recommend wearing your thickest hiking socks during break-in and using leather conditioner to accelerate the process while maintaining the boot's water resistance. The wide construction actually helps during break-in, as there's less pressure on vulnerable areas compared to narrow boots.

Can I use custom insoles with Lowa Renegade GTX Wide boots?

Yes, the Lowa Renegade GTX wide comes with removable insoles, making them compatible with custom orthotics or aftermarket insoles. The wide last provides extra volume that accommodates thicker custom insoles without compromising fit. I've successfully used Superfeet and Dr. Scholl's insoles in mine. Just ensure your replacement insoles are designed for wide feet and don't exceed the original thickness, as this could affect the boot's internal volume and comfort. The women's version also accepts custom insoles with the same considerations.

How do Lowa Renegade GTX Wide boots perform in different weather conditions?

The Gore-Tex lining makes these boots excellent for wet conditions, keeping feet completely dry in rain, snow, and stream crossings. However, breathability is limited, making them less ideal for hot summer hiking. The boots excel in spring and fall conditions, and the wide design doesn't compromise waterproofing. In winter, they provide good insulation but aren't specifically designed for extreme cold. I've found them most comfortable in temperatures between 30-70Â°F. For color options that work in different seasons, check out the navy colorway or navy mid version.

What size should I order in Lowa Renegade GTX Wide compared to my regular shoe size?

Most hikers should order their true-to-size in Lowa Renegade GTX wide boots. Unlike some European brands that run small, Lowa's sizing aligns well with US standards. However, try them on in the afternoon when your feet are naturally swollen, and wear your intended hiking socks. Leave about a thumb's width (1/2 inch) between your longest toe and the boot's end. If you're between sizes, I recommend going up rather than down, as the wide last provides the width you need. The women's sizing follows the same guidelines but uses women's-specific last construction.
