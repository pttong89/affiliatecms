Sizing and Fit Guide

Getting the right fit in she outdoor hunting boots is crucial for both comfort and performance. Through years of fitting boots for female hunters and my own extensive field experience, I've learned that proper sizing goes far beyond simply knowing your normal shoe size. Women's hunting boots require careful attention to width, arch support, and toe room to prevent the common issues that can ruin a hunting experience.

Start by measuring your feet at the end of the day when they're slightly swollen, as this more accurately represents how your feet will feel after hours of hunting. SHE outdoor hunting boots tend to run true to size, but I recommend trying them on with the actual hunting socks you'll wear afield. Thick merino wool hunting socks can significantly impact fit, so factor this into your sizing decision.

The woman-specific last used in SHE outdoor boots accommodates the typical female foot shape with a narrower heel and lower instep. However, foot shapes vary significantly among individuals. If you have wider feet, consider ordering a half-size up, while narrow-footed hunters may find their normal size provides the best fit. Pay particular attention to heel slippage – your heel should stay firmly planted when walking, especially on inclines.

For hunters investing in quality women's hunting boots, break-in time is essential. Start wearing your new SHE outdoor hunting boots around the house and on short walks weeks before your hunting season. This gradual break-in process allows the materials to conform to your feet and identifies any potential pressure points before they become serious problems in the field.

Pro Tip from the Field:

Always try on boots with the insoles removed first. If your foot hangs over the edges of the footbed, you need a larger size regardless of how the boot feels with the insole in place.

What insulation options are available in SHE outdoor hunting boots?

SHE outdoor offers insulation ranging from uninsulated models perfect for early season and active hunting, to heavily insulated boots with up to 1200 grams for extreme cold weather hunting. The 400-gram models work well for most hunting conditions, providing warmth without excessive bulk. I recommend 800-gram insulation for stand hunting in temperatures below 30°F, while 1200-gram boots are ideal for ice fishing or prolonged exposure to frigid conditions. Consider your hunting style – active hunters generate more body heat and need less insulation than those sitting motionless for hours.

How should I care for and maintain my SHE outdoor hunting boots?

Proper care significantly extends the life of your she outdoor hunting boots. After each hunt, remove mud and debris with a soft brush and lukewarm water. Allow boots to air dry completely before storage, but never place them near direct heat sources which can damage the waterproof membrane. Apply a quality leather conditioner to leather models every few months, and inspect the waterproof treatment annually. If water begins to bead poorly on the exterior, reapply a DWR treatment. Store boots in a cool, dry place with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture.
