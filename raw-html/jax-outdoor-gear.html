The JAX Story: 70 Years of Outdoor Excellence

The story of JAX outdoor gear begins in 1955 when Marvin and Lola Quinlan opened Ames Surplus in their home state of Iowa. What started as a small operation selling government surplus items from their home has evolved into one of the most trusted names in outdoor retail across the Midwest and Mountain West. The Quinlan family's vision was simple yet powerful: provide quality outdoor equipment with exceptional customer service that treats every customer like family.

Under the leadership of Jim Quinlan, Marvin and Lola's son, the business expanded significantly throughout the 1980s and 1990s. The first Colorado location opened in Fort Collins, recognizing the growing outdoor recreation market along the Front Range. This strategic expansion allowed JAX outdoor gear to tap into Colorado's booming outdoor culture while maintaining its Midwest roots and values.

In 2022, JAX made a significant transition by becoming an employee-owned company, ensuring that the culture of customer service and community involvement that defined the Quinlan family's approach would continue for generations. This employee ownership model has strengthened JAX's commitment to both customers and local communities, with staff members who are genuinely passionate about the outdoors and invested in the company's success.

Pack fitting involves loading the pack with weight and adjusting for your torso length and hiking style. Staff share personal experience with products and honest assessments of suitability. Allow extra time for consultations - rushing defeats the purpose of JAX outdoor gear's personalized approach.
