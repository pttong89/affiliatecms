Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Kinvara models and which should I buy?

The main differences between Saucony Kinvara models involve stack height and minor upper refinements. The Kinvara 15 currently offers the best balance with its 29mm stack height, improved breathability, and refined fit. If budget is a concern, the Kinvara 14 remains excellent despite its slightly higher stack. The new Kinvara 16 shows minimal changes from the successful 15. I recommend the 15 for most runners seeking the optimal Kinvara experience, while the 13 works well for those preferring the lowest possible stack height.

Are Saucony Kinvaras good for beginners or long distance running?

The Saucony Kinvara can work for beginners with efficient running form, but it's not ideal for everyone starting out. The 4mm drop and firm platform require stronger calves and adapted running mechanics. For long distances, I've successfully run up to half marathon distance in Kinvaras, but they're better suited for runs under 15 miles. Beginners should consider starting with just a few miles per week in Kinvaras while maintaining a higher-cushioned shoe for longer runs. The key is gradual adaptation â€“ don't switch completely overnight from traditional trainers.

How does the Saucony Kinvara compare to Nike Pegasus or Brooks Ghost?

The Saucony Kinvara sits in a different category than the Nike Pegasus or Brooks Ghost, which are traditional neutral trainers with higher drops (10-12mm) and more cushioning. Kinvaras are significantly lighter (6.7 oz vs 8.5+ oz), have less stack height, and offer more ground feel. While Pegasus and Ghost excel for easy miles and recovery runs, the Kinvara shines during tempo work and speed training. Think of it as a more minimal, performance-oriented alternative that rewards efficient running form but requires more from the runner in terms of strength and technique.

Do Saucony Kinvaras run true to size and how's the durability?

Saucony Kinvaras run remarkably true to size with consistent sizing across different models â€“ a major advantage for online ordering. However, they do run slightly narrow, so runners with wide feet should consider the available wide options. Durability has been excellent in my testing, with most pairs lasting 250-400 miles depending on running surfaces and body weight. The exposed foam outsole design initially concerned me, but modern PWRRUN foam proves quite resilient. I typically see wear on the heel strike area first, but the rest of the sole maintains its structure well beyond 300 miles.

Can I use Kinvaras for racing and what about wet weather performance?

The Saucony Kinvara makes an excellent racing shoe for 5K through half marathon distances, offering lightweight performance without the cost of carbon-plated racers. I've raced multiple times in various Kinvara models with great success. However, wet weather performance is a limitation due to minimal rubber coverage. On wet pavement or trails, I exercise extra caution and often choose shoes with more aggressive tread patterns. For racing in dry conditions, especially on tracks or roads, the Kinvara provides an ideal combination of lightweight speed and adequate cushioning for most runners.
