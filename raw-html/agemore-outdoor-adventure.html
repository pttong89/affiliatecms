How should I care for and maintain my AGEMORE sleeping bag?

Proper care extends the life of AGEMORE sleeping bags significantly. Always store bags uncompressed in breathable storage sacks - never leave them in compression sacks long-term. Wash only when necessary using gentle, down-specific detergents and low-heat drying cycles with tennis balls to maintain loft. Air-dry thoroughly after each camping trip to prevent mold and mildew. The cotton flannel linings require special attention - spot clean when possible and ensure complete drying before storage. I've found that properly maintained AGEMORE bags maintain their loft and warmth for years. Avoid storing in damp conditions and periodically fluff the insulation to prevent clumping. Minor zipper issues can often be resolved with zipper lubricant or graphite from a pencil.

Are AGEMORE sleeping bags waterproof or just water-resistant?

AGEMORE sleeping bags feature water-resistant 210T polyester shells rather than fully waterproof materials. This design choice provides better breathability while offering protection against light moisture and condensation. During my testing in various weather conditions, the bags effectively repelled morning dew and light drizzle but shouldn't be relied upon as primary rain protection. For wet conditions, I always recommend using a quality tent or tarp shelter. The water-resistant coating has proven durable through multiple washing cycles and extensive use. While not completely waterproof, the moisture resistance is sufficient for typical camping scenarios where proper shelter is used. The breathable fabric prevents internal condensation better than fully waterproof materials.
