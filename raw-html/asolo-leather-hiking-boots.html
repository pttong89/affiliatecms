Sizing and Fit Considerations

Asolo uses European sizing, which typically runs about half a size smaller than American sizing. However, proper fitting goes far beyond simple size conversion. I recommend trying boots on in the afternoon when feet are slightly swollen, mimicking hiking conditions. Wear your actual hiking socks – the sock thickness significantly impacts fit and comfort. Your heel should lock securely with no slipping, while toes should have approximately half-inch clearance.

The European last shape tends to be narrower than American boots, which works exceptionally well for people with normal to narrow feet but can be problematic for wide feet. Asolo does offer wide width options in select models, and some users size up and use thicker insoles to accommodate wider feet. Proper fit is crucial because leather boots, while they'll mold to your feet, won't stretch significantly in width.
