Buffalo Aspha boots typically run 0.5 to 1 size smaller than standard US sizing.

Based on my testing and user feedback, I recommend ordering at least a half size up from your normal shoe size, possibly a full size up if you have wider feet or plan to wear thick hiking socks.

Sizing Guide

  • • Narrow feet: Order 0.5 sizes up
  • • Normal width: Order 0.5-1 sizes up
  • • Wide feet: Order 1-1.5 sizes up
  • • Thick socks: Add 0.5 additional sizes
  • • Extended wear: Consider 1 full size up

Break-in Timeline

  • • Day 1-2: Indoor wear for 2-3 hours
  • • Day 3-5: Short walks (1-2 miles)
  • • Week 2: Medium hikes (3-5 miles)
  • • Week 3+: Full hiking adventures
  • • Complete: Usually within 20-30 miles

Care and Maintenance Protocol

Proper care extends the life of your Buffalo Aspha boots significantly. After a year of intensive testing, including muddy trails and stream crossings, my boots still look and perform exceptionally well due to consistent maintenance practices.

After Each Hike:
  • • Remove dirt and debris with soft brush
  • • Wipe down with damp cloth
  • • Air dry away from direct heat
  • • Remove insoles if very wet
Weekly Maintenance:
  • • Deep clean with mild soap solution
  • • Condition synthetic materials if needed
  • • Check for wear patterns
  • • Rotate between multiple pairs if possible

Common Fit Issues and Solutions

Issue: Heel slipping during downhill sections

Solution: Try heel grips, tighter lacing, or consider sizing down if excessive room exists.

Issue: Toe pressure on long descents

Solution: Ensure adequate toe room (thumb width) and consider toe separators or different lacing techniques.

Issue: Platform feels unstable initially

Solution: Start with easier terrain and allow 5-10 hikes for adaptation. The stability improves with familiarity.
