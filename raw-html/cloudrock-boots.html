What Are Cloudrock Boots?

Cloudrock boots represent On Running's bold entry into the hiking footwear market, combining their signature CloudTec technology with robust outdoor-ready construction. These Swiss-engineered boots blur the line between lightweight trail runners and traditional hiking boots, creating what On calls "speed hiking" footwear. The revolutionary CloudTec sole features hollow pods that compress on impact and spring back for energy return, delivering a uniquely cushioned hiking experience.

What sets cloudrock boots apart is their lightweight construction - typically weighing 2-2.5 pounds per pair compared to traditional hiking boots that often exceed 3 pounds. The synthetic upper materials provide quick break-in times while maintaining durability for rugged trail use. On's proprietary waterproof membrane offers weather protection without the bulk of traditional hiking boot construction, making these boots ideal for fast-paced hiking adventures.

The On Cloudrock series includes multiple variants designed for different hiking styles and conditions. From the original Cloudrock to the updated Cloudrock 2 with enhanced features, these boots cater to day hikers, ultralight backpackers, and outdoor enthusiasts who prioritize comfort and agility on the trail. The innovative design philosophy emphasizes natural foot movement while providing essential protection and support for extended hiking adventures.

Key Innovation: On's CloudTec technology uses hollow pods that compress individually, providing targeted cushioning exactly where your foot needs it most during hiking.

Performance & Features Analysis

After logging over 300 miles in cloudrock boots across diverse terrains from desert slickrock to forest trails, I've gained deep insights into their real-world performance. The CloudTec cushioning system delivers exceptional comfort on hard-packed trails and rocky surfaces, effectively absorbing impact that would typically jar your feet in traditional hiking boots. However, this comfort comes with trade-offs in certain conditions.

Traction and Grip Performance

The MissionGrip outsole compound provides adequate traction on dry rock and hardpacked dirt, but struggles in muddy or loose conditions. During my testing in Olympic National Park's notoriously muddy trails, the relatively shallow lug depth and softer rubber compound resulted in less confidence-inspiring grip compared to traditional Vibram-soled boots. The hiking boot traction works best on well-maintained trails rather than technical off-trail terrain.

Waterproofing and Breathability

On's proprietary waterproof membrane performs admirably in light to moderate wet conditions. I tested the waterproof hiking boot capabilities during stream crossings and morning dew scenarios with excellent results. However, user reports suggest waterproofing effectiveness may decline after 150-200 miles of use, particularly in challenging wet conditions. Breathability surpasses many competitors, reducing foot overheating during strenuous climbs.

Support and Stability

The polypropylene shank provides moderate underfoot support, suitable for day hiking with light to moderate pack weights (up to 25-30 pounds). The ankle collar height offers basic ankle support without the restriction of traditional hiking boots. For technical terrain or heavy backpacking loads, the waterproof hiking boots may feel insufficient compared to full-duty backpacking boots.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Cloudrock boots good for hiking?

Cloudrock boots are excellent for day hiking and light backpacking on well-maintained trails. They offer superior comfort with CloudTec cushioning technology, lightweight construction, and moderate ankle support. However, they're not ideal for heavy backpacking loads over 30 pounds or technical terrain with loose rocks and mud due to their softer outsole compound. I've found them perfect for National Park day hikes, speed hiking adventures, and casual outdoor exploration where comfort takes priority over maximum durability.

How long do Cloudrock boots last?

Based on user experiences and my own testing, Cloudrock boots typically last 200-400 miles depending on terrain and usage patterns. The synthetic upper materials are durable, but the soft CloudTec midsole compound may show wear sooner than traditional hiking boots. Users report waterproofing effectiveness declining after 150-200 miles of use in wet conditions. For casual hikers doing 50-100 miles per year, expect 2-4 years of service life. Heavy users covering 200+ miles annually may need replacement within 1-2 years.

What's the difference between Cloudrock and Cloudrock 2?

The Cloudrock 2 features several improvements over the original: upgraded MissionGrip outsole with better traction, revised lacing system with four hooks for better ankle support, reduced weight by several ounces, and enhanced waterproof membrane with improved breathability. The Cloudrock 2 also has a more protective rubber outsole covering the CloudTec pods, addressing durability concerns from the original model. After testing both versions, I strongly recommend the Cloudrock 2 for the superior traction and enhanced ankle lockdown system.

Do Cloudrock boots run true to size?

Cloudrock boots generally run true to size, with most hikers sizing up a half size from their street shoe size for optimal comfort. The toe box offers adequate space for toe movement, and the boot has a snug but not restrictive fit around the midfoot and heel. Try them on with hiking socks to ensure proper fit for extended wear. Women with narrow feet may find the standard width slightly roomy, while those with wider feet report comfortable fit without needing wide sizes in most cases.

Can you use Cloudrock boots for winter hiking?

Cloudrock waterproof models can handle light winter conditions and dry snow, but they're not designed for serious winter hiking. The boots lack insulation and aggressive traction for icy conditions. For winter hiking, consider adding microspikes or crampons for traction, and wear warm wool socks. They're better suited for three-season use. The waterproof membrane helps with slushy conditions, but the shallow tread pattern struggles on packed snow and ice. I recommend dedicated winter boots for serious cold-weather hiking adventures.
