ACE Adventure Camping Overview

When people search for "ace adventure camping," they're looking for more than just a place to pitch a tent – they want an all-encompassing outdoor adventure experience. Located on 1,500 wooded acres adjacent to New River Gorge National Park in West Virginia, ACE Adventure Resort has perfected the art of combining rugged outdoor camping with resort-style amenities and world-class adventure activities.

What sets ACE Adventure camping apart from typical campgrounds is its comprehensive approach to outdoor recreation. This isn't just a camping destination; it's an adventure hub where you can start your day with mountain-top sunrise views, spend afternoons navigating Class V rapids on the famous Gauley River, and end your evening around a campfire sharing stories with fellow adventurers from across the country.

The combination of convenience, adventure variety, and community atmosphere is unmatched. They have individual bathrooms with hot showers. Want to try adventure camping but don't own gear? They offer complete rental packages. This flexibility makes ACE Adventure camping accessible to everyone from hardcore backpackers to families trying camping for the first time.

Camping Options & Accommodations

ACE Adventure camping offers diverse accommodation options to suit every budget, group size, and comfort preference. Understanding these choices is crucial for planning your perfect New River Gorge adventure. Here's my detailed breakdown of each option based on personal experience and extensive research.

Mountain Top Camping

The most popular choice for traditional adventure bound camping enthusiasts. These spacious sites offer stunning mountain views, fire rings, and picnic tables. Each site accommodates tents and provides access to clean restroom facilities within walking distance.

Rate: $15 per person, per night

Best for: Families, groups wanting authentic camping experience

RV Camping Sites

Full-hookup RV sites accommodate everything from pop-up campers to large motorhomes. These sites include water, electric, and sewer connections, making them perfect for extended stays or those who prefer RV comfort while enjoying outdoor adventures.

Rate: $35-50 per night depending on site size

Best for: RV owners, longer stays, families with young children

Request sites N15-N25 for the best sunrise views and wind protection. Sites closer to the bathhouse (Q10-Q20) offer convenience but can be noisier during peak season. For RV camping, full-hookup sites near the main resort provide easy access to dining and activities but sacrifice some mountain atmosphere.

Adventure Activities & Experiences

The true magic of ACE Adventure camping lies in the incredible variety of activities available right from your campsite. This isn't just about having things to do – it's about having access to some of the East Coast's premier adventure experiences without the hassle of traveling between multiple operators or locations.

Whitewater Rafting: The Crown Jewel

Whitewater rafting is undoubtedly the star attraction that draws most people to ACE Adventure camping. The New River and Gauley River systems offer some of North America's most exciting and scenic whitewater experiences. What makes ACE special is their range of options suitable for every skill level and age group.

The Upper New River trip is perfect for beginners and families. With Class I-III rapids, it provides excitement without overwhelming first-timers. I've guided friends and family on this trip multiple times, and it never fails to create lasting memories. The scenery is spectacular, with towering canyon walls and abundant wildlife sightings. Professional guides share local history and geology, making it educational as well as thrilling.

Rafting Pros

  • Professional guides with extensive safety training
  • All equipment provided including wetsuits
  • Riverside lunch included on full-day trips
  • Transportation to/from campground included
  • Options for all skill levels available

Rafting Cons

  • Weather-dependent cancellations possible
  • Peak season crowds can mean larger raft groups
  • Additional cost beyond camping fees
  • Physical demands may challenge some participants
  • Limited photography opportunities during active rapids

Beyond whitewater rafting, ACE Adventure camping provides access to zip line canopy tours, rock climbing instruction, mountain biking trails, and their unique 5-acre waterpark built on a natural spring-fed lake. The zip line tour is particularly noteworthy as it's the only one in the area offering views of the New River Gorge Bridge – an engineering marvel and popular tourist destination.

For evening entertainment, the Lost Paddle restaurant and bar creates a social hub where campers gather to share adventure stories over local craft beers and hearty mountain cuisine. The atmosphere is casual and welcoming, with live music on weekends during peak season. This community aspect significantly enhances the overall ACE Adventure camping experience, creating connections with like-minded outdoor enthusiasts from diverse backgrounds.

Lessons Learned the Hard Way

My first ACE Adventure camping trip taught me valuable lessons about gear selection. I brought cotton clothing that stayed damp for days after rafting, creating uncomfortable and potentially dangerous conditions during cool mountain evenings.

Similarly, cheap rain gear failed during an unexpected afternoon thunderstorm, soaking everything in my tent. Investing in quality waterproof equipment isn't just about comfort – it's about safety and trip enjoyment. The mountains demand respect through proper preparation.

Seasonal Considerations

ACE Adventure camping gear needs vary significantly by season. Spring (April-May) requires versatile layering systems as temperatures can swing from near-freezing mornings to warm afternoons. Rain gear is essential as spring brings frequent showers that can quickly flood low-lying areas.

Summer camping (June-August) focuses on heat management and sun protection. Lightweight, breathable fabrics become crucial, along with quality camping boots that can handle both hiking trails and river environments. Don't underestimate the importance of proper footwear – I've seen numerous injuries from inadequate shoes during water activities.

Advance Booking Strategy

ACE Adventure camping operates on a hybrid reservation system that requires understanding for optimal results. Mountain-top camping sites can typically be booked 2-3 months in advance for regular seasons, but Gauley season (September-October) requires 4-6 months advance planning due to extreme popularity among whitewater enthusiasts nationwide.

The key insight I've learned is that activity reservations and camping reservations operate independently but affect each other significantly. Book your priority activities first (especially rafting), then secure camping accommodations that complement your activity schedule. This approach prevents the disappointment of having great camping but missing out on preferred adventure experiences.

Insider Booking Tips from a Repeat Visitor

Call ACE directly rather than booking online when possible. Their reservation staff can provide real-time availability updates, suggest optimal activity sequencing, and sometimes access last-minute cancellations not yet reflected in online systems.

I've secured prime Gauley season reservations through phone conversations when online systems showed full availability. The personal touch often reveals flexible options that automated systems can't accommodate.

Weather Contingency Planning

Mountain weather patterns around New River Gorge change rapidly and dramatically impact both camping comfort and activity availability. Successful ACE Adventure camping requires flexible planning that accommodates weather disruptions while maintaining trip enjoyment.

Always pack for conditions 20 degrees colder than forecasted and include rain protection regardless of predictions. Mountain elevation creates microclimates that valley weather forecasts don't accurately reflect. I've experienced surprise snow in May and unexpected heat waves in October – both scenarios requiring gear and attitude adjustments.

Consider building buffer time into your activity schedule. Weather-cancelled rafting trips can usually be rescheduled within your stay, but only if you have flexible timing. My most successful trips include one "flex day" dedicated to backup activities or extended relaxation, depending on weather conditions and energy levels.

Sample 3-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive, set up camp, explore resort facilities, sunset at New River Gorge Bridge

Day 2: Full-day Lower New River rafting, evening at Lost Paddle restaurant and bar

Day 3: Morning zip line tour, afternoon waterpark or hiking, evening campfire and star gazing

Note: Build in weather alternatives and don't over-schedule – part of camping's magic is spontaneous relaxation.

Food planning deserves special consideration for ACE Adventure camping. The resort offers excellent dining options, but costs accumulate quickly for longer stays or larger groups. I typically plan a hybrid approach: utilize on-site dining for one meal per day (usually dinner for social atmosphere) while preparing breakfast and lunch at camp. This strategy balances convenience, cost, and camping authenticity. Stock up on camping food supplies before arriving, as local options are limited and expensive.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes ACE Adventure camping different from other New River Gorge campgrounds?

ACE Adventure camping stands out with its comprehensive resort amenities including on-site whitewater rafting, zipline tours, waterpark access, multiple dining options, and various lodging levels from tent camping to luxury cabins. The 1,500-acre property offers both mountain-top camping with scenic views and full-hookup RV sites, plus unique features like equipment rentals and pre-setup tent options. Unlike traditional campgrounds, ACE integrates adventure activities directly with accommodations, eliminating travel between locations and providing professional guides for all skill levels. The community atmosphere, quality facilities, and convenient access to New River Gorge National Park activities create an unmatched adventure vacation experience.

What camping gear is essential for ACE Adventure camping trips?

Essential gear includes a quality waterproof tent, sleeping bag rated for mountain temperatures, camping cookware set, waterproof dry bags for rafting days, quick-dry clothing, and sturdy hiking boots. ACE offers tent and sleeping kit rentals for $15 total, making it convenient for fly-in visitors. Don't forget waterproof storage for electronics and extra clothes for post-rafting comfort. Mountain weather changes rapidly, so pack layers and rain protection regardless of forecasts. For water activities, synthetic clothing that retains warmth when wet is crucial. Consider multi-function gear like portable camp stoves and lightweight cookware to maximize space efficiency while ensuring camping comfort and safety.

When is the best time to visit ACE Adventure camping?

Peak season is May through October, with June-August offering warmest weather and full activity operations. September-October features stunning fall foliage and famous Gauley River rafting season but requires advance booking. Spring (April-May) provides cooler temperatures, fewer crowds, and wildflower blooms, perfect for hiking and early season rafting. Each season offers unique advantages depending on your preferred activities and weather tolerance. Fall is most popular among serious whitewater enthusiasts due to optimal river conditions. Summer provides maximum activity availability and warmest camping weather but expect larger crowds. Spring offers excellent value with moderate weather and reduced reservation pressure.

Can beginners handle whitewater rafting from ACE Adventure camping?

Absolutely! ACE offers trips for all skill levels, from beginner-friendly Upper New River (Class I-III rapids) to advanced Lower New River and Gauley River experiences. Professional guides provide safety instruction and equipment. I recommend starting with the Upper New River trip, which is perfect for families and first-time rafters, featuring gentler rapids and scenic mountain views. All necessary equipment including wetsuits, helmets, and life jackets is provided. Guides are extensively trained in water rescue and first aid. The Upper New River offers thrilling excitement without overwhelming beginners, while experienced rafters can challenge themselves on Class V Gauley River rapids during fall season.

How much should I budget for an ACE Adventure camping trip?

Budget $15-25 per person nightly for mountain-top camping, $35-50 for RV sites with hookups. Whitewater rafting costs $89-139 per person depending on the trip. Add $30-50 daily for meals if using on-site dining, or bring your own groceries. Total weekend cost typically ranges $200-400 per person including camping, one rafting trip, and meals, making it excellent value for an adventure vacation. Additional activities like zip lining ($69-89) and waterpark access add to costs but provide unique experiences. Equipment rentals are reasonably priced if you don't own camping gear. Consider package deals that bundle activities with accommodations for potential savings.
