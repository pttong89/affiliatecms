Pro Sizing Tips

Proper fit is crucial for hiking performance. Here's what I've learned from fitting hundreds of hikers:

  • Teva sandals typically run 1/2 size large - size down for most people
  • Your heel should sit comfortably in the heel cup with minimal overhang
  • Allow 1/4 inch of space beyond your longest toe when straps are secured
  • Try on sandals in the afternoon when feet are naturally slightly swollen
  • Adjustable straps accommodate width variations, but length must be correct

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Teva sandals good for hiking?

Teva sandals excel at hiking due to their durable construction, excellent traction, and quick-drying materials. The Hurricane XLT2 features a cushioned EVA midsole for comfort, aggressive outsole lugs for grip, and adjustable straps for a secure fit. They're lightweight, breathable, and perform well in wet conditions, making them ideal for water crossings and humid climates. The open design prevents overheating while maintaining the support needed for moderate hiking terrain.

Should I choose Hurricane XLT2 or Original Universal for hiking?

For hiking, the Hurricane XLT2 is superior to the Original Universal. The XLT2 offers 31mm of heel cushioning versus 16mm in the Universal, more aggressive tread patterns, better arch support, and enhanced stability. While the Universal works for light trails, the Hurricane XLT2 handles moderate to challenging terrain more effectively with improved shock absorption and durability. The additional investment pays off for regular hikers.

How do Teva sandals compare to Chaco for hiking?

Teva sandals are lighter and more versatile for varied terrain, while Chacos offer superior arch support and durability. Tevas excel in quick-drying situations and provide a more sneaker-like feel. Chacos are better for hikers needing maximum support and don't mind extra weight. Both brands perform well, but Tevas offer better value and versatility for most hiking scenarios. The choice often comes down to personal foot shape and arch support needs.

Can I wear Teva sandals for long-distance hiking?

Yes, many hikers successfully complete long-distance trails in Teva sandals. The Hurricane XLT2 provides adequate cushioning and support for extended wear. However, your feet need time to adapt, and sandals offer less protection than boots. Start with shorter hikes to build tolerance, consider terrain difficulty, and pack blister prevention supplies for longer adventures. Many thru-hikers choose Tevas for their lightweight, quick-drying properties on trails with frequent water crossings.

What size should I choose for Teva hiking sandals?

Teva recommends sizing down if you're a half-size, as their sandals tend to run slightly large. Ensure your heel sits properly in the heel cup with about 1/4 inch space at the toe. The adjustable straps accommodate width variations, but the length must be correct. Try them on later in the day when feet are slightly swollen for the most accurate fit. If between sizes, choose the smaller size for a more secure hiking experience.
