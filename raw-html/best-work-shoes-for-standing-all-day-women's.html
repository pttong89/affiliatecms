What size should I buy for work shoes?

Shop for work shoes in the afternoon when your feet are naturally swollen, similar to how they'll be during work shifts. Consider going up a half size from your regular shoe size to accommodate foot swelling and thicker work socks. Ensure there's thumb-width space between your longest toe and shoe front. Women's feet can swell significantly during long work periods, especially in warm environments or during certain times of the menstrual cycle. Professional fitting is recommended, as many people wear incorrect sizes. Consider the type of socks you'll wear at work‚Äîcompression socks or cushioned work socks may require additional room. Width is equally important as length‚Äînarrow or wide feet need appropriate sizing to prevent pressure points. If you're between sizes, sizing up is generally better than sizing down for work shoes. Remember that different brands fit differently, so don't assume your size is consistent across all manufacturers.
