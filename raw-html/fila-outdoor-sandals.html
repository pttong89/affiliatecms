Width Considerations

FILA outdoor sandals accommodate a standard to slightly wide foot profile comfortably. The strap system is forgiving for different foot widths, and the open design prevents the constricted feeling that can occur with closed-toe outdoor shoes. However, individuals with particularly narrow feet might find the minimum strap adjustment still somewhat loose, while those with very wide feet should test the maximum strap extension to ensure adequate coverage and support.

During my testing with friends who have varying foot widths, the consensus was that the sandals work best for average to moderately wide feet. The synthetic strap material does stretch slightly with wear, which can be beneficial for achieving a custom fit over time. For those with specific width concerns, I recommend trying the sandals with the typical outdoor socks you'd wear during activities to ensure the best fit assessment.

Fit Tips from the Trail

Through extensive field testing, I've developed several practical recommendations for achieving the best fit with FILA outdoor sandals. First, adjust the straps with the sandals on your feet rather than pre-setting them, as foot position and arch support affect the ideal tightness. Second, ensure there's approximately a thumb-width of space between your longest toe and the front edge of the sandal – this prevents stubbing on rocks or roots during outdoor activities.

How should I care for and maintain my FILA outdoor sandals?

Maintaining FILA outdoor sandals is straightforward and essential for maximizing their lifespan and performance. After outdoor use, remove loose debris by tapping the soles together and brushing off dirt and sand with a soft brush or cloth. For deeper cleaning, use warm water and mild soap to scrub the synthetic materials, paying special attention to the velcro straps where debris can accumulate. The synthetic materials handle water well, so thorough rinsing is safe and recommended. Allow them to air dry completely before storage, avoiding direct heat sources that could damage the EVA foam or cause materials to crack. Periodically clean the velcro strips with a small brush or old toothbrush to remove lint, hair, and debris that can reduce their holding power. For stubborn stains, a gentle scrub brush with soap and water typically removes most outdoor residue. Store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to prevent material degradation.
