Frequently Asked Questions

What makes New Balance shoes ideal for standing all day?

New Balance shoes excel for standing all day due to their Fresh Foam cushioning technology, superior arch support, and wide toe box design. Which New Balance model offers the most cushioning for sore feet?

The New Balance Fresh Foam X More v5 provides maximum cushioning with its 42.1mm heel stack height and ultra-plush Fresh Foam X technology. This model features the thickest midsole in New Balance's lineup, offering 22.8% less impact load compared to average shoes. How do I choose the right size in women's New Balance shoes?

New Balance generally runs true to size with their standard D width being equivalent to medium width in women's shoes. Measure your feet in the evening when they're naturally swollen, and consider sizing up half a size if you plan to wear thick socks or orthotic inserts. New Balance offers width options from 2A (extra narrow) to 6E (extra wide), so focus on getting the proper width as much as length for optimal comfort. I recommend measuring both feet as they often differ slightly, and always use the measurements from your larger foot when selecting size.

Are New Balance shoes good for people with flat feet or high arches?

Yes, New Balance offers excellent options for both flat feet and high arches. For flat feet, models like the 860 v14 and Vongo v6 provide motion control and stability features with dual-density midsoles that prevent overpronation. For high arches, the 1080 v14 and More v5 offer neutral cushioning with excellent shock absorption to accommodate the higher arch profile. The brand's heritage in arch support technology, dating back to their founding as an arch support company, makes them particularly suitable for various foot types and biomechanical needs.

How long do New Balance shoes typically last with daily standing?

Quality New Balance shoes typically last 6-12 months with daily standing wear, depending on the model and usage intensity. Premium models like the 990v5 can last longer due to superior materials and construction quality. Signs it's time to replace include compressed midsole foam, worn outsole tread, or decreased comfort and support. During my testing, I found that rotating between two pairs can extend overall lifespan and ensure you always have supportive footwear available. The More v5 and 1080 v14 showed excellent durability even after 10+ months of intensive healthcare use.
