Introduction & Overview

The Topo Athletic Trailventure WP represents a revolutionary approach to waterproof hiking boots, combining the lightweight comfort of trail runners with the protection and stability needed for serious outdoor adventures. Frequently Asked Questions

Are the Topo Athletic Trailventure WP boots truly waterproof?

Yes, the Topo Athletic Trailventure WP boots feature a full eVent waterproof inner bootie construction that effectively keeps water out. During my extensive testing through streams, snow, and heavy rain, my feet stayed completely dry. The eVent membrane is actually more breathable than Gore-Tex, allowing better moisture management while maintaining waterproof protection. However, like all waterproof boots, they're only waterproof up to the collar height - about 5.2 inches above the sole. The newer Trailventure 2 maintains this excellent waterproof performance.

How does the wide toe box compare to other hiking boots?

The Topo Athletic Trailventure WP features one of the most generous toe boxes in the hiking boot market. It's significantly wider than traditional boots like Merrell or Salomon, allowing natural toe splay and preventing common issues like blisters and hot spots. This makes them ideal for hikers with wider feet, bunions, or conditions like Morton's neuroma. The wide toe box doesn't compromise the secure fit around the midfoot and heel, thanks to Topo's thoughtful design approach. Many users transitioning from narrow boots experience immediate relief from pressure-related foot pain.

What's the difference between the WP and non-WP versions?

The main differences are waterproofing, weight, and price. The WP version includes a full eVent waterproof bootie, full-length ESS rock plate, and weighs about 2 ounces more per boot. It costs $185 compared to $160 for the non-WP version. The waterproof version is less breathable and takes longer to dry if water does get in, while the non-WP version offers better ventilation for hot weather hiking. Both share the same ZipFoam midsole and Vibram Megagrip outsole, ensuring consistent performance across conditions.

How long do these boots typically last with regular use?

Based on user reports and my testing, the Topo Athletic Trailventure WP typically lasts 400-600 miles of hiking, depending on terrain and usage. The Vibram outsole holds up well, but the mesh upper and eVent bootie may show wear around the ankle collar after 6-10 months of regular use. One reviewer reported destroying their boots after 10 months and over 500 miles of cross-country hiking in Southern California's harsh terrain, which is actually quite impressive for such a lightweight boot. For comparison, the original Trailventure WP showed similar durability patterns.

Do I need to size up for the Trailventure WP?

Most users find the Trailventure WP runs about a half size large, so you may want to size down from your normal hiking boot size. However, the wide toe box can be deceiving - while there's plenty of room width-wise, the length should fit snugly to prevent your foot from sliding forward on descents. I recommend trying them on if possible, or ordering your normal running shoe size first. For thru-hiking or extended trips, some hikers go up a half size to accommodate foot swelling. The fit is consistent with other models in the Trailventure line.
