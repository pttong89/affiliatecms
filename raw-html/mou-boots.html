History and Brand Heritage

The story of mou boots begins in 2002 on the vibrant streets of London's Portobello Road, where founder Shelley Tichborne started selling her handcrafted creations to stylists and curious passersby. What began as a small storefront operation has evolved into a globally recognized luxury footwear brand, celebrated for its unique fusion of traditional craftsmanship and contemporary urban styling.

Tichborne's vision was revolutionary for its time—creating footwear that would serve as an antidote to the pressures of modern city life while maintaining the highest standards of luxury and comfort. The brand name "mou" itself reflects this philosophy, evoking softness and comfort in its very pronunciation. From those early days on Portobello Road, word-of-mouth recommendations from fashion insiders quickly elevated the brand to cult status.

Today, mou boots represent more than just footwear—they embody a lifestyle choice for those who value artisanal quality, ethical production, and distinctive design. The brand has successfully maintained its core principles while expanding internationally, with each pair still reflecting the original commitment to handcrafted excellence that made them famous among London's fashion elite.

Stain Treatment: Address stains immediately with specialized sheepskin cleaner and damp cloth
Protection: Apply suede protector spray before first wear and seasonally thereafter
Storage: Use cedar shoe trees and store in breathable dust bags

Detailed Care and Maintenance Procedures

Maintaining mou boots requires understanding the unique properties of premium sheepskin and suede materials. Unlike synthetic materials, these natural fibers require gentle, specific care to preserve their appearance and performance. Proper maintenance not only extends the life of your boots but also ensures they continue to provide optimal comfort and weather resistance.

Seasonal Care Schedule

Monthly Maintenance
  • • Gentle brushing with suede brush
  • • Inspection for damage or excessive wear
  • • Rotation with other footwear to allow recovery
  • • Checking and replacing cedar shoe trees
Seasonal Care
  • • Professional cleaning if heavily soiled
  • • Reapplication of protective sprays
  • • Conditioning of leather components
  • • Storage preparation for off-season

What NOT to Do

  • Never machine wash or submerge in water - this can cause shrinkage and damage natural fibers
  • Avoid direct heat sources - radiators, hair dryers, and direct sunlight can crack and damage materials
  • Don't use harsh chemicals - stick to products specifically designed for sheepskin and suede
  • Never wear on consecutive days - natural materials need time to breathe and recover shape

Frequently Asked Questions

Are MOU boots worth the investment?

Yes, MOU boots are absolutely worth the investment for several compelling reasons. These handcrafted sheepskin boots offer exceptional durability, with many users reporting years of wear without significant deterioration. The premium materials, including double-face sheepskin and hand-crochet wool stitching, justify the higher price point. Unlike mass-produced alternatives, each pair undergoes artisanal craftsmanship that ensures superior comfort and longevity. The brand's commitment to ethical production and sustainable materials adds value beyond just aesthetics. While the initial cost is higher than conventional boots, the cost-per-wear over several seasons makes them economically sensible. Additionally, the distinctive styling and luxury positioning often result in better resale value compared to mainstream brands.

How do MOU boots compare to UGG boots?

MOU boots offer a more fashion-forward alternative to UGG boots with several key differences. While both use premium sheepskin, MOU boots feature distinctive hand-crochet detailing and more sophisticated styling that transitions better from casual to semi-dressy occasions. MOU boots tend to have a slimmer silhouette and more varied color options, including metallic and embellished designs. However, UGG boots generally offer better weather protection with their non-slip soles and water-resistant treatments. MOU boots are more suited for urban environments, while UGGs excel in harsh weather conditions. Price-wise, they're comparable, but MOU offers more unique designs for those seeking something beyond the mainstream UGG aesthetic. The choice ultimately depends on whether you prioritize distinctive fashion styling (MOU) or maximum practical functionality (UGG).

What size should I order in MOU boots?

MOU boots generally run true to European sizing, but since they only come in full sizes, the brand recommends sizing up to the nearest whole size if you're between sizes. For US customers, this typically means ordering one size up from your usual US shoe size. For example, if you wear a US 8, order a 39 in MOU boots. The sheepskin lining will compress slightly with wear, creating a custom fit over time. However, they should feel snug but not tight initially, with about a thumb's width of space at the toe. Since the natural materials conform to your feet, avoid ordering too large as this can cause slipping and reduce the boots' supportive qualities. If purchasing online, check the retailer's return policy to exchange sizes if needed, as proper fit is crucial for both comfort and longevity.

How should I care for my MOU boots?

Proper care extends the life of your MOU boots significantly. Start by brushing the suede exterior with a soft suede brush to maintain the nap and remove surface dirt. For the sheepskin areas, use a specialized sheepskin cleaner applied with a damp cloth, then allow to air dry naturally away from direct heat. Never machine wash or submerge in water, as this can damage the natural fibers and cause shrinkage. Apply a suede protector spray before first wear and reapply seasonally to resist stains and moisture. Store with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape, and avoid wearing on consecutive days to allow the natural materials to breathe and recover their shape. For stubborn stains, consider professional cleaning rather than aggressive home treatment. Regular maintenance prevents most issues and preserves the boots' appearance and performance for years.

Are MOU boots suitable for snow and winter weather?

MOU boots are water-resistant and suitable for light snow and winter conditions, but they're not fully waterproof. They excel in dry winter conditions and light snow, providing excellent insulation through their sheepskin lining. However, avoid walking through puddles, slush, or heavy wet snow as the suede exterior can absorb moisture and potentially stain. The rubber soles provide adequate traction on most surfaces but aren't as aggressive as dedicated snow boot treads. For harsh winter climates with frequent wet conditions, consider them more as fashionable cold-weather boots rather than technical winter footwear. They're perfect for urban winter wear, casual winter outings, and dry cold conditions where style is as important as warmth. For extreme weather conditions, pair them with weather protection sprays and limit exposure to heavy moisture.
