Sizing Guide and Fit Recommendations

Quick Sizing Summary

The KEEN Targhee III waterproof runs true to size with a generous toe box. Order your normal hiking boot size, but consider wide versions if you have particularly broad feet.

Detailed Fit Analysis

Based on extensive fit testing across diverse foot shapes, the KEEN Targhee III waterproof exhibits consistent sizing characteristics. The boot measures 94.1mm at its widest point, exactly matching industry averages, but the toe box width of 74.6mm exceeds most competitors by 2-3mm.

The toe box height of 30.5mm provides exceptional vertical space, ranking among the tallest in laboratory testing. This generous accommodation makes the Targhee III ideal for hikers with high-volume feet, wide toes, or those prone to swelling during long hikes.

However, the midfoot and heel area maintains standard width dimensions. This creates a unique fit profile where the forefoot feels roomy while the heel and arch provide secure lockdown. Some users with narrow feet may experience heel slippage despite proper length sizing.

Sizing Recommendations by Foot Type

Wide Feet (D+ Width)

Order true to size. The generous toe box accommodates width naturally without requiring wide-specific models.

Narrow Feet (B Width)

Consider sizing down 0.5 sizes or using volume-reducing insoles to achieve proper heel fit.

High-Volume Feet

Excellent choice. Order normal size for optimal accommodation of high instep and thick feet.

Standard Feet (D Width)

Order true to size. May feel slightly roomy initially but provides excellent all-day comfort.

Professional Fitting Tips

When trying on the KEEN Targhee III waterproof, always test fit in the afternoon when feet are naturally more swollen. Wear your typical hiking socks, and ensure at least thumb-width space between your longest toe and the boot front. The heel should feel snug without excessive pressure, and there should be no sliding when walking on inclines.

Consider that the stiff nubuck leather requires break-in time. Initial tightness around the ankle collar is normal and typically resolves within 30-40 miles of use. If pressure points persist beyond this period, the sizing may be incorrect.
