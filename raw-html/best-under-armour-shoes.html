How do Under Armour shoes fit compared to Nike or Adidas sizing?

Based on my extensive testing and user feedback analysis, Under Armour shoes typically run about a half-size smaller than Nike and slightly smaller than Adidas. The brand tends to have a narrower last construction, which can feel restrictive for people with wider feet. I recommend trying on shoes in-store when possible, or ordering both your normal size and a half-size up when purchasing online. Training models like the TriBase series tend to run more narrow than running shoes. However, once you find your correct Under Armour size, it's remarkably consistent across different models. The brand has also been improving their fit in recent years, with newer models offering slightly more room in the toe box while maintaining their signature secure midfoot lockdown.

How long do Under Armour shoes typically last?

Durability is one of Under Armour's strongest advantages in the athletic footwear market. In my testing, running shoes like the HOVR Infinite series maintain their cushioning and structural integrity for 400-500 miles, which is above average for the category. Training shoes demonstrate even more impressive longevity—I've used TriBase Reign models for over 18 months of regular CrossFit training without significant performance degradation. Basketball shoes show excellent durability both indoors and outdoors, with the rubber compounds and upper materials withstanding intensive court use. The key factors that contribute to this longevity include high-quality material selection, reinforced high-wear areas, and construction methods that prevent premature breakdown. While initial investment may be higher than some alternatives, the extended lifespan typically results in better cost-per-use value over time.

Are Under Armour shoes worth the price compared to cheaper alternatives?

The value proposition of Under Armour shoes becomes clear when you consider total cost of ownership rather than just upfront price. While premium models like the Flow Velociti Elite or high-end Curry basketball shoes carry significant initial costs, their extended lifespan and consistent performance often result in better long-term value. Mid-tier options like the HOVR Sonic or TriBase Reign series offer excellent performance-to-price ratios, competing favorably with similar models from major brands. The key is matching your needs to the appropriate model—you don't need elite racing shoes for casual jogging, but the investment makes sense for serious training or competition. I've found that the durability advantage alone often justifies the price difference, as you'll replace cheaper shoes more frequently. Additionally, the consistent quality control means you're less likely to receive defective products, reducing the hassle and cost of returns or replacements.

Which Under Armour shoe is best for people with flat feet or specific foot issues?

For individuals with flat feet or pronation issues, I recommend starting with the HOVR Infinite series, which offers substantial arch support and motion control features. The TriBase Reign models also work well due to their low drop and stable platform, which can help with foot alignment during activities. The brand's newer models include improved orthotic-friendly designs with removable insoles, allowing custom orthotics when needed. However, Under Armour shoes tend to have firmer midsoles compared to some competitors, which may require an adjustment period but ultimately provides better long-term support. People with wider feet should consider the Flow Dynamic or newer HOVR models that feature slightly wider toe boxes. I always recommend consulting with a podiatrist or specialty running store for specific foot issues, but many users with common pronation problems find Under Armour's supportive designs beneficial once they adapt to the firmer feel and find their correct size.
