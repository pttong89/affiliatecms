What's the difference between regular and wide width Renegade GTX boots?

The Lowa Renegade GTX Low wide and mid versions provide additional room in the forefoot and midfoot areas while maintaining the same heel width. The wide versions use the same high-quality materials and construction as standard width models. If you typically require wide width shoes in athletic footwear, the wide Lowa versions will likely provide better comfort and prevent pressure points during long hikes.

Can these boots be used for winter hiking and snowshoeing?

The Lowa Renegade GTX womens boots perform adequately for mild winter conditions and snowshoeing, but they're not specifically designed for cold weather use. The GORE-TEX lining provides waterproof protection against snow, and the insulation properties of the leather upper offer moderate warmth. However, for extended winter hiking or temperatures below 20°F, dedicated winter boots with additional insulation would be more appropriate. The aggressive tread pattern provides decent traction on packed snow, but microspikes or crampons may be necessary for icy conditions.

How do I properly maintain the leather uppers?

Proper maintenance is crucial for maximizing the lifespan and performance of the nubuck leather upper. After each use, remove loose dirt with a soft brush and allow boots to dry naturally away from direct heat sources. Every 3-4 months or after significant wet exposure, clean with specialized nubuck cleaner and apply leather waterproofing treatment. Nikwax and similar products work effectively without damaging the GORE-TEX lining. Store boots in a cool, dry place with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture. Avoid harsh chemicals or conventional leather conditioners designed for smooth leather, as these can damage the nubuck texture.
