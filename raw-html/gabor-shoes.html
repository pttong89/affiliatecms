Gabor's width fitting system addresses one of the most common footwear complaints among women: shoes that are too narrow. Complete Sizing and Fit Guide

Getting the right fit with Gabor shoes requires understanding their unique sizing philosophy. After years of helping fellow travelers and park visitors choose appropriate footwear, I've developed a systematic approach to Gabor sizing that accounts for their European roots, width options, and style variations. The investment in proper fitting pays dividends in comfort and longevity.

Size Conversion Tips

  • Gabor uses European sizing (36-42)
  • Generally runs true to size compared to other European brands
  • Consider going up half size if between sizes
  • Width fitting is more important than length adjustment

Width Selection Guide

  • G Fitting: Standard comfort fit, roomier than typical shoes
  • H Fitting: Wide fit for broader feet
  • K Fitting: Extra wide for maximum room

Measuring Your Feet Properly

I recommend measuring your feet in the late afternoon when they're at their largest due to daily swelling. Use a Brannock device if available, or trace your foot on paper while standing. Pay attention to both length and width measurements. For Gabor shoes specifically, consider how you plan to use them—hiking and extended walking may require slightly more room than office wear.

One unique aspect of Gabor sizing is their consideration for foot changes throughout the day and across seasons. Their comfort fit philosophy accounts for natural foot expansion, which is particularly relevant for active users. When I'm selecting Gabor shoes for extended outdoor activities, I factor in sock thickness and potential foot swelling from long days of hiking.
