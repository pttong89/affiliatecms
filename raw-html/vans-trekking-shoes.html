Performance Analysis & Testing

Traction and Grip Assessment

One of the most critical aspects of any trekking shoe is its ability to provide reliable traction across various surfaces. I've tested vans trekking shoes on everything from wet granite slabs in Yosemite to loose scree slopes in the Cascade Range. The results have been consistently impressive, particularly with the newer MTE Crosspath models.

The updated lug pattern on Vans trekking shoes provides excellent grip on most surfaces. However, I did notice some limitations on extremely wet surfaces, particularly leaf-covered trails after rain. This is where traditional hiking boots from Vans with more aggressive treads might be preferable.

The regenerative rubber compounds used in recent models show excellent wear resistance. Frequently Asked Questions

Are Vans trekking shoes waterproof?

Most Vans trekking shoes feature water-resistant rather than fully waterproof construction. Models like the MTE Crosspath use water-repellent treatments that handle light rain and wet conditions effectively, but prolonged submersion will result in water penetration. For fully waterproof options, look for models with Gore-Tex membranes like the UltraRange EXO Hi Gore-Tex MTE-3. In my testing, the water-resistant models performed well in typical hiking conditions including stream crossings and moderate rain, but I recommend waterproofing sprays for enhanced protection in very wet environments.

How do Vans trekking shoes compare to traditional hiking boots?

Vans trekking shoes occupy a middle ground between casual sneakers and heavy-duty hiking boots. They offer superior comfort and style versatility compared to traditional boots while providing adequate protection for day hiking and light backpacking. Traditional hiking boots excel in ankle support, weather protection, and durability under extreme conditions. However, Vans models are significantly lighter, more comfortable for long wear, and better suited for mixed urban-outdoor use. For technical mountaineering or multi-day backpacking with heavy loads, traditional boots remain superior, but for most recreational hiking scenarios, Vans trekking shoes provide an excellent balance of performance and lifestyle compatibility.

What's the best Vans model for serious hiking?

For serious hiking applications, the MTE Crosspath XC represents the pinnacle of Vans' technical footwear development. This model features enhanced weather protection, superior grip patterns, and reinforced construction designed specifically for demanding outdoor use. The UltraRange EXO series also performs excellently for technical hiking, particularly in the Hi versions that provide additional ankle support. My personal recommendation varies by season: MTE Crosspath for spring and summer hiking, SK8-Hi MTE-2 for winter conditions, and UltraRange EXO for year-round versatility. Consider your typical hiking conditions, load weight, and personal comfort preferences when choosing between models.

Do Vans trekking shoes run true to size?

Sizing varies between Vans trekking models, but most run slightly smaller than standard Vans sneakers. Based on community feedback and personal experience, I recommend ordering a half size up from your normal Vans size, especially for hiking applications where you'll wear thicker socks. The MTE Crosspath tends to fit more snugly across the forefoot, while UltraRange models offer more consistent sizing. Always consider the thickness of hiking socks you'll use, potential foot swelling during long hikes, and whether you prefer a snug or relaxed fit. If possible, try them on in-store with appropriate socks, or order from retailers with generous return policies to ensure proper fit.

How long do Vans trekking shoes typically last?

Durability depends heavily on usage patterns and terrain types, but typical lifespans range from 300-800 miles of hiking. My personal testing shows the MTE Crosspath maintaining structural integrity after 500+ miles of mixed terrain use, though sole wear becomes noticeable after 300 miles on abrasive surfaces. Urban use significantly extends lifespan since pavement is less demanding than rocky trails. The rapidweld construction in newer models improves longevity by eliminating failure points common in stitched footwear. For maximum durability, rotate between multiple pairs, clean regularly, and avoid wearing the same pair on consecutive long hikes. Considering the price point, the durability compares favorably to similarly priced hiking footwear from traditional outdoor brands.

Can I use Vans trekking shoes for backpacking?

Vans trekking shoes work well for lightweight backpacking and short overnight trips, particularly with modern ultralight gear that reduces pack weight. I've successfully used them on 2-3 day backpacking trips with pack weights under 25 pounds without issues. However, they're not ideal for traditional heavy backpacking or extended thru-hiking where ankle support and enhanced protection become critical. The comfort advantage becomes particularly valuable during long hiking days, but the trade-off is reduced support and protection compared to dedicated backpacking boots. For serious backpackers, consider them for resupply runs, zero days, or as camp shoes rather than primary backpacking footwear. Always match your footwear choice to your pack weight, hiking experience, and terrain difficulty.
