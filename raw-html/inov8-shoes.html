Frequently Asked Questions

Are Inov8 shoes true to size?

Based on extensive testing across multiple models, inov8 shoes consistently run approximately half a size small compared to standard athletic shoe sizing. This sizing characteristic stems from their European heritage and anatomical last design. I recommend ordering a half size larger than your typical running shoe size for optimal fit. The anatomical toe box accommodates this sizing approach well, providing ample room for toe splay without feeling oversized. For wide-footed runners, the sizing-up strategy proves particularly beneficial, as it provides necessary width accommodation without compromising heel security. Always consider your intended sock thickness when selecting size, as thicker hiking or winter socks may require additional space consideration.

Are Inov8 shoes good for wide feet?

The anatomical toe box design makes most inov8 shoes exceptionally accommodating for wide feet, particularly in the forefoot region where width issues typically manifest. However, heel width remains relatively narrow to maintain secure lockdown, which suits most foot shapes well. Wide-footed runners should focus on the newer models with anatomical lasts rather than older narrow designs. The sizing-up strategy mentioned previously works particularly well for width accommodation without compromising performance. Always test shoes with your intended sock system, as thicker socks may require additional size consideration for optimal comfort and performance.

Can Inov8 shoes be used for road running?

While designed primarily for off-road applications, several inov8 models adapt reasonably well to road running, particularly for runners seeking enhanced ground feel and natural movement patterns. However, expect accelerated wear when using trail-specific models on abrasive asphalt surfaces. Consider inov8 road models for dedicated pavement running or reserve trail models for mixed-terrain applications where occasional road segments are unavoidable. The enhanced proprioception and natural movement encouraged by inov8 design benefits road runners seeking alternatives to heavily cushioned traditional options.

How do I properly care for my Inov8 shoes?

Proper maintenance significantly extends shoe lifespan and maintains optimal performance characteristics. Remove debris immediately after each use, paying special attention to lug spaces where rocks and mud accumulate. Rinse with cool water and mild soap when necessary, avoiding harsh chemicals that may degrade rubber compounds. Air dry completely before storage, removing insoles to accelerate drying and prevent bacterial growth. Avoid direct heat sources that may damage adhesives and synthetic materials. Rotate between multiple pairs when possible to allow complete drying between uses. Store in cool, dry locations away from direct sunlight and temperature extremes. Consider waterproofing treatments for hiking models used in consistently wet conditions, though breathability may be somewhat compromised. Regular inspection for wear patterns helps optimize replacement timing and identify gait issues requiring attention.
