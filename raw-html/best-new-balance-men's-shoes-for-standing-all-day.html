How long do New Balance shoes last for daily standing?

With daily standing use, quality New Balance models typically last 6-12 months depending on usage intensity, surface conditions, and care. The 860 v14 and 1080 v14 generally show the best durability due to their construction quality. Factors affecting lifespan include: daily hours of use (8+ hours accelerates wear), surface type (concrete is harder on shoes than carpet), body weight, and foot strike patterns. To maximize longevity, rotate between two pairs when possible, allowing 24 hours between wears for foam recovery. Signs it's time to replace include visible heel wear, compressed midsole foam, or return of foot fatigue that the shoes previously eliminated.

Do I need arch support in my New Balance standing shoes?

Arch support needs depend on your foot type and any existing conditions. Those with flat feet, overpronation, or plantar fasciitis typically benefit from stability models like the 860 v14 or Vongo v6. People with normal arches can choose neutral options like the 1080 v14 or More v5. During my testing, colleagues with arch issues reported significant improvement with stability models, while those with normal feet found neutral options perfectly comfortable. If you experience arch pain, knee discomfort, or uneven shoe wear patterns, consider a stability model. When in doubt, consult with a podiatrist or visit a specialty running store for gait analysis to determine your specific support needs.
