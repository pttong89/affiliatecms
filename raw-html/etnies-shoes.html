The Legacy Behind Etnies Shoes: 35+ Years of Skateboarding Innovation

The story of etnies shoes begins in 1986 when French professional skateboarder Pierre André Sénizergues founded what would become skateboarding's most innovative footwear brand. Originally launched as "Etnics" in France, the brand quickly rebranded to etnies due to legal challenges, but the vision remained unchanged: create skateboarding shoes by skateboarders, for skateboarders.

What sets etnies apart from other skate shoe brands is their groundbreaking contribution to skateboarding history. In 1987, etnies made history by creating the world's first professional model skateboarding shoe - the legendary Etnies Natas, designed in collaboration with skateboarding icon Natas Kaupas. This wasn't just a shoe; it was a revolution that changed how the industry viewed skateboarding footwear forever.

Under Sénizergues' leadership, etnies pioneered numerous technological advances that modern etnies shoes still benefit from today. Classic models like the Senix, Lo-Cut, Low-Top Rap, Intercity, and Scam were meticulously designed to enhance every rider's skateboarding experience, blending fashion with uncompromising durability. These early innovations laid the foundation for etnies' reputation as the thinking skater's choice.

The brand's commitment to skateboarding culture extends far beyond footwear. In 2003, etnies partnered with Lake Forest, California, to open the largest public skatepark in the state - a massive 40,000 square foot facility that continues to serve the skateboarding community today. Through initiatives like their "Buy a Shoe, Plant a Tree" program, which has resulted in over 2 million trees planted, etnies demonstrates that great etnies shoes come from a brand that genuinely cares about the world we skate in.
