Sizing Guide: Getting the Perfect Fit

Critical Sizing Information

Based on extensive user feedback and my testing, the adidas AX4 Gore-Tex consistently runs small and narrow. Here's what you need to know:

  • • Size up by 0.5-1 full size from your normal shoe size
  • • Consider wide width options if available
  • • Pay special attention to toe box fit

Fit Recommendations

For Normal to Narrow Feet:

Order 0.5 size up from your normal hiking shoe size. The aggressively tapered toe box will feel snug initially but should be manageable for most foot shapes.

For Wide Feet:

Order a full size up and strongly consider the wide width option if available. The narrow toe box can cause significant discomfort for wider feet over long distances.

For High-Volume Feet:

Consider alternatives. The AX4's low-profile design may not accommodate high-volume feet comfortably, even with sizing up.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Adidas AX4 Gore-Tex truly waterproof?

Yes, the adidas AX4 Gore-Tex features a genuine Gore-Tex membrane that provides excellent waterproof protection. In my testing, including deliberate stream crossings and hiking through heavy rain, these shoes kept my feet completely dry. The waterproof membrane is complemented by a gusseted tongue that effectively seals out water and debris. However, remember that Gore-Tex works best when the outer fabric isn't completely saturated, and proper care is essential for maintaining waterproof performance over time.

How should I size the AX4 Gore-Tex compared to other hiking shoes?

The AX4 Gore-Tex runs noticeably small and narrow compared to most hiking shoes. I recommend sizing up by 0.5 to 1 full size from your normal hiking shoe size, depending on your foot width. The toe box is particularly narrow, which can cause discomfort during longer hikes if not properly sized. If you have wide feet, strongly consider ordering a full size up or looking for wide width options. When compared to other Adidas mid Gore-Tex walking shoes, the sizing is generally consistent within the Terrex line.

Are these shoes suitable for hot weather hiking?

The adidas AX4 Gore-Tex is not ideal for hot weather hiking. The Gore-Tex membrane, while excellent for waterproofing, significantly restricts breathability compared to non-waterproof hiking shoes. During my summer testing in desert conditions, my feet became uncomfortably hot and sweaty. These shoes truly shine in cooler, wet conditions where the waterproof benefits outweigh the breathability limitations. For hot weather hiking, consider the non-Gore-Tex version of the AX4 or explore other Terrex Gore-Tex hiking shoes designed with enhanced ventilation.

How durable is the AX4 Gore-Tex for regular hiking?

Durability is a mixed bag with the AX4 Gore-Tex. The upper construction and Gore-Tex membrane hold up well over time, maintaining waterproof integrity even after extensive use. However, the Continental rubber outsole, while providing excellent grip, tends to wear faster than expected, particularly on abrasive surfaces like granite or concrete. In my testing over 300+ miles, I noticed noticeable sole wear after about 200 miles of mixed terrain hiking. The lightweight construction means some trade-offs in overall durability compared to burlier hiking boots, but for the intended use case of day hiking and light backpacking, the durability is generally acceptable for the price point.

Can the AX4 Gore-Tex handle technical terrain and scrambling?

The AX4 Gore-Tex can handle moderate technical terrain but has limitations for serious scrambling. The shoe provides excellent grip on wet rocks and stable footing on uneven surfaces, making it suitable for most day hiking scenarios including rocky trails and moderate scrambles. However, the minimal toe protection and relatively soft construction mean it's not ideal for extensive rock scrambling or routes requiring significant foot protection. The flexible sole provides good sensitivity for rock placement but lacks the stiffness some prefer for technical terrain. For serious scrambling, consider more robust options, though for general trail hiking with occasional technical sections, the AX4 performs admirably.
