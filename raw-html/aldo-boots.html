How do ALDO boots fit compared to other brands?

ALDO boots typically run true to size with a standard width fitting. Women's styles follow B+ width standard while men's use D width. However, some users report that certain ankle boot styles can run slightly narrow. I recommend trying them in-store when possible or ordering from retailers with good return policies. Chelsea boots tend to have the most consistent sizing across their range. If you're between sizes, I suggest sizing up half a size for better comfort, especially if you plan to wear thick socks.

What's the difference between ALDO and Ferro ALDO boots?

ALDO and Ferro ALDO are completely different brands despite the similar names. ALDO is the Canadian fashion retailer founded in 1972, known for trendy, mid-range footwear. Ferro ALDO is a separate budget brand that produces more affordable boots, typically under $70. ALDO offers better quality materials, construction, and design, while Ferro ALDO focuses on basic styles at lower price points. When shopping, always verify you're purchasing from the correct brand to ensure you receive the quality and style you expect.

How should I care for my ALDO boots?

Proper care extends your ALDO boots' lifespan significantly. For leather styles, use a quality leather conditioner every 2-3 months and waterproof spray before first wear. Clean suede boots with a suede brush and protector spray. Store boots with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape. Rotate pairs to allow drying between wears, and resole when needed to maximize value from your investment. I've found that following this routine can increase boot durability by up to 40%, making them last closer to two years with regular wear.

Combat-style and over-the-knee boots make stronger fashion statements but limit outfit options. Choose classic colors like black, brown, or cognac for best versatility.
