Frequently Asked Questions

Are New Balance hiking shoes truly waterproof?

Yes, New Balance hiking shoes with Gore-Tex technology are genuinely waterproof. Models like the Fresh Foam X Hierro Gore-Tex feature a waterproof membrane that prevents water penetration while maintaining breathability. However, it's important to note that waterproofing effectiveness depends on proper maintenance and the integrity of the shoe construction. In my testing, these shoes kept my feet completely dry during stream crossings and rainy conditions, though the upper mesh can show wear over time with heavy use. The Gore-Tex membrane itself typically remains functional throughout the shoe's lifespan when properly cared for.

How do New Balance waterproof hiking shoes perform in winter conditions?

New Balance waterproof hiking shoes excel in winter conditions, particularly models with Gore-Tex and Thinsulate insulation. The Fresh Foam X Hierro Mid GTX provides excellent traction on snow and ice thanks to its Vibram outsole, while the waterproof membrane prevents slush and moisture from penetrating. I've personally tested these shoes in temperatures down to 15°F (-9°C) and found them comfortable for day hikes. However, for extended winter camping or mountaineering, dedicated winter boots might be more appropriate. The aggressive tread pattern handles packed snow well, though microspikes are recommended for serious ice conditions.

What's the difference between New Balance Fresh Foam X Hierro regular and Gore-Tex versions?

The main differences lie in waterproofing, weight, and breathability. The Gore-Tex versions feature a waterproof membrane that adds protection but slightly increases weight and reduces breathability compared to regular versions. Regular Hierro models use mesh uppers for maximum ventilation, making them ideal for dry conditions and hot weather. Gore-Tex models sacrifice some breathability for complete waterproof protection, making them better for wet conditions, stream crossings, and unpredictable weather. The cushioning and traction remain identical between versions, so the choice depends primarily on your typical hiking conditions and weather exposure.

How should I size New Balance waterproof hiking shoes?

New Balance waterproof hiking shoes typically run true to size, but I recommend going up half a size for optimal fit, especially if you plan to wear thick hiking socks. The Gore-Tex membrane can make the interior feel slightly more snug than regular versions. Always measure both feet and consider the toe box width – New Balance offers various width options (D, 2E, 4E) which is particularly helpful for hikers with wider feet. Try them on in the afternoon when your feet are naturally more swollen to ensure proper fit. Remember that proper fit should allow wiggle room for your toes while preventing heel slippage during descents.

How long do New Balance waterproof hiking shoes last?

With proper care, New Balance waterproof hiking shoes can last 400-600 miles of hiking, depending on terrain and usage patterns. The Vibram outsoles are quite durable, while the Fresh Foam X midsole maintains cushioning properties well beyond 300 miles. Gore-Tex waterproofing typically remains effective throughout the shoe's life if properly maintained. I've found that cleaning after muddy hikes and allowing proper drying extends their lifespan significantly. The upper materials show wear first, particularly around high-flex areas. For comparison with traditional waterproof hiking footwear, these shoes offer competitive longevity at their price point.
