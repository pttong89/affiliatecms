How do I know if I need stability or neutral Hoka shoes?

Determine your pronation type by examining your current shoe wear patterns. If the inner edge of your soles wears faster, you likely overpronate and need stability shoes like the Gaviota 5 or Arahi 8. If wear is even across the sole, neutral shoes like the Clifton 10 or Bondi 9 work best. You can also perform the wet foot test: step on paper with wet feet, and if your footprint shows the full foot including arch area, you probably need stability features. Consulting with a specialty running store for gait analysis provides the most accurate assessment.

How long do Hoka walking shoes typically last?

Hoka walking shoes typically last 400-500 miles with proper care and rotation. Factors affecting longevity include walking surface (concrete wears shoes faster than softer surfaces), body weight, walking style, and maintenance. Signs it's time for replacement include visible midsole compression, uneven wear patterns, decreased cushioning sensation, or development of aches and pains during or after walking. To maximize lifespan, rotate between two pairs, allow 24 hours between wears for foam recovery, and store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
