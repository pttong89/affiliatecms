DMT Shoes Sizing, Fit, and Buying Guide

Proper sizing represents the most critical factor in DMT shoe satisfaction, and their unique knit construction requires different considerations than traditional cycling shoes. Understanding DMT's Italian Sizing

DMT uses European sizing that typically runs slightly different from other cycling brands. Their knit construction creates a closer, more sock-like fit that can feel restrictive if sized incorrectly. After measuring dozens of cyclists at local bike shops, I've found that most riders need to size up approximately 0.5 sizes compared to brands like Shimano or Specialized. The measurement process requires precision. Rather than relying on street shoe sizes, measure both feet in centimeters at the end of the day when feet are naturally slightly swollen. DMT's sizing chart provides foot length measurements for each size, which proves more accurate than comparing across different brand sizing systems. Width becomes particularly important with DMT shoes. Their Italian last tends toward narrow, which suits many competitive cyclists but can be problematic for those with wider feet. The knit does provide some accommodation, but only to a limited degree. Cyclists requiring wide-width shoes in other brands should carefully consider whether DMT's fit will work for their foot shape.

Model-Specific Fit Characteristics

Different DMT models exhibit subtle fit variations that impact sizing decisions. The **KR0** and **KR30** share similar lasts but differ in adjustability due to their closure systems. The dual BOA dials on the KR0 allow more precise fit customization, while the single dial KR30 requires more careful initial sizing. The **SH10** runs slightly more generously in the toe box, making it better for riders with longer toes or those who prefer more space. During hot weather testing, this extra room proved beneficial for comfort during foot swelling. MTB models like the **KM30** typically fit more generously to accommodate the need for walking and varied foot positions during technical riding. Gravel shoes such as the **GK1** fall somewhere between road and MTB sizing, offering versatility for mixed riding styles.

How should I clean and maintain DMT knit shoes?

DMT knit shoes require more careful maintenance than traditional cycling shoes, particularly light-colored models. Clean immediately after dusty or muddy rides using warm water and mild dish soap (Dawn works excellently). Use a soft brush to gently work soap through the knit material, then rinse thoroughly and air dry. Avoid harsh chemicals, bleach, or machine washing, which can damage the knit structure. For deep cleaning, remove insoles and laces before washing. The knit material dries quickly due to its breathable nature, but always air dry rather than using direct heat. Regular maintenance prevents stains from setting and extends the shoes' attractive appearance significantly.

What's the difference between DMT's road, MTB, and gravel shoe models?

DMT's road shoes (KR0, KR30, SH10) prioritize maximum stiffness and aerodynamics for pure pedaling efficiency, featuring the stiffest carbon soles and most aggressive geometry. MTB models like the KM30 balance pedaling efficiency with walkability, using slightly more flexible soles and reinforced uppers for off-bike activities. Gravel shoes such as the GK1 blend road efficiency with MTB practicality, offering moderate sole stiffness, lace closures for infinite adjustability, and tread patterns suitable for mixed surfaces. All categories benefit from DMT's knit technology, but each applies it differently based on intended use, environmental demands, and the specific biomechanical requirements of different cycling disciplines.
