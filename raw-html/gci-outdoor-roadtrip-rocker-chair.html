Setup & Usage Guide

Step-by-Step Setup

1

Remove from carry bag

The chair comes in a well-designed carry bag with shoulder strap. Simply unzip and remove the folded chair.

2

Unfold the main frame

Grip the chair by the armrests and gently pull apart. The spring-loaded frame will naturally want to open.

3

Lock the frame

Ensure all joints click into place. The chair should feel solid and stable before sitting.

4

Test the rocking motion

Gently push down on the seat to activate the spring system and ensure smooth operation.

Pro Setup Tips

  • • Always set up on the most level ground available
  • • Check that the spring mechanism moves freely before first use
  • • Position the cup holder on your preferred side
  • • Allow extra space behind for rocking motion

Maintenance & Care

Based on my experience and feedback from other users, the most important maintenance task is keeping the spring mechanism clean and lubricated. After extended use, dust and debris can cause squeaking—a common complaint among users.

Maintenance Schedule

  • • After each trip: Wipe down frame and fabric
  • • Monthly: Clean spring mechanism with compressed air
  • • Seasonally: Apply light lubricant to moving parts
  • • Storage: Keep in carry bag when not in use

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the GCI Outdoor RoadTrip Rocker Chair weigh?

The GCI Outdoor RoadTrip Rocker Chair weighs approximately 12.8-13.2 pounds, making it heavier than ultralight backpacking chairs but reasonable for car camping and tailgating where comfort is prioritized over weight. This weight contributes to its stability and durability, making it an excellent choice for base camp scenarios where you'll be staying in one location for extended periods.

What is the weight capacity of the RoadTrip Rocker?

The GCI Outdoor RoadTrip Rocker has a weight capacity of 250 pounds. The sturdy steel frame and robust spring-action mechanism are designed to support users comfortably within this limit. During my testing, I've observed users up to 240 pounds using the chair comfortably for extended periods without any structural concerns or performance degradation.

How does the spring-action rocking technology work?

The patented Spring-Action Rocking Technology uses a shock-absorber system built into the chair's base that allows for smooth rocking motion on various surfaces. The springs compress and extend as you rock, providing a stable and comfortable rocking experience. Unlike traditional rockers that require curved bases, this system works effectively on uneven terrain, making it perfect for camping environments where ground conditions vary.

Is the RoadTrip Rocker suitable for backpacking?

No, the RoadTrip Rocker is not ideal for backpacking due to its 12.8-pound weight and larger packed size. It's designed for car camping, tailgating, RV trips, and base camp scenarios where comfort takes priority over portability. If you need a rocking chair for backpacking, consider the lighter GCI Freestyle Rocker which weighs significantly less while maintaining rocking functionality.

What are common issues with the GCI RoadTrip Rocker?

The most common issue reported by users is squeaking from the spring mechanism after extended use, which can be addressed with periodic cleaning and lubrication using a light machine oil or silicone spray. Some users also report durability concerns with newer models, particularly fabric wear around seat edges. Additionally, the 20-inch seat height can be uncomfortable for users shorter than 5'6". Regular maintenance and proper storage can significantly extend the chair's lifespan and performance.
