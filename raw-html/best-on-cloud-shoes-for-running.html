How to Choose the Right On Cloud Running Shoe

Selecting the best On Cloud shoes for running depends on several key factors that I've learned to prioritize through extensive testing. Your choice should align with your running goals, biomechanics, and personal preferences.

Consider Your Running Distance and Frequency

For daily runs under 10 miles, the Cloudsurfer 2 offers the perfect balance of cushioning and responsiveness. When I transitioned to higher mileage weeks (70+ miles), the Cloudmonster 2 became essential for recovery runs and long weekend sessions. Marathon runners should seriously consider having both in their rotation.

Terrain and Weather Considerations

During Pacific Northwest winters, I discovered that certain On models handle wet conditions better than others. The CloudTech technology can collect water and debris, so consider the Cloudvista Trail for mixed terrain running.

Your Gait and Biomechanics

Through gait analysis sessions, I learned that On shoes work exceptionally well for midfoot and forefoot strikers. Heel strikers might find the lower drop models (6mm or less) require an adjustment period. The Cloudrunner 2 accommodates various strike patterns better than other models.

Budget and Value Considerations

On shoes command premium prices, but my cost-per-mile analysis shows they offer good value for dedicated runners. The Cloudsurfer 2 at $160 delivered over 400 miles before showing significant wear, making it competitive with other high-end trainers. For budget-conscious runners, look for previous year models or consider the Cloudflow series.

Sizing Guide and Fit Considerations

After helping dozens of runners find their perfect On Cloud fit, I've identified common sizing challenges and solutions. The best On Cloud shoes for running require careful attention to fit, as the brand's unique construction affects how they feel on your feet.

General Sizing Guidelines

Most On models run true to size, but with important caveats. I typically wear size 10.5 in Nike and found the same size perfect in Cloudsurfer 2 and Cloudmonster models. However, the Cloudrunner 2 felt slightly more generous, and I could have sized down half a size for a snugger fit.

Pro Tip:

Always account for foot swelling during longer runs. I recommend trying On shoes in the afternoon when your feet are naturally more swollen, similar to how they'll feel during extended running sessions.

Width Considerations

On doesn't offer dedicated wide widths, but some models accommodate wider feet better than others. The Cloudeclipse and Cloudmonster 2 provide more room in the forefoot, while the Cloudsurfer 2 runs narrower through the midfoot.

Seasonal Fitting Adjustments

During summer training in Arizona, I learned that foot swelling becomes more pronounced in hot weather. Consider going up half a size if you primarily run in warm climates or plan to use thicker socks. Winter runners using moisture-wicking socks might find their normal size perfect.

Break-in Period Expectations

Unlike some running shoes that feel great immediately, On Cloud models often require 20-30 miles to fully break in. The CloudTec elements soften slightly, and the upper materials conform to your foot shape. Plan for this adjustment period when incorporating new On shoes into your training routine.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are On Cloud shoes good for daily running?

Absolutely. After logging over 1,500 miles across various On models, I can confidently say the best On Cloud shoes for running excel for daily training. The Cloudsurfer 2 and Cloudmonster 2 are particularly well-suited for daily use, offering excellent cushioning and durability. However, they work best for easy to moderate paces rather than speed work. The CloudTec technology provides unique energy return that becomes more noticeable as your legs fatigue during longer runs. For runners averaging 30-50 miles per week, these shoes can handle the bulk of your training volume while keeping your legs fresh for key workouts.

How do On Cloud shoes compare to Nike and Adidas for running?

Having tested extensively across all three brands, On shoes offer a distinctly different experience. While Nike focuses on responsive foam and Adidas emphasizes Boost technology, On's CloudTec provides a unique "rolling" sensation that's either loved or tolerated - rarely neutral. In terms of durability, On shoes consistently outperform Nike (averaging 350+ miles vs 250-300) but may not quite match Adidas Ultraboost longevity. The price point is comparable to premium Nike and Adidas models, but On's aesthetic and comfort for casual wear give them an edge for runners who want versatile shoes. For pure performance, Nike's Vaporfly line still dominates racing, but for daily training comfort, On Cloud shoes are competitive or superior.

What's the difference between CloudTec and CloudTec Phase technology?

The evolution from CloudTec to CloudTec Phase represents a significant advancement in On's cushioning system. Original CloudTec features individual "cloud" pods that compress independently, providing a unique but sometimes choppy ride. CloudTec Phase, found in newer models like the Cloudsurfer 2, connects these elements in a cascading sequence that creates a smoother, rolling motion from heel to toe. During my testing, Phase technology felt more natural and less "gimmicky" than the original system. The new design also reduces debris collection - a common complaint with earlier On models. However, some runners prefer the more pronounced cushioning sensation of traditional CloudTec. The Phase system works particularly well for runners with longer strides and midfoot striking patterns, as it enhances the natural rolling motion of your gait.

Do On Cloud running shoes work for wide feet?

This is one of On's biggest limitations. Most models run narrow through the midfoot and toe box, which can be problematic for runners with wide feet. During my fit testing sessions, I found that the Cloudmonster 2 and Cloudeclipse offer the most room, while the Cloudsurfer 2 and racing models are quite narrow. For wide-footed runners, I recommend going up half a size and trying them in-store first. The lack of dedicated wide widths is surprising for a premium brand, and it's something On should address. Some runners find success with aftermarket insoles or different lacing techniques, but this shouldn't be necessary at this price point. If you have significantly wide feet, brands like New Balance or Altra might be better options for your daily trainers.

How long do On Cloud running shoes typically last?

Based on my testing and community feedback, On Cloud shoes offer above-average durability. Most models deliver 350-450 miles before showing significant wear, which is excellent for the cushioned trainer category. The Cloudmonster series tends to last longest due to the robust construction and quality materials. However, durability varies significantly based on running style, body weight, and terrain. Heel strikers on concrete may see faster wear than midfoot strikers on softer surfaces. The outsole rubber is generally excellent, but the upper materials can show wear sooner than expected, particularly around high-flex areas. For the premium pricing, I expect and generally receive durability that justifies the investment. Regular rotation with other shoes can extend lifespan significantly - I typically get 500+ miles when rotating between two pairs versus using one exclusively.

Are On Cloud shoes worth the high price for recreational runners?

The value proposition depends heavily on your running frequency and priorities. For recreational runners logging 15-25 miles per week, the premium price can be justified by the exceptional comfort, durability, and versatility of these shoes. They work excellently for both running and casual wear, effectively serving as two shoes in one. However, if you're only running 2-3 times per week or are budget-conscious, you might find similar performance in less expensive options from Saucony or Brooks. The best On Cloud shoes for running truly shine when you're running consistently and can appreciate the subtle performance benefits. I recommend them most highly to runners who value comfort over speed, prioritize daily wearability, and are willing to invest in quality gear. For purely recreational use, consider previous year models or sales to get the On experience at a lower price point.
