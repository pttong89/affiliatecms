The JAX Story: 70 Years of Outdoor Excellence

The story of jacks outdoor gear begins in 1955 when Marvin and Lola Quinlan opened Ames Surplus, a small military surplus store in Ames, Iowa. What started as a modest operation grew into something much more significant when their son, Jim Quinlan, recognized the potential to expand westward into Colorado's outdoor recreation market. In 1983, Jim moved to Fort Collins and opened Rocky Mountain Surplus, which eventually evolved into the JAX Outdoor Gear we know today.

This family legacy represents more than just business success—it embodies a genuine passion for outdoor adventure that permeates every aspect of JAX's operations. During my conversations with long-time employees, I've learned that this commitment to outdoor recreation isn't just marketing speak. The Quinlan family and their staff are genuine outdoor enthusiasts who understand that quality gear can mean the difference between a memorable adventure and a dangerous situation.

The transition from military surplus to comprehensive outdoor gear retailer reflects Colorado's outdoor culture evolution. As recreational activities like rock climbing, backcountry skiing, and technical hiking gained popularity, JAX adapted their inventory and expertise to meet these growing demands. Today, they maintain that original surplus store character while offering cutting-edge outdoor technology and equipment.

What impresses me most about JAX's history is their commitment to community involvement. They've sponsored countless local outdoor events, supported search and rescue operations, and provided gear donations to outdoor education programs. This community focus has created a loyal customer base that spans multiple generations—I regularly see families shopping together, with grandparents, parents, and children all getting outfitted for their adventures.

Insider Tip: Ask about the "JAX Family" discount program when you visit. Long-time customers often receive special pricing on seasonal gear, and the staff genuinely remembers your previous purchases and outdoor interests.
