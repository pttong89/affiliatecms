What is Nike ACG?

Having spent countless hours researching the history and testing these shoes across diverse environments, I can confidently say that ACG hiking shoes represent Nike's serious commitment to outdoor performance.

The ACG philosophy centers around the motto "Designed, Tested and Made on Planet Earth. For Outdoor Use." This isn't just marketing speak – I've witnessed firsthand how these shoes perform in Pacific Northwest rainforests, Arizona desert trails, and Colorado mountain paths. The line bridges the gap between technical outdoor gear and urban style, making them perfect for adventurers who transition from ACG boots on the trail to city streets seamlessly.

The ACG lineup encompasses everything from water-resistant day hikers to full waterproof Nike ACG hiking boots, each engineered with specific use cases in mind. Understanding this range is crucial for selecting the right pair for your adventures.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are ACG hiking shoes waterproof?

Many ACG hiking shoes feature GORE-TEX waterproof membranes, particularly models like the Mountain Fly 2 Low GORE-TEX and Gaiadome. However, not all ACG models are waterproof - some focus on breathability for summer hiking. The waterproof versions excel in wet conditions but may sacrifice some breathability. I've tested the GORE-TEX versions in Pacific Northwest rain, and they kept my feet completely dry during 8-hour hikes through muddy trails and stream crossings.

How do ACG hiking shoes compare to traditional hiking boots?

ACG hiking shoes prioritize style and urban versatility alongside trail performance, while traditional hiking boots focus purely on rugged outdoor functionality. ACG shoes typically feature more cushioned midsoles and contemporary designs but may lack the aggressive tread patterns and ankle support of dedicated hiking boots. From my experience hiking both Yosemite and city trails, ACG shoes excel on moderate terrain and day hikes but fall short on technical mountaineering routes where brands like Salomon or Merrell shine.

What's the best ACG hiking shoe for beginners?

The Nike ACG Mountain Fly 2 Low is ideal for beginners due to its balanced comfort, moderate price point, and versatile performance. It offers excellent cushioning for long walks, decent traction on various surfaces, and a design that works both on trails and in urban settings. During my first season guiding novice hikers in Colorado, this model consistently received positive feedback for comfort and confidence-inspiring grip on moderate terrain.

Do ACG hiking shoes run true to size?

Most ACG hiking shoes run true to size, with the Mountain Fly 2 Low receiving 105 votes for true-to-size fit according to RunRepeat user data. However, some users recommend going up half a size for models with GORE-TEX lining due to the additional membrane thickness. I personally wear the same size in ACG shoes as my regular Nike sneakers, but I always recommend trying them on with hiking socks to ensure proper fit, especially for longer hikes where foot swelling occurs.

Can you use ACG hiking shoes for running?

Yes, many ACG hiking shoes double as trail running shoes, particularly the lighter models like the Mountain Fly series. They feature responsive cushioning and flexible outsoles suitable for running on moderate trails. However, they tend to be heavier than dedicated trail runners. I've used ACG shoes for trail running in the Sierra Nevada, and while they provide excellent protection and comfort, the extra weight becomes noticeable on longer runs compared to purpose-built trail running shoes.
