Frequently Asked Questions

Are Farfetch shoes authentic and safe to buy?

Yes, Farfetch shoes are completely authentic and safe to purchase. Farfetch partners exclusively with authorized boutiques and verified luxury retailers worldwide, ensuring every item meets strict authenticity standards. Their authentication process includes boutique verification, item inspection, and a 100% authenticity guarantee with full refund protection. I've personally purchased over twelve pairs through Farfetch without encountering any authenticity issues, and each item arrived with original packaging, dust bags, and authenticity documentation exactly as expected from legitimate luxury retailers.

How does Farfetch pricing compare to other luxury retailers?

Farfetch shoes typically offer 10-25% savings compared to direct brand purchases, with even greater discounts during seasonal sales. The global boutique network creates competitive pricing as different regions may offer the same items at varying price points. During my price comparison analysis, I found consistent savings on brands like Balenciaga, Saint Laurent, and Golden Goose compared to flagship boutiques. However, factor in potential shipping fees and customs duties for smaller orders. The platform's value proposition improves significantly during private sales events, where I've secured discounts of 40-60% on previous season inventory.

What is Farfetch's return policy for shoes?

Farfetch offers a 14-day return window from the delivery date, with free return shipping worldwide. Items must be returned in original condition with all packaging materials, dust bags, and authenticity cards intact. The return process is straightforward through their website portal, where you can schedule pickup or drop-off at partner locations. Processing typically takes 6-8 business days after they receive your return. I've successfully returned three pairs over the years due to sizing issues, and each experience was handled professionally with full refunds processed without complications. Their customer service team is particularly helpful if you need extensions due to shipping delays.

How should I determine sizing when buying Farfetch shoes internationally?

Sizing presents the biggest challenge with Farfetch shoes due to international variations and brand-specific fitting. I recommend contacting customer service for brand-specific guidance, as they maintain sizing databases and can connect you with boutiques for detailed fitting advice. Create a personal measurement reference by trying shoes in physical stores and noting your size in different brand systems. European boutiques often stock EU sizing exclusively, requiring accurate conversion charts. Pay attention to product descriptions mentioning "runs large" or "runs small," and consider brands' traditional fitting characteristics – Italian shoes typically run narrow, while German brands often run wider.

Which shoe brands offer the best value on Farfetch?

The best value Farfetch shoes come from brands with consistent quality and strong resale value. Golden Goose sneakers offer excellent investment potential with unique artisanal details unavailable elsewhere. Maison Margiela provides avant-garde design at reasonable price points, while Common Projects delivers minimalist luxury with outstanding leather quality. For women's shoes, Isabel Marant boots combine French craftsmanship with versatile styling, and The Row offers understated luxury with exceptional comfort. Emerging designers like Jacquemus and Ganni provide contemporary luxury at lower entry points than established houses, often becoming valuable collectibles as the brands gain recognition.

How long does shipping take and what are the costs?

Farfetch shoes shipping varies by boutique location and your destination, typically ranging from 3-7 business days for express delivery and 5-10 days for standard shipping. Boutiques have 2 business days to process orders before dispatch. Free shipping thresholds vary by region – $200 for US customers, £400 for UK, and €400 for EU. Express shipping fees range from $25-50 depending on location. I've found that European boutiques generally ship faster to US destinations than Asian boutiques, likely due to established logistics networks. Customs duties may apply depending on your country's regulations, though Farfetch clearly indicates any additional costs at checkout for transparency.
