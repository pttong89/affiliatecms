The Complete Sizing and Fit Guide

Proper fit is crucial for cowboy boots, and Tecovas sizing can be confusing for first-time buyers. Unlike athletic shoes or popular sneaker brands, western boots follow traditional sizing conventions that prioritize proper heel fit and arch support.

Tecovas Sizing Rules

General Rule: Order the same size as your dress shoes or quality sneakers. This differs from brands like specialty athletic footwear where sizing can vary significantly.

For Wide Feet: Choose EE width rather than sizing up. Tecovas runs narrower than some brands.

Between Sizes: Choose the smaller size – leather will stretch and conform to your foot.

During my sizing research, I discovered that Tecovas fits differently than technical footwear or orthopedic shoe brands. The key is understanding that some heel slippage is normal and desirable – it indicates proper fit that will eliminate as the leather molds to your foot.

Quality Analysis: Construction and Materials

Understanding boot construction is essential when evaluating value, especially when comparing Tecovas to everything from extreme weather boots to casual fashion footwear. Tecovas employs traditional Goodyear welt construction, a method that ensures durability and repairability.

The Goodyear welt process involves stitching the upper leather, welt, and sole together in a way that creates a waterproof barrier while allowing the boot to be resoled multiple times.
