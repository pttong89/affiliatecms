Sizing and Fit Considerations

Proper sizing represents the most critical factor in Kricely shoe satisfaction. The brand's wide toe box design accommodates natural foot shape better than conventional athletic shoes, but this can initially feel unfamiliar to users accustomed to narrower fits. Generally, ordering your standard athletic shoe size provides appropriate fit, though users with particularly wide feet benefit from the dedicated wide-fit versions.

The zero-drop construction affects fit perception, particularly in the heel area. Users transitioning from traditional heel-elevated shoes may initially feel the heel sits lower than expected. This sensation typically disappears within several days of regular wear as your foot adapts to the more natural positioning. Consider this adjustment period when evaluating initial fit impressions.

Professional Fitting Tips

Based on extensive fitting experience with various foot types:

  • Measure feet in the afternoon when they're naturally expanded
  • Allow thumb-width space between longest toe and shoe front
  • Ensure heel doesn't slip during walking motion
  • Test with hiking socks if planning outdoor use
  • Walk on various surfaces during fitting process
