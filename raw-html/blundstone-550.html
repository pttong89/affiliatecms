Sizing & Fit Guide

Critical Sizing Information

Blundstone uses Australian sizing, which differs significantly from US sizes. The 550 series runs true to Australian size but has a slightly wider fit than the 500 series due to the leather lining.

Fit Characteristics

  • Length: True to Australian size, never stretches lengthwise
  • Width: Slightly wider than 500 series due to leather lining
  • Heel: Should have slight lift but not slip
  • Toe Box: Quarter-inch space recommended
  • Instep: Will stretch and mold over time

Pro Tip

Order your regular US size for a snug fit, or half-size up if you prefer more room or have wider feet. The included extra footbeds allow for fine-tuning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Blundstone 550 boots waterproof?

The Blundstone 550 boots are water-resistant but not fully waterproof. They handle light rain, puddles, and brief creek crossings well, but prolonged water exposure will eventually penetrate the leather. For extended wet conditions, consider applying additional waterproofing treatment or choosing a dedicated waterproof model from Blundstone's lineup.

How long do Blundstone 550 boots typically last?

With regular use, you can expect 2-4 years from a pair of Blundstone 550 boots, depending on usage intensity and care. I've gotten three solid years from my pair with over 1,000 miles of use. The TPU sole is extremely durable, but the leather may show wear sooner if not properly maintained. Regular conditioning and proper storage significantly extend their lifespan.

Should I size up or down for Blundstone 550?

Blundstone 550 boots run true to Australian size, which is typically one size down from US men's sizes and three sizes down from US women's sizes. For most people, ordering their converted size works perfectly. If you have wider feet or prefer more room, consider going up half a size. The included extra footbeds allow for fine-tuning the fit.

Can Blundstone 550 boots be resoled?

Unfortunately, the Blundstone 550 boots cannot be traditionally resoled due to their cemented TPU sole construction. This is different from Goodyear welted boots that can have new soles attached. However, some cobblers can perform repairs to extend their life, such as patching holes or replacing elastic side panels. The trade-off is immediate comfort and lightweight construction versus long-term resolability.

What's the difference between 550 and other Blundstone models?

The 550 is part of Blundstone's Classic series, featuring leather lining, XRD heel technology, and enhanced cushioning compared to the Original 500 series. It offers a slightly wider fit and more comfort features while maintaining the iconic Chelsea boot silhouette. The 550 strikes the perfect balance between the traditional 500 and more specialized models like work boots or dress boots.

Are Blundstone 550 good for hiking?

Yes, Blundstone 550 boots are excellent for day hiking and light backpacking on well-established trails. The steel shank provides good arch support, and the TPU sole offers excellent traction on varied terrain. However, they're not ideal for technical climbing or multi-day backpacking where dedicated hiking boots with ankle support would be better. I've successfully hiked hundreds of miles in mine across different terrains.
