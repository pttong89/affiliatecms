Brand History and Mediterranean Heritage

The Founding Story

The story of soludos shoes begins with Nick Brown, a British entrepreneur who moved from London to New York City in 2006. Frustrated by the lack of quality espadrilles available in the American market, Brown recognized an opportunity to introduce authentic Mediterranean footwear to a broader audience. Having grown up wearing traditional espadrilles during family vacations along the Spanish coast, he understood the comfort and versatility these shoes could offer.

In 2010, Brown officially launched Soludos with a mission to celebrate "joyful living and travel" through footwear that embodied the relaxed Mediterranean lifestyle. The brand name itself reflects this philosophy, combining "sol" (Spanish for sun) with "ludos" (referencing play and leisure), creating a perfect encapsulation of the carefree spirit their shoes represent.

Traditional Craftsmanship Meets Modern Innovation

What makes soludos shoes authentic is their commitment to traditional espadrille construction methods that have been passed down through generations of Mediterranean craftspeople. The signature jute rope soles are hand-wrapped using techniques that date back centuries, creating the distinctive look and feel that espadrille enthusiasts recognize and appreciate.

During my visits to traditional espadrille workshops in Spain, I've witnessed firsthand the skill required to create these soles. The process involves precisely wrapping natural jute fibers around a sole-shaped form, creating the characteristic ribbed pattern that provides both flexibility and durability. Soludos has maintained these traditional methods while incorporating modern materials like rubber reinforcement layers for enhanced longevity and weather resistance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Soludos shoes run true to size?

Soludos shoes typically run about half a size small, especially in their espadrille collection. I recommend sizing up by half a size for most styles. The jute sole material stretches naturally over the first 30-60 minutes of wear to better fit your foot. So if they are a bit snug at first, take a few laps around your living room to see if they adjust to your feet! For their sneaker collection, sizing tends to be more true to size, though those with wider feet may still want to consider sizing up.

Are Soludos espadrilles comfortable for walking?

Yes, Soludos espadrilles are surprisingly comfortable for casual walking and daily wear. The natural jute sole provides excellent breathability, while the rope construction offers flexible support. However, they're not designed for extensive hiking or athletic activities. I find them perfect for city walks, beach strolls, and travel days. The platform styles offer additional cushioning, making them ideal for longer periods of standing or walking on pavement. For optimal comfort, choose leather versions for extended wear and ensure proper sizing.

How long do Soludos shoes typically last?

With regular wear, a quality pair of Soludos shoes typically lasts 12-18 months. The durability depends heavily on usage patterns and care. Canvas espadrilles may show wear sooner than leather versions, particularly in the toe area. The jute sole naturally compresses over time, which is normal. To extend lifespan, avoid wearing them in wet conditions, rotate with other shoes, and store them in a dry place between wears. I've found that leather platform styles tend to have the longest lifespan due to their more structured construction and premium materials.

Can Soludos shoes get wet?

While Soludos shoes have a thin rubber layer on the sole, they should not be worn in heavy rain or through puddles. The jute material and canvas upper can absorb water, leading to damage and potential odor issues. Light moisture from morning dew or brief drizzle is usually fine, but always allow them to air dry completely. For beach wear, they're perfect on dry sand but should be removed before entering water. I learned this lesson the hard way during a sudden downpour in Rome - waterlogged espadrilles take days to properly dry and may never feel quite the same.

What's the difference between Soludos espadrilles and sneakers?

Soludos espadrilles feature traditional jute rope soles with canvas or leather uppers, offering a classic Mediterranean aesthetic perfect for casual, warm-weather wear. Their sneakers combine espadrille-inspired rope detailing with more structured rubber soles and athletic-style construction. Sneakers provide better arch support and durability for active wear, while espadrilles excel in breathability and relaxed style. Both maintain the brand's commitment to comfort and Mediterranean-inspired design. If you need shoes for more active pursuits or extended walking, consider the sneaker collection; for casual wear and authentic espadrille experience, stick with their traditional styles.
