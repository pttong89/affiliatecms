The Everest UEPU: Flagship Hiking Model

The Everest UEPU represents Redback's flagship hiking design, engineered specifically for serious outdoor adventures. After extensive testing across various terrain types, I can confidently say this model addresses the primary challenges hikers face: waterproofing, comfort, and durability. The UEPU designation refers to the specific construction combining three-piece flexibility with one-piece upper protection, creating an optimal balance for hiking applications.

The waterproofing system utilizes Protec Aquapel leather treatment, which provides superior water resistance compared to membrane-based systems found in many hiking boots. Unlike synthetic waterproof membranes that can fail catastrophically, treated leather maintains water resistance even if minor damage occurs. The full-height bellows tongue design prevents water infiltration during stream crossings, a feature I've thoroughly tested during Colorado creek navigations. This tongue folds into itself when laced, creating an effective barrier against water and debris entry.

Technical Specifications

  • Upper Material: Premium full-grain Protec Aquapel waterproof leather
  • Lining: 3-zone air net mesh with moisture-wicking properties
  • Midsole: Lightweight air cushion technology for shock absorption
  • Outsole: Slip-resistant rubber with hiking-optimized tread pattern
  • Weight: Approximately 2.2 pounds per pair (size 10)
  • Price Range: $200-250 USD

Unlike simple fabric linings, this system features different mesh densities in high-wear areas, toe box, and heel regions. The mesh allows air circulation while wicking moisture away from the foot, maintaining comfort during extended wear periods.
