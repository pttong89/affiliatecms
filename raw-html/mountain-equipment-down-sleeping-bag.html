What's the difference between comfort and limit temperature ratings?

Comfort temperature is where a 'standard woman' feels comfortable in a relaxed position. Limit temperature is where a 'standard man' stays warm in a curled position. Mountain Equipment's 'Good Night's Sleep' rating falls between these, based on real-world testing and represents the practical minimum temperature for experienced users. This rating system accounts for factors like sleeping pad insulation, shelter conditions, and user experience level. The comfort rating is typically 5-10°C warmer than the limit rating, while the extreme rating represents survival conditions only and should never be relied upon for comfort or safety planning.

How should I care for my Mountain Equipment down sleeping bag?

Store uncompressed in a cool, dry place. Wash only when necessary using down-specific detergent in a front-loading machine with gentle cycle. Dry thoroughly with tennis balls in a large dryer on low heat, checking frequently. Avoid compression for extended periods and use a storage sack rather than the stuff sack for long-term storage. Regular airing helps maintain loft and prevents odors. For field care, always air-dry the bag when possible and avoid storing it damp. Professional cleaning services familiar with down products can extend the bag's lifespan significantly.
