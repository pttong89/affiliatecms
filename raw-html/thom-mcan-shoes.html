The Rich History of Thom McAn

The story of Thom McAn shoes begins in 1922 when Ward Melville partnered with J. Franklin McElwain, a New Hampshire shoe manufacturer, to create what would become one of America's most recognizable footwear brands. The name itself pays homage to Scottish golfer Thomas McCann, reflecting the founders' appreciation for both athletic prowess and craftsmanship.

During my research into vintage American footwear, I discovered that the first Thom McAn retail store opened in New York City with a revolutionary concept for its time: selling quality shoes at fixed, affordable prices. This approach was groundbreaking in an era when shoe shopping often involved lengthy negotiations and uncertain quality standards.

The brand's rapid expansion was nothing short of remarkable. Within just five years, 300 stores dotted the American landscape, and by 1939, over 650 locations served customers nationwide. This growth coincided with America's economic recovery and the emerging middle class's desire for reliable, stylish footwear.

What truly set Thom McAn shoes apart during their golden era was their commitment to democratizing fashion. While expensive European imports dominated the high-end market, and cheap, poorly-made shoes filled the low end, Thom McAn occupied the crucial middle ground. They offered shoes that looked sophisticated enough for business wear yet remained affordable for the average American worker.

The acquisition by Melville Corporation in 1952 marked a new chapter in the company's evolution. By the 1960s, Melville had become the largest footwear retailer in the United States, operating over 1,400 stores. However, changing consumer preferences and the rise of athletic footwear began to challenge traditional shoe retailers in the 1980s and 1990s, ultimately leading to the closure of standalone Thom McAn shoes stores in 1996.

Care and Maintenance Tips

Proper care is essential for maximizing the lifespan of your Thom McAn shoes. Having maintained vintage pairs for over two decades, I've learned that these shoes respond exceptionally well to traditional leather care techniques.

Daily Maintenance Routine

After each wear, I remove my Thom McAn shoes and insert cedar shoe trees immediately. This practice prevents creasing and helps maintain the leather's shape. Cedar also absorbs moisture and odors, which is particularly important for shoes worn in various weather conditions during national park visits.

Weekly brushing with a horsehair brush removes dirt and restores the leather's natural luster. For suede or nubuck versions, I use a specialized suede brush, working gently to maintain the material's texture without damaging the fibers.

Deep Cleaning and Conditioning

Monthly conditioning with high-quality leather cream keeps the material supple and prevents cracking. I prefer neutral-colored products that won't alter the shoe's original patina. This approach has kept my oldest pair of Thom McAn shoes looking remarkably fresh despite decades of use.

For deep stains or scuffs, I use gentle leather cleaners applied with soft cloths. The key is patience‚Äîmultiple light treatments work better than aggressive scrubbing, which can damage the leather's surface permanently.

Storage Best Practices

Proper storage extends the life of any quality footwear significantly. I store my collection in a climate-controlled closet with good air circulation. Each pair sits in individual dust bags to prevent scuffing and color transfer between different leather types.

Are Thom McAn shoes still being made?

Yes, Thom McAn shoes are still manufactured today, though the retail chain closed in 1996. The brand is currently owned by Sears Brands, LLC and shoes are sold through Kmart, Sears, and various online retailers including Amazon and eBay. However, the quality and selection have significantly diminished compared to the brand's heyday. Modern production focuses on basic styles rather than the diverse range once available. For the best quality and variety, vintage pairs from the 1960s-1980s remain the preferred choice among enthusiasts and collectors.

What makes Thom McAn shoes special?

Thom McAn shoes were known for their affordable pricing, durability, and classic American styling. Founded in 1922, they became synonymous with reliable everyday footwear that lasted for years, making them a nostalgic favorite for many Americans. The brand pioneered the concept of fixed-price, quality footwear, making well-made shoes accessible to the growing middle class. Their construction emphasized comfort and longevity over fashion trends, resulting in shoes that could serve wearers for decades. This practical approach to footwear design, combined with timeless styling, created a uniquely American aesthetic that remains appealing today.
