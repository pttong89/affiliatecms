What Makes a Great Camping Surplus Store

Finding an exceptional camping surplus store near me requires knowing what separates the wheat from the chaff. During my extensive travels, I've discovered that the best surplus stores share several key characteristics that make them worth your time and money.

Authentic Military Gear Selection

A quality surplus store stocks genuine military-issued equipment, not knock-offs or civilian replicas. I've learned to spot the difference – authentic gear bears military contract numbers, specific manufacturer markings, and shows signs of actual military use. The best stores I've visited, like The Surplus Store in Los Angeles (operating since 1946), maintain relationships with military contractors and government liquidators to ensure authenticity.

Knowledgeable staff makes all the difference. At quality camping supply stores, employees understand both military specifications and civilian applications. They can explain why a NATO sleeping bag works better than commercial alternatives for winter camping, or how to properly waterproof vintage canvas gear.

Pro Tip from Experience

The best surplus stores inspect all gear before selling and offer basic guarantees on functionality. I always ask about their return policy and whether they test equipment like sleeping bags for zipper function and insulation integrity.

Pros and Cons of Shopping Surplus vs. New Gear

After comparing surplus and retail gear across hundreds of camping trips, I've identified clear advantages and limitations to military surplus shopping. Understanding these trade-offs helps you make informed decisions based on your specific camping needs and budget constraints.

Advantages of Surplus Gear

  • Cost Savings: Typically 40-70% less than comparable new gear
  • Proven Durability: Designed for harsh military conditions
  • Quality Materials: Heavy-duty fabrics, genuine leather, solid hardware
  • Unique Features: Military-specific designs often superior to civilian versions
  • Repairability: Simple construction allows easy field repairs
  • Authenticity: Genuine military history adds character

Limitations to Consider

  • Weight Penalty: Military gear prioritizes durability over weight
  • Limited Sizes: Military sizing may not fit all body types
  • Condition Varies: Used gear requires careful inspection
  • Outdated Technology: Newer materials may perform better
  • Availability Issues: Specific items may be hard to find
  • No Warranties: Usually sold "as-is" without guarantees

Real-World Performance Comparisons

I've conducted side-by-side testing of surplus versus modern gear across various conditions. For car camping and base camp situations, surplus gear consistently wins on value and durability. My military canvas wall tent has withstood winds that destroyed neighboring modern nylon tents, despite costing one-third the price.

However, for backpacking and weight-conscious activities, modern gear often makes more sense. My surplus wool blanket weighs 4.5 pounds compared to a down quilt's 1.2 pounds. When every ounce matters, this weight difference significantly impacts hiking comfort and endurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for in a quality camping surplus store?

Look for stores with knowledgeable staff, authentic military surplus items, fair pricing, good return policies, and a wide selection. Check if they inspect gear for quality, offer guarantees on functionality, and have been in business for several years. The best surplus stores will have everything from sleeping systems to backpacks, with staff who understand both military specifications and civilian outdoor needs. Also verify they can explain military contract numbers and provide condition descriptions for used items.

Is military surplus gear actually good for camping?

Yes, military surplus gear is excellent for camping when chosen carefully. Military equipment is designed to withstand harsh conditions and extensive use. Items like wool blankets, canvas tarps, and steel canteens often outperform civilian alternatives. However, some surplus gear may be heavier than modern ultralight options, so consider your specific needs and camping style when shopping. Military gear prioritizes durability and reliability over weight savings, making it ideal for car camping and base camp situations.

How much can I save buying surplus gear instead of new camping equipment?

You can typically save 40-70% compared to new outdoor gear prices. For example, genuine military wool blankets cost $30-50 versus $100+ for comparable new outdoor blankets. Military backpacks range from $25-60 compared to $150-300 for similar civilian packs. The savings are most significant on durable items like tents, sleeping bags, and field equipment. However, factor in that surplus gear is usually sold "as-is" without warranties, so inspect items carefully before purchasing.

What are the most essential surplus items for new campers?

Start with a military wool blanket for warmth, a canvas tarp for shelter, a steel canteen for water, and a surplus backpack. These four items provide the foundation for any camping setup. Add a military mess kit, field jacket, and bivvy sack as your budget allows. These essentials will serve you well for years and cost a fraction of new gear prices. Focus on items made from natural materials like wool and canvas, as these tend to age better and perform consistently across various conditions.

Where can I find reputable camping surplus stores?

Look for established family-owned surplus stores that have been operating for decades. Check online reviews, ask local outdoor enthusiasts for recommendations, and visit stores in person when possible. Many cities have at least one quality surplus store. Online options include Major Surplus, Coleman's Military Surplus, and other established retailers with good return policies and authentic gear. Also check near military bases, as these areas often have multiple surplus stores competing for service member business, resulting in better selection and prices.
