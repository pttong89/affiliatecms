Frequently Asked Questions

Are On Cloud women's hiking boots waterproof?

Yes, most On Cloud women's hiking boots feature waterproof membranes. The Cloudrock Mid WP and Cloudrock 2 Waterproof models use proprietary waterproof technology that keeps feet dry while maintaining breathability. However, some users report that waterproofing may diminish over time with heavy use. The membrane performs well in light rain and creek crossings but may not withstand prolonged submersion. Regular care and reproofing treatments can help maintain water resistance. For extreme wet conditions, consider additional waterproof socks as backup protection.

How do On Cloud women's hiking boots fit compared to regular shoes?

On Cloud women's hiking boots typically run true to size, but many users recommend going up half a size for hiking comfort. The toe box is generally roomy, but the heel and midfoot provide a snug, secure fit. Always try them on with hiking socks before purchasing. The unique CloudTec sole adds height, which some users find takes adjustment. Width-wise, they accommodate average to slightly wide feet well. If you have very narrow or very wide feet, consider trying them on first. The break-in period is minimal compared to traditional leather boots, usually comfortable within the first few wears.

What's the difference between Cloudrock 2 and Cloudrock Mid WP?

The main differences are height and features. Cloudrock Mid WP is a mid-cut boot offering more ankle support, while Cloudrock 2 is lower profile. Both feature waterproof membranes, but the Mid WP has enhanced Missiongrip outsole and improved lacing system for better security on technical terrain. The Mid WP weighs slightly more but provides superior stability for backpacking. Cloudrock 2 excels in agility and speed hiking scenarios. Price difference is about $20, with Mid WP being more expensive. Choose Mid WP for technical trails and overnight trips; choose Cloudrock 2 for day hiking and urban adventures.

How durable are On Cloud women's hiking boots?

Durability varies by model and usage. While On Cloud boots feature quality construction, some users report faster wear on the outsole compared to traditional hiking boots. They're best suited for light to moderate hiking rather than extreme conditions or heavy backpacking. The CloudTec sole technology, while comfortable, may wear faster on abrasive surfaces like sharp rocks. Upper materials are generally durable with good stitching quality. Most users get 300-500 miles of hiking use, similar to trail running shoes. For maximum durability, rotate between multiple pairs and avoid consistently hiking on very rough terrain. Consider them lifestyle hiking boots rather than expedition-grade equipment.

Can I use On Cloud hiking boots for everyday wear?

Absolutely! On Cloud women's hiking boots are designed with style and comfort in mind, making them excellent for everyday urban wear. Their lightweight construction and modern aesthetics make them versatile enough for both trail and street use. The CloudTec technology provides exceptional comfort for walking on pavement and concrete. Many users appreciate them as stylish alternatives to traditional sneakers or casual boots. The waterproof versions work well in various weather conditions for daily activities. They pair well with outdoor casual wear, athleisure, and even some business casual outfits. This versatility justifies the premium price for many users who want one boot for multiple purposes.
