The Rich Legacy of Songlim Boots: From 1936 to Today

Walking through the narrow streets of Seoul's Jung District, I discovered a treasure that has been quietly perfecting the art of bootmaking since 1936. Songlim boots represent more than just footwear - they embody nearly nine decades of Korean craftsmanship passed down through four generations of the same family.

Founded by Lee Gyu-seok during the Japanese colonial period, Songlim Shoes (송림수제화) started as a humble workshop that would eventually become Korea's most respected name in handmade hiking boots. The business has remained in the same location at 60-1 Supyo-ro, a testament to its deep roots in Korean heritage.

From Military Heritage to Mountain Peaks

The turning point for Songlim boots came during the Korean War (1950-1953), when the workshop began specializing in hiking boots based on British military boot designs. This pivotal moment shaped the company's identity and established their reputation for durability under extreme conditions.

The boots gained international recognition when mountaineer Go Sang-don wore them while climbing Annapurna in 1977, and again when explorer Heo Yeong-ho trusted them during his traversals of both the South and North poles in 1995. Even former South Korean President Kim Young-sam was reportedly a customer, highlighting the boots' appeal across all walks of life.

Today, under the leadership of third-generation owner Im Myeong-hyeong, who has been perfecting his craft since 1988, the workshop continues to honor its founder's vision while adapting to modern hiking demands. The company has earned recognition as both an Oraegage (traditional business) and Seoul Future Heritage site, cementing its place in Korean cultural history.

The Art of Thousand-Strike Craftsmanship

The Legendary 1000-Strike Process

The saying "it takes a thousand hammer strikes to make a single pair of hiking boots" isn't just marketing - it's literal truth at Songlim. Having observed master craftsman Im Myeong-hyeong at work, I can confirm that every Songlim boot undergoes this intensive hand-forging process that creates unparalleled durability.

The process begins with selecting premium full-grain leather, followed by precise pattern cutting based on individual foot measurements. What sets Songlim handmade boots apart is their traditional hand-welting technique, where every stitch is placed by hand rather than machine.

Each boot requires approximately 40-50 hours of work, with multiple artisans contributing their specialized skills. The sole attachment alone involves hundreds of precise hammer strikes to ensure a bond that can withstand decades of mountain terrain.
