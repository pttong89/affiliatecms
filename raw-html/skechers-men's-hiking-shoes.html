Do I need to size up or down when buying Skechers hiking shoes?

Most skechers men's hiking shoes run true to size, but there are important considerations for optimal fit. I recommend ordering your normal athletic shoe size for most models, but consider going up half a size if you plan to wear thick hiking socks or have wider feet. The Relaxed Fit models provide extra room through the toe box and are excellent for hikers who typically struggle with narrow shoes. Always account for foot swelling during long hikes by ensuring about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the shoe front. If you're between sizes, I suggest going larger – you can always adjust with different sock thickness, but too-tight shoes will cause discomfort and blisters.

Can Skechers hiking shoes handle wet and muddy conditions?

Yes, but model selection is crucial! Waterproof models like the Selmen Enago feature genuine waterproof membranes that keep feet completely dry during stream crossings and heavy rain – I've personally tested them through Pacific Northwest winters. However, non-waterproof models like the standard Segment Garnet will absorb water but dry relatively quickly. The aggressive tread patterns on most skechers men's hiking shoes provide excellent traction on muddy trails and wet rocks. For consistently wet conditions, invest in a waterproof model and pair with quality hiking socks. The combination has kept my feet comfortable and dry through conditions that would challenge much more expensive boots.

Are Skechers hiking shoes suitable for people with foot problems?

Skechers men's hiking shoes are often excellent choices for hikers with common foot issues, thanks to their superior comfort features. The Air-Cooled Memory Foam insoles provide exceptional cushioning for plantar fasciitis sufferers, while the Relaxed Fit models accommodate bunions and wider feet comfortably. Many models feature arch support technology that helps with flat feet or high arches. However, severe foot problems should be addressed with proper medical orthotics, which can typically be inserted into Skechers' removable insole designs. I've received numerous reports from hikers who switched to Skechers specifically because traditional hiking boots aggravated their foot problems, finding immediate relief and improved hiking enjoyment.
