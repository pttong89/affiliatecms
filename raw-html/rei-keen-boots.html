Complete Buying Guide and Sizing Tips

Selecting the right REI Keen boots requires understanding your hiking style, foot anatomy, and intended use. After helping dozens of friends and fellow hikers choose their perfect boots, I've developed a systematic approach that eliminates guesswork and prevents expensive mistakes.

Understanding Keen's Sizing Philosophy

Keen boots traditionally run about half a size larger than athletic shoes, but this varies between models. The Targhee series tends to be most true to size, while the NXIS line runs slightly smaller due to its performance-oriented last. I always recommend measuring both feet in the afternoon when they're naturally swollen to simulate hiking conditions.

The width options deserve special attention. Keen's regular width accommodates most hikers comfortably, but their wide options are genuinely wider than many competitors. If you've struggled with narrow boots from other brands, REI men's hiking boots from Keen might solve persistent fit issues.

Essential Sizing Tips:

  • Always try boots on with your intended hiking socks
  • Ensure a thumb's width of space between longest toe and boot end
  • Check for heel slippage when walking inclines and declines
  • Test different lacing techniques to optimize fit
  • Consider foot swelling during long hikes

Matching Boots to Your Hiking Style

Day hikers benefit most from lighter models like the Zionic or NXIS series. These boots provide adequate protection without the bulk of full mountaineering boots. For weekend backpackers carrying moderate loads, the Targhee IV strikes the perfect balance between protection and agility. Serious backpackers and mountaineers should consider the Pyrenees for maximum durability and support.

Climate considerations significantly impact boot selection. In consistently wet environments, prioritize models with proven waterproofing like the Targhee series. For desert hiking or dry climates, the improved breathability of the NXIS line becomes more important than ultimate waterproofing. Understanding these trade-offs helps optimize your camping REI gear selections.
