The Ten Collection: A Revolution in Sneaker Design

When Virgil Abloh unveiled "The Ten" collection in September 2017, it wasn't just another sneaker collaboration—it was a cultural earthquake that redefined what off white nike shoes could represent. Having been fortunate enough to witness the initial drop firsthand at a Nike store in Chicago, I can attest to the unprecedented hype and genuine innovation these shoes brought to the table.

The collection featured ten iconic Nike silhouettes, each deconstructed and reimagined through Abloh's architectural lens. What made these shoes revolutionary wasn't just their aesthetic—though the exposed foam, quotation marks, and signature zip-ties certainly caught attention—but how they challenged traditional sneaker construction. Each pair told a story of creative destruction and rebuilding, embodying Abloh's "3% rule" of making minimal changes that create maximum impact.

Design Philosophy Behind The Ten

Abloh's approach to off white nike shoes was fundamentally different from typical collaborations. Rather than simply changing colorways, he dissected each silhouette to understand its DNA, then rebuilt it with industrial design principles. This methodology transformed classics like the Air Jordan 1 and introduced forgotten gems like the Air Presto to a new generation of sneakerheads.

The original ten silhouettes—Air Jordan 1, Blazer Mid, Air Max 90, Air Presto, React Hyperdunk 2017, VaporMax, Air Max 97, Zoom Fly, Air Force 1, and Chuck Taylor All-Star—each represented a different era of Nike's history. This strategic selection wasn't coincidental; it demonstrated Abloh's deep understanding of sneaker culture and his ability to bridge generational gaps through design.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Off-White Nike shoes worth the high resale prices?

After owning multiple pairs for seven years, I believe certain models justify their prices through exceptional build quality, cultural significance, and investment potential. The Air Jordan 1 "Chicago" and "UNC" colorways have proven their worth both as wearable art and financial investments. However, newer releases with similar pricing may not offer the same value proposition. I recommend focusing on iconic colorways from "The Ten" collection if budget allows, as these consistently hold or increase their value while offering the authentic Off-White Nike experience.

How can I authenticate Off-White Nike shoes before purchasing?

Authentication requires examining multiple details simultaneously. Key indicators include the precision of quotation mark text (should be thin and properly spaced), zip-tie quality (thick, sturdy plastic with correct Off-White branding), "AIR" text placement on midsoles (perfectly centered and properly sized), and box authenticity (correct fonts, materials, and included accessories). I strongly recommend using professional authentication services like StockX, GOAT, or Flight Club for high-value purchases. These platforms charge authentication fees but provide peace of mind and buyer protection that justify the cost, especially given the sophisticated replica market.

Which Off-White Nike model is best for beginners?

For newcomers to off white nike shoes, I recommend starting with the Air Force 1 or Blazer silhouettes. These models offer the signature Off-White aesthetic at relatively accessible price points (though still premium), feature comfortable, true-to-size fits, and work well with various styling options. The Air Force 1 Low in black or white colorways provides excellent versatility for daily wear, while the Blazer Mid offers a slightly more unique profile without being too bold. Both models demonstrate Off-White's design language clearly while being approachable for those new to the brand's aesthetic.

Should I keep the zip-tie on my Off-White Nike shoes?

This remains a hotly debated topic in sneaker communities. From my personal experience, I keep zip-ties on pairs intended for special occasions or display, but remove them for daily wear due to practical concerns. The zip-tie can catch on clothing, feel uncomfortable during extended wear, and potentially damage over time with regular use. However, keeping it intact maintains the complete Off-White aesthetic and may preserve resale value. My recommendation: if you're wearing them regularly, remove and safely store the zip-tie. For collection pieces or occasional wear, leave it attached to maintain the original design intent.

What's the future of Off-White Nike collaborations after Virgil Abloh?

Following Virgil Abloh's passing in November 2021, Nike has continued releasing previously designed Off-White collaborations, including the Blazer Low in 2022. The brand has stated commitment to honoring Abloh's legacy through continued releases of his existing designs. However, new creative direction remains uncertain. Current market trends suggest existing Off-White Nike shoes will likely appreciate further due to the finite nature of the collaboration. I anticipate Nike will eventually announce new creative partnerships, but Abloh's original designs will maintain their iconic status and investment potential. Collectors should focus on acquiring key pieces from "The Ten" and subsequent releases while they remain accessible.
