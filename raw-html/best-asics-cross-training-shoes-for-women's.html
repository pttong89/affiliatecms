Frequently Asked Questions

Are ASICS good for cross training?

Yes, ASICS offers excellent cross training shoes for women. While primarily known for running shoes, ASICS has developed specific models like the Gel-Noosa Tri series and DS Trainer that excel in cross training environments. These shoes provide the lateral support, stability, and durability needed for multi-directional movements, weightlifting, and high-intensity workouts. The brand's focus on biomechanics and quality construction makes them particularly suitable for women who engage in diverse training activities.

What's the difference between ASICS running shoes and cross training shoes?

ASICS cross training shoes differ from running shoes in several key ways: they have flatter soles for better ground contact during lifting, reinforced sidewalls for lateral movement support, more durable outsole materials for gym surfaces, and firmer midsole construction for stability during multi-directional activities. Running shoes prioritize forward motion and shock absorption, featuring higher heel drops and softer cushioning. Cross trainers like the Gel-Noosa Tri series typically have 6mm drops or less, while running shoes often have 10mm+ drops for heel-to-toe transition.

Which ASICS model is best for cross training women?

The ASICS Gel-Noosa Tri series is widely considered the best for women's cross training. These shoes feature a low drop design, excellent lateral support, quick-lacing system, and durable construction. The Gel-Noosa Tri 16 is the latest model, offering improved stability and comfort for various training activities including HIIT, functional fitness, and light weightlifting. For heavier lifting focused workouts, the Gel-Fit Sana provides even more stability with its 4mm drop and reinforced platform design.

How long do ASICS cross training shoes last?

ASICS cross training shoes typically last 6-12 months with regular use, depending on training frequency and intensity. For someone training 4-5 times per week, expect 300-500 miles of wear. Signs it's time to replace them include worn outsole tread, compressed midsole cushioning, uneven wear patterns, or loss of lateral support structure. The Gel-Noosa Tri series tends to last longer due to its robust construction, while lighter models like the Versablast may show wear sooner with intensive use.

Can I use ASICS cross training shoes for running?

While ASICS cross training shoes can handle short runs and treadmill work, they're not ideal for longer distance running. Cross trainers have firmer midsoles and less heel-to-toe drop, which can cause discomfort during extended running sessions. For serious running, stick to dedicated ASICS running shoes like the Gel-Nimbus or Gel-Cumulus series. However, if you're doing short cardio intervals or treadmill sprints as part of your cross training, models like the Gel-Contend 9 can handle these activities comfortably.

What size should I get in ASICS cross training shoes?

ASICS typically runs true to size, but for cross training, consider going up half a size to accommodate foot swelling during intense workouts. Ensure there's about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the shoe front. Try shoes on in the afternoon when feet are naturally more swollen to get the best fit for training sessions. If you have wide feet, look for 2E width options or consider models like the Gel-Contend series which tend to run slightly wider than the Gel-Noosa Tri.
