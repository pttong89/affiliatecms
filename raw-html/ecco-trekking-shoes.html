Complete Buying Guide and Sizing Tips

Selecting the right ECCO trekking shoes requires understanding both your hiking needs and ECCO's unique sizing characteristics. Unlike many outdoor brands that follow standard athletic shoe sizing, ECCO uses European sizing principles that can affect fit, particularly for those accustomed to American hiking boot brands.

Understanding ECCO Sizing

ECCO shoes typically run true to European size but may feel different than expected if you're used to brands like Merrell or Keen. The toe box tends to be slightly narrower, while the heel cup provides excellent security. For most hikers, I recommend staying with your normal hiking boot size, but those with wider feet should consider going up half a size.

The premium leather construction means ECCO shoes will stretch and conform to your feet over time. This characteristic can be both an advantage and a consideration – while the eventual fit becomes incredibly personalized, initial fitting should account for this stretching. When trying on ECCO trekking shoes, ensure you have adequate room in the toe box without excessive heel slippage.
