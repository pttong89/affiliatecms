Can LOWA Renegade GTX Mid boots be resoled?

No, LOWA Renegade GTX Mid hiking boots cannot be resoled due to their injection-molded construction. This is one of the main criticisms of the model, especially considering the $240-280 price point. The DuraPU midsole and Vibram outsole are permanently bonded during manufacturing using a process that makes separation and replacement impossible without destroying the boot. This means when the sole wears out (typically after 500-1000 miles depending on terrain and usage), you'll need to purchase new boots. Consider this limitation when evaluating long-term cost versus models like the LOWA Tibet that can be resoled for approximately $100-150.
