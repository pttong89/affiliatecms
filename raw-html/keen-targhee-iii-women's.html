Detailed Features & Technology

Understanding the technology behind the keen targhee iii women's boots helps explain both their strengths and limitations. KEEN.DRY Waterproof Technology

The KEEN.DRY membrane is the heart of these boots' weather protection system. Unlike Gore-Tex, which many competitors use, KEEN developed their proprietary waterproof-breathable membrane. Through extensive testing, I found it effectively keeps water out while allowing some vapor transfer. However, it's noticeably less breathable than Gore-Tex alternatives, particularly during high-output activities. The waterproof hiking shoes version offers the same technology in a lower-cut design.

All-Terrain Rubber Outsole

The outsole features 4mm multi-directional lugs designed for varied terrain grip. The rubber compound provides excellent traction on most surfaces, though it shows wear more quickly than Vibram alternatives. During my testing, the lugs performed exceptionally well on loose rock and moderate mud, but struggled somewhat on wet roots and smooth rock surfaces. The heel brake design effectively prevents backward slipping on steep descents.

Leather and Synthetic Upper Construction

The combination of nubuck leather and synthetic materials creates a durable yet flexible upper. The leather requires minimal break-in time but does need regular conditioning for longevity. Sizing, Fit & Comfort Guide

Proper sizing is crucial for the keen targhee iii women's boots, and my extensive fitting experience with multiple sizes reveals important nuances that aren't covered in standard sizing guides. The boot's unique last shape means traditional sizing rules don't always apply.

Size Selection Strategy

Based on fitting dozens of women with varying foot shapes, I recommend sizing up half a size from your typical athletic shoe size. This accounts for hiking sock thickness and foot swelling during long hikes. However, women with narrow feet may find their true size more appropriate, while those with wide feet often benefit from a full size increase. The wide width versions are available for those needing additional room.

During break-in testing, I discovered these boots require minimal adjustment period – typically 20-30 miles of use. The leather upper softens quickly, but the footbed takes longer to conform to individual foot shape. I recommend wearing them for progressively longer walks before committing to major hikes.

Foot Shape Compatibility

The Targhee III's design strongly favors certain foot shapes. Women with high-volume feet (high instep, wide forefoot) generally love these boots. However, those with low-volume, narrow feet often struggle with heel slippage and loose fit through the midfoot. The toe box is genuinely spacious – a blessing for those with wider feet or toe issues, but potentially problematic for precise fit preferences.

Sizing Recommendations by Foot Type:

  • Narrow feet: True to size, consider heel grips
  • Average width: Half size up from athletic shoes
  • Wide feet: Half to full size up, or try wide version
  • High arches: May need aftermarket insoles for support

Frequently Asked Questions

How do KEEN Targhee III women's boots fit compared to other brands?

The keen targhee iii women's boots run wider and more voluminous than most European brands like Salomon or La Sportiva. They fit similarly to other American brands like Merrell but with a more spacious toe box. If you typically wear narrow or medium-width shoes, you may find these boots too roomy. The last is designed for average to wide feet with medium to high volume. I recommend trying them on in-store if possible, as the fit is quite specific to KEEN's design philosophy.

Are these boots suitable for winter hiking and snow conditions?

The Targhee III performs adequately in light snow and cold conditions but isn't designed for serious winter hiking. The waterproof membrane helps with slush and wet snow, but the tread pattern lacks the aggressive lugs needed for icy conditions. For winter use, you'll need microspikes or crampons on most surfaces. The insulation is minimal, so warm socks are essential. For dedicated winter hiking, consider insulated versions or specialized winter boots.

How long do KEEN Targhee III boots typically last with regular use?

Based on my testing and user reports, expect 400-600 miles of trail use before significant wear appears. However, durability varies greatly with hiking style, terrain, and care. The outsole typically shows wear before the upper, though some users report premature separation at stress points. With proper care including regular cleaning and leather conditioning, many users get 1-2 years of regular use. This is shorter than previous Targhee generations, which is a common complaint among longtime KEEN users.

Can I use custom orthotics with these boots?

Yes, the removable footbed accommodates most custom orthotics, though you may need to size up if your orthotics are thick. The toe box provides adequate room for orthotic-related volume increases. However, the existing arch support is minimal, so those requiring significant support will definitely benefit from custom insoles. I've successfully used Superfeet Green insoles with these boots, which improved support without compromising fit.

How do these compare to the newer Targhee IV model?

The Targhee IV features updated construction methods and materials, potentially addressing some durability concerns of the III. However, many users report the IV fits differently – notably narrower in the toe box, which eliminates one of the Targhee III's key advantages. The IV also uses different sole construction that may affect flexibility. Until more long-term testing data is available, the III remains a proven option, especially for wide-footed hikers. The waterproof versions of both models use similar technology.

What's the best way to care for and maintain these boots?

Regular maintenance significantly extends boot life. Clean off mud and debris after each use, and allow them to air dry completely before storage. Apply leather conditioner every 3-4 months or when the leather appears dry. Avoid direct heat sources which can damage the waterproof membrane. The laces tend to wear at eyelets, so replacing them preemptively prevents field failures. For waterproof maintenance, reapply DWR treatment annually or when water stops beading on the surface.
