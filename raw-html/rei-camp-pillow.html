Understanding REI Camp Pillow Types

REI's pillow lineup represents decades of co-op member feedback and field testing across diverse outdoor conditions. Care & Maintenance Tips

Proper maintenance extends the life of your rei camp pillow significantly while maintaining optimal performance. Through years of field use and consultation with REI gear specialists, I've developed comprehensive care protocols that maximize pillow longevity and comfort retention.

Cleaning Protocols by Pillow Type

Foam-filled pillows like the Base Camp and Trailmade models require gentle machine washing with mild detergent. The key insight from my maintenance experience is using the gentle cycle with cold water to prevent foam compression and cover shrinkage. I learned this lesson the hard way during a cleaning mishap that left my favorite pillow permanently flattened.

Essential Maintenance Kit

  • Specialized down wash for down pillows
  • Gentle fabric detergent for synthetic models
  • Clean tennis balls for dryer fluffing
  • Waterproof storage bags for long-term storage
  • Patch kit for inflatable models

Down pillows require specialized care using products like Nikwax Down Wash to maintain loft and water resistance. Regular detergents strip natural oils from down, leading to clumping and reduced insulation properties. During a consultation with a REI gear specialist in Seattle, I learned that proper washing actually improves down performance by removing accumulated body oils and dirt.

Drying techniques prove crucial for maintaining pillow integrity. High heat damages both synthetic and natural fills, so I always use low heat settings with clean tennis balls to promote even drying and prevent clumping. For down pillows, this process can take several hours, but patience prevents costly replacement needs.

Storage and Field Care

Proper storage between camping seasons significantly impacts pillow longevity. Down pillows should never be stored compressed, as this permanently damages the down's loft properties. I maintain dedicated storage space for uncompressed gear, treating it as an investment in future comfort and avoiding replacement costs.

Field care during camping trips prevents many maintenance issues. Using pillowcases or pillow protectors reduces direct contact with skin oils and environmental contamination. This simple step, recommended by a park ranger during my stay at Great Smoky Mountains National Park, extends time between deep cleaning cycles substantially.

How do I clean and maintain my REI camp pillow?

Most REI foam pillows are machine washable on gentle cycle with mild detergent. For down pillows, use specialized down wash like Nikwax to maintain loft and water resistance. Between uses, store uncompressed and use pillowcases to reduce direct contact with skin oils. Regular maintenance extends pillow life significantly and maintains optimal performance.
