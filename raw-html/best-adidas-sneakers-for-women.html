How to Measure Your Feet Correctly

  1. Measure both feet in the evening when they're naturally swollen
  2. Use a ruler to measure from heel to longest toe
  3. Measure the width at the widest part of your foot
  4. Always go with the larger foot's measurements
  5. Consider your intended use - running shoes can be 0.5 size larger

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most popular Adidas sneakers for women in 2025?

The most popular Adidas sneakers for women in 2025 include the iconic Samba OG, versatile Gazelle Indoor, performance-focused Ultraboost 5X, and the classic Stan Smith. These models combine timeless design with modern comfort technologies, making them perfect for both casual wear and athletic activities. The Samba OG particularly stands out for its leather construction and heritage appeal, while the Ultraboost 5X excels in technical performance with its Boost midsole technology.

How do Adidas women's sneakers fit compared to other brands?

Adidas women's sneakers typically run true to size, but some models like the Samba and Gazelle can run slightly narrow. The Ultraboost line offers a more accommodating fit with its Primeknit upper that adapts to your foot shape. Compared to Nike, Adidas generally offers a slightly roomier toe box, while compared to New Balance, they tend to run about half a size smaller. I recommend trying them on first or ordering a half size up if you have wider feet, especially for leather models like the Samba OG.

Which Adidas sneakers are best for everyday walking?

For everyday walking, the Adidas Ultraboost 5X stands out with its superior cushioning and energy-return technology that reduces fatigue during long walking sessions. The Cloudfoam series also offers excellent comfort for all-day wear with its soft midsole construction. If you prefer a classic look, the Stan Smith provides adequate comfort for casual walking, though it lacks the advanced cushioning of the Ultraboost. These sneakers provide the support and comfort needed for extended walking periods while maintaining versatile style that works with various outfits.

Are Adidas Samba sneakers good for wide feet?

Adidas Samba sneakers tend to run narrow, which can be challenging for those with wide feet. The leather upper does provide some stretch over time, but initial comfort may be compromised. If you have wide feet, consider going up half a size or exploring the Gazelle Bold, which offers a slightly roomier fit while maintaining the classic aesthetic. Alternatively, the Ultraboost series with its Primeknit upper provides much better accommodation for wider feet while still offering the iconic three-stripe design that makes Adidas sneakers so recognizable.

What's the difference between Samba and Gazelle sneakers?

The main differences lie in their origins, construction, and aesthetic appeal. Samba features a leather and nubuck upper with a distinctive gum rubber sole, originally designed for indoor soccer in the 1950s. Gazelle has a suede upper with a standard rubber sole, initially created for training purposes in the 1960s. Samba offers more structure and durability due to its leather construction, while Gazelle provides a softer, more casual aesthetic with significantly more color options - over 40 different colorways compared to Samba's more limited palette. Both are excellent choices, but Samba excels in longevity while Gazelle wins in style versatility.
