Waterproof vs Non-Waterproof Options

One of the most important decisions when choosing Clarks walking boots for men is whether to opt for waterproof or non-waterproof versions. Having tested both extensively in various conditions, I can share insights that will help you make the right choice for your needs.

Waterproof Technologies

Clarks incorporates several waterproofing technologies in their walking boots. The most common is GORE-TEX, which I've found to be highly effective during my hikes through the Pacific Northwest's notoriously wet trails. The Clarks waterproof walking boots using GORE-TEX maintain breathability while keeping feet completely dry in wet conditions.

During a particularly challenging hike through Olympic National Park's rainforest trails, my GORE-TEX equipped Clarks boots kept my feet dry despite six hours of walking through puddles and stream crossings. The membrane technology allows moisture vapor to escape while preventing water from entering, creating a comfortable microclimate inside the boot.

When to Choose Waterproof

I recommend waterproof options for anyone planning to hike in wet climates, cross streams, or walk in snow. They're essential for spring hiking when snowmelt creates muddy conditions, and invaluable for year-round outdoor activities in regions with frequent precipitation.

Benefits of Non-Waterproof Boots

However, non-waterproof Clarks walking boots mens models have their advantages. They typically offer superior breathability, making them ideal for hot weather hiking or high-intensity activities where foot temperature regulation is crucial. During my summer backpacking trips through Utah's desert national parks, non-waterproof boots prevented overheating and excessive sweating.

Pro Tip: Consider your primary use case and local climate when deciding. If you'll be walking in consistently dry conditions, non-waterproof boots offer better breathability and are often more affordable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Clarks walking boots good for hiking?

Clarks walking boots excel for light to moderate hiking, particularly day hikes and well-maintained trails. Models like the ATL Trek series offer excellent traction, waterproofing, and comfort for hiking distances up to 15-20 miles. However, for serious technical hiking, mountaineering, or multi-day backpacking, you might want specialized hiking boots with more aggressive treads and additional ankle support. I've successfully used my Clarks boots on numerous national park trails, finding them perfect for most recreational hiking activities.

How long do Clarks walking boots typically last?

With proper care and moderate use, Clarks walking boots typically last 18-24 months of regular wear. Heavy hiking use may reduce this to 12-18 months, while casual walking can extend life to 2-3 years or more. Factors affecting durability include terrain type, frequency of use, care routine, and specific model construction. Premium models with reinforced construction generally last longer than fashion-oriented styles. I've had pairs last over two years with regular maintenance and appropriate use.

Do Clarks walking boots require a break-in period?

Most Clarks walking boots require minimal break-in time, typically 2-5 days of normal wear. This is significantly less than many hiking boot brands, making them excellent for immediate use on trips. The brand's focus on comfort means most models feel good right out of the box. However, I recommend wearing them for progressively longer periods during the first week to ensure optimal fit and comfort. Leather models may require slightly more break-in time than synthetic materials.

Are Clarks boots waterproof?

Clarks offers both waterproof and non-waterproof walking boots. Models featuring GORE-TEX or other waterproof membranes provide excellent water protection while maintaining breathability. Non-waterproof models offer superior breathability for dry conditions and hot weather. I recommend checking specific model descriptions, as waterproofing varies by style. Look for "GTX," "WP," or "waterproof" in the model name or description to identify water-resistant options.

How do Clarks walking boots fit compared to other brands?

Clarks walking boots generally run true to size with a slightly roomier toe box compared to athletic shoe brands. The width tends to accommodate average to slightly wider feet comfortably. If you typically wear hiking boots a half-size larger, you may find Clarks boots comfortable in your normal shoe size. However, sizing can vary between different Clarks models, so I recommend trying on specific styles when possible or ordering from retailers with generous return policies for online purchases.

What's the difference between Clarks Desert Boots and walking boots?

Clarks Desert Boots are primarily fashion-oriented footwear with moderate walking capability, featuring crepe soles and minimal ankle support. Walking boots are designed specifically for extended walking and light hiking, offering superior sole construction, better traction, often waterproof options, and enhanced comfort features. While Desert Boots work well for casual wear and short walks, dedicated walking boots provide better performance for serious walking activities, longer distances, and varied terrain. Both have their place depending on intended use and style preferences.
