My Proven Sizing Method:

  2. Check Merrell's specific size chart for each model
  3. Read recent reviews mentioning fit and sizing
  4. When in doubt, order two sizes and return one
  5. Consider width options – Merrell offers wide sizes in most models

What's the difference between Merrell's hiking shoes and walking shoes?

The primary differences lie in construction, tread pattern, and intended use. Hiking shoes like the Moab series feature more aggressive Vibram outsoles, reinforced toe caps, and stiffer midsoles for technical terrain and heavy loads. Walking shoes emphasize comfort for pavement and light trails, with softer cushioning and less aggressive tread patterns. Hiking shoes typically use more durable materials and construction methods, making them heavier but longer-lasting. Walking shoes often incorporate more breathable materials and flexible construction for all-day comfort on easier terrain. For versatility, I recommend hiking shoes if you plan any trail use, as they perform well on pavement while offering capabilities walking shoes lack.
