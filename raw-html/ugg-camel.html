Sizing & Fit Guide: Getting It Right

Important Sizing Note:

Based on my experience and extensive research of customer reviews, UGG sizing can be tricky. The sheepskin lining compresses over time, so what feels tight initially will likely become perfect after a few wears.

After purchasing multiple pairs of ugg camel boots over the years and helping friends with their sizing decisions, I've developed a foolproof approach to getting the perfect fit. Here's everything you need to know about UGG sizing, based on real-world experience and feedback from fellow UGG enthusiasts.

My Personal Sizing Journey

I typically wear a size 8 in most shoes, but my first pair of UGG camel boots were purchased in size 7, following the common advice to "size down." Initially, they felt uncomfortably tight around the ankle area, and I questioned the decision. However, after about two weeks of regular wear, the sheepskin compressed and molded to my feet perfectly. Now, three years later, they fit like they were custom-made for me.

When I needed to replace my worn-out pair, I confidently ordered the same size 7 in the Classic Mini II style. However, I noticed that newer UGG manufacturing seems slightly different – the boots felt less snug initially compared to my original pair. This experience taught me that consistency in sizing can vary slightly between production runs and styles.

My Sizing Recommendations:

  • 1. For narrow to medium feet: Order 0.5-1 size down from your typical shoe size
  • 2. For wide feet: Order your true shoe size or only 0.5 size down
  • 3. Between sizes? Go with the smaller size for classic styles
  • 4. Plan to wear thick socks? Consider your true size

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between UGG camel and chestnut colors?

Having owned both colors, the main difference is depth and warmth of tone. Camel is a lighter, more neutral beige-tan shade, while chestnut is deeper with reddish-brown undertones. Camel works better with cooler-toned wardrobes and summer colors, while chestnut complements fall and winter palettes beautifully. Both are incredibly versatile, but camel offers more year-round styling flexibility. For comparison with other earth-toned outdoor footwear, chestnut aligns more closely with traditional hiking boot colors.

Do UGG camel boots run true to size?

In my experience, UGG camel boots typically run large initially but fit perfectly after the sheepskin compresses. I recommend ordering 0.5 to 1 size down from your normal shoe size for most feet types. The boots should feel snug when new – almost uncomfortably tight around the ankle area. After 1-2 weeks of wear, they'll stretch and mold to your feet perfectly. If you have wide feet, consider ordering only 0.5 size down, and if you plan to wear thick socks regularly, your true size might work better.

Can you wear UGG camel boots in rain or snow?

UGG camel boots can handle light moisture and snow with proper treatment, but they're not inherently waterproof. I always apply UGG's water and stain repellent before wearing them outside. They perform well in light snow and dewy conditions, but I avoid wearing them in heavy rain or deep snow. The suede exterior can stain and the sheepskin lining takes a long time to dry if it gets saturated. For more weather-resistant options during harsh conditions, I switch to dedicated waterproof boots and reserve my UGGs for dry or lightly moist conditions.

How do you clean and maintain UGG camel boots?

Regular maintenance is crucial for keeping UGG camel boots looking their best. I use a suede brush weekly to restore the nap and remove surface dirt. For deeper cleaning, I use UGG's official cleaner and conditioner products following their specific instructions. The key is to clean stains immediately before they set, and always allow boots to air dry naturally – never use direct heat. I also apply water repellent treatment seasonally to maintain protection. Store them with boot shapers or stuff with newspaper to maintain their shape when not wearing them regularly.

Are UGG camel boots worth the high price?

Based on my five years of wearing UGG camel boots regularly, I believe they're worth the investment if you prioritize comfort and plan to wear them frequently. The cost per wear works out reasonably when you consider their durability and versatility. The genuine sheepskin construction, superior comfort, and timeless styling justify the price point compared to cheaper alternatives I've tried. However, if you only need boots for occasional wear or have limited budget, there are more affordable options available. The value proposition improves significantly if you find them on sale or purchase during end-of-season clearances.

What outfits work best with UGG camel boots?

UGG camel boots work beautifully with casual to business casual outfits in neutral and earth-tone color palettes. My favorite combinations include high-waisted leggings with oversized sweaters, straight-leg jeans with flannel shirts, and midi dresses with denim jackets. The camel color pairs exceptionally well with cream, white, navy, olive green, rust, and other warm neutrals. For seasonal styling, they're perfect with fall colors like burgundy and forest green, and winter whites and grays. Avoid pairing them with formal wear or bright, contrasting colors that might clash with the warm neutral tone.
