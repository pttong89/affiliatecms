Addressing Quality Concerns: What's Really Changed

I need to address the elephant in the room - quality control issues that have emerged since Anta's acquisition of Salomon's parent company. As someone who has used Salomon boots for over a decade, I've witnessed these changes firsthand and feel obligated to share my honest assessment.

Complete Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Model

Salomon sizing differs significantly from athletic shoes and even other hiking boots. Based on measuring dozens of pairs, here's my sizing advice: order 0.5 sizes larger than your normal athletic shoe size. For the Quest 4 specifically, some hikers need a full size up due to the narrow toe box.

Width is crucial - Salomon traditionally runs narrow. If you have wide feet, consider the Gore-Tex mens walking boots from brands like Merrell or Keen instead, or look for Salomon's limited wide-width options.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Salomon hiking boots true to size?

Salomon boots typically run 0.5 sizes small compared to athletic shoes. I recommend ordering a half size larger than your normal shoe size, and a full size larger for the Quest 4 model if you have wider feet. The narrow European last design requires careful sizing, especially for hikers accustomed to American boot brands. Always try boots with your preferred hiking socks, and remember that proper fit should allow wiggle room for toes while preventing heel slippage during downhill hiking.

How long do Salomon Gore-Tex boots last?

Based on my testing and user reports, well-manufactured Salomon Gore-Tex boots typically last 400-600 miles of hiking before showing significant wear. Quality control issues since 2020 have reduced this somewhat, with some pairs failing prematurely due to sole separation or Gore-Tex membrane damage. Proper care including regular cleaning, appropriate waterproofing treatments, and avoiding extended concrete walking can extend boot life significantly. The Quest 4 generally outlasts lighter models due to more robust construction.

Can you resoled Salomon hiking boots?

Unfortunately, Salomon hiking boots gore tex cannot be professionally resoled due to their molded construction and integrated Gore-Tex membrane. The Contagrip sole is permanently bonded to the midsole and waterproof bootie, making traditional cobbler repair impossible. This is a significant drawback compared to traditional leather boots from brands like Danner or Asolo. When the sole wears out, the entire boot must be replaced, which factors into the long-term cost calculation for serious hikers.

Do Salomon boots run wide or narrow?

Salomon boots definitely run narrow, following European last designs that prioritize precision fit over accommodation of wider feet. This narrow profile works well for technical hiking but can cause discomfort for hikers with wide feet or those accustomed to American boot brands. Limited wide-width options exist in select models, but availability is inconsistent. Hikers with wide feet should seriously consider alternatives like Merrell or Keen, or plan to size up significantly in Salomon boots while accepting potential heel slippage.

What's the difference between X Ultra and Quest series?

The X Ultra series prioritizes lightweight performance for day hiking and fast-packing, while Quest boots are built for heavy-duty backpacking with serious loads. X Ultra models weigh around 1 lb 15 oz and use lighter materials, making them ideal for covering long distances quickly. Quest boots weigh nearly 3 lbs per pair but provide superior ankle support, more durable materials, and better protection for technical terrain. Choose X Ultra for speed and agility, Quest for support and durability under challenging conditions.

Are Salomon boots good for winter hiking?

Salomon Gore-Tex boots perform well in winter conditions when properly paired with appropriate socks and gaiters. The Quest 4 provides the best cold-weather performance due to its higher cuff and more robust construction. However, they're not insulated boots, so temperature ratings depend heavily on sock choice and activity level. For serious winter mountaineering, consider dedicated winter boots with integrated insulation. For general winter hiking and snowshoeing, Salomon Gore-Tex boots work excellently when combined with microspikes or light crampons.
