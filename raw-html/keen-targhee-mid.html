Buying Guide and Recommendations

Sizing and Fit Considerations

Proper sizing is crucial for optimal performance from your keen targhee mid boots. KEEN boots typically run about a half-size large, meaning if you wear a size 10 in running shoes, you'll likely need a 9.5 in KEEN boots. However, this can vary based on your foot shape and sock preference. I recommend trying boots on late in the day when your feet are naturally swollen, simulating the conditions you'll experience during long hikes.

The wide toe box design means these boots accommodate various foot shapes better than traditional hiking boots. However, if you have particularly narrow feet, you might experience heel slippage or general looseness. For narrow-footed hikers, consider adding volume-reducing insoles or exploring the regular width options before moving to wide sizes. The boots should feel snug but not tight, with about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the boot's front.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are KEEN Targhee Mid boots true to size?

KEEN Targhee Mid boots typically run about a half-size large compared to athletic shoes. Most hikers find they need to size down from their sneaker size. The wide toe box design accommodates various foot shapes, but if you have narrow feet, you might need to experiment with sizing or consider volume-reducing insoles. I recommend trying them on late in the day when your feet are naturally swollen, and always test with the hiking socks you plan to wear on the trail.

How long do KEEN Targhee Mid boots last?

The durability of KEEN Targhee Mid boots varies significantly between generations and individual use patterns. Based on my testing and user feedback analysis, expect 300-500 miles of moderate hiking from newer models, with some users reporting up to 1000+ miles from older Targhee II versions. Factors affecting longevity include hiking frequency, terrain type, storage conditions, and individual gait patterns. The waterproof membrane typically maintains effectiveness for 12-18 months of regular use, though the boots remain functional beyond this period with reduced water resistance.

Can you resole KEEN Targhee Mid boots?

Unfortunately, KEEN Targhee Mid boots cannot be resoled due to their direct-attach construction method where the sole is permanently bonded to the upper. This differs from traditional hiking boots that use replaceable Vibram soles attached with screws or stitching. While this construction contributes to the boots' comfort and waterproofing, it means the entire boot must be replaced when the sole wears out. Some specialized cobblers may attempt repairs for minor sole damage, but complete resoling isn't economically feasible or technically reliable for this boot design.

What's the difference between Targhee II, III, and IV?

The main differences lie in construction methods and materials. Targhee II features traditional cement construction with proven durability but heavier weight. Targhee III introduced updated materials and slightly modified fit, though with mixed durability reports. Targhee IV represents the biggest change, using direct injection molding and TPU sole construction for improved flexibility and lighter weight. From my testing, Targhee II offers the most proven track record, while Targhee IV provides the best immediate comfort and ground feel. Choose based on whether you prioritize proven reliability or modern comfort features.

Are KEEN Targhee Mid boots good for backpacking?

KEEN Targhee Mid boots perform well for backpacking with light to moderate pack weights (up to 35-40 pounds). The mid-height design provides adequate ankle support for most backpacking scenarios, while the comfortable fit reduces fatigue during long days. However, for heavy loads or technical terrain, you might want more robust ankle support from a full-height boot. I've successfully used them on 5-day backpacking trips with positive results, though the midsole compression becomes noticeable after 3-4 consecutive days of heavy use. They excel for weekend backpacking and light multi-day trips.

Do KEEN Targhee Mid boots run wide?

Yes, KEEN Targhee Mid boots are designed with a notably wider toe box than most hiking boots, which is one of their signature features. This wide design accommodates natural toe splay and reduces pressure points during long hikes. However, "wide" primarily refers to the toe area rather than the overall foot width. If you have genuinely wide feet throughout, KEEN also offers specific wide-width models. For narrow-footed hikers, the generous toe box might feel oversized, though the heel and midfoot typically provide adequate retention when properly sized.
