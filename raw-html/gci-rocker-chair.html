Do GCI rocker chairs work well on uneven terrain?

Yes, GCI rocker chairs perform remarkably well on uneven surfaces thanks to their flexible rocking mechanism that adapts to ground contours. The spring-loaded system automatically adjusts to minor terrain variations, maintaining stability and comfort where rigid chairs would feel awkward or unstable. I've successfully used them on rocky campgrounds, sandy beaches, and sloped surfaces with excellent results. However, extremely uneven terrain or soft surfaces like deep sand may affect performance. The chairs work best on relatively stable ground where the feet can maintain consistent contact. For very rough terrain, consider clearing a small area or using ground pads under the feet to optimize stability and protect the frame from excessive wear.

How do I maintain my GCI rocker chair for longevity?

Proper maintenance extends GCI rocker chair lifespan significantly and prevents common issues like squeaking mechanisms. After each use, brush off dirt and debris, paying special attention to the rocking mechanism joints where particles can accumulate. For deep cleaning, use mild soap and water, ensuring complete drying before storage to prevent mold or corrosion. Lubricate the shock system periodically with white lithium grease or similar appropriate lubricant to maintain smooth operation and prevent squeaking. Store the chair in a dry location when not in use, as extended outdoor exposure can accelerate fabric and frame degradation. Inspect regularly for loose bolts, torn fabric, or bent components that might compromise safety or performance. Address minor issues promptly to prevent major repairs.
