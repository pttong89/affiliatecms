Sizing & Fit Guide: Getting Your Mammut Pants Right

Proper sizing is crucial for mammut mens ski pants performance and comfort. European sizing conventions differ significantly from American brands, and Mammut's technical fits require careful measurement and consideration of intended use.

Size Selection Strategy

After fitting hundreds of skiers with Mammut gear, I recommend going up one size from your usual pant size for most models. This accounts for the European cut, layer integration, and the fact that ski pants need room for base layers and movement. A size 32 jean wearer typically needs size 34 in mammut ski pants.

The key measurements to focus on are waist and inseam, but also consider thigh room for layering and knee placement for articulated designs. Mammut offers Regular, Long, and Short inseam options in most models, crucial for proper boot interface and safety.

Sizing Tips from the Field:

Care & Maintenance: Extending Your Investment

Proper care dramatically extends the life and performance of mammut mens ski pants. These premium technical garments require specific maintenance practices different from regular clothing. Here's what I've learned from maintaining high-performance ski gear over 15 years.

Washing & Cleaning Protocol

The biggest mistake I see skiers make is overwashing their technical pants. Mammut ski pants should only be washed when genuinely necessary—usually every 10-15 ski days or when visibly soiled. Each wash cycle gradually degrades the DWR coating and can stress seam taping.

When washing is required, use only technical wash products like Nikwax Tech Wash or Grangers Performance Wash. Never use regular detergent, bleach, or fabric softener as these permanently damage waterproof membranes. I prefer hand washing in cool water when possible, but gentle machine cycles work if you close all zippers and velcro tabs first.

DWR Treatment & Restoration

The Durable Water Repellent coating is your ski pants' first line of defense against moisture. When water stops beading on the fabric surface and instead soaks in, it's time for DWR restoration. This typically happens annually with regular use.

I use Nikwax TX.Direct spray-on treatment applied to clean, damp fabric. The key is even application and proper heat activation—either tumble drying on low heat or careful ironing with a pressing cloth. Fresh DWR coating can restore 80-90% of original water repellency performance.

Maintenance Schedule:

  • After each use: Air dry completely, wipe clean exterior
  • Every 5-7 days: Check zippers and vents for debris
  • Monthly: Inspect seam taping and high-wear areas
  • Seasonally: Professional cleaning and DWR treatment
  • Annually: Complete inspection and any needed repairs

Storage & Seasonal Prep

Off-season storage significantly impacts mammut mens ski pants longevity. Store in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Never leave pants compressed in stuff sacks long-term as this can damage waterproof membranes and insulation.

Before each season, inspect all seams, zippers, and reinforcement areas. Small repairs made early prevent major failures later. I keep a repair kit with seam sealer, fabric patches, and zipper lubricant for field maintenance during ski trips.

What's the difference between hardshell and softshell ski pants?

Hardshell ski pants like Mammut's Stoney HS series offer maximum weather protection with waterproof, windproof membranes. They're best for resort skiing, wet conditions, and backcountry touring where complete weather protection is essential. Softshell pants like the Taiss series are more breathable and stretchy, ideal for high-output activities like ski touring uphill, but offer less absolute weather protection. Hardshells work well in all conditions but can feel less mobile, while softshells excel in specific activity-focused scenarios but may not handle sustained wet weather. Choose hardshell for versatility and maximum protection, softshell for specific high-output activities where breathability and mobility matter more than absolute weather sealing.
