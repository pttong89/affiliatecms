Safety Features and Certifications

Safety certification represents the foundation of any quality steel toe footwear, and Skechers steel toe shoes exceed industry standards across multiple protection categories. Understanding these certifications helps workers make informed decisions about their safety footwear investments, particularly when workplace regulations mandate specific protection levels.

ASTM Safety Standards Compliance

All Skechers steel toe models comply with ASTM F2412-18a and F2413-18 standards, which establish minimum performance requirements for protective footwear. These standards mandate that steel toes withstand impact forces up to 75 foot-pounds and compression loads up to 2,500 pounds. In practical terms, this means protection against dropped tools, falling materials, and compression hazards commonly encountered in industrial environments.

The ASTM electrical hazard (EH) rating found in most Skechers steel toe shoes provides secondary protection against electrical shock and electrocution. This certification requires shoes to withstand 18,000 volts at 60 Hz for one minute without current flow exceeding 1.0 milliampere. For workers in electrical trades or around powered equipment, this protection level offers crucial safety margins.
