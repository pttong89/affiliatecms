Buying Guide & Recommendations

Sizing and Fit Guidelines

Proper sizing is crucial for the Oboz Firebrand II Low hiking shoes. Based on my experience and conversations with other hikers, these shoes run true to length but slightly narrow in the forefoot. I recommend trying them on in the afternoon when feet are naturally swollen, wearing the socks you'll use for hiking.

The generous toe box accommodates most foot shapes comfortably, but those with particularly wide feet should consider going up half a size. The nubuck leather does stretch slightly during the break-in period, so avoid sizing up too aggressively. Most hikers find their normal athletic shoe size works perfectly.

Care and Maintenance

Proper care extends the life of your Oboz Firebrand II Low hiking shoes significantly. After each hike, remove excess mud and debris with a soft brush. For deep cleaning, use a damp cloth and mild soap, avoiding harsh chemicals that could damage the waterproof membrane.

The nubuck leather benefits from occasional conditioning with products specifically designed for waterproof footwear. I've had excellent results with Nikwax Nubuck & Suede Proof, which maintains waterproofing while keeping the leather supple. Always air dry your shoes away from direct heat sources, which can crack leather and damage the membrane.

Maintenance Schedule

  • After each hike: Clean debris, air dry thoroughly
  • Monthly: Inspect for wear, clean with damp cloth
  • Every 3-4 months: Apply nubuck conditioner/waterproofing treatment
  • Seasonally: Deep clean, inspect eyelets and stitching
