Understanding CloudTec Technology: What Makes On Cloud Shoes Special

When I first encountered men's on cloud shoes, I was skeptical about the unique appearance of the sole. Those distinctive hollow pods, known as CloudTec technology, seemed more like a gimmick than genuine innovation. However, after extensive testing across different terrains, I've come to appreciate how this Swiss engineering actually works.

The CloudTec system consists of individual hollow pods made from Zero-Gravity foam that compress both vertically and horizontally upon impact. This dual-compression creates what On calls a "soft landing, explosive take-off" sensation. Unlike traditional EVA foam midsoles, these pods adapt to your specific foot strike pattern, providing customized cushioning.

During my mountain trail excursions in Colorado, I noticed how the CloudTec pods responded differently on various surfaces. On rocky terrain, the pods provided excellent ground feedback while maintaining comfort. On softer trails, they offered more cushioning by compressing deeper. This adaptability sets On Cloud shoes apart from conventional hiking shoes.

The technology isn't without drawbacks. I've observed that the pod design can collect debris more easily than traditional soles, and some users report squeaking sounds after extended use. Additionally, the unique construction may not provide the same lateral stability as conventional athletic shoes, making them less suitable for sports requiring quick directional changes.

Pro Tip from the Trail:

The CloudTec technology works best for forward motion activities like walking, light running, and hiking. If you're looking for tennis shoes or court sports footwear, consider other options in the On lineup.
