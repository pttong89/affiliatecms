Sizing and Fit Guide: Getting It Right

Proper sizing makes the difference between hiking bliss and painful misery. After fitting dozens of hikers with Mountain 600s and collecting feedback from fellow outdoor enthusiasts, I've developed a comprehensive sizing strategy that eliminates guesswork.

The Danner Sizing Controversy Resolved

Online reviews present conflicting sizing advice: some claim the Mountain 600 runs large and recommend sizing down, while others insist they run true to size. After extensive testing with multiple foot shapes, here's the definitive guidance:

For narrow to medium feet (B-D width): Order your true athletic shoe size. The boot's medium width provides optimal fit without excess volume. My size 10.5D athletic shoes translated perfectly to 10.5D Mountain 600s.

For wide feet (E+ width): Consider sizing up a half size unless you purchase the wide version. Danner offers 2E and 4E widths that accommodate broader feet without the volume issues that plague oversized regular-width boots.

For high-volume feet: Those with prominent arches or thick ankles benefit from the half-size-up approach. The additional length prevents pressure points during foot swelling on long hikes.

The Professional Fitting Process

I recommend measuring feet later in the day when natural swelling mimics hiking conditions. Stand on both feet during measurement – many people have slight size differences between left and right feet. The Brannock device provides the most accurate measurements, available at quality outdoor retailers and shoe stores.

When trying on Danner men's Mountain 600 boots, wear hiking socks similar to your trail preferences. Merino wool or synthetic blend socks with medium cushioning replicate actual hiking conditions. Avoid cotton socks during fitting – they don't represent the moisture-wicking properties of hiking-specific socks.

Break-In Period and Initial Comfort

Unlike traditional hiking boots requiring extensive break-in periods, the Mountain 600 feels comfortable immediately. The secret lies in the soft leather upper and cushioned ankle collar that eliminates pressure points. However, optimal performance develops over the first 50 miles as the leather molds to your foot shape.

During this initial period, I recommend gradually increasing hike distances. Start with 3-5 mile day hikes, progress to 8-10 mile adventures, then tackle longer distances. This approach allows the leather to conform naturally while identifying any fit issues before committing to major expeditions.

Pro Sizing Tips

  • Measure feet in the afternoon when they're naturally swollen
  • Try on boots with hiking socks, not dress socks
  • Ensure 1/2 inch (thumb width) space between longest toe and boot front
  • Walk up and down inclines to test heel lock and toe room
  • Consider wide versions if you typically wear wide athletic shoes

Frequently Asked Questions

How do Danner Mountain 600s fit compared to athletic shoes?

The Mountain 600 generally fits true to athletic shoe size for medium-width feet. The boot's D-width construction accommodates most foot shapes without the volume issues common in hiking boots. However, those with wide feet should consider the 2E or 4E width options, or size up a half size in regular width. The leather upper provides more structure than athletic shoes but doesn't feel restrictive. I recommend trying them on with hiking socks to replicate actual use conditions. The immediate comfort eliminates traditional break-in periods, making size selection critical for long-term satisfaction.

Are these boots suitable for winter hiking and snowshoeing?

Yes, the Mountain 600 performs excellently in winter conditions with proper sock selection. The waterproof Danner Dry membrane prevents snow infiltration, while the leather upper provides wind resistance. The Vibram outsole maintains traction on packed snow and icy surfaces, though microspikes enhance safety on glare ice. I've used mine for winter hiking in temperatures down to 10°F with merino wool socks and experienced no cold-related discomfort. The boot's thermal properties improve with merino wool or synthetic insulation socks. However, for extreme cold below 0°F or extended snow camping, consider insulated boot alternatives specifically designed for winter mountaineering.

How long do Danner Mountain 600 boots typically last?

With proper care and maintenance, Mountain 600s can last 800-1200 miles of hiking, significantly longer than most competitors. The full-grain leather construction and robust sole attachment contribute to exceptional longevity. My personal pair shows minimal wear after 500+ miles across diverse terrain. The recraftable construction extends lifespan further – Danner's factory service rebuilds worn soles while preserving the broken-in upper, effectively creating a "new" boot at fraction of replacement cost. Regular leather conditioning and proper storage prevent premature degradation. Heavy backpackers carrying 40+ pound loads may experience faster wear, while day hikers can expect the upper range of durability estimates.

Do these boots work well for desert hiking in hot weather?

The Mountain 600 functions adequately in desert conditions but isn't optimized for extreme heat. The waterproof membrane limits breathability compared to non-waterproof alternatives, potentially causing overheating during summer desert hiking. However, the leather upper provides excellent protection against thorns, cacti, and abrasive rock. Early morning and late afternoon hiking maximizes comfort in hot climates. Consider removing the boots during extended breaks to allow cooling. For dedicated hot weather hiking, Danner offers the Desert TFX or Mountain 600 non-waterproof versions that prioritize breathability over waterproofing. The standard Mountain 600 works best in desert shoulder seasons when temperatures remain moderate and occasional water crossings require waterproof protection.

Can I use custom orthotics with Mountain 600 boots?

Absolutely – the Mountain 600 features removable OrthoLite footbeds that accommodate custom orthotics easily. The boot's generous volume prevents cramped conditions when replacing standard insoles with thicker custom devices. I've successfully used custom orthotics for plantar fasciitis management without comfort issues. The boot's structured heel counter and arch support complement orthotic function rather than interfering with corrective properties. When using custom orthotics, ensure proper fit by trying the combination before extended hiking. Some thick orthotics may require sizing up a half size, though most standard orthotic prescriptions fit within normal sizing parameters. The removable footbed design also simplifies cleaning and maintenance.

How do I properly care for and maintain these leather boots?

Proper Mountain 600 maintenance begins with post-hike cleaning using warm water and soft brush to remove debris. Allow complete drying away from direct heat sources, which can crack leather and compromise waterproof membranes. Apply high-quality leather conditioner monthly during active use seasons, focusing on flex points where cracking typically develops. Avoid products containing excessive oils that can clog membrane pores. Store boots in cool, dry locations with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape. The removable footbeds should be air-dried separately to prevent bacterial growth. Regular inspection identifies early wear patterns or membrane damage requiring professional attention. Danner's recrafting service provides professional restoration when outsoles wear down, extending boot lifespan significantly beyond typical replacement timelines.
