What's Covered vs. What's Not Covered

Understanding exactly what the outdoor research infinite guarantee covers—and what it doesn't—is crucial for any outdoor enthusiast investing in quality gear. After processing several warranty claims myself and speaking with dozens of fellow adventurers, I've compiled this definitive breakdown of coverage parameters.

What's Covered

  • Manufacturing defects in materials or workmanship
  • Zipper failures during normal use
  • Seam separation or delamination
  • Premature waterproofing loss due to material failure
  • Hardware malfunction (buckles, snaps, drawcords)
  • Fabric tearing at stress points due to design flaws

What's Not Covered

  • Normal wear and tear from regular use
  • User-created damage (rips, tears, burns, holes)
  • Size and fit issues
  • Damage from improper care or storage
  • Products purchased from non-authorized dealers
  • Items altered with patches or embroidery

The key distinction lies in understanding "manufacturing defect" versus "user damage." When I submitted a claim for my Outdoor Research ski jacket after the membrane began delaminating near the shoulder seams, this clearly fell under manufacturing defect coverage since the failure occurred at stress points designed to handle normal ski motion.

Conversely, when a fellow hiker asked about warranty coverage for a jacket torn by thorns during an off-trail scramble in Olympic National Park, this represented user-created damage that wouldn't qualify for warranty replacement. The distinction isn't always immediately obvious, which is why documenting your gear usage and failure circumstances becomes important.

Pro Tip: Gray Area Situations

Sometimes failures fall into gray areas. If you're unsure whether your issue qualifies, submit the claim anyway with detailed photos and usage description. Outdoor Research customer service representatives are knowledgeable and fair in their assessments, often erring on the side of customer satisfaction when situations are ambiguous.

Step-by-Step Warranty Claim Process

Filing a successful warranty claim under the outdoor research infinite guarantee requires following specific steps and providing proper documentation. Based on my personal experience and consultation with Outdoor Research customer service representatives, here's the most efficient approach to warranty claims.

1

Document the Issue Thoroughly

Before initiating your claim, gather comprehensive documentation of the problem. Take clear, well-lit photos from multiple angles showing the defect or failure. Include close-up shots that clearly demonstrate the issue, and wider shots that show the item's overall condition.

  • Close-up photos of the specific problem area
  • Overall condition shots of the entire item
  • Product tags showing model and size information
  • Receipt or purchase information if available
2

Visit the Official Warranty Portal

Navigate to the official Outdoor Research warranty submission page on their website. The online portal streamlines the process and ensures your claim reaches the right department immediately.

Pro Tip: Bookmark their warranty page for future reference. The online system tracks your claim status and provides estimated processing timelines.

3

Complete the Warranty Form

Provide detailed information about your gear and the specific problem. Be honest and specific about usage patterns, care practices, and failure circumstances. This information helps them determine appropriate solutions.

  • Product model, color, and size details
  • Purchase date and retailer information
  • Detailed description of the problem
  • Usage frequency and typical conditions
4

Submit Photos and Wait for Assessment

Upload your documentation photos and submit the claim. You'll receive an automatic confirmation email with a claim number for tracking purposes. Most assessments are completed within 5-10 business days.

5

Respond to Claim Decision

If approved, you'll receive instructions for shipping your item (if required) or direct replacement shipping. Follow packaging guidelines carefully to prevent damage during transit.

Shipping Tip: For small components like missing straps or buckles, contact customer service first. They often have spare parts available and can ship them directly without requiring you to send in your entire item.

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does the Outdoor Research Infinite Guarantee cover?

The Outdoor Research Infinite Guarantee covers manufacturing defects, material failures, and workmanship issues throughout the lifetime of the product. This includes problems like zipper failures, seam separation, fabric delamination, and other issues that occur during normal use. However, it does not cover normal wear and tear, user-created damage like rips or tears, size/fit issues, or products purchased from non-authorized dealers. The key is distinguishing between manufacturing problems and damage from regular use or accidents. In my experience, Outdoor Research customer service is fair and knowledgeable when evaluating borderline cases, often erring toward customer satisfaction when situations are ambiguous.

How long does it take to process a warranty claim with Outdoor Research?

Current processing times for Outdoor Research warranty claims are typically up to 2 business weeks, though this can vary by season and warranty volume. You'll receive a confirmation email with the most accurate estimate when you submit your claim. During peak seasons like fall and winter, processing times may be longer due to higher demand from ski and mountaineering gear users. From my personal experience, most straightforward claims with clear photos and good documentation are processed within 7-10 days. More complex issues requiring additional evaluation may take the full two weeks or slightly longer, but communication throughout the process is excellent.

Can I claim warranty on Outdoor Research gear I didn't buy directly from them?

Yes, as long as your item is genuine Outdoor Research gear and hasn't been previously warrantied through a retailer, it's covered under the Infinite Guarantee regardless of where you purchased it. This includes items bought from authorized retailers like REI, Backcountry, or other outdoor gear stores. However, products from non-authorized dealers are not covered. This policy makes the warranty particularly valuable since you don't lose coverage by shopping sales or using retailer discounts. I've successfully claimed warranty on gear purchased from multiple different retailers over the years without any issues. Just ensure you're buying authentic products from legitimate outdoor gear retailers.

What should I do if I'm missing a small component like a strap or buckle?

Before sending in your entire item, contact Outdoor Research customer service with photos and a description of what's missing. They often have spare straps, buckles, and snaps available in their office and can send these small components directly to you, saving you the time and cost of shipping your entire item for warranty processing. This approach has worked well for me in the past when I needed replacement drawcords and buckles. Their parts inventory is surprisingly comprehensive, and they're generally happy to send small components at no charge. If they don't have the specific part in stock, they'll guide you through the standard warranty process, but it's always worth checking first for these minor replacements.

Has the Outdoor Research warranty policy changed over the years?

Yes, around 2018, Outdoor Research updated their warranty language to be more specific about coverage limits. The policy now emphasizes coverage 'throughout the lifetime of the product' and is stricter about excluding user-created damage. While they still honor legitimate warranty claims for defects, they're more careful about distinguishing between manufacturing defects and normal wear or user damage. This change was necessary to maintain program sustainability as the company grew and warranty volume increased. Despite these clarifications, my experience with actual claims processing has remained positive, and they continue to provide excellent service for legitimate manufacturing issues. The core commitment to quality and customer satisfaction hasn't changed, just the language around coverage boundaries.
