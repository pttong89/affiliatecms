Sacramento River Fishing Adventures

The Sacramento River fishing program offered through CICC Outdoor Adventures provides access to what many consider California's premier freshwater fishing destination. Their home base along the Sacramento River features pristine stretches lined with ancient cottonwoods and willows, creating the perfect environment for targeting the river's legendary fish populations. These aren't generic fishing charters - they're specialized expeditions led by U.S. Coast Guard licensed captains with decades of experience on these specific waters.

Spring represents the pinnacle of Sacramento River fishing, when massive runs of king salmon begin their upstream migration. These aren't just any salmon - California king salmon are among the largest and most challenging freshwater game fish in North America, with specimens regularly exceeding 20 pounds and trophy fish approaching 40 pounds or more. The guides understand salmon behavior intimately, positioning boats in proven holding areas and employing time-tested techniques including back-trolling with roe, pulling plugs, and drift fishing with fresh baits.

Striped bass fishing represents another highlight of the CICC river program. These powerful anadromous fish migrate from San Francisco Bay into the Sacramento River system, creating spectacular fishing opportunities from late winter through early summer. The river's striped bass population includes both resident fish and ocean-run specimens, with the latter being particularly large and strong. Guide techniques include trolling with diving plugs, drifting live bait, and casting lures to structure and current breaks.

White sturgeon fishing adds an element of big-game angling that few freshwater destinations can match. These prehistoric giants, some exceeding 100 pounds, require specialized equipment and techniques. CICC guides employ heavy tackle, large baits, and strategic positioning in deep holes and current seams where sturgeon congregate. The experience of battling a large sturgeon in river current creates memories that last a lifetime, and the guides' expertise ensures both fish welfare and angler safety during these epic encounters.

Equipment and technique selection varies dramatically based on target species, seasonal conditions, and river levels. Guides provide all necessary tackle, but they also educate clients on proper techniques, equipment selection, and fish handling practices. Their boats are specifically designed for river fishing, featuring shallow drafts, powerful motors, and specialized equipment for anchoring in current and fighting large fish safely.

Big Game Hunting Opportunities

The big game hunting program at CICC Outdoor Adventures encompasses over 7,000 acres of pristine coastal mountain range property in the Colusa County foothills, land that's managed exclusively for wildlife habitat and hunting purposes. This vast expanse of diverse terrain includes oak woodlands, chaparral-covered hillsides, seasonal grasslands, and riparian corridors that provide ideal habitat for wild hogs, predators, and seasonal turkey populations. The property's isolation and minimal human disturbance have created conditions where wildlife thrives in truly wild settings.

Wild hog hunting represents the cornerstone of CICC's big game offerings, with populations of Russian-influenced wild boars that range from table-sized meat pigs to massive trophy boars exceeding 300 pounds. These aren't pen-raised animals but truly wild hogs that have established sustainable populations throughout the property's varied terrain. The hunting is conducted entirely as fair-chase, spot-and-stalk adventures that test hunters' skills in tracking, stalking, and marksmanship while providing authentic wild game experiences.

The terrain's diversity creates multiple hunting scenarios and challenges. Dense oak thickets provide excellent cover for stalking, while open grassland areas require long-range shooting skills. Rocky outcroppings and steep hillsides add physical challenges that make successful hunts even more rewarding. Guides possess intimate knowledge of hog behavior patterns, feeding areas, and travel corridors developed through years of observation and experience on these specific properties.

Predator hunting opportunities include bobcats and coyotes, species that thrive in the property's diverse ecosystems. These hunts require different skills and techniques than hog hunting, emphasizing calling, camouflage, and patience. Coyote hunting often employs electronic calls, decoys, and long-range shooting, while bobcat hunting requires intimate knowledge of their preferred habitat and travel patterns. Both species provide challenging hunting opportunities for experienced predator hunters.

Spring and fall turkey hunting adds another dimension to CICC's big game program. The property supports healthy populations of wild turkeys that utilize the diverse habitats for feeding, roosting, and nesting. Spring gobbler hunting during the breeding season provides classic turkey hunting experiences with gobbling, strutting, and the chess match between hunter and bird. Fall hunting focuses on breaking up flocks and calling birds back, requiring different tactics and skills.

Spring (March-May)

Spring represents prime time for turkey hunting and Sacramento River fishing. Tom turkeys are actively gobbling and responding to calls during breeding season. King salmon begin their upstream runs, providing exceptional fishing opportunities.

  • Peak turkey hunting season
  • Excellent salmon fishing
  • Mild weather conditions
  • Longer daylight hours

Summer (June-August)

Summer focuses primarily on fishing activities as hunting seasons are closed. Striped bass fishing peaks during warm weather months, with excellent opportunities for both river and delta fishing.

  • Prime striped bass season
  • Warm weather fishing
  • Long fishing days
  • Comfortable conditions

Fall (September-November)

Fall represents the most diverse season with waterfowl hunting opening, continued fishing opportunities, and big game hunting. This is peak season for most outdoor activities at CICC.

  • Excellent weather conditions
  • Multiple hunting opportunities
  • Prime booking period

Winter (December-February)

Winter provides peak waterfowl hunting with massive migrations of ducks and geese. Cold weather concentrates birds in optimal feeding areas, creating spectacular hunting opportunities.
