Frequently Asked Questions

How long do On CloudRock waterproof boots typically last with regular hiking use?

Based on my extensive testing and community feedback, On CloudRock waterproof boots typically provide 400-600 miles of reliable performance, depending on terrain type and hiking intensity. The CloudTec sole system shows initial wear around 200 miles but remains functional well beyond this point. For hikers who primarily stick to maintained trails, you can expect the higher end of this range. More aggressive terrain with sharp rocks may reduce longevity to the lower range. The waterproof membrane generally maintains effectiveness throughout the boot's lifespan, with proper care and maintenance extending overall durability.

Are On CloudRock waterproof boots suitable for winter hiking and snowshoeing?

The On CloudRock waterproof boots perform well in moderate winter conditions, providing excellent waterproof protection and adequate insulation for temperatures down to approximately 20°F (-7°C). However, they're not specifically designed for extreme cold weather hiking or mountaineering. For winter activities, I recommend pairing them with quality wool socks and consider adding microspikes for traction on icy surfaces. The boots work exceptionally well for snowshoeing on packed trails. For serious winter mountaineering or extended cold weather exposure, dedicated winter boots with enhanced insulation would be more appropriate. The women's CloudRock waterproof models offer similar winter performance characteristics.

How does the CloudTec sole system perform on wet rocks and slippery surfaces?

The CloudTec sole system combined with the Missiongrip rubber compound provides above-average traction on wet surfaces, though it's not the absolute best in extremely slippery conditions. During my testing in wet environments, including stream crossings and rain-soaked granite, the boots performed admirably with careful foot placement. The individual cloud pods help maintain ground contact and provide multiple pressure points for improved grip. However, on polished wet rock or extremely muddy conditions, additional caution is necessary. The boots excel on mixed terrain where the CloudTec system can adapt to surface irregularities. For technical rock climbing or extremely slippery conditions, specialized approach shoes might be preferable, but for general hiking including moderate wet conditions, the CloudRock waterproof provides reliable performance.

What's the difference between CloudRock and CloudRock 2, and is the upgrade worth it?

The CloudRock 2 represents a significant improvement over the original design, addressing many durability concerns while maintaining the innovative comfort characteristics. Key improvements include enhanced materials construction, upgraded Missiongrip outsole compound, improved lacing system, and better overall build quality. The CloudRock 2 also features refined CloudTec pod design that provides better longevity without sacrificing comfort. From my testing experience, the upgrade is worthwhile for serious hikers who plan extensive use. The improved durability justifies the additional cost, especially for those hiking on abrasive terrain. The CloudRock 2 waterproof models also offer better breathability and refined fit. If budget allows, the CloudRock 2 provides better long-term value despite the higher initial investment.

Can I use custom orthotics with On CloudRock waterproof boots?

Yes, the On CloudRock waterproof boots accommodate custom orthotics quite well, though some considerations apply. The removable insole provides adequate space for most orthotic designs, and the boot's volume is generally sufficient for this modification. However, you may need to size up an additional half size to accommodate thicker orthotics comfortably. The CloudTec sole system works harmoniously with orthotics, as the external cushioning complements internal support rather than interfering with it. During my testing with various orthotic users, most reported excellent compatibility and improved comfort. The boot's secure lacing system helps maintain proper foot positioning with orthotics. For high-volume orthotics, consider the CloudRock Mid models which offer slightly more internal volume. I recommend trying the combination before extended hikes to ensure optimal fit and comfort.
