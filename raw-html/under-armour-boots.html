Frequently Asked Questions

Based on my extensive research and testing, here are the most common questions about under armour boots with detailed answers based on real-world experience.

Are Under Armour boots good for hiking?

Under Armour boots excel in day hiking and moderate trail conditions. Models like the Valsetz and HOVR Dawn offer excellent comfort and lightweight construction perfect for day hikes. However, for serious backpacking or technical terrain, traditional hiking boots from specialized brands may provide better durability and support. I've personally used UA boots on trails in Yellowstone and Great Smoky Mountains with great success for distances up to 10 miles. The key is matching expectations ‚Äì these boots prioritize comfort and weight reduction over maximum ruggedness. For casual hikers and those transitioning from heavy traditional boots, they represent an excellent introduction to lightweight hiking footwear.

How long do Under Armour boots last?

Under Armour boot durability varies significantly by model and usage. Based on user reviews and my testing, expect 6-18 months with regular use. The Charged series tends to last longer than Micro G models. Tactical users report 8-12 months, while casual wearers may get 18+ months. The main failure points are sole separation and upper material wear. Proper care including regular cleaning and conditioning can extend lifespan considerably. While this may seem short compared to traditional leather boots, many users find the superior comfort justifies more frequent replacement. Factor replacement costs into your decision-making process.

What's the difference between Valsetz and Charged Valsetz boots?

The main difference lies in cushioning technology and price point. Standard Valsetz boots use Micro G foam for lightweight comfort, while Charged Valsetz feature UA's Charged Cushioning¬Æ that provides more energy return and impact absorption. Charged models typically cost $20-40 more but offer superior all-day comfort for extended wear. Do Under Armour boots run true to size?

Under Armour boots typically run large, especially in tactical models. Most users recommend ordering 0.5 to 1 full size down from your regular shoe size. This varies by foot width and model - the Charged series tends to run more true to size than Micro G models. I personally wear size 10 in most brands but use 9.5 in UA tactical boots. Always check the specific model reviews as sizing can vary between different boot lines within the Under Armour lineup. The generous toe box accommodates wider feet well, while narrow-footed users often need to size down further. When in doubt, order from retailers with flexible return policies to ensure proper fit.

Are Under Armour boots waterproof?

Not all Under Armour boots are waterproof - it depends on the specific model. Waterproof versions are clearly labeled and typically cost $20-30 more than standard versions. Models like the Charged Valsetz Zip Waterproof and Valsetz Trek Waterproof feature membrane construction for weather protection. Non-waterproof models use Storm technology for water resistance and better breathability. For serious wet conditions, choose specifically labeled waterproof models rather than relying on water-resistant treatments. During my testing in various weather conditions, the waterproof models performed well in light to moderate rain but showed some breathability limitations during warm weather activities. Consider your primary use environment when choosing between waterproof and breathable options.
