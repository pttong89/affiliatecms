Complete Trail Overview and What to Expect

The trail of ten falls forms a spectacular 7.2-mile loop through Silver Falls State Park, Oregon's largest state park at 9,000 acres. This nationally recognized hiking trail combines the Canyon Trail and Rim Trail, creating what many consider the Pacific Northwest's premier waterfall experience. During my multiple visits, I've discovered that understanding the trail's layout and logistics makes the difference between a good hike and an extraordinary adventure.

The trail features approximately 800 feet of elevation change, which sounds modest but translates to rolling terrain with several steep sections, particularly the stairs leading down to and up from waterfalls. I found the difficulty level genuinely moderate â€“ challenging enough to feel accomplished but accessible to hikers with basic fitness levels. The AllTrails reviews consistently rate it 4.5+ stars, with over 21,000 reviews praising its well-maintained paths and spectacular scenery.

Navigation follows a straightforward clockwise or counter-clockwise route, though I recommend clockwise starting from South Falls Lodge. This direction saves the most impressive waterfalls (North Falls and Winter Falls) for the second half when you've built momentum and confidence. The trail connects seamlessly with shorter options if your group includes varying fitness levels â€“ cutoff trails allow for 4.8-mile and 5.5-mile alternatives without missing the major waterfalls.

Lower South Falls (93 feet) provides a gentler waterfall experience with wide, sheet-like flow over a broad basalt ledge. Summer (June-August) offers the most comfortable hiking conditions with warm, dry weather and maximum daylight hours. Some smaller waterfalls may be mere trickles by August. If you prioritize ease of hiking over dramatic waterfalls, summer is your season. Fall (September-November) provides a compelling middle ground with moderate crowds, comfortable temperatures, and the added bonus of spectacular autumn foliage. Waterfall flow varies significantly depending on early fall rains, but the forest beauty compensates for any reduced water volume. This season works well for photographers seeking diverse compositions combining waterfalls with autumn colors.

Winter (December-February) appeals to serious hikers seeking solitude and dramatic conditions. Waterfalls reach impressive volumes again, and ice formations create unique photographic opportunities. However, winter hiking on the trail of ten falls requires advanced preparation, appropriate gear, and experience with hazardous conditions. Trails become extremely slippery, daylight hours are limited, and weather can change rapidly. Winter visits reward experienced hikers with incredible solitude and some of the most dramatic waterfall conditions of the year.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to hike the Trail of Ten Falls?

The complete trail of ten falls loop typically takes 3-5 hours to complete, depending on your pace, photography stops, and time spent at each waterfall. The 7.2-mile loop includes moderate terrain with about 800 feet of elevation change. I personally completed it in 4 hours during my spring visit, taking time to walk behind each accessible waterfall and enjoy lunch at North Falls. Faster hikers might finish in 3 hours, while those who prefer a leisurely pace with extensive photography could take up to 5-6 hours.

Are dogs allowed on the Trail of Ten Falls?

No, dogs are not allowed on the Canyon Trail portion of the trail of ten falls where the waterfalls are located. However, pets on leash are permitted on other trails in Silver Falls State Park, including the Rim Trail and areas around the visitor facilities. This restriction exists for safety reasons and to protect the delicate ecosystem around the waterfalls. If you're traveling with pets, consider leaving them at your accommodation or having someone stay with them while others hike the main trail.

What is the parking fee at Silver Falls State Park?

Silver Falls State Park charges a $10 day-use parking fee per vehicle for Oregon residents and $12 for non-residents. You can pay at automated stations near park entries and most trailheads using cash or card. Annual Oregon State Parks passes are available for $50 (residents) or $60 (non-residents) and provide excellent value if you plan multiple visits. During my visit, I found the South Falls parking area well-organized with clear signage for payment options and multiple payment stations to avoid long lines.

Which waterfalls can you walk behind on the Trail of Ten Falls?

You can walk behind four waterfalls on the trail of ten falls: South Falls, Lower South Falls, North Falls, and Middle North Falls. These waterfalls feature natural grottos carved by centuries of erosion, creating cathedral-like spaces behind the falling water. South Falls (177 feet) and North Falls (136 feet) offer the most dramatic behind-the-falls experiences. During my visit, walking behind North Falls was absolutely magical â€“ the sound of the water and the cool mist created an otherworldly atmosphere that's unlike anything else in Oregon.

What is the best time of year to visit the Trail of Ten Falls?

The best time to visit the trail of ten falls is during late winter through early summer (February to June) when waterfalls flow at their strongest due to snowmelt and spring rains. My March visit revealed waterfalls in full force with stunning flow rates. Fall offers beautiful foliage, while summer provides the most comfortable hiking conditions despite reduced water flow. Winter hiking is possible but requires extra caution due to wet, slippery conditions. I'd avoid July-September if dramatic waterfalls are your priority, as some may reduce to trickles during Oregon's dry season.
