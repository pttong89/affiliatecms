How do LOWA walking shoes fit compared to other brands?

LOWA walking shoes typically run true to size but have a generous width, particularly in the toe box area. I recommend trying them on in-store if possible, as the European sizing and wider last can feel different from American brands like Merrell or Salomon. The Renegade series fits most comfortably for medium to wide feet, while the Innovo offers a slightly more streamlined fit. LOWA also offers wide versions of popular models. Many users find they need to size up half a size for thick hiking socks, and the break-in period is minimal compared to traditional leather hiking boots. The accommodating fit makes them particularly suitable for hikers with foot issues or those who experience significant foot swelling during long hikes.

What's the difference between LOWA hiking boots and walking shoes?

The main differences lie in ankle support, weight, and intended use. LOWA walking shoes (low-cut models) offer greater freedom of movement and are lighter, making them ideal for day hikes, trail walking, and fast-paced activities. Hiking boots (mid-cut models) provide ankle support and protection for uneven terrain, heavy pack loads, and multi-day adventures. Walking shoes typically feature more flexible soles for natural foot movement, while hiking boots have stiffer construction for stability. In my experience, walking shoes excel for maintained trails and lighter loads, while boots are essential for rocky terrain and backpacking trips. The choice depends on your specific hiking style, terrain preferences, and pack weight considerations. Both offer LOWA's signature comfort and quality construction.

How do you maintain and care for LOWA walking shoes?

Proper maintenance extends the life of LOWA walking shoes significantly. After each use, remove dirt and debris with a soft brush, allowing them to air dry naturally away from direct heat. For leather models, apply a leather conditioner every 3-4 months or after exposure to wet conditions. The Gore-Tex membrane requires periodic reproof treatment using products like Nikwax to maintain waterproofing. Store shoes with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture. Replace insoles annually for optimal comfort and hygiene. I've found that consistent care can extend the life of LOWA shoes by 1-2 years beyond normal wear expectations. Avoid machine washing, as this can damage waterproof membranes and adhesive bonds. Regular maintenance also helps maintain the resale value if you decide to upgrade to newer models.

Can LOWA walking shoes be resoled?

Some LOWA walking shoes can be resoled, but it depends on the construction method. Models with stitched construction like the Tibet and some Camino variants are resoleable through LOWA's official repair service in Germany. However, most walking shoes including the popular Renegade series use cemented construction and cannot be traditionally resoled. LOWA offers a comprehensive repair service for eligible models, though shipping costs to Germany make it economical only for premium boots. The typical cost ranges from $100-150 for resoling, making it worthwhile for high-end models but often impractical for walking shoes that typically cost $150-250 new. This limitation is worth considering when making your initial purchase decision, especially if long-term repairability is important to your buying criteria. The trade-off is that cemented construction provides better waterproofing and initial durability for walking shoes.
