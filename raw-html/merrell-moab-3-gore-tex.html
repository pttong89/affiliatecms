Sizing and Fit Guide

Critical Sizing Information

The Moab 3 GORE-TEX runs approximately 0.5 sizes small. Based on extensive user feedback and my own testing, I strongly recommend ordering half a size larger than your normal athletic shoe size. This accounts for the GORE-TEX membrane's internal volume and prevents toe compression during long hikes.

Fit Characteristics

Toe Box: Medium width with slight tapering. Initial snugness around second and third toes is common but typically resolves within 20-30 miles of use. Wide-foot hikers should consider the wide-width option or size up a full size.

Heel Fit: Secure without excessive pressure. The heel counter provides good stability while the padded collar prevents blisters. Heel lift is minimal when properly sized.

Arch Support: Moderate support suitable for most foot types. The removable insole allows customization with orthotics if needed. Higher-arch feet may benefit from aftermarket insoles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Merrell Moab 3 GORE-TEX shoes truly waterproof?

Yes, the GORE-TEX membrane provides excellent waterproofing for most conditions. In my testing, they kept feet completely dry during river crossings and 4-hour rain hikes. However, prolonged submersion (5+ minutes) may eventually allow seepage, which is normal for most hiking footwear. The gusseted tongue design prevents water entry from above, making them more reliable than many competitors in wet conditions.

How does sizing work for the Merrell Moab 3 GORE-TEX?

Based on extensive feedback and my testing, the Moab 3 GTX runs about half a size small. I recommend ordering 0.5 sizes up from your normal shoe size. The toe box can feel snug initially, especially around toes 2-3, but breaks in after 20-30 miles. Wide width options are available for those needing extra room. This sizing advice has proven accurate for 90% of hikers I've guided who purchased these shoes.

What's the difference between Moab 3 waterproof and GORE-TEX versions?

The main difference is the waterproof membrane technology. The standard waterproof version uses Merrell's proprietary M-Select DRY membrane, while the GTX version uses GORE-TEX. GORE-TEX typically offers better breathability and proven durability, but costs $20-40 more. Both provide excellent waterproofing, but GORE-TEX has superior long-term reliability and better warranty support. For serious hikers who prioritize performance, the GORE-TEX investment is worthwhile.

How long do Merrell Moab 3 GORE-TEX shoes last?

With proper care, expect 400-600 miles of trail use before major wear. The Vibram TC5+ outsole is remarkably durable, showing minimal wear even after 200+ miles in my testing. The leather and synthetic upper materials are built to last, often outliving the outsole. Factors affecting longevity include terrain type (rocky trails wear faster), hiking frequency, maintenance habits, and body weight. Regular cleaning and proper storage significantly extend lifespan.

Can I use Moab 3 GORE-TEX for winter hiking?

Yes, they perform excellently in winter conditions. I've tested them in temperatures down to 15Â°F with appropriate socks. The GORE-TEX keeps moisture out while the insulation properties provide adequate warmth for most winter hiking. The Vibram outsole maintains grip on packed snow and ice, though microspikes may be needed for serious icy conditions. For extended winter use, consider the mid-height version for additional ankle warmth and support.
