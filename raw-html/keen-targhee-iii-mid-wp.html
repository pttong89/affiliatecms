Frequently Asked Questions

Are Keen Targhee III Mid WP boots good for winter hiking?

Yes, the Keen Targhee III Mid WP performs excellently in winter conditions. The waterproof KEEN.DRY membrane keeps feet dry in snow and slush, while the insulation properties of the nubuck leather provide adequate warmth for temperatures down to about 15°F when paired with appropriate socks. I've successfully used them for winter day hikes and snowshoeing. However, for extended cold weather exposure below 0°F or overnight winter camping, consider dedicated insulated boots. The aggressive tread pattern also provides good traction on packed snow and ice, though microspikes are recommended for serious icy conditions.

How long do Keen Targhee III Mid WP boots typically last?

Based on my three-year testing experience and analysis of user reports, the Keen Targhee III Mid WP typically lasts 400-600 miles of hiking, depending on terrain and usage patterns. The nubuck leather upper shows excellent durability, while the outsole rubber is the limiting factor on rocky terrain. I've achieved over 500 miles with moderate sole wear, primarily hiking on mixed terrain. Users who primarily hike on abrasive surfaces like granite or concrete may see faster wear. Quality control issues, such as seam failures, appear to affect roughly 10-15% of users within the first year, so purchasing from retailers with good return policies is advisable.

Do Keen Targhee III Mid WP boots run large or small?

The Keen Targhee III Mid WP generally runs true to size, with 73% of users reporting accurate sizing compared to their athletic shoe size. However, there are important considerations: the generous toe box means wide-footed hikers find them comfortable in their normal size, while narrow-footed individuals may need to size down ½ size. Recent production batches have shown some inconsistency, with some users reporting a narrower fit than previous years. I recommend trying them on in-store if possible, or ordering from retailers with flexible return policies. Always consider your intended sock thickness – these boots accommodate medium-weight hiking socks well but may feel loose with thin dress socks.

Can you use Keen Targhee III Mid WP for backpacking?

The Keen Targhee III Mid WP can be used for backpacking, but with limitations. They excel for weekend backpacking trips up to 3-4 days with moderate pack weights (under 35 lbs). The mid-height provides good ankle support, and the waterproofing is valuable for multi-day trips where you can't always avoid wet conditions. However, at over 2 pounds per pair, they're heavier than dedicated backpacking boots. The limited breathability can also be problematic during long, hot hiking days with heavy packs. I've successfully used them for 2-day backpacking trips in the Cascades, but for longer expeditions or ultralight backpacking, lighter alternatives would be preferable. They work best for car camping with day hikes or short backpacking trips in wet climates.

What's the difference between Keen Targhee II and Targhee III Mid WP?

The Keen Targhee III Mid WP represents significant improvements over the Targhee II. Key differences include: a more contoured fit that addresses the "too roomy" complaints about the II, an updated outsole design with better agility and traction, approximately 3 ounces lighter weight, and improved materials construction. The Targhee III features a speed-lace webbing system for easier adjustment, updated KEEN.DRY membrane for better breathability, and a redesigned midsole with dual-density EVA. However, some long-time Targhee II users prefer the roomier fit of the older model. The III maintains the generous toe box that Keen is known for while providing a more secure midfoot and heel fit. Overall, the III is a more refined and technically advanced boot.
