Is the GCI Freestyle Rocker easy to set up and fold down?

Yes, the GCI Freestyle Rocker is incredibly easy to set up and fold down, taking literally seconds in both directions. To set up, you simply pull the chair open and push down on both sides of the seat until it clicks into place. What makes the rocking motion smooth on different surfaces?

The smooth rocking motion comes from GCI's patented Spring-Action Rocking Technology, which uses two shock absorbers positioned as the rear legs. These shocks provide consistent, controlled movement regardless of whether you're on hard pavement, soft grass, or uneven terrain. The technology automatically adjusts to surface conditions, maintaining the same gentle rocking experience. I've used this chair on everything from concrete patios to sandy beaches, and the motion remains consistently smooth. The spring system is sealed and maintenance-free, though I do recommend occasional cleaning to prevent debris buildup around the shock mechanisms.

How does the Freestyle Rocker compare to other GCI models?

The Freestyle Rocker sits in the middle of GCI's rocker lineup in terms of features and price. Can you leave the GCI Freestyle Rocker outside in weather?

While the GCI Freestyle Rocker features weather-resistant materials including a powder-coated steel frame and UV-resistant polyester fabric, I don't recommend leaving it outside permanently. The chair can handle occasional rain and sun exposure, but prolonged exposure to harsh weather will accelerate wear and potentially cause rust or fabric degradation. I always bring mine inside or store it in a covered area when not in use. The mesh backrest dries quickly after getting wet, usually within an hour or two. For best longevity, treat it like you would any quality outdoor furniture - enjoy it outside but provide proper storage when possible.
