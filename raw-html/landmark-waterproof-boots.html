Unlike leather boots that stretch and conform over time, the rubber construction maintains its original shape. Maintenance and Care Tips

Extending the life of landmark waterproof boots requires consistent maintenance practices. After each use, remove debris from treads using a stiff brush, paying particular attention to stones lodged in lug patterns. Rinse exterior surfaces with clean water to remove salt, mud, and organic matter that can degrade rubber compounds over time.

The removable liners should be extracted and air-dried separately from the boot shell. This practice prevents moisture buildup that can lead to odor and material degradation. For optimal results, rotate between two sets of liners, allowing 24-48 hours drying time between uses. Store boots in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight, which can cause rubber deterioration.
