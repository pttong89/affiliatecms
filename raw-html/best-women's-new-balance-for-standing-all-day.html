How do I choose the right width in New Balance shoes for extended standing?

Proper width selection is crucial for all-day comfort and foot health. New Balance offers widths from 2A (narrow) to 4E (extra wide). For extended standing, measure your feet at the end of a typical work day when they're naturally swollen. The shoe should provide 1/2 inch of space beyond your longest toe and allow your foot to spread naturally without pinching. If you're between sizes, opt for the wider option, especially if you'll be wearing thicker work socks or orthotic insoles. Healthcare workers often find that going up one width from their regular shoe size prevents pressure points that develop during long standing periods. The investment in proper width fitting pays dividends in comfort and foot health over time.

What's the difference between neutral and stability New Balance shoes for standing?

The primary difference lies in the midsole construction and support features. Neutral shoes like the 1080 v14 and More v5 provide cushioning without corrective elements, making them ideal for those with normal arch height and foot mechanics. Stability shoes like the 860 v14 and Vongo v6 incorporate dual-density midsoles, medial wedges, or motion control features to prevent overpronation (inward foot rolling). For extended standing, stability becomes more critical as muscle fatigue can lead to foot collapse. If you experience arch pain, inside heel discomfort, or have flat feet, stability models provide better long-term support. However, if you have high arches or supinate (roll outward), neutral models typically offer better comfort. The choice often depends on your foot mechanics and any existing pain patterns you experience during long standing periods.

What's the best way to break in new New Balance shoes for work?

Proper break-in is essential for workplace comfort and injury prevention. Start with 2-3 hour periods at home, gradually increasing to 4-6 hours before wearing for full shifts. New Balance Fresh Foam models typically require minimal break-in due to their adaptive foam construction, but the upper materials may need time to conform to your foot shape. During break-in, monitor pressure points, especially around the heel counter and toe box areas. If you experience hot spots or discomfort, address these immediately with padding or lacing adjustments rather than pushing through. For healthcare workers, plan the break-in period during lighter schedule days or when you have time for foot rest between shifts. The 1080 v14 usually feels comfortable within 2-3 days, while stability models like the 860 v14 may require up to a week due to their firmer support structures. Never wear brand new shoes for extended work shifts without proper break-in period.
