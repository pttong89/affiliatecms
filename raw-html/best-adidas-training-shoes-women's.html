What's the difference between running shoes and training shoes?

Training shoes are designed for multi-directional movement, lateral stability, and versatility across different exercises. They typically have flatter soles, more durable uppers, and firmer midsoles for stability during weightlifting. Running shoes prioritize forward motion, heel-to-toe drop, and maximum cushioning for impact absorption during repetitive heel strikes. In my experience, using running shoes for strength training leads to instability and potential injury risk, while training shoes work adequately for short runs but lack the specialized cushioning for longer distances. For those needing both capabilities, I recommend having dedicated pairs or looking into best running training shoes hybrid options.

Can I use Adidas training shoes for running?

While Adidas training shoes can handle short runs and treadmill work, they're not optimal for longer distances. Models like the RapidMove ADV 2 work well for runs under 3 miles, but for serious running, dedicated running shoes like the Ultraboost series provide better cushioning, energy return, and biomechanical support for extended distances. During my testing, I found training shoes adequate for warm-up runs and HIIT intervals but noticed increased fatigue and discomfort during longer runs. The firmer midsoles that provide stability for lifting become a disadvantage for repetitive impact absorption.
