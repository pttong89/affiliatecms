Introduction to On Running Shoes

When I first discovered on running shoes during a trail run in the Swiss Alps, I was skeptical about their unique cloud-pod design. However, after testing multiple models over the past three years, I can confidently say that On has revolutionized the running shoe industry with their innovative approach to cushioning and performance.

Founded in 2010 by Swiss triathlete Oliver Bernhard alongside David Allemann and Caspar Coppetti, On running shoes emerged from a simple yet ambitious goal: create footwear that provides soft landings and explosive takeoffs. This Swiss engineering marvel has grown from a startup to one of the fastest-growing athletic footwear brands globally, with endorsements from professional athletes and everyday runners alike.

What sets on running shoes apart from traditional running footwear is their patented CloudTec technology, which features distinctive hollow pods on the outsole. These "clouds" compress on impact to absorb landing forces, then spring back to provide energy return during toe-off. It's a system that challenges conventional midsole design while delivering a unique running experience that many athletes swear by.

CloudTec Technology Explained

The Science Behind the Clouds

After extensively testing various on running shoes models, I've come to appreciate the sophisticated engineering behind CloudTec technology. The system consists of individual hollow pods made from premium EVA foam, strategically positioned across the outsole to create distinct pressure zones that adapt to different phases of your gait cycle.

Impact Phase

Clouds compress vertically and horizontally, absorbing impact forces and reducing stress on joints.

Stance Phase

Pods lock in position, creating a stable platform for efficient weight transfer.

Push-off Phase

Springs back to original shape, providing energy return and forward propulsion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are On running shoes worth the premium price?

Yes, if you value innovation and unique technology. The CloudTec system provides a genuinely different running experience that you can't find elsewhere. However, traditional runners seeking maximum cushioning value might find better options with Brooks or Hoka at lower price points. The Swiss engineering, sustainability initiatives, and versatile styling justify the premium for many runners, but consider your budget and running priorities. I recommend trying them on first, as the firm ride isn't for everyone.

How long do On running shoes typically last?

Most On models provide 300-500 miles of running, comparable to other premium brands. The CloudTec pods maintain their structure well, but upper materials can show wear earlier than expected, especially on lifestyle models like the Cloud 6. In my experience, performance models like Cloudmonster and Cloudflow offer better durability for serious runners. Proper maintenance, including regular cleaning of the cloud pods and rotation between multiple pairs, significantly extends lifespan. Trail models show excellent outsole durability but require more cleaning attention.

Do On running shoes run true to size?

Generally yes, but with model-specific variations. Most runners find their normal size works perfectly, particularly for Cloud 6 and Cloudswift models. However, if you have wide feet, consider going up half a size, especially in performance models like Cloudflow. The Cloudmonster runs slightly large, so some runners prefer their true size rather than sizing up. Always measure your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally more swollen, and ensure thumb-width space at the toe. The speed lacing systems provide good adjustability for fine-tuning fit.

What's the difference between CloudTec and CloudTec Phase?

CloudTec features individual hollow pods that compress independently, while CloudTec Phase connects the pods to collapse in sequence like dominoes. Phase technology offers a smoother transition and softer feel, found in models like Cloudsurfer 2 and Cloudeclipse. Traditional CloudTec provides more responsive, firm cushioning with the distinctive "cloud" sensation. Phase removes the need for a Speedboard, creating a more natural rolling motion. Both systems offer excellent impact absorption, but Phase feels more forgiving during daily training while traditional CloudTec excels in responsive performance applications.

Which On model is best for beginners?

The Cloud 6 is ideal for beginners due to its versatility, moderate cushioning, and forgiving speed lacing system. It works well for both running and walking, allowing new runners to ease into the sport without committing to a pure performance shoe. The Cloudrunner 2 offers additional stability features for beginners who need mild support. Avoid starting with highly cushioned models like Cloudmonster or firm performance shoes like Cloudflow until you've developed your natural gait pattern. The key is finding a shoe that feels comfortable immediately rather than requiring extended break-in periods.

Can you use On running shoes for other activities besides running?

Absolutely! On shoes excel at multi-sport activities. Models like Cloud X are specifically designed for cross-training, gym workouts, and fitness classes. The Cloud 6 works exceptionally well for casual wear, travel, and all-day walking. However, avoid using highly cushioned models like Cloudmonster for lateral movement sports, as the soft pods can compromise stability. For hiking, trail-specific models like Cloudvista perform well on moderate terrain. The breathable design and lightweight construction make them excellent for warm-weather activities, while the unique aesthetic ensures you'll look stylish in any setting.
