Sizing and Fit Guidelines

Important Sizing Note

ASICS shoes typically run true to size, but some runners find they need a half-size up for longer distances. Always try shoes on in the afternoon when feet are naturally swollen.

  • Width Options: Most models available in regular (D) and wide (2E) options
  • Toe Room: Should have thumb-width space between longest toe and shoe end
  • Heel Lock: Heel should feel secure without slipping during movement
  • Break-in Period: Most ASICS need 25-50 miles to fully break in

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best ASICS trainers for running in 2025?

The best ASICS trainers for running in 2025 include the Novablast 5 for daily training, Gel-Nimbus 27 for maximum comfort, Superblast 2 for versatility, Gel-Kayano 32 for stability, and Metaspeed Sky Paris for racing. Each serves different running needs and preferences. After testing all these models extensively, I'd recommend starting with the Novablast 5 for most runners due to its exceptional balance of performance, comfort, and value. For beginners or those prioritizing maximum cushioning, the Gel-Nimbus 27 provides unmatched comfort and protection.

Which ASICS shoe is best for beginners?

The ASICS Novablast 5 is excellent for beginners due to its versatile design, comfortable cushioning, and ability to handle various paces. The Gel-Nimbus 27 is also great for new runners who prioritize maximum comfort and cushioning during their early training phases. From my experience coaching new runners, the Novablast 5's $140 price point and adaptability make it ideal for someone unsure about their running style. The shoe works equally well for 10-minute miles and faster paces as fitness improves. The wide platform also provides stability that builds confidence for nervous beginners.

How do I choose the right ASICS running shoe?

Consider your running style, weekly mileage, preferred terrain, and any stability needs. Daily trainers like the Novablast 5 work for most runners, while the Gel-Kayano 32 suits those needing stability support. For racing, consider the Metaspeed series. Always try shoes on and test them if possible. I recommend visiting a specialty running store for gait analysis if you're unsure about your needs. Additionally, consider your local climate and terrain - hot weather runners should prioritize breathability, while those in wet conditions need models that handle moisture well. Your weekly mileage and running goals are crucial factors in making the right choice.

Are ASICS trainers good for long-distance running?

Yes, ASICS trainers excel for long-distance running. The Gel-Nimbus 27 and Superblast 2 are particularly excellent for marathon training and long runs, offering superior cushioning and durability. Many marathon runners successfully use ASICS for training and racing. In my own marathon training, I've used the Superblast 2 for runs up to 22 miles with excellent results. The FF Turbo+ foam provides sustained energy return even during the final miles when fatigue sets in. Professional and amateur marathoners consistently rank ASICS among the top brands for long-distance comfort and performance. The key is choosing the right model based on your pace and comfort preferences.

What's the difference between ASICS Novablast 5 and Gel-Nimbus 27?

The Novablast 5 is lighter and more versatile, suitable for various paces including tempo runs. The Gel-Nimbus 27 is heavier with maximum cushioning, ideal for easy runs and recovery. The Novablast 5 costs less ($140) compared to Gel-Nimbus 27 ($165), making it better value for most runners. From a practical standpoint, the Novablast 5 works as a single shoe for most runners, while the Gel-Nimbus 27 is more specialized for comfort-focused runs. The Novablast's lighter weight (9 oz vs 10.8 oz) makes it more suitable for tempo work and racing, while the Nimbus excels during high-mileage weeks when your legs need maximum protection and cushioning.
