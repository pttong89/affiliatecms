Waterproof vs Non-Waterproof: Which Trailventure 2 Should You Buy?

One of the most critical decisions when choosing your Topo Athletic Trailventure is whether to invest in the waterproof (WP) version. Having extensively tested both the Trailventure 2 WP and standard versions, I can provide practical guidance based on real conditions rather than laboratory testing.

eVent vs GORE-TEX: The Technology Difference

The Topo Athletic Trailventure WP uses eVent waterproof-breathable membrane rather than the more common GORE-TEX. In theory, eVent offers superior breathability through direct venting technology. In practice, I found minimal difference in breathability compared to GORE-TEX alternatives, but eVent does seem more durable against repeated flex cycles.

During stream crossings and prolonged rain exposure, the waterproofing performed reliably for the first 400-500 miles. However, after my backpacking trip on Pennsylvania's Allegheny Front Trail with a heavier pack, I noticed some compromise in water resistance. This suggests eVent may be less durable under stress than premium GORE-TEX implementations.

Breathability and Comfort Trade-offs

The non-waterproof version breathes noticeably better during high-exertion activities. When hiking in New Mexico's high desert climate, the standard Trailventure 2 felt significantly cooler and dried faster after water crossings. The weight difference is also meaningful—2 ounces might seem negligible, but it's noticeable during long days.

For most hikers, I recommend the non-waterproof version unless you frequently encounter sustained wet conditions. Modern synthetic materials dry surprisingly quickly, and the improved breathability more than compensates for occasional moisture penetration during brief rain showers.

Sizing and Fit Recommendations

Proper sizing proves critical with the Topo Athletic Trailventure because the wide toe box can mask length issues that become apparent only after extended wear. Based on user feedback analysis, most people should order their normal hiking boot size—typically half a size larger than street shoes. The generous width accommodates most feet without sizing up.

Pay particular attention to heel fit. The secure heel cup should hold your foot without slippage, but avoid the temptation to overtighten laces to compensate for length issues. Proper lacing technique using the locking eyelets prevents forward slide during descents while maintaining ankle comfort.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Topo Athletic Trailventure boots good for backpacking?

The Topo Athletic Trailventure works well for lightweight backpacking with pack weights under 25 pounds. I've successfully used them on 2-3 day trips, but the moderate ankle support may not be adequate for heavy loads or highly unstable terrain. For traditional backpacking with heavier packs, consider more supportive boots. However, for fastpacking and ultralight backpacking, they offer an excellent balance of comfort, weight savings, and adequate protection. The key is matching the boot to your load and terrain demands rather than following traditional hiking conventions.

How long do Topo Athletic Trailventure boots last?

Based on extensive testing and user feedback analysis, expect 400-800 miles from the Topo Athletic Trailventure depending on terrain and care. The Vibram MegaGrip outsole consistently outlasts competitor rubber compounds, often showing minimal wear at 500 miles. Upper durability varies significantly based on terrain—rocky, abrasive surfaces accelerate mesh wear while dirt trails preserve the upper well. Proper care including regular cleaning, complete drying between uses, and avoiding storage in hot areas significantly extends boot life. My first pair lasted 650 miles before the eVent membrane began showing wear, which is competitive with premium waterproof boots.

Do I need the waterproof version for most hiking conditions?

For most hiking conditions, the non-waterproof Topo Athletic Trailventure provides better overall performance due to superior breathability and faster drying. I recommend waterproof versions only for consistently wet environments, spring snowpack conditions, or frequent stream crossings. Modern synthetic materials dry quickly, and the breathability advantage of non-waterproof boots outweighs occasional moisture penetration in most climates. The 2-ounce weight penalty and $20 price premium of waterproof versions add up over long distances and multiple purchases. Consider your typical hiking conditions rather than worst-case scenarios when choosing between versions.

How does the Topo Athletic Trailventure compare to Altra hiking boots?

While both brands emphasize wide toe boxes and natural foot positioning, the Topo Athletic Trailventure offers superior ankle support and more durable construction. Altra's zero-drop design appeals to minimalist enthusiasts, but the Trailventure's 5mm drop provides better Achilles comfort for most hikers transitioning from traditional boots. User feedback consistently reports faster outsole wear with Altra boots compared to Topo's Vibram MegaGrip compound. The Trailventure's mid-height design also provides more debris protection and ankle stability than Altra's low-cut options. Choose Altra for pure minimalist philosophy, Topo for practical wide-toe-box performance with reasonable support and durability.

What socks work best with Topo Athletic Trailventure boots?

The generous toe box of the Topo Athletic Trailventure accommodates thicker cushioned socks without pressure issues, unlike narrow traditional boots. I recommend merino wool blend socks with light-to-moderate cushioning for most conditions. Avoid cotton socks which retain moisture and cause blisters. For wet conditions with waterproof boots, consider moisture-wicking synthetic blends. The roomy fit also works well with liner sock systems for blister prevention, though most users find this unnecessary due to the pressure-free toe design. Proper sock choice enhances the Trailventure's comfort advantages while preventing the foot health issues common with narrow hiking boots.

Are Topo Athletic Trailventure boots suitable for wide feet?

Yes, the Topo Athletic Trailventure excels for wide feet due to its generous toe box and accommodating midfoot design. Unlike traditional boots that offer wide sizes with proportionally increased length, the Trailventure's natural foot shape provides width without excess length. Users with wide feet consistently report immediate comfort without the break-in period required by narrow boots. The secure heel cup prevents slippage while the wide forefoot prevents pressure points and toe crowding. This design particularly benefits hikers with bunions, hammertoes, or other width-related foot issues. However, narrow-footed hikers may find insufficient security and should consider other options or experiment with thicker socks and careful lacing techniques.
