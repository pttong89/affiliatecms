How long do Nike shoes last for daily standing work?

Nike shoes typically last 400-600 miles depending on the model and usage conditions. For daily standing work, this translates to 6-12 months of regular use. The Pegasus 41 and Air Max models tend to have longer lifespans due to their durable construction and balanced cushioning systems, while ultra-cushioned models like the Invincible 3 may show compression faster under constant pressure. However, even with some compression, they often remain more comfortable than new traditional work shoes. I recommend tracking your hours and replacing shoes when you notice decreased cushioning or uneven wear patterns, as worn shoes can contribute to foot and leg problems.

Should I size up Nike shoes for standing all day?

Yes, consider sizing up 0.5 sizes for all-day standing. Most Nike models run true to size, but the additional space ensures comfort throughout 8-12 hour work periods while maintaining proper support. I learned this lesson during my first week of extended standing work when my normally-sized shoes became uncomfortably tight by mid-afternoon. The extra space also allows for thicker insoles if needed and better circulation, which becomes crucial during summer months when feet tend to swell more.
