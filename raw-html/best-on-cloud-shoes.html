What Makes On Cloud Shoes Special

When I first encountered On Cloud shoes three years ago during a hiking trip in Colorado, I was skeptical about the unique "cloud" design on the sole. However, after putting in over 500 miles across various terrains, I can confidently say that the best On cloud shoes offer a distinctive running and walking experience that sets them apart from traditional athletic footwear.

The revolutionary CloudTec® technology consists of hollow pods that compress on landing and then create a firm platform for push-off. This innovation provides what On Running describes as "running on clouds" – a sensation I've personally experienced on everything from concrete sidewalks to rocky mountain trails.

What truly impressed me during my testing was how these shoes adapt to different running styles and surfaces. Whether you're a heel striker or forefoot runner, the CloudTec pods respond differently, providing customized cushioning that traditional foam midsoles simply can't match.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are On Cloud shoes worth the high price?

Based on my extensive testing, the best On cloud shoes justify their premium pricing through innovative technology, quality construction, and versatile performance. The CloudTec technology offers a genuinely unique experience that many runners find superior to traditional foam cushioning. However, value depends on your priorities – if you primarily need basic running shoes, there are excellent cheaper alternatives. For runners seeking innovation, comfort, and style, the investment often pays off through improved performance and all-day wearability.

How long do On Cloud shoes typically last?

In my experience testing multiple models, most On Cloud shoes last 400-500 miles for running, which is comparable to other premium brands. The CloudTec pods show wear gradually but maintain functionality throughout their lifespan. For walking or casual wear, they can last significantly longer. The key is rotating between pairs and using them primarily for their intended purpose. Proper care and rotation can extend their life considerably.

Do On Cloud shoes run true to size?

Most On Cloud models run true to size, but fit can vary slightly between models. The Cloud 6 and Cloudsurfer 2 fit true to size for most people, while the Cloudmonster 2 runs slightly large. I recommend trying them on if possible, or ordering your normal size with the option to exchange. The toe box is generally adequate for average-width feet, but those with wider feet should consider sizing up half a size or choosing models like the Cloudmonster 2 that naturally accommodate wider feet better.

Can I use On Cloud shoes for walking only?

Absolutely! Many of the best On cloud shoes excel for walking and all-day wear. The Cloud 6, Cloudtilt, and Cloud 5 are particularly popular among people who primarily walk or need comfortable shoes for work. The CloudTec technology provides excellent support and cushioning for walking, and many users report reduced fatigue during long days on their feet. For those interested in specialized walking models for women, several options cater specifically to this need.

What makes CloudTec technology different from regular foam?

CloudTec technology uses hollow pods instead of traditional foam, creating a unique compression and release cycle. During landing, the pods compress individually to absorb impact, then firm up during push-off for energy return. This provides softer landings but firmer push-offs compared to foam, which compresses uniformly. The result is a more responsive, adaptable ride that adjusts to your individual gait pattern. Traditional foam offers consistent compression throughout, while CloudTec provides variable response based on impact location and force.

Are On Cloud shoes good for flat feet or plantar fasciitis?

While On Cloud shoes aren't specifically designed as orthopedic footwear, many users with flat feet find certain models helpful. The Cloudflyer 5 offers the most arch support within the On lineup, making it the best choice for those with flat feet or mild overpronation. For plantar fasciitis, the CloudTec cushioning can provide relief by reducing impact forces. However, those with severe foot conditions should consult a podiatrist and consider specialized orthotic-friendly models or custom insoles.
