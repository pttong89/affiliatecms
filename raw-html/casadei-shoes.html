Heritage and Italian Legacy

The story of Casadei shoes begins in 1958 in the picturesque town of San Mauro Pascoli, just outside Rimini, where Quinto and Flora Casadei established their small workshop. What started as a modest operation creating sandals for tourists along the Italian Riviera has evolved into one of the world's most prestigious luxury footwear brands. The Casadei legacy represents more than six decades of unwavering commitment to Italian craftsmanship excellence.

When Cesare Casadei, born in 1962 to the founders, took creative control in 1994, he brought international vision shaped by his Swiss education and deep understanding of global fashion trends. His leadership during the crucial globalization period of luxury fashion helped transform Casadei shoes from a regional Italian brand into an internationally coveted symbol of sophistication and innovation.

The Iconic Blade Collection

The Blade collection represents Casadei's most revolutionary and recognizable contribution to luxury footwear design. This architectural marvel of engineering transforms the traditional stiletto heel into a sculptural masterpiece that defies conventional expectations. The Blade heel's distinctive geometric shape isn't just visually striking—it's a triumph of functional design that provides superior stability and weight distribution compared to traditional round heels.

Engineering Marvel

The development of the Blade heel required years of research and engineering refinement. Unlike standard heels that concentrate weight on a single point, the Blade's innovative architecture distributes pressure more effectively across the foot. This ingenious design allows wearers to enjoy the drama and elegance of a high heel while maintaining remarkable comfort and stability throughout extended wear.

Signature Styles and Variations

The Blade collection encompasses various heights and finishes, from the dramatic 120mm version to more moderate 85mm options. How do Casadei shoes fit compared to other luxury brands?

Casadei shoes typically run true to size but with a slightly narrow fit, characteristic of Italian luxury footwear. Compared to brands like Jimmy Choo or Louboutin, Casadei offers a more structured fit with excellent arch support. The brand's use of premium leather means some initial stiffness that softens beautifully with wear. I recommend trying them on if possible, or ordering your regular size with the understanding that the leather will mold to your foot over the first few wears. Their customer service is excellent for size exchanges, and many boutiques offer professional fitting services to ensure optimal comfort.

What makes the Blade heel so special?

The Blade heel is Casadei's signature innovation - a revolutionary stiletto design that's both structurally superior and visually striking. Unlike traditional round heels, the Blade's architectural shape distributes weight more effectively, providing better stability despite its dramatic height. The design was perfected over years of engineering, combining aesthetic beauty with functional performance. This iconic heel has become synonymous with the Casadei brand and represents the perfect marriage of Italian craftsmanship and innovative design thinking. The heel's unique geometry also creates a distinctive sound and movement that adds to the overall experience of wearing Casadei shoes.

How should I care for my Casadei shoes?

Proper care is essential for maintaining Casadei shoes' luxury appearance. Store them in dust bags with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture. For leather styles, use high-quality leather conditioner every few months and avoid exposure to excessive moisture. Patent leather requires gentle cleaning with a soft, damp cloth and occasional application of patent leather cleaner. The signature Blade heels need special attention - inspect regularly for wear and have them professionally resoled when needed. Rotate wearing to allow leather to rest between uses, and consider professional cleaning annually for best results. Never use harsh chemicals or household cleaners on luxury leather.
