European sizing can be confusing for American consumers, but understanding the conversion process ensures a perfect fit.

Size Conversion Made Simple

US Women 7 = EU 38 | US Women 8 = EU 39 | US Women 9 = EU 40
Most users report true-to-size fitting, but consider sizing up half a size for wider feet or thick winter socks.

My personal experience with sizing various models revealed interesting differences. The Kenova model fits slightly roomier than the Blanca, which has a more narrow European cut. The elastic side panels provide some accommodation for foot width variations.

Always measure your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally swollen to their daily maximum size. Consider the socks you'll typically wear with your vagabond shoes during the measuring process.

Care and Maintenance Tips

Proper maintenance ensures your vagabond chelsea boots remain beautiful and functional for years. Essential Cleaning Routine

Begin with regular dusting using a soft cloth or brush to remove surface dirt before it embeds into the leather grain. For deeper cleaning, use a damp cloth with mild leather cleaner, working in circular motions. I learned this technique from a cobbler in Vermont who emphasized the importance of gentle, consistent care over aggressive cleaning methods.

Weekly Maintenance Checklist

  • Wipe down with microfiber cloth
  • Check elastic side panels for stretching
  • Apply leather conditioner monthly
  • Store with cedar shoe trees
  • Rotate between pairs to allow rest

Weather Protection Strategies

Weather resistance requires proactive protection rather than reactive treatment. I apply waterproofing spray seasonally, focusing extra attention on seam areas where water typically penetrates. During harsh winter conditions, I learned to clean salt stains immediately to prevent permanent leather damage that I witnessed on other boots.

Drying wet boots properly prevents cracking and maintains shape integrity. Never place vagabond chelsea boots near direct heat sources like radiators or fireplaces. Instead, stuff them with newspaper and allow natural air drying at room temperature. This method preserved the leather quality through numerous rainy hiking adventures.
