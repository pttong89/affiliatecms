Seasonal Walleye Patterns and Techniques

What I learned from Ross Outdoor Adventures is that successful walleye fishing on Potholes Reservoir requires understanding the fish's seasonal movements. During spring (March-May), walleye move into shallow spawning areas, making this prime time for targeting pre-spawn and post-spawn fish. Shelby's expertise shines during this period, as he positions the boat perfectly to intercept feeding walleye moving between deep winter haunts and shallow spawning areas.

Prime Walleye Fishing Seasons

Spring (March-May)

Pre-spawn and post-spawn action in shallow bays and rocky areas

Summer (June-August)

Deep water trolling and structure fishing for suspended fish

Fall (September-November)

Aggressive feeding patterns as fish prepare for winter

Winter (December-February)

Deep water jigging and slow presentation techniques

The summer months reveal another side of Ross Outdoor Adventures' expertise. As walleye move to deeper structures and suspend in the water column, Shelby employs advanced trolling techniques and precise depth control that I've rarely seen matched. His knowledge of underwater contours, combined with modern electronics, consistently puts clients on fish even during the challenging mid-summer period. Extreme outdoor adventures like this require this level of professional expertise.

Fall fishing with Ross Outdoor Adventures offers some of the most exciting walleye action of the year. As water temperatures drop, walleye become increasingly aggressive, feeding heavily before winter sets in. I've experienced some of my best walleye fishing during October and November trips, when Shelby's combination of jigging techniques and strategic positioning resulted in multiple limits of chunky walleye. Do I need to bring my own equipment and licenses?

Ross Outdoor Adventures provides all fishing equipment, including rods, reels, tackle, and bait. For cast-and-blast trips, both fishing and hunting licenses are required, plus federal duck stamp for waterfowl hunting. I recommend purchasing licenses in advance to avoid delays on trip day.
