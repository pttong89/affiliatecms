Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose the right size for walking sandals?

Sizing for the best sandals for walking all day women's differs from regular shoes. Measure your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally swollen, and allow 1/2 to 3/4 inch of space beyond your longest toe. Many brands like Birkenstock and Chaco have unique sizing systems, so always consult their specific size charts. Consider that your feet may swell during long walks, so a slightly roomier fit often proves more comfortable than a snug one. If you're between sizes, generally size up rather than down, especially for extended walking activities.

Can walking sandals provide adequate arch support for flat feet?

Absolutely! Modern walking sandals often provide superior arch support compared to traditional sneakers. Brands like Chaco, Vionic, and ABEO specifically design their footbeds with podiatrist-certified arch support systems. The Chaco Z/1 Classic features the LUVSEAT midsole that's certified by the American Podiatric Medical Association. For severe flat feet or plantar fasciitis, look for sandals with contoured footbeds, heel cups, and adjustable strapping systems. Many users with foot conditions find these sandals more comfortable than orthotic inserts in regular shoes. For additional support options, explore our guides on best all-day walking shoes for women.

Are walking sandals suitable for long-distance hiking?

The best sandals for walking all day women's can absolutely handle long-distance hiking, but choice depends on terrain and personal preference. Closed-toe models like KEEN Newport or Merrell All Out Blaze provide protection while maintaining breathability. For technical trails with loose rocks, consider sandals with aggressive tread patterns and toe protection. Many hikers successfully complete long-distance trails in sandals, citing reduced weight, better ventilation, and fewer blister issues. However, they offer less protection than boots, so evaluate your hiking style, terrain preferences, and weather conditions. For mixed terrain adventures, consider having both sandals and all-day walking boots available.

How do I maintain and care for my walking sandals?

Proper maintenance extends the life of your walking sandals significantly. For synthetic materials like Teva or Chaco sandals, rinse with water after each use and scrub with mild soap weekly. Air dry completely before storing to prevent odor and material degradation. Leather sandals like Birkenstock or ECCO require different care: clean with damp cloth, apply leather conditioner monthly, and avoid soaking. Replace footbed inserts when they show significant wear, and inspect straps regularly for fraying. Store sandals in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Many manufacturers offer resoling services for premium models, making them a sustainable long-term investment for frequent walkers.

What's the difference between hiking sandals and walking sandals?

While overlap exists, hiking sandals typically feature more aggressive tread patterns, enhanced toe protection, and burlier construction for rough terrain. Walking sandals prioritize comfort for extended urban or paved surface use, often with more cushioning and style-conscious designs. Hiking sandals like Bedrock Cairn or Chaco Z/Rapid Pro use Vibram outsoles for superior grip on wet rocks and loose surfaces. Walking sandals such as KEEN Elle or Teva Universal focus on all-day comfort for city exploration, travel, and general wear. The best sandals for walking all day women's often bridge both categories, offering versatility for various activities. Consider your primary use case, but many modern sandals excel in multiple scenarios.
