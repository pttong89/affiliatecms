Overview and First Impressions

The Vasque Sunsetter represents a significant evolution from the legendary Sundowner, trading some traditional durability for modern comfort and sustainability. When I first unboxed these boots, the immediate impression was one of refined craftsmanship - the soft suede upper feels premium, and the iconic red laces immediately signal Vasque heritage.

Inspired by the 1984 Sundowner that built Vasque's reputation, the Sunsetter incorporates contemporary materials and construction techniques. The most notable upgrade is the Nature-Tex waterproof membrane, manufactured with 50% recycled content, replacing traditional Gore-Tex in Vasque's sustainability push. At 2 pounds 2 ounces for a men's size 9, these boots strike a balance between support and weight that's immediately noticeable on the trail.

Design and Construction Analysis

The design philosophy behind the Vasque Sunsetter centers on modernizing classic hiking boot aesthetics while improving comfort and environmental impact. The one-piece suede upper eliminates potential failure points while providing a clean, timeless appearance that works equally well on mountain trails and city streets.

The molded mesh collar deserves particular attention - it's arguably the boot's most innovative feature. Unlike traditional padded collars that can retain moisture and create pressure points, this design hugs the ankle naturally while allowing air circulation. During my testing in humid conditions, this feature proved valuable for temperature regulation, though it does sacrifice some of the robust ankle support found in traditional Vasque hiking boots.

Construction Highlights

  • Upper Material: Premium suede leather with natural water resistance
  • Lining: 100% recycled mesh for sustainability and breathability
  • Waterproof Membrane: Nature-Tex with 50% recycled content
  • Footbed: Anatomical high-rebound design for all-day comfort
  • Lacing System: Traditional metal eyelets with spare lace set included

The Nature-Tex waterproofing represents Vasque's shift toward sustainable materials without compromising performance. In my experience, it performs comparably to Gore-Tex in terms of waterproofing and breathability, though the long-term durability remains to be proven. The membrane integrates seamlessly with the boot's construction, showing no delamination or hotspots after six months of regular use.

One area where the Sunsetter shows its modern design philosophy is in the midsole construction. The compression-molded EVA provides significantly more cushioning than traditional hiking boots, making it particularly suitable for hikers transitioning from running shoes. However, this comes at the cost of some stability on uneven terrain - a trade-off that reflects the boot's positioning as an everyday adventure shoe rather than a technical hiking boot.

The Vasque Micro Plus outsole features a relatively aggressive lug pattern that provides excellent traction on most surfaces. During testing on wet rocks, loose gravel, and muddy trails, the outsole performed admirably, though the compound feels slightly softer than traditional Vibram options. This translates to good grip but potentially faster wear on abrasive surfaces - another reflection of the boot's focus on comfort over extreme durability.

Trail Performance Testing

Real-world performance testing of the Vasque Sunsetter has been conducted across diverse environments, from desert canyons in Utah to temperate rainforests in the Pacific Northwest. This comprehensive evaluation reveals both the strengths and limitations of Vasque's modern approach to hiking boot design.

In dry conditions, the Sunsetter excels. The suede upper provides natural abrasion resistance, and the flexible design allows for natural foot movement on technical terrain. During a challenging ascent of Angel's Landing in Zion National Park, the boots provided adequate support while remaining comfortable during the 5-hour round trip. The grip on sandstone was particularly impressive, with the Micro Plus outsole providing confidence-inspiring traction on steep, smooth rock sections.

My Personal Experience

My relationship with the Vasque Sunsetter began with skepticism. Having worn the original Sundowner for years, I was curious whether this modern interpretation could live up to the legacy while addressing contemporary hiking needs. After six months and over 300 miles of testing, I've developed a nuanced appreciation for what Vasque has accomplished.

The break-in period was remarkably gentle - a stark contrast to traditional hiking boots that require weeks of gradual conditioning. Within three short hikes totaling about 15 miles, the Sunsetter felt completely natural. This immediate comfort proved invaluable during a last-minute invitation to join a group hike in the Cascade Mountains, where I was able to wear them confidently without fear of blisters or hot spots.

One memorable test came during an early season attempt on Mount Washington's Tuckerman Ravine trail. The combination of snow, ice, mud, and rocky terrain provided an excellent proving ground for the boot's versatility. The Nature-Tex membrane kept my feet dry through snow and stream crossings, while the flexible design allowed for confident movement on technical sections. However, I did notice some heel slip on steep descents that wouldn't occur with more traditional, structured boots.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Vasque Sunsetter boots worth the investment?

The Vasque Sunsetter offers good value for hikers seeking a comfortable, waterproof boot with modern features. While not as durable as the legendary Sundowner, it provides excellent comfort and sustainable materials at a reasonable price point. Best suited for day hikes and light backpacking rather than extreme conditions. Consider your hiking frequency and intensity - if you hike more than 100 miles annually or tackle challenging terrain regularly, the investment is worthwhile. For occasional hikers, budget alternatives might suffice.

How does the Vasque Sunsetter compare to the original Sundowner?

The Sunsetter is lighter (997g vs 1200g+) and more comfortable than the original Sundowner, featuring modern cushioning and sustainable materials. However, it sacrifices some durability - the original Italian-made Sundowner was known for lasting decades, while the Sunsetter prioritizes comfort and lighter weight over extreme longevity. The waterproofing is comparable, but the newer Nature-Tex membrane offers better breathability than early Gore-Tex versions. Expect 3-5 years of regular use from the Sunsetter versus 10-15 years from vintage Sundowners.

What sizes and colors are available for the Vasque Sunsetter?

The Vasque Sunsetter is available in men's sizes 7-14 and women's sizes 6-12, both with half sizes. Color options include Lion (tan/brown), Ebony (dark brown/black), and Thyme (olive green). The boots tend to run slightly narrow, so wide-footed hikers may need to size up or consider alternative models. Women's versions are available in the same colorways with appropriate last adjustments. I recommend trying them on in-store if possible, as the fit differs significantly from athletic shoes and some other hiking boot brands.

Is the Nature-Tex waterproofing effective in wet conditions?

Yes, the Nature-Tex membrane provides reliable waterproofing for stream crossings, wet trails, and light snow. Made with 50% recycled materials, it offers breathability comparable to Gore-Tex. However, like all waterproof membranes, it requires proper DWR treatment maintenance on the outer leather to perform optimally. In my testing, it remained waterproof through 6+ inch stream crossings and extended rain exposure. Breathability is adequate for moderate exertion but can feel clammy during high-intensity activities in warm weather.

How long is the break-in period for Vasque Sunsetter boots?

The Vasque Sunsetter has a relatively short break-in period of 2-3 short hikes or about 15-20 miles of walking. The soft suede upper and padded collar minimize hot spots. I recommend wearing them around town for a few days before your first serious hike to ensure proper fit and comfort. Unlike traditional leather boots that require extensive conditioning and gradual break-in, the Sunsetter can be worn confidently on moderate hikes almost immediately. This makes them excellent for spontaneous adventures or hikers who don't want to invest time in boot conditioning.
