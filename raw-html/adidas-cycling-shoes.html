Frequently Asked Questions

Are Adidas cycling shoes worth buying in 2025?

Absolutely, especially for specific use cases. Adidas cycling shoes excel in urban cycling, commuting, and recreational riding where style matters as much as function. While they may not match the pure performance of brands like Sidi or high-end Shimano models, they offer excellent value for money, superior comfort, and unique aesthetics. The Velosamba line is particularly compelling for riders who want cycling shoes that don't look like traditional cycling shoes. However, serious competitive cyclists might want to consider more performance-focused alternatives.

How do Adidas cycling shoes compare to Shimano and Sidi?

Each brand targets different priorities. Adidas focuses on style and comfort, making their shoes ideal for wider feet and casual to recreational riding. Shimano offers the broadest range from budget to pro-level with reliable performance across all categories. Sidi specializes in high-performance racing shoes with narrow fits and maximum power transfer. In terms of pricing, Adidas sits in the value segment ($50-180), Shimano covers all ranges ($80-400), while Sidi targets the premium market ($150-500). For urban cycling and commuting, Adidas often provides the best overall experience combining function with street-appropriate styling.

What's the difference between Adidas Velosamba and The Road models?

The Velosamba is designed for urban cycling and lifestyle use, featuring a casual Samba sneaker aesthetic with SPD cleat compatibility. It prioritizes walkability and street style over maximum performance. The Road series targets more serious cycling with dedicated road cycling features, available in both SPD and SPD-SL versions, BOA closure options, and stiffer soles for better power transfer. Velosamba works best for commuting and casual rides up to 3 hours, while The Road handles longer rides and higher-intensity cycling more effectively. Price-wise, Velosambas range from $80-120, while The Road models cost $120-180 depending on closure system and features.

Do Adidas cycling shoes run true to size?

Generally yes, but with some model-specific variations. The Road models typically run true to size with their performance-oriented fit. Velosamba models tend to run slightly large, so many riders size down 0.5 for a more secure fit. Five Ten mountain bike shoes usually run true to size but with a wider toe box. Adidas cycling shoes generally work well for wider feet compared to brands like Sidi. For the most accurate fit, I recommend trying them on in person or ordering from retailers with good return policies. The Primeknit upper in The Road models adapts well to different foot shapes, while the Velosamba maintains the classic Samba's roomy last.

Can you walk normally in Adidas cycling shoes?

This depends on the model. Velosamba shoes are specifically designed for walkability and feel much like regular sneakers when walking. The Road models are less walking-friendly due to their stiffer soles and road cycling orientation, though still more walkable than many traditional road shoes. Five Ten mountain bike shoes offer excellent walkability with their grippy outsoles. The key factor is sole stiffness â€“ softer soles like those in Velosamba models flex naturally when walking, while stiffer performance soles create a more awkward walking gait. For maximum walkability in cycling shoes, the Velosamba is your best bet among Adidas options, making it perfect for bike commuters who need to walk around during their day.

Are Adidas cycling shoes good for beginners?

Excellent choice for beginners, particularly those new to clipless pedals. Adidas cycling shoes offer several beginner-friendly features: comfortable fit from day one without break-in period, forgiving sole flex that doesn't penalize imperfect pedaling technique, reasonable pricing for trying clipless cycling, and attractive styling that doesn't scream "cycling gear." The Velosamba is especially good for beginners because it looks like a regular sneaker, reducing the intimidation factor of specialized cycling gear. The Indoor model is perfect for those starting with spin classes. However, beginners should understand that while these shoes provide good performance for recreational riding, they may eventually want something stiffer if they progress to more serious cycling. Starting with Adidas allows new cyclists to experience clipless benefits without a huge investment.
