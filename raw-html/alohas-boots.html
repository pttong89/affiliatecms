Measuring Your Feet for Perfect Fit

  1. Measure both feet in the evening when they're slightly swollen
  2. Stand on a piece of paper and trace around your foot
  3. Measure the longest distance from heel to toe
  4. For knee-high styles, measure your calf at the widest point
  5. Compare measurements to Alohas' official size chart

Calf fit is particularly important for knee-high styles like the East boots. My calves measure 14 inches, and the boots fit perfectly with just enough room for thick tights in winter. If your calves are 15+ inches, you might find the boots tight initially, but the leather does stretch with wear. I've seen friends with 16-inch calves successfully wear Alohas knee-high boots after a few weeks of breaking them in.

The leather quality affects fit over time in important ways. All my Alohas boots have stretched slightly with wear, particularly around the ankle and upper foot area. This is normal and actually improves the fit â€“ what starts as snug becomes perfectly molded to your foot. This is why I recommend sticking with your true size even if the boots feel slightly tight initially.

Pro Tip: If you're between sizes, consider the style. For ankle boots, size up a half size. for knee-high boots, stick with your true size to ensure proper calf fit. The leather will stretch, but you can't shrink boots that are too large.

Breaking in new Alohas boots requires patience but isn't difficult. I always start with thick socks for the first few wears, gradually transitioning to regular socks as the leather softens. Wearing them for short periods initially (2-3 hours) helps avoid blisters while allowing the leather to start conforming to your feet. Leather conditioner applied after the first few wears can speed up the softening process.

Width considerations are important too. Alohas boots are designed for average-width feet. If you have particularly wide feet, you might want to size up half a size, but be aware this could affect the ankle and calf fit. For narrow feet, the boots may feel a bit loose initially, but they'll feel more secure as the leather molds to your foot shape.
