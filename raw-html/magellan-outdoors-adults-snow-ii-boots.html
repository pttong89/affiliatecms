Frequently Asked Questions

Are Magellan Outdoors Adults Snow II Boots truly waterproof?

Yes, the Magellan Outdoors Adults Snow II Boots feature genuine waterproof construction with sealed seams and waterproof membrane technology. I've tested these boots through puddles, slush, and wet snow during my winter hikes in Colorado, and they consistently kept my feet completely dry. However, like all waterproof boots, the membrane may begin to show wear after 18-24 months of heavy use, particularly in high-flex areas. For optimal waterproof performance, I recommend treating the boots with a waterproof spray every few months and allowing them to dry completely between uses.

What temperature range are these boots suitable for?

Based on my extensive testing across three winter seasons, the Magellan Outdoors Adults Snow II Boots are comfortable in temperatures ranging from 40°F down to approximately 10°F (-12°C). The insulation and thermal lining provide adequate warmth for most winter activities in this range. For temperatures below 10°F, I recommend pairing them with thick wool socks, though comfort begins to diminish below 5°F (-15°C). During active use like hiking or snow shoveling, they remain comfortable down to -5°F (-21°C), but for stationary activities in extreme cold, more specialized boots would be preferable.

How does the sizing run for Magellan Snow II Boots?

The sizing tends to run slightly large compared to standard athletic shoes. I recommend ordering a half size down from your regular shoe size, especially if you plan to wear thin to medium-thickness socks. This ensures a snug fit that prevents heel slippage while maintaining comfort for extended wear. If you typically wear thick winter socks or have wide feet, your true size should work well. The boots have a roomy toe box, so there's adequate space for toe movement even with the smaller size. Academy Sports typically offers free exchanges if you need to adjust sizing.

What's the difference between Snow II and regular Snow boots?

The Snow II version features several important improvements over the original Snow boots. The TPR outsoles have enhanced traction patterns with deeper lugs for better grip on ice and packed snow. The die-cut EVA insoles provide superior arch support and cushioning compared to the basic foam insoles in the original version. Additionally, the waterproofing technology has been upgraded with better seam sealing and a more durable membrane system. The upper construction includes reinforced stitching at stress points for improved longevity. These improvements make the Snow II version worth the typically small price premium over the original Snow boots.

Are these boots suitable for hiking or just casual wear?

While primarily designed for casual winter wear and light outdoor activities, I've successfully used these boots for moderate winter hiking on established trails up to 5-6 miles in length. The TPR outsole provides adequate traction on most winter hiking surfaces, and the ankle support is sufficient for moderate terrain. However, for serious hiking, mountaineering, or off-trail winter activities, dedicated hiking boots would be more appropriate. The Magellan boots are heavier than purpose-built hiking boots and lack some advanced features like crampon compatibility or specialized hiking lug patterns. They excel for urban winter use, snow sports access, and light trail hiking but aren't optimal for technical outdoor pursuits.
