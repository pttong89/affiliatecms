Targhee III Overview & Key Features

The Targhee III represents KEEN's evolution of their most successful hiking boot design, building upon the foundation laid by the beloved Targhee II. Released in 2017, this third generation addressed many user complaints about the previous model while maintaining the core features that made the Targhee line legendary among hikers. During my extensive testing across Olympic National Park's diverse terrain, I've discovered what makes this boot special.

What sets the Targhee III apart is its refined approach to the hiking boot fundamentals. The boot features a more contoured fit compared to its predecessor, addressing the "too much volume" complaints that plagued the Targhee II. At 17.1 oz per shoe, it's notably lighter than many competitors while maintaining the robust protection KEEN is known for. The updated outsole design improves agility and traction significantly.

Technical Specifications That Matter

The boot's construction centers around KEEN's proprietary KEEN.DRY waterproof membrane, which I've found performs comparably to Gore-Tex while offering better breathability. The Nubuck leather upper provides excellent durability – after hundreds of miles on rocky Pacific Northwest trails, mine show minimal wear beyond expected scuffs. The KEEN.ALL-TERRAIN rubber outsole features 4.5mm lugs that dig effectively into various surfaces.

Key Technical Features:

  • • KEEN.DRY waterproof breathable membrane
  • • Nubuck leather and performance mesh upper
  • • Dual-density EVA midsole for cushioning
  • • 12.5mm heel-to-toe drop for natural stride
  • • Metatomical footbed for arch support
  • • Speed-lace webbing system

For hikers considering different variants, the Targhee III Mid offers ankle support, while the Targhee III Low provides a lighter, more flexible option for day hikes. The waterproof version is what most hikers choose, and what I'll focus on in this review.

Real-World Performance Testing

Traction & Grip Performance

After testing the Targhee III on everything from wet Washington slate to loose Cascade scree, I can confidently say the traction exceeds expectations for a boot in this price range. The KEEN.ALL-TERRAIN outsole with its 4.5mm multidirectional lugs provides excellent bite on most surfaces. During a challenging descent down Mount Pilchuck's rocky approach trail, these boots kept me stable and confident.

Where the Targhee III particularly shines is in muddy conditions. The lug pattern sheds mud effectively, unlike some competitors that become slippery skating rinks. However, I've noticed the outsole flexibility can be a limitation on technical terrain requiring precise edging. This isn't necessarily a fault – it's a design choice that prioritizes comfort over technical performance.

Waterproofing & Breathability

KEEN's proprietary KEEN.DRY membrane has impressed me throughout Pacific Northwest winters. During stream crossings and prolonged exposure to rain, my feet stayed completely dry. What's equally important is that the membrane doesn't create the suffocating feeling common with some waterproof boots. The breathability, while not matching non-waterproof alternatives, is notably better than Gore-Tex equipped boots I've tested.

Temperature regulation becomes crucial during longer hikes, and the Targhee III performs admirably here. The mesh panels in the upper work alongside the membrane to move moisture away from feet. That said, in hot summer conditions above 80°F, these boots can feel warm. They're ideally suited for three-season use in temperate climates.

Comfort & Fit Analysis

The Targhee III's fit philosophy centers on KEEN's signature roomy toe box, which I've grown to appreciate during long descents when feet naturally swell. However, this generous forefoot volume might feel loose for hikers accustomed to more athletic-cut boots. The midfoot and heel provide a more standard fit, creating an interesting contrast that works well for most foot shapes but may feel unbalanced to some.

My Personal Experience

Three Years on Pacific Northwest Trails

My relationship with the Targhee III began in early 2022 when I needed reliable boots for year-round hiking in Washington State. After wearing through two pairs of previous boots in eighteen months, I was skeptical but hopeful. The first test came during a challenging February hike to Wallace Falls during a particularly wet winter. The boots performed flawlessly, keeping my feet dry through stream crossings and hours of rain-soaked trail conditions.

What struck me immediately was the out-of-box comfort. Unlike many hiking boots that require extensive breaking-in periods, the Targhee III felt ready for serious mileage from day one. The dual-density EVA midsole provided just enough cushioning without feeling squishy, and the Nubuck leather upper, while initially stiff, molded to my feet within the first 20 miles.

Seasonal Performance Insights

Spring hiking in the Cascades revealed the boots' excellent mud-shedding capabilities. During the notorious "mud season" on trails like Mailbox Peak, the Targhee III maintained traction where other boots failed. The aggressive tread pattern cleared itself naturally, preventing the dangerous buildup that can turn hiking into ice skating.

Summer testing on Mount Rainier's Tolmie Peak trail highlighted both strengths and limitations. While the waterproof membrane felt excessive on 85°F days, the superior foot protection proved valuable when navigating loose scree fields. I learned to appreciate the trade-off between breathability and protection – a lesson that influenced my gear recommendations for clients.

Winter performance exceeded expectations. The Targhee III remained flexible in sub-freezing temperatures, unlike some boots that become rigid and uncomfortable. Paired with quality wool socks, they provided adequate warmth for day hikes down to 20°F. The KEEN.DRY membrane prevented snow infiltration while allowing enough breathability to prevent sweat buildup.

Lessons Learned from 400+ Trail Miles:

  • • Best performance in temperatures 35-75°F
  • • Excels in muddy, rocky, and mixed terrain
  • • Requires quality socks to prevent hot spots
  • • Not ideal for technical scrambling or hot weather
  • • Outstanding durability with minimal maintenance

Durability Assessment After Extended Use

After three years and hundreds of miles, my Targhee III boots show impressive durability. The Nubuck leather upper displays expected scuffs and scratches but maintains structural integrity. The outsole shows even wear patterns with approximately 2mm of lug height remaining – impressive considering the rocky terrain I frequent. The only notable wear appears at the heel counter, where repeated boot removal has slightly compressed the padding.

How It Compares to Competitors

Targhee III vs. Merrell Moab 3

The most common comparison I encounter pits the Targhee III against the Merrell Moab 3, both considered entry-to-intermediate hiking boots. Having tested both extensively, each excels in different areas. The Targhee III offers superior waterproofing and more durable construction, while the Moab 3 provides better breathability and lighter weight for long-distance comfort.

Traction-wise, both perform admirably, but the Targhee III's deeper lugs provide better grip in muddy conditions. The Moab 3's Vibram sole offers excellent performance on rock, but I've noticed faster wear rates compared to KEEN's proprietary rubber compound. For wet-weather hiking, the Targhee III is my clear preference; for dry, warm conditions, the Moab 3 takes the edge.

Targhee III vs. Targhee IV Evolution

The newer Targhee IV addresses several limitations of the III, particularly breathability. The IV features integrated mesh panels around the tongue area, significantly improving airflow. Having tested both, I can confirm the IV runs noticeably cooler during active hiking. However, this improvement comes at a price premium that may not justify the upgrade for many hikers.

The Targhee III remains an excellent choice, especially considering current pricing. With the IV's release, III prices have dropped significantly, making it one of the best values in hiking boots. Unless you regularly hike in very warm conditions or need the absolute latest technology, the III provides 95% of the performance at 70% of the cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are KEEN Targhee III boots worth it in 2025?

Yes, the KEEN Targhee III remains a solid choice in 2025, especially at reduced prices due to the Targhee IV release. They offer excellent waterproofing, durability, and traction for day hikes and moderate backpacking. While not the lightest option available, their proven track record and comfortable fit make them worthwhile for hikers seeking reliable, budget-friendly boots with strong performance on varied terrain. Current pricing makes them exceptional value.

How do Targhee III compare to Merrell Moab 3?

The Targhee III offers superior waterproofing with KEEN.DRY technology and more durable construction, while the Moab 3 provides better breathability and lighter weight. Targhee III excels in wet conditions and rocky terrain with deeper lugs, whereas Moab 3 is more comfortable for long distances and warmer weather. Both are excellent mid-range options, with choice depending on your priority: waterproof durability (Targhee III) versus breathable comfort (Moab 3).

What's the difference between Targhee III and Targhee IV?

The main differences include improved breathability in the Targhee IV with integrated tongue mesh, updated KEEN.FUSION construction for enhanced durability, and slightly refined fit. Targhee IV is more expensive but offers better ventilation and longevity. Targhee III remains excellent value with proven performance, while IV represents the latest evolution. For budget-conscious buyers, III is still highly capable; for those wanting the newest technology, IV is worth the premium.

Do KEEN Targhee III run true to size?

KEEN Targhee III generally run true to size, but the fit varies by foot shape. The toe box is notably wide and accommodating, while the midfoot and heel run more standard to narrow. Most hikers find their normal size works well, but those with very wide feet may benefit from the wide version. The roomy toe box is a signature KEEN feature, providing comfort for long hikes but potentially feeling loose for those accustomed to more tapered boots.

How long do KEEN Targhee III boots last?

KEEN Targhee III boots typically last 500-800 miles of hiking, depending on terrain and usage patterns. The durable Nubuck leather upper and robust outsole construction contribute to longevity. Rocky terrain and frequent use will reduce lifespan, while occasional day hiking on moderate trails can extend it significantly. Many users report 2-3 years of regular use. The one-year warranty from KEEN provides additional confidence, though most quality issues appear within the first few months of use.

Are Targhee III good for winter hiking?

Yes, Targhee III performs well in winter conditions thanks to excellent cold-weather stability and waterproofing. The KEEN.DRY membrane keeps feet dry in snow and slush, while the midsole maintains flexibility in cold temperatures better than many competitors. The deep lugs provide good traction on packed snow and ice, though dedicated winter boots or microspikes may be needed for severe conditions. The insulation is moderate, suitable for active winter hiking but may require warm socks in extreme cold.
