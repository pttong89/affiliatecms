Do I need waterproof Nike hiking shoes?

The need for waterproof Nike hiking shoes depends primarily on your typical hiking conditions and personal preferences. How should Nike trail shoes fit for hiking?

Proper fit is crucial for optimal performance and comfort in Nike trail shoes. The ideal fit provides approximately 1/2 inch (thumb width) of space between your longest toe and the shoe's front, allowing for natural foot swelling during long hikes. The midfoot should feel secure but not tight, with the heel remaining stable without slipping during downhill sections. Most hikers benefit from ordering a half-size larger than their typical athletic shoe size to accommodate hiking sock thickness. The width should comfortably house your foot without creating pressure points, with models like the Terra Kiger 9 offering particularly generous forefoot space. Test fit in the afternoon when feet are naturally swollen for the most accurate assessment of best women's walking shoes nike sizing.
