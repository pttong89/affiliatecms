Sizing & Fit Guide

Getting the right fit with Lundhags boots requires understanding their unique sizing philosophy and construction differences from typical hiking boots. Unlike mass-produced footwear, these Swedish boots follow European sizing standards with specific considerations for their shell boot construction and intended use with thicker socks.

My experience, and that of most experienced users, suggests sizing up one full size from your normal shoe size. This extra room accommodates thick wool socks, allows for foot expansion during long hikes, and provides the proper fit for the boot's shell construction. When I first ordered my Forest II boots, I initially chose my regular size (US 10/EU 43) but quickly realized the need for a size 44 to achieve optimal comfort and performance.

The break-in period for Lundhags boots differs significantly from conventional hiking boots due to their rocker sole design and unlined construction. Rather than requiring extensive conditioning, these boots adapt to your feet relatively quickly, usually within 50-100 miles of wear. The key is starting with shorter hikes and gradually increasing distance as the boots conform to your foot shape.

Seasonal considerations also affect sizing choices. For winter use with thick wool socks and possibly felt insoles, some users prefer going up 1.5 sizes. However, for three-season hiking with standard merino wool socks, one size up typically provides the perfect fit. This sizing approach differs from brands like Brunt boots or Clark shoes, which follow different fitting philosophies.

Critical Sizing Tips

  • Size up one full size from your normal shoe size
  • Try with thick socks - wool hiking socks are essential
  • Consider seasonal use - winter might need 1.5 sizes up
  • Break-in gradually - start with short hikes, build up distance
  • UK availability limited - order from European retailers

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Lundhags boots really waterproof?

Yes, Lundhags boots offer excellent waterproofing through their shell construction, combining waterproof rubber lowers with water-resistant leather uppers. However, they require regular maintenance with appropriate waxes and balms to maintain optimal water resistance. The rubber-leather junction needs particular attention during care routines. While not completely impervious like rubber boots, they provide superior breathability while keeping feet dry in most wet conditions. Many users report staying dry during extended rain exposure, though water can enter if it overtops the boot height.

How much do Lundhags boots weigh compared to modern hiking boots?

Lundhags boots are significantly heavier than modern lightweight hiking boots, typically weighing 1-1.5 kg per pair depending on the model. For comparison, ultralight hiking shoes might weigh 500-800g per pair. This weight difference reflects their robust construction, full leather uppers, and substantial sole systems. While the extra weight requires more energy during hiking, many users find the superior support, protection, and durability justify the trade-off, especially when carrying heavy packs or traversing challenging terrain where ankle protection becomes crucial.

Should I size up when buying Lundhags boots?

Most experienced users recommend sizing up one full size from your normal shoe size when purchasing Lundhags boots. This accommodates thick wool socks, allows foot expansion during long hikes, and provides proper fit for the shell construction. Some users prefer going up 1.5 sizes for winter use with felt insoles. The boots should feel roomy initially but not loose, as they'll conform to your feet during the break-in period. If ordering online, consider purchasing from retailers with good return policies to ensure proper fit.

How long is the break-in period for Lundhags boots?

The break-in period for Lundhags boots typically ranges from 50-100 miles of wear, though this varies by model and individual feet. Unlike traditional hiking boots requiring extensive conditioning, the rocker sole design and quality leather construction make them relatively comfortable from the start. Begin with shorter hikes and gradually increase distance as the boots adapt to your foot shape. The unlined construction allows them to mold naturally to your feet rather than fighting against synthetic materials. Most users report significant comfort improvements within the first few weeks of regular wear.

Can Lundhags boots be repaired if damaged?

Yes, Lundhags boots are designed with repairability as a core feature. The company offers comprehensive repair services including sole replacement, leather restoration, and hardware repairs. This commitment to repairability reflects their sustainability philosophy and helps justify the initial investment. Many users report having boots repaired multiple times over decades of use, making them more economical long-term than cheaper alternatives requiring replacement. Contact Lundhags directly or authorized dealers for repair services, though shipping costs from some countries should be considered when evaluating repair versus replacement decisions.

Are Lundhags boots suitable for winter hiking?

Lundhags boots excel in winter conditions when properly configured. Their shell construction provides excellent insulation properties, while the waterproof design keeps feet dry in snow and slush. For winter use, many hikers add felt insoles for extra insulation and warmth. The boots' durability and traction make them suitable for winter hiking, snowshoeing, and cold-weather outdoor work. However, they're not designed for technical mountaineering or ice climbing applications. The unlined construction allows moisture management that prevents the sweating and subsequent cooling common with heavily insulated winter boots, making them comfortable for active winter pursuits.
