How do I properly care for and maintain the waterproof membrane?

Maintaining the waterproof performance requires regular cleaning and periodic reapplication of DWR (Durable Water Repellent) treatment to the outer materials. After each hiking trip, remove dirt and debris with a soft brush and lukewarm water, avoiding harsh detergents that can damage the membrane. Allow boots to air dry completely away from direct heat sources, which can damage both the membrane and adhesives. Every 6-12 months of regular use, apply a waterproof treatment specifically designed for leather and synthetic hiking boots. Signs that maintenance is needed include water absorbing into the upper materials rather than beading and rolling off. The membrane itself typically maintains integrity for the life of the boot, but the outer materials require care to prevent water from saturating through to the membrane layer. Store boots in a cool, dry place and avoid extreme temperatures that can degrade materials over time.
