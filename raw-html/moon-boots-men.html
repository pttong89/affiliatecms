Professional Fitting Tips

When trying on moon boots, wear the same type of socks you'll use during winter activities. I recommend high-quality wool or synthetic blend socks that provide additional insulation and moisture management. Your toes should have room to wiggle freely, and your heel should not slide excessively when walking. The boots should feel spacious but not so large that your foot moves around uncontrollably inside.

Temperature considerations play a crucial role in sizing decisions. In extremely cold conditions, your feet may swell slightly, and the extra space becomes even more valuable. I've found that sizing up rather than down is almost always the better choice with moon boots. Care and Maintenance Guide

Proper care and maintenance significantly extend the life and performance of your moon boots while preserving their appearance and functionality. Through years of regular use in harsh conditions, I've developed a comprehensive maintenance routine that keeps my moon boots men performing like new season after season. Understanding the unique materials and construction methods helps inform the best care practices.

Daily Care Routine

After each use, I recommend removing any surface dirt or snow before it has a chance to penetrate the materials. A soft brush or damp cloth works effectively for routine cleaning. Allow the boots to air dry at room temperature, avoiding direct heat sources that can damage the synthetic materials or cause shrinkage. Proper drying prevents moisture buildup that could compromise the insulation properties or create odor issues.

Stuffing the boots with newspaper or boot trees helps maintain their shape during drying and storage. This practice prevents the distinctive puffy silhouette from becoming compressed or distorted over time. For men who frequently switch between different winter footwear, maintaining multiple pairs allows proper drying time between uses while extending overall lifespan.

Deep Cleaning and Storage

Monthly deep cleaning involves more thorough attention to all surfaces and components. Remove the insoles if possible and clean them separately with mild soap and water. The outer nylon shell can typically be cleaned with specialized technical fabric cleaners that preserve water-repellent coatings. Avoid harsh detergents or bleach-based products that could compromise the material integrity.

Seasonal Storage Checklist

  • Clean thoroughly and ensure complete drying
  • Apply water-repellent treatment if needed
  • Store in breathable bags, not plastic
  • Keep in cool, dry environment
  • Check periodically for pest or moisture issues

For long-term storage, I recommend treating the boots with appropriate waterproofing products annually. This maintains their weather resistance and prevents premature deterioration of the outer materials. Quality waterproofing treatments designed for technical fabrics work best and won't interfere with breathability. Are moon boots waterproof?

Water resistance varies significantly between moon boot models and manufacturers, ranging from basic water-repellent coatings to fully waterproof construction. For wet winter conditions involving slush, rain, or extended snow exposure, look for models specifically labeled as waterproof rather than just water-resistant. The distinction is crucial because water-resistant treatments can fail under sustained moisture exposure, while properly waterproofed boots maintain protection even in challenging wet conditions.
