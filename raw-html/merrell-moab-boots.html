Sizing and Fit Guidelines

Proper fit is crucial for hiking comfort and safety. Merrell Moab boots generally run true to size, but individual foot characteristics require consideration. I recommend trying boots on later in the day when feet have naturally expanded, simulating hiking conditions where feet swell from prolonged activity and heat.

The toe box should provide adequate room for toe movement without feeling loose. A good test is ensuring you can wiggle your toes freely while wearing appropriate hiking socks. The heel should feel secure without slipping, and the instep should feel supported without pressure points. The lacing system allows fine-tuning, but the basic fit should feel comfortable immediately.

For those needing wider fits, Merrell Moab boots are available in wide versions across most models. Women's specific designs account for anatomical differences in foot shape and volume, ensuring optimal fit regardless of gender. When ordering online, check the return policy to allow for size exchanges if needed.

Consider seasonal factors when sizing: feet swell more during summer hiking, and thicker winter socks require additional room. My general recommendation is ensuring thumb-width space between longest toe and boot front when standing on an incline, mimicking downhill hiking conditions where foot sliding is most problematic.
