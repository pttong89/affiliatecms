Are Skechers walking shoes good for wide feet?

Yes, many Skechers walking shoes are excellent for wide feet. Models like the Go Walk Joy, Ultra Flex 3.0, and Max Cushioning series are available in wide width options. The brand's stretchy knit uppers and memory foam construction naturally accommodate wider feet. I particularly recommend the Go Walk Flex series for wide feet, as the fabric upper expands comfortably while maintaining support around the midfoot area. The lack of rigid materials means less pressure on wide foot areas that typically experience discomfort in traditional athletic shoes.

How long do Skechers walking shoes last?

Skechers walking shoes typically last 300-400 miles of walking, or about 4-6 months with regular daily use. The durability varies by model - the Max Cushioning series tends to last longer due to its robust construction, while lighter models like Go Walk Joy may show wear sooner. Memory foam insoles compress over time, but many models feature removable insoles that can be replaced to extend the shoe's life. From my experience, proper rotation between two pairs significantly extends the lifespan of both.

Can I wash my Skechers walking shoes?

Most Skechers walking shoes with mesh or fabric uppers are machine washable on a gentle cycle with cold water. Remove the insoles first and air dry completely - avoid putting them in the dryer as heat can damage the memory foam and cause shrinkage. For leather or synthetic leather models, use a damp cloth and mild soap instead. Always check the care label inside your specific model for manufacturer recommendations. I've successfully washed my Go Walk models dozens of times without significant deterioration.
