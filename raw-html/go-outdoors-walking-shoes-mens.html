Complete Buying Guide & Features to Consider

Choosing the right go outdoors walking shoes mens requires understanding your specific needs and matching them to appropriate features. After years of testing and conversations with fellow hikers, I've identified the critical factors that separate exceptional shoes from disappointing purchases.

Waterproofing vs Breathability Trade-offs

The eternal hiking dilemma centers on waterproofing versus breathability. Waterproof membranes like Gore-Tex keep external moisture out but also trap internal moisture from perspiration. For UK conditions with frequent rain, I recommend waterproof shoes for autumn and winter hiking, switching to breathable non-waterproof options for summer when overheating becomes a bigger concern than wet feet. Consider your typical hiking conditions and personal heat tolerance when deciding.

Sole Technology and Traction Patterns

Sole design dramatically affects performance on different terrain types. Vibram soles consistently outperform generic rubber compounds, offering superior grip on wet rocks and muddy surfaces. Look for multidirectional tread patterns rather than simple directional designs—these provide better traction when descending steep slopes or navigating uneven ground. Deep lugs (4-5mm) work better for muddy conditions, while shallower patterns suit rocky terrain.

Maintenance & Care Tips

Proper maintenance significantly extends the lifespan of your walking shoes while maintaining their performance characteristics. After consulting with GO Outdoors staff and experienced hikers, I've developed a comprehensive care routine that has doubled the effective lifespan of my hiking footwear.

Post-hike cleaning should begin immediately after returning from muddy or wet conditions. Remove loose dirt with a soft brush before it dries and hardens, making removal much more difficult. For stubborn mud, use lukewarm water and a gentle brush—avoid hot water which can damage adhesives and synthetic materials. Never machine wash hiking shoes, as the agitation and heat can separate sole bonds and damage waterproof membranes.

Drying requires patience and proper technique. Remove insoles and laces, then stuff shoes with newspaper to absorb moisture and maintain shape. Change the newspaper several times during drying, and avoid direct heat sources like radiators or fires which can crack leather and damage synthetic materials. Proper drying typically takes 24-48 hours depending on conditions and materials.

Regular conditioning maintains leather flexibility and water resistance. Apply appropriate leather conditioner every few months or after particularly wet conditions, following manufacturer instructions carefully. Over-conditioning can soften leather excessively, reducing support, while under-conditioning leads to cracking and premature wear. Different leather types require specific products—consult GO Outdoors staff for appropriate recommendations.

Storage considerations often get overlooked but significantly impact longevity. Store shoes in a cool, dry place with good air circulation, avoiding damp basements or hot attics. Use cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb residual moisture, preventing permanent deformation that affects fit and comfort. For long-term storage, ensure shoes are completely clean and dry to prevent mold and material degradation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes GO Outdoors walking shoes different from regular sneakers?

GO Outdoors walking shoes are specifically engineered for outdoor terrain with features like reinforced toe caps, waterproof membranes, aggressive tread patterns, and durable construction materials. Unlike regular sneakers, they offer superior ankle support, moisture-wicking properties, and protection against rocks, roots, and rough surfaces you'll encounter on trails. The specialized rubber compounds provide better grip on wet and uneven surfaces, while the construction methods prioritize durability over fashion.

How do I choose the right size for mens walking shoes?

For walking shoes, choose a size that's half to full size larger than your regular shoe size. This accounts for foot swelling during long walks and allows space for thicker hiking socks. Your longest toe should have about a thumb's width of space from the front. Always try shoes on in the afternoon when feet are naturally more swollen, and wear the type of socks you'll use for hiking. Consider that different brands have varying fit characteristics—Merrell tends to run wider while Salomon runs narrower.

Are waterproof walking shoes worth the extra cost?

Waterproof walking shoes are definitely worth it for UK conditions and year-round hiking. They keep feet dry in wet grass, puddles, and light rain, preventing blisters and discomfort. However, they're less breathable than non-waterproof options, so consider your typical walking conditions and climate when deciding. For summer hiking or hot climates, breathable non-waterproof shoes might be more comfortable. The key is having options for different conditions rather than one pair for everything.

How long should quality mens walking shoes last?

Quality GO Outdoors walking shoes should last 500-800 miles of hiking, or 2-4 years with regular use. Factors affecting lifespan include terrain difficulty, frequency of use, and care maintenance. Signs it's time to replace include worn tread, compressed midsole, or visible damage to the upper materials. Premium brands like Merrell and Salomon typically last longer than budget options, making them more cost-effective over time. Proper care and rotation between multiple pairs significantly extends total lifespan.

What's the best way to break in new walking shoes?

Break in new walking shoes gradually by wearing them for short periods indoors first, then on easy walks before tackling challenging terrain. Start with 30-minute walks, gradually increasing duration. Use your actual hiking socks during break-in, and consider leather conditioner for leather uppers to speed up the softening process. Pay attention to pressure points and hot spots—quality shoes should feel comfortable from day one with only minor adjustments needed. If shoes cause pain or significant discomfort during break-in, they may not be the right fit.
