Complete Buying Guide and Expert Tips

Selecting the right New Balance walking shoes for plantar fasciitis requires understanding both your specific foot characteristics and the available technology options. Through my extensive testing and research, I've developed a systematic approach to choosing footwear that maximizes relief and comfort.

Essential Features to Look For

The most crucial element in any plantar fasciitis shoe is motion control technology. New Balance's ROLLBAR and medial posting systems work by preventing excessive inward rolling of the foot, which can strain the plantar fascia. During my testing, shoes without adequate motion control consistently failed to provide lasting relief, even when they felt comfortable initially.

Arch support must be firm yet contoured to your foot shape. Contrary to popular belief, extremely soft arch support can actually worsen plantar fasciitis by allowing the foot to collapse during walking. The best New Balance models feature graduated support that's firmest under the arch and gradually softens toward the heel and forefoot.

Heel cushioning should be substantial but not excessive. The goal is to absorb impact while maintaining foot stability. I've found that shoes with heel drops between 10-12mm work best for most women with plantar fasciitis, as this reduces strain on the plantar fascia while maintaining natural walking mechanics.

Sizing and Fit Considerations

Proper sizing is critical for plantar fasciitis relief. Many women need to size up a half or full size from their regular shoe size to accommodate foot swelling and custom orthotics. I recommend shopping for shoes in the afternoon when your feet are at their largest, as morning measurements can be misleading.

Professional Fitting Tips

  • Measure both feet, as size differences are common with plantar fasciitis
  • Ensure thumb-width space between longest toe and shoe front
  • Check for heel slippage during walking motion
  • Test with your regular socks or orthotic inserts
  • Walk on different surfaces during fitting process

Width is equally important as length. New Balance offers extensive width options, from narrow (2A) to extra-wide (6E), accommodating virtually every foot shape. I've found that many women with plantar fasciitis benefit from slightly wider shoes to reduce pressure points and accommodate swelling.

How do I know if New Balance shoes will help my plantar fasciitis?

Look for New Balance models with stability features, firm midsole support, and adequate heel cushioning. Walking shoes typically offer better motion control and stability features specifically designed for heel-to-toe walking motion. However, some New Balance running shoes like the 860v13 stability model can also work well for walking with plantar fasciitis. Walking shoes generally have firmer heel counters, more durable construction, and stability features optimized for the walking gait pattern. How often should I replace New Balance shoes for plantar fasciitis?

Replace your New Balance walking shoes every 300-500 miles or when you notice decreased arch support, worn outsole patterns, or return of plantar fasciitis symptoms. Quality walking shoes maintain their supportive structure longer than running shoes, but plantar fasciitis sufferers should be particularly attentive to support degradation. Signs it's time to replace include: compressed midsole foam, uneven wear patterns, reduced arch support, or return of morning heel pain. I recommend tracking your mileage and replacing shoes proactively rather than waiting for obvious wear signs.

Can I use custom orthotics with New Balance walking shoes?

Yes, most New Balance walking shoes accommodate custom orthotics well. Remove the original insole and replace it with your prescribed orthotic.
