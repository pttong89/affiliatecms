Sizing and Fit Guidelines

Proper fit is the foundation of hiking boot satisfaction. I recommend visiting a specialty outdoor retailer in the afternoon when your feet are naturally swollen, simulating conditions during extended hiking. Bring the socks you plan to use for hiking â€“ this makes a significant difference in fit assessment. Your heel should sit securely without lifting during walking motion, while toes should have enough room to wiggle without touching the front of the boot.

Width considerations are crucial for long-term comfort. Timberland hiking shoes women's models often provide better width options than men's versions. If you have wide feet, consider ordering wide sizes rather than sizing up in length, which can cause heel slippage and blistering. Timberland's customer service can provide specific width availability for different models â€“ don't hesitate to call them directly.

Fit Checklist

  • No heel lifting during walking motion
