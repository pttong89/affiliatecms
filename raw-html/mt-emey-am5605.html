Sizing Guide and Fit Considerations

Getting the right fit with the mt emey am5605 requires understanding both its unique construction and your specific foot needs. After helping numerous fellow hikers find their correct size, I've developed a comprehensive approach to sizing these specialized boots.

Important Sizing Considerations

The AM5605's therapeutic design means standard sizing rules don't always apply. The extra depth construction and wide toe box create different spatial dynamics than conventional boots.

  • Size up ½ size if using thick orthotics
  • Consider width upgrades for foot conditions causing swelling
  • Account for hiking sock thickness in your sizing decision
  • Allow for natural foot expansion during long hikes

My Sizing Experience and Recommendations

I typically wear size 10.5 in most hiking boots, but found the perfect fit in a size 11 4E with the AM5605. The extra space accommodates my custom orthotics while providing room for toe movement during descents. This sizing allows for the slight foot swelling that occurs during full-day hikes.

Proper Fit Indicators

  • Thumb-width space between longest toe and boot end
  • No pressure points when standing with orthotics
  • Heel stays in place during walking motion
  • No pinching at widest part of foot
  • Comfortable lacing without over-tightening

Poor Fit Warning Signs

  • Toes touching or pressing against boot front
  • Pressure points around ankle or instep
  • Heel lifting when walking uphill
  • Numbness or tingling after short wear
  • Difficulty inserting custom orthotics

Width Selection Guide

The AM5605's three width options (D, 4E, 6E) cater to different foot shapes and conditions. Choosing the wrong width can significantly impact both comfort and therapeutic benefits, making this decision crucial for outdoor performance.

Medium (D) Width

Standard foot width without significant swelling or deformities. Best for users with normal foot shape using thin orthotics.

Extra Wide (4E)

Ideal for mild to moderate bunions, slight swelling, or thick custom orthotics. Most popular width for therapeutic users.

XX-Wide (6E)

Severe foot deformities, significant swelling, or accommodation for AFO braces. Specialized medical needs.

Professional Sizing Recommendations

Based on consultations with pedorthists and my experience fitting these boots for various foot conditions:

  • Have feet measured in the afternoon when they're naturally larger
  • Bring your orthotics and hiking socks to any fitting session
  • Consider seasonal swelling patterns if you have circulation issues
  • Test the boots' performance with your typical hiking pack weight
  • Allow for at least 30 minutes of in-store wear before deciding
