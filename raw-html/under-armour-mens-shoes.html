Under Armour Technology Overview

Understanding the technology behind under armour mens shoes is crucial for making the right choice. Having tested these innovations firsthand during my trail running adventures in Yellowstone and basketball games in local leagues, I can attest to their real-world performance benefits.

HOVR Technology

Energy return cushioning system that provides a "zero gravity feel" with superior impact absorption and responsiveness.

Charged Foam

Dual-density midsole that combines soft cushioning with firm support for versatile performance across activities.

Flow Technology

Grippy, lightweight foam that eliminates the rubber outsole for reduced weight and enhanced court feel.

The HOVR system stands out as Under Armour's flagship innovation, using a compression-molded Energy Web that contains and returns energy with every step. During my 10-mile runs through Rocky Mountain trails, I noticed significantly less leg fatigue compared to traditional EVA foam shoes. This technology is particularly beneficial in models like the HOVR Phantom and Sonic series.

Pro Tip: HOVR technology works best for runners with neutral to slight overpronation. If you're a severe overpronator, consider models with additional stability features.

Frequently Asked Questions

Popular models like the Charged Assert 10 and HOVR Infinite offer great value for money with reliable performance across different running distances and terrains. During my marathon training, I found the energy return particularly beneficial during longer runs, helping reduce overall leg fatigue compared to traditional EVA foam shoes.

What makes Under Armour HOVR technology special?

HOVR technology uses a compression-molded Energy Web that contains and returns energy while providing soft, responsive cushioning. This technology reduces impact forces during running and provides a 'zero gravity feel' that helps reduce fatigue. The Energy Web is encased in a firm foam carrier that maintains the shoe's responsiveness while delivering consistent performance throughout the shoe's lifespan. Unlike traditional foam that compresses and loses effectiveness over time, HOVR maintains its energy return properties for hundreds of miles. I've personally tested this technology over 800+ miles and can confirm the cushioning remains consistent even as the shoe ages.

How do Under Armour Curry basketball shoes perform?

Under Armour Curry basketball shoes deliver exceptional court performance with features like Flow foam technology for lightweight cushioning and superior traction patterns designed for quick cuts and stops. The Curry line offers excellent lockdown and support for guards and versatile players. Models like the Curry 12 and Curry Flow 10 provide responsive cushioning and reliable grip that performs well on both indoor and outdoor courts. During my league play testing, I found the traction particularly impressive on clean indoor courts, though outdoor performance varies depending on court surface conditions. The Flow technology eliminates rubber weight while maintaining durability.

Do Under Armour shoes run true to size?

Under Armour shoes generally run about a half size larger than Nike equivalents, particularly in basketball and training models. Running shoes tend to be more true to size, but individual fit can vary significantly based on foot shape and personal preferences. I recommend ordering your normal size first, but be prepared to exchange for a half size down if you prefer a snugger fit. The brand tends to accommodate wider feet better than many competitors, which can make them feel roomier for narrow-footed athletes. Always check specific model reviews and sizing guides, as knit uppers may fit differently than synthetic materials. For the most accurate fit, try them on in-store when possible or order from retailers with generous return policies.

What's the difference between HOVR and Charged cushioning?

HOVR technology focuses on energy return and provides a softer, more responsive feel with its compression-molded Energy Web system. It's designed for athletes who want maximum energy return during activities like running and basketball. Charged cushioning, on the other hand, uses a dual-density midsole that combines soft impact absorption with firm support and responsiveness. Charged foam is more versatile and cost-effective, making it ideal for training shoes and value-oriented running models. HOVR typically appears in premium models ($100+), while Charged cushioning is found in mid-range options ($60-90). Both technologies offer excellent durability, but HOVR provides more advanced performance characteristics for serious athletes. Consider HOVR for specialized athletic performance and Charged for versatile, everyday training needs.

Are Under Armour shoes good for wide feet?

Yes, Under Armour shoes are generally more accommodating for wide feet compared to many competitors like Nike or New Balance. Many models feature roomier toe boxes and wider midfoot construction that works well for athletes with broader feet. The knit uppers in models like the HOVR Phantom series provide stretch and adaptability that conforms to different foot shapes. However, fit can still vary by model, so wide-footed athletes should pay attention to upper materials and construction. For the widest options, look for models with traditional mesh or synthetic uppers rather than performance knit materials. Some training shoes like the Project Rock series are designed with stability in mind and tend to offer more generous width. Similar considerations apply to women's models for those seeking family shoe options.
