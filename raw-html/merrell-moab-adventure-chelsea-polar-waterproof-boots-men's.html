Sock Selection Impact on Fit

Sock choice significantly impacts the fit and performance of these boots. During my testing, I experimented with various sock types to determine optimal combinations. For cold weather use, I recommend medium-cushion merino wool socks, which provide warmth without excessive bulk.

Thin dress socks can leave too much room in the boot, potentially causing blisters during extended walking. Conversely, thick cushioned socks can make the boots feel tight and restrict circulation. The sweet spot is medium-thickness hiking socks that provide cushioning while maintaining proper fit.

For those planning extended cold weather use, consider that you might want to wear thicker socks occasionally. In such cases, ordering your true size (rather than sizing down) might be preferable to accommodate varied sock thickness throughout different seasons and activities.

Break-in Period & Fit Evolution

Unlike many leather boots that require extensive break-in periods, these boots are relatively comfortable out of the box. However, there is still a noticeable break-in period of approximately 10-15 hours of wear before they reach optimal comfort levels.

During the initial break-in phase, the leather upper softens and conforms to your foot shape, while the stretch gore panels adjust to your specific foot volume. The boots will feel slightly snugger initially, particularly around the ankle area, but this improves significantly after a few days of regular wear.

I recommend wearing them for short periods initially – perhaps 2-3 hours per day for the first week – to allow gradual adaptation. This approach minimizes the risk of blisters or discomfort while allowing the boots to conform properly to your feet.
