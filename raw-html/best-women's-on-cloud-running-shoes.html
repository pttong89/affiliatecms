Frequently Asked Questions

Are On Cloud shoes good for women with plantar fasciitis?

Yes, many women with plantar fasciitis find relief in On Cloud shoes, particularly the Cloudmonster 2 and Cloudrunner 2. The CloudTec cushioning system provides excellent heel impact absorption, which can reduce stress on the plantar fascia. However, the firmer midsole may require an adjustment period compared to traditional cushioned shoes. I recommend starting with shorter runs and gradually increasing mileage. The Cloudrunner 2's higher heel drop (10mm) often works well for plantar fasciitis sufferers as it reduces strain on the Achilles tendon and calf muscles. Always consult with a sports medicine professional for persistent foot pain, and consider custom orthotics if needed.

How long do women's On Cloud running shoes typically last?

Based on my testing and feedback from coached athletes, the best women's on cloud running shoes typically provide 400-500 miles of reliable performance, which is above average for running shoes. The CloudTec pods show remarkable durability – I've seen pairs with 600+ miles that still provide adequate cushioning. However, longevity depends on several factors: runner weight, foot strike pattern, training surfaces, and storage conditions. Heavier runners or those who train primarily on concrete may see reduced mileage. The outsole rubber compound is quite durable, and I rarely see premature wear patterns. To maximize lifespan, rotate between multiple pairs, allow shoes to dry completely between runs, and avoid using them for non-running activities. Most runners will get 6-8 months of training from a pair.

Do On Cloud shoes run true to size for women?

On Cloud shoes generally run 0.5 size small for most women, based on my extensive fitting experience with female runners. The European sizing translates differently than typical American athletic brands. I recommend ordering 0.5 size larger than your normal running shoe size, especially if you have average to wide feet. For narrow-footed runners, true to size may work, but 0.5 up is still safer. The toe box varies by model – the Cloudmonster 2 runs roomiest, while the Cloud X 4 feels more snug. Always ensure thumb-width space between your longest toe and shoe front. Consider that feet swell during long runs, so proper sizing prevents blisters and black toenails. If purchasing online, buy from retailers with generous return policies to ensure proper fit before committing to training.
