True-to-Size with Important Caveats

The Merrell Moab 2 GTX generally runs true to size compared to standard athletic shoes, but several factors influence the optimal fit. The toe box offers moderate width, accommodating most foot shapes without feeling overly spacious. However, the heel cup runs slightly narrow, which can affect comfort for hikers with wider heels.

For day hiking, I recommend ordering your standard athletic shoe size. For multi-day backpacking or extended hiking sessions, consider ordering a half-size up to accommodate foot swelling and thicker hiking socks. This extra room becomes crucial during long descent sections where your feet slide forward in the shoe.

Width Considerations and Options

Merrell offers wide-width options in most popular sizes, designated as "W" in the model number. If you typically wear wide shoes or experience pressure points across the forefoot, the wide option provides necessary relief without compromising performance. The standard Moab 2 GTX fits most hikers, but don't hesitate to invest in proper width if needed.

Break-in Period and Initial Comfort

Unlike traditional leather hiking boots, the Moab 2 GTX requires minimal break-in time. Most hikers report immediate comfort, with the shoe reaching optimal fit within 20-30 miles of use.
