Safety Standards and Protection Levels

Safety certification represents a critical consideration when selecting under armour work boots for professional environments. Understanding the specific standards these boots meet and exceed helps ensure compliance with workplace safety requirements while providing adequate protection for various occupational hazards. Through my extensive testing and research, I've evaluated how Under Armour's safety features compare to industry standards and real-world performance requirements.

ASTM F2413-18 Compliance

All Under Armour work boots meet or exceed ASTM F2413-18 standards for protective footwear. This includes impact resistance (I/75), compression resistance (C/75), and electrical hazard protection (EH) where applicable.

  • Impact Protection: 75 foot-pounds of impact resistance
  • Compression Protection: 2,500 pounds of static compression
  • Electrical Hazard: Protection up to 600 volts in dry conditions

The composite toe technology used in models like the Stellar Protect offers several advantages over traditional steel toe construction. During impact testing, I found the composite material provides equivalent protection while distributing force more evenly across the toe area. This results in improved comfort during long wear periods and better thermal insulation in extreme temperature conditions.

Slip resistance testing reveals impressive performance across multiple surface types and conditions. The specialized rubber compounds used in Under Armour outsoles maintain grip effectiveness on wet concrete, oily surfaces, and various industrial flooring materials. This multi-surface capability makes these boots suitable for diverse work environments where surface conditions frequently change throughout the workday.

For professionals requiring additional safety features, the black Under Armour boots line includes models with enhanced visibility features, puncture-resistant plates, and specialized chemical resistance properties. These additional safety elements expand the range of work environments where Under Armour boots provide appropriate protection.

How long do Under Armour work boots typically last?

Based on my experience and user feedback analysis, Under Armour work boots typically last 8-12 months with daily professional use. The Valsetz series shows better durability in my testing, lasting up to 14 months in moderate construction environments. Factors affecting longevity include work environment harshness, proper care, and usage frequency. While not the most durable option available, the comfort-to-durability ratio makes them worthwhile for many professionals, especially considering the 60-day return policy from Under Armour direct purchases.

Do Under Armour work boots run true to size?

Under Armour work boots generally run true to size, but I recommend trying them on if possible. In my testing across multiple models, the Valsetz series fits most consistently to standard sizing, while the Stellar models can run slightly narrow in the toe box. For optimal fit, wear your typical work socks when trying them on, ensure half-inch space beyond your longest toe, and consider that the boots will break in slightly over the first week of wear.

Are Under Armour tactical boots suitable for everyday work?

Absolutely. I've found Under Armour tactical boots highly suitable for everyday work applications beyond military and law enforcement use.
