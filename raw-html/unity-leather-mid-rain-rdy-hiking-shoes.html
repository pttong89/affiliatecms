Frequently Asked Questions

Are Unity Leather Mid Rain RDY shoes truly waterproof?

Yes, these shoes feature Adidas' RAIN.RDY technology which creates a waterproof barrier. I've tested them in heavy rain and stream crossings, and my feet stayed completely dry. The membrane is breathable yet waterproof, making them reliable for wet conditions while preventing overheating during active hiking. Unlike basic water-resistant treatments, RAIN.RDY provides true waterproof protection that maintains effectiveness over time. The nubuck leather upper also provides natural water resistance, adding an extra layer of protection.

How do they compare to other waterproof hiking shoes?

Compared to Gore-Tex alternatives, the RAIN.RDY technology offers similar waterproofing with better breathability in my experience. They're lighter than traditional leather boots but more durable than synthetic alternatives. The price point is competitive with other premium waterproof hiking shoes, offering good value for the technology and construction quality. The styling is more versatile than technical hiking boots, making them suitable for both outdoor and urban environments. However, they may lack the rigid support of heavy-duty mountaineering boots for technical terrain.

What's the best way to care for these leather hiking shoes?

Clean with a soft brush and mild soap after each hike, paying special attention to removing dirt from the tread pattern. Allow them to air dry naturally - never use direct heat sources like radiators or hair dryers, which can damage the leather and waterproof membrane. Apply leather conditioner monthly to maintain the nubuck leather's suppleness and water resistance. Store in a cool, dry place with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and prevent odors. Regular care will extend their lifespan and maintain waterproof performance significantly.

Are they suitable for long-distance hiking?

These shoes excel on day hikes and short backpacking trips up to 3-4 days. For longer treks, some users report the EVA midsole compresses over time, reducing cushioning and support. They're ideal for well-maintained trails and urban hiking but may lack the rigid support needed for extended backpacking with heavy loads on rough terrain. The comfort and waterproofing make them excellent for most recreational hiking needs, but serious long-distance hikers might want more specialized footwear with stiffer midsoles and enhanced ankle support for multi-week adventures.

Do they run true to size?

Most users find they run slightly large, and I recommend ordering a half-size smaller than your normal shoe size. The toe box is roomy, which is great for downhill hiking and foot swelling during long hikes, but the extra space can cause heel slippage if sized incorrectly. Always try them on with hiking socks for the best fit assessment, as sock thickness significantly affects fit. If you're between sizes, go with the smaller option. The nubuck leather will stretch slightly with wear, so a snug initial fit often becomes perfect after a few wears. Consider the type of hiking you'll do - day hiking versus backpacking may require different fit preferences.
