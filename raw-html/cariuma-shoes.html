The Sustainability Story Behind Cariuma

When I first discovered cariuma shoes during a research trip for sustainable outdoor gear, I was immediately drawn to their environmental mission. Founded in 2018 by Brazilian entrepreneurs David Python and Fernando Porto at Harvard University, Cariuma emerged from a simple yet powerful vision: creating footwear that doesn't compromise the planet for profit.

The brand's commitment goes far beyond marketing buzzwords. For every pair of cariuma shoes sold, they plant two trees in the Brazilian rainforest through their ecological restoration program. Having witnessed deforestation firsthand during my Amazon expeditions, this initiative resonates deeply with me. To date, they've planted over 2.5 million trees, directly contributing to biodiversity preservation and climate change mitigation.

What sets Cariuma apart in the sustainable footwear space is their B-Corp certification, which requires meeting rigorous standards for social and environmental performance. Their manufacturing partners pay wages 79% above legal minimum requirements, with the lowest wage still 47% above minimum wage standards. This ethical approach extends to their material sourcing, using organic cotton, bamboo textiles, cork insoles, and LWG-certified leather from responsible tanneries.

The sustainability features that particularly impressed me include their vegan insoles made from cork and bio-based foam, recycled materials in shoe construction, and fully traceable supply chain. Unlike many brands that simply slap "eco-friendly" labels on conventional products, Cariuma has redesigned their entire production process around environmental responsibility. This comprehensive approach makes cariuma shoes a genuine choice for environmentally conscious consumers who refuse to compromise on quality.

Care and Maintenance Strategy

Proper care extends the lifespan of cariuma shoes significantly while maintaining their appearance and performance. The natural materials require different approaches than synthetic alternatives, but the maintenance routine is straightforward and environmentally friendly.

Canvas Care

  • • Hand wash with neutral soap
  • • Air dry away from direct sunlight
  • • Use soft brush for stubborn stains
  • • Apply fabric protector spray

Leather Care

  • • Wipe with damp cloth regularly
  • • Condition every 3-4 months
  • • Remove laces before cleaning
  • • Use cedar shoe trees when storing

Suede Care

  • • Brush gently in circular motions
  • • Use suede eraser for scuffs
  • • Steam lightly to restore nap
  • • Store with moisture absorbers

Investment protection becomes crucial at cariuma shoes price points. I recommend rotating between two pairs to allow materials to recover between wears, using shoe trees to maintain shape, and applying protective treatments before first wear.
