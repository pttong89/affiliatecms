Understanding Hallux Valgus and Its Impact on Hiking

Hallux valgus affects millions of hikers worldwide, causing the big toe to lean inward toward the second toe while creating a bony protrusion at the base of the big toe joint. This condition, often exacerbated by wearing ill-fitting shoes or high heels, can make hiking extremely uncomfortable or even impossible without proper footwear.

The German brand Hanwag recognized this significant gap in the hiking boot market and developed their bunion collection to address the specific needs of affected hikers. Sizing and Fit Guide

Proper sizing is crucial for Hanwag bunion boots to provide optimal comfort and support. Based on my experience fitting numerous hikers and extensive personal testing, I've developed this comprehensive sizing approach.

Measuring Your Feet

  1. Measure feet in the afternoon when they're naturally swollen
  2. Measure both feet (most people have slightly different sizes)
  3. Measure length in millimeters for accuracy
  4. Consider the width of your bunion protrusion
  5. Account for hiking sock thickness

Sizing Tips

  • Size up 0.5-1 size from your street shoe
  • Ensure thumb-width space between longest toe and boot front
  • The bunion area should feel spacious, not tight
  • Heel should not slip when walking downhill
  • Consider seasonal sock thickness variations
