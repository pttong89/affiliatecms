Speedfit 2.0 Overview & First Impressions

The Under Armour speedfit 2.0 represents a significant evolution from its predecessor, addressing many of the original Speedfit's shortcomings while maintaining its lightweight philosophy. At just 11.04 ounces per boot, these hiking shoes blur the line between traditional boots and trail runners.

Key Specifications:

  • • Weight: 11.04 oz per boot
  • • Upper: Synthetic & textile
  • • Sole: High-traction rubber
  • • Support: Mid-cut ankle design
  • • Price Range: $120-$140

What immediately struck me about the speedfit 2.0 was its aggressive, almost athletic appearance. Traction and Grip Analysis

The outsole design of the speedfit 2.0 impressed me during extensive testing on varied surfaces. The dual-density rubber construction features softer rubber for grip and harder compound for durability. On my test routes through Zion National Park's riverside trails, the boots provided excellent traction on wet rocks and sandy surfaces, though I noticed some slippage on loose scree compared to more aggressive tread patterns found on dedicated Thursday boots.

Breathability and Moisture Management

During summer hiking in 90°F+ temperatures, the synthetic and mesh upper construction proved its worth. The boots maintained excellent airflow, preventing the swamp-foot syndrome common with traditional leather boots. However, this breathability comes at the cost of water resistance – expect wet feet if caught in prolonged rain or creek crossings.

Important Note

The speedfit 2.0 lacks true waterproofing. While suitable for dry conditions and light moisture, they're not recommended for sustained wet weather hiking without additional protection.

My Personal Experience with Speedfit 2.0

300+ Mile Field Test Results

Over eight months of intensive testing, I put my speedfit 2.0 boots through conditions ranging from Colorado's 14,000-foot peaks to Arizona's desert canyons. Here's my unvarnished assessment based on real-world performance rather than laboratory specifications.

Trail-Specific Performance Notes

Rocky Mountain National Park - Emerald Lake Trail

Distance: 3.2 miles roundtrip | Elevation gain: 650 feet | Conditions: Mixed terrain, some snow patches

The speedfit 2.0 excelled on the maintained switchbacks and provided adequate traction on rocky sections. However, I noticed some sole flex on sharp granite edges that more rigid boots would handle better. The lightweight design was most appreciated during the steady climb to the lake.

Zion National Park - Riverside Walk Extension

Distance: 5.4 miles roundtrip | Elevation gain: 200 feet | Conditions: River crossings, sand, slickrock

This test highlighted both strengths and weaknesses. The boots performed admirably on sand and dry rock, but the river crossings exposed their water-resistance limitations. Drainage was quick, but foot comfort decreased noticeably when wet. Similar challenges I've experienced when testing footwear on AllTrail-rated difficult hikes.

Unexpected Discoveries

During a particularly challenging day on Arizona's Camelback Mountain, I discovered the speedfit 2.0 boots' unexpected advantage in scrambling situations. The flexible sole provided better ground feel than traditional rigid boots, allowing more precise foot placement on technical rock moves. This insight came from a local ranger who mentioned seeing many climbers choose lightweight boots for mixed hiking-scrambling routes.

Durability Reality Check

After 300+ miles, my boots show moderate outsole wear, particularly on the heel and forefoot areas. The upper remains in excellent condition with no tears or significant scuffing. Based on wear patterns, I estimate 400-500 total miles before replacement becomes necessary – shorter than traditional leather boots but acceptable given the weight and comfort advantages.

Lessons Learned & Pro Tips

  • Pair with merino wool socks to maximize moisture-wicking benefits

  • Consider bringing backup footwear for multi-day trips in wet conditions

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Under Armour Speedfit 2.0 boots waterproof?

The speedfit 2.0 boots are not fully waterproof but offer water-resistant properties. The synthetic and textile upper provides some moisture protection for light rain and dew, but they're not designed for deep water crossings or prolonged wet conditions. For serious waterproof needs, consider pairing them with gaiters or look at Under Armour's Gore-Tex alternatives. Based on my field testing, expect 15-20 minutes of protection in light rain before moisture penetration occurs.

How does the Speedfit 2.0 sizing run compared to regular shoes?

Based on extensive user feedback and my personal testing, the speedfit 2.0 runs true to size for most hikers. The boots accommodate wide feet well, which is uncommon in this category. I recommend ordering your normal shoe size, but if you prefer extra room for thick hiking socks or have particularly wide feet, going up half a size works perfectly. The anatomically molded upper design provides a custom-like fit that adapts to various foot shapes better than traditional hiking boots.

What's the weight difference between Speedfit 2.0 and competitors?

The speedfit 2.0 weighs approximately 11.04 ounces per boot, making it significantly lighter than traditional hiking boots like the Merrell Moab series (around 2 pounds) or Salomon Quest boots (1.8 pounds). This weight advantage comes from the synthetic construction and streamlined design, though you sacrifice some durability and protection compared to heavier alternatives. The weight difference becomes most noticeable on hikes exceeding 8 miles, where reduced fatigue is substantial.

Can you use Speedfit 2.0 for winter hiking?

The speedfit 2.0 is primarily designed for warm weather hiking and lacks insulation for winter conditions. The lightweight construction and breathable materials make them unsuitable for snow or freezing temperatures. For winter hiking, I recommend insulated boots or adding warm socks and gaiters, though better options exist specifically for cold weather adventures. During my testing in 35°F conditions, my feet became uncomfortably cold within 30 minutes of hiking.

How long do Under Armour Speedfit 2.0 boots typically last?

With regular use on moderate trails, expect 400-600 miles of hiking from the speedfit 2.0. The lightweight construction prioritizes comfort over longevity. Heavy users report sole wear after 6-8 months of frequent use, while occasional hikers see 1-2 years of service. The upper materials hold up well, but the outsole rubber shows wear relatively quickly on rocky terrain. This lifespan is shorter than premium leather boots but reasonable considering the performance benefits and price point.
