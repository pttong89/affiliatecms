Sizing: The Make-or-Break Decision

Dr. Martens sizing can be tricky, and getting it wrong ruins the entire experience. I recommend ordering your normal shoe size if you have narrow to medium width feet, but going up half a size if you have wide feet or prefer thicker socks. For Dr. Martens boots women specifically, many find the fit runs slightly larger than typical women's shoes, so consider your usual boot size rather than sneaker size.

Leather Types: Understanding Your Options

Brown Dr. Martens come in several leather types, each with distinct characteristics. Smooth leather offers the classic look with moderate break-in time. Crazy Horse leather provides immediate comfort and incredible aging properties – it's my personal favorite for Dr. Martens Crazy Horse enthusiasts. Nubuck offers a softer texture but requires more maintenance to prevent staining.

Breaking in brown Dr. Martens requires patience, but I've found several effective methods through personal experience. Start by wearing thick wool socks and the boots for 30-minute intervals indoors.
