The Story Behind Chota Outdoor Gear

Founded by Frank Bryant in Charleston, Tennessee, Chota outdoor gear emerged from a passionate fly fisherman's frustration with uncomfortable wading equipment. After more than 50 years of fishing experience, Bryant recognized that existing gear failed to address the real challenges anglers faced during long fishing sessions. His vision was simple yet revolutionary: create the world's most comfortable wading gear.

The company's breakthrough came with their innovative approach to boot design and materials. Working alongside Mark Brown, a lifelong fisherman and whitewater paddler, and Chris Bertucci, they developed a product line that prioritized comfort without sacrificing durability. This philosophy earned them the prestigious 2017 Award of Excellence, cementing their reputation in the outdoor industry.

What sets Chota apart is their commitment to real-world testing. Every product undergoes extensive field testing in Tennessee's challenging waters, from the rocky streams of the Great Smoky Mountains to the fast-flowing rivers of East Tennessee. This hands-on approach ensures that each piece of chota outdoor gear performs when it matters most. Their headquarters at 9055 Hiwassee Street serves as both a design center and testing ground, where innovations are born from actual fishing experiences.

Expert Buying Guide and Recommendations

Sizing and Fit Guidelines

Proper sizing is crucial for maximizing the benefits of chota outdoor gear. Unlike regular footwear, wading boots should accommodate neoprene socks and provide room for toe movement during long periods of standing in cold water. I recommend ordering one full size larger than your regular shoe size for optimal comfort. This sizing approach allows for proper circulation and prevents pressure points that can develop during extended fishing sessions.

The QuickLash system provides excellent adjustability, allowing fine-tuning of fit based on sock thickness and personal preference. This feature is particularly valuable for anglers who fish in varying weather conditions throughout the season. For those exploring outdoor winter gear options, Chota boots accommodate thicker insulation without sacrificing mobility or comfort.
