The Rich Heritage of Justin's Boots Since 1879

When Herman Joseph Justin started repairing boots in Spanish Fort, Texas in 1879, he couldn't have imagined that his small operation would become one of America's most respected bootmakers. What began as a humble cobbler's shop has evolved into a legendary brand that embodies the spirit of the American West.

I've always been fascinated by the story behind Justin's boots. Herman Joseph, the son of a cigar maker, possessed an innovative spirit that would define the brand for generations. His early use of decorative stitching wasn't just about aesthetics—those rows of stitches across boot tops served a practical purpose, stiffening the leather to prevent folding around ankles. This attention to both form and function remains a hallmark of Justin's boots today.

The company's growth mirrors America's westward expansion. In 1887, when Herman married Louanna "Annie" Allen, the couple revolutionized boot sales by developing a self-measuring kit. This innovation allowed customers nationwide to order custom boots by mail—a groundbreaking concept that predated modern e-commerce by over a century.

When the railroad came to Nocona, Texas in 1889, the Justins wisely relocated to capitalize on improved transportation networks. The family business truly flourished when Herman told his sons John and Earl they would become equal partners in 1908, changing the company name to H.J. Justin & Sons. This transition to Fort Worth in 1925 marked another pivotal moment, establishing the headquarters that continues operations today.

What strikes me most about Justin's heritage is their resilience through economic challenges and industry changes. The company survived the Great Depression, adapted to changing consumer preferences, and embraced technological advances while maintaining their commitment to traditional craftsmanship. When Berkshire Hathaway acquired Justin Industries in 2000, it validated what many of us boot enthusiasts already knew—Justin boots for men and women represent exceptional value and enduring quality.

Expert Buying Guide and Recommendations

Selecting the perfect pair of Justin's boots requires understanding your specific needs, foot shape, and intended use. After years of helping customers choose appropriate boots, I've developed a systematic approach that ensures satisfaction and proper fit.

Sizing and Fit Guidelines

Proper sizing forms the foundation of boot satisfaction. Justin's boots generally run true to size, but cowboy boots fit differently than athletic shoes. Order your normal dress shoe size, not your athletic shoe size. The boot should feel snug initially—leather stretches and conforms to your foot shape during the break-in period.

For wide feet, consider EE width options available in most Justin models. Round toe styles naturally accommodate wider feet better than square or pointed toes. If you're between sizes, choose the larger size for work boots and the smaller size for dress cowboy boots.

Seasonal and Climate Considerations

Climate significantly influences boot selection. For hot, humid conditions, choose unlined boots with breathable leather construction. In cold climates, insulated models provide essential warmth without bulk. Waterproof membranes excel in wet conditions but may reduce breathability in hot weather.

I've found unlined boots ideal for year-round use in temperate climates, while insulated models serve specific cold-weather applications better.

Care and Maintenance Tips

Proper care extends boot life significantly and maintains appearance. Clean boots after every few wears using a soft brush to remove dirt and debris. Condition leather every 2-3 months or when it appears dry, using quality leather conditioner applied in thin, even coats.

Store boots with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture. Avoid direct heat or sunlight when drying, and rotate between multiple pairs when possible. These simple practices have helped my Justin's boots last over a decade with regular use.

How should I care for my Justin's boots?
