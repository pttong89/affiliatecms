How should Go Outdoors ladies walking boots fit properly?

Proper fit is crucial for comfort and performance in Go Outdoors ladies walking boots. Your boots should feel snug but not tight, with approximately a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the boot front when standing on an incline. The heel should not slip when walking, and you shouldn't feel pressure points around the ankle or across the top of your foot. Width is equally important – boots that are too narrow cause pain and blisters, while too-wide boots allow excessive foot movement. Always try boots on with your hiking socks, preferably at the end of the day when feet are naturally swollen. Walk around the store, test on inclines if available, and don't rush the decision.

What's the best way to break in new Go Outdoors ladies walking boots?

Breaking in Go Outdoors ladies walking boots requires patience and gradual progression to avoid blisters and discomfort. Start with short walks around your neighborhood, wearing them for 30-60 minutes daily for the first week. Gradually increase distance and difficulty, but avoid long hikes until the boots feel completely comfortable. Leather boots typically require longer break-in periods than synthetic materials. Use hiking socks during break-in, apply moleskin to pressure points if needed, and consider using boot stretching techniques for tight spots. Never attempt a long hike in completely new boots – this mistake has ended many hiking trips prematurely. Quality boots from Go Outdoors should start feeling comfortable within 20-30 miles of walking.
