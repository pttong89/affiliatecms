Trail Overview & Access

The Pfeiffer Falls Trail is located within Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, approximately 26 miles south of Carmel along the legendary Pacific Coast Highway (Highway 1). This moderate 2.4-mile round-trip hike leads to a stunning 60-foot waterfall that cascades over granite cliffs into a serene pool below, surrounded by some of California's most majestic coastal redwoods.

Important Trail Status Update

As of 2024, the original direct route to Pfeiffer Falls remains closed due to storm damage from January 2023. However, the waterfall is still accessible via the Valley View Trail alternate route, which actually provides a more scenic and rewarding hiking experience with panoramic valley views as a bonus destination.

Getting There & Parking

From Highway 1, enter Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park (day-use fee: $10 per vehicle). Park in Day Use Lot #1 or #2 near the Big Sur Lodge. The trailhead is conveniently located across from the lodge building, making it easily accessible for families and hikers of all experience levels. During peak season (May-September), I recommend arriving before 9 AM to secure parking, as lots fill quickly.

For those interested in exploring more waterfall trails, consider visiting Laurel Falls Trail or Grotto Falls Trail for equally spectacular experiences. The nearby Trail of 10 Falls offers an epic multi-waterfall adventure for more ambitious hikers.

What to Expect on the Trail

The current route to Pfeiffer Falls Trail begins with a gentle walk through the River Path, crossing the iconic redwood deck before ascending into the forest. The trail splits after 0.6 miles, offering two spectacular destinations: Valley View (elevation gain: 350 feet) and Pfeiffer Falls (additional 200 feet). Most hikers, myself included, visit both destinations in a single loop for the complete experience.

Trail Highlights & Landmarks

Redwood Grove (Mile 0.2): Enter the cathedral-like grove where 300-year-old coastal redwoods create a living ceiling overhead. Look for the interpretive "tree cookie" display showing growth rings marked with historical events. This grove survived the 2016 Soberanes Fire and demonstrates nature's remarkable resilience.

Trail Junction (Mile 0.6): The well-marked split between Valley View Trail (left) and Pfeiffer Falls Trail (right). I always recommend taking the Valley View path first to get the elevation gain done while you're fresh, then enjoying the downhill approach to the waterfall.

Valley View Overlook (Mile 0.95): A breathtaking panoramic vista of the Big Sur River Valley stretching toward Andrew Molera State Park and the Pacific Ocean. On clear days, you can spot Point Sur Lighthouse in the distance. This viewpoint rivals any overlook I've experienced in Big Sur.

Pfeiffer Falls (Mile 1.2): The crown jewel of the hike – a 60-foot waterfall cascading over moss-covered granite cliffs. The viewing area includes two wooden benches and informative panels about the Los Padres National Forest boundary. During peak flow (winter/spring), the sound is thunderous; in summer, it becomes a delicate ribbon of water.

Wildlife Encounters

During my visits, I've spotted Steller's jays, Pacific wrens, and occasional deer near the waterfall. Black bears are present but rarely seen. Always maintain a respectful distance from wildlife and secure all food items in bear-proof containers available at the trailhead.

Adventurous hikers might also enjoy the challenging Ricketts Glen Falls Trail or the family-friendly Stewart Falls Trail for different waterfall experiences across the country.

Seasonal Visitor's Guide

Having hiked the Pfeiffer Falls Trail throughout the year, I can provide detailed insights into what each season offers. The trail's character completely transforms with the seasons, making it worth multiple visits to experience its full personality.

Winter (Dec-Feb)

Best season for waterfall viewing! Heavy rains create spectacular flow rates. Expect muddy conditions, fewer crowds, and dramatic storm clouds. Pack rain gear and waterproof boots. Temperature range: 35-60°F.

Spring (Mar-May)

Perfect balance of good weather and strong water flow. Wildflowers bloom along the Valley View section. This is my recommended season for first-time visitors. Temperature range: 45-70°F.

Summer (Jun-Aug)

Warmest weather but minimal waterfall flow (often just a trickle). Great for families and photography of the redwood groves. Arrive very early to avoid crowds. Temperature range: 55-80°F.

Fall (Sep-Nov)

Beautiful foliage colors, pleasant temperatures, and fewer tourists. Waterfall flow depends on early rains. Excellent photography conditions. Temperature range: 50-75°F.

Crowd Management Tips

Based on my observations, the trail experiences peak congestion between 11 AM and 3 PM, especially on weekends. For a more peaceful experience, I recommend starting between 7:30-8:30 AM or after 4 PM. During summer holidays, the trail can see 200+ visitors per day, transforming from a serene nature walk into a crowded procession.

Consider combining your visit with other Big Sur attractions like the Dark Hollow Falls Trail or explore the comprehensive Trail of Ten Falls for an extended waterfall adventure.

Critical Safety Warnings

  • Never climb on or behind the waterfall - the granite is extremely slippery
  • Stay on designated trails to protect fragile redwood root systems
  • Check weather conditions - flash floods can occur during heavy rains
  • Carry a first aid kit and inform someone of your hiking plans
  • Cell service is extremely limited throughout the trail

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Pfeiffer Falls Trail currently open?

Yes, the Pfeiffer Falls Trail is currently accessible via the Valley View Trail route. While the original direct trail to the falls was closed due to storm damage in 2023, hikers can still reach the beautiful 60-foot waterfall by taking the Valley View Trail and following the designated spur trail to the falls viewing area. This alternate route actually provides a more comprehensive hiking experience with both waterfall and valley overlook destinations. Always check the official Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park website for the most current trail conditions before your visit.

How difficult is the Pfeiffer Falls Trail hike?

The Pfeiffer Falls Trail is rated as easy to moderate, making it suitable for hikers of all skill levels including families with children 8+. The total distance is approximately 2.4 miles round trip with 550 feet of elevation gain spread gradually throughout the hike. The well-maintained trail features some steep sections with wooden steps and handrails, but most reasonably fit individuals can complete it comfortably in 1.5-2 hours. The most challenging sections involve navigating wet wooden steps near the waterfall during rainy seasons, which is why proper footwear is essential for safety and enjoyment.

What's the best time of year to visit Pfeiffer Falls?

The best time to visit Pfeiffer Falls Trail is during winter and early spring (December through April) when water flow is strongest from seasonal rains, creating the most spectacular waterfall display. February and March typically offer the perfect combination of robust waterfall flow and manageable weather conditions. Summer visits offer pleasant hiking weather and fewer muddy conditions, though the waterfall may be reduced to a trickle during dry periods. Fall provides beautiful foliage colors in the surrounding oak woodlands, while early winter storms can create dramatic cloud formations and mist effects around the redwood groves.

Do I need permits or reservations for Pfeiffer Falls Trail?

No permits or reservations are required for the Pfeiffer Falls Trail hike itself. However, there is a $10 day-use parking fee required for entry into Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, payable at the entrance station or self-service kiosks. During peak seasons (summer weekends and holidays), parking lots can fill up as early as 10 AM, so arriving early is strongly recommended. The park accepts cash, credit cards, and California State Parks annual passes. If you're planning to stay overnight, camping reservations are required and should be made well in advance through ReserveCalifornia.com, especially during peak seasons when sites book up months ahead.

Are dogs allowed on Pfeiffer Falls Trail?

No, dogs are not allowed on the Pfeiffer Falls Trail or Valley View Trail within Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, with the exception of documented service animals. This policy helps protect the sensitive redwood ecosystem, native wildlife habitats, and ensures the safety of all visitors on the sometimes narrow and steep trail sections. However, dogs are welcome on the River Path and Nature Trail within the park, which offer beautiful redwood grove experiences closer to the lodge area. For dog-friendly waterfall hikes in the Big Sur area, consider exploring trails in the adjacent Los Padres National Forest where leashed dogs are typically permitted on designated routes.
