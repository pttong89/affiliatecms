Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Outdoor Research dry bags different from other brands?

Additionally, OR's environmental responsibility through PFC-free coatings demonstrates their commitment to sustainable outdoor recreation without compromising performance standards.

How waterproof are Outdoor Research dry bags really?

OR dry bags offer varying levels of waterproofing depending on the model and intended application. CarryOut series bags with welded seams provide complete submersion protection and can withstand 24+ hours underwater without moisture infiltration when properly closed. PackOut series bags with taped seams offer excellent rain and splash protection but aren't designed for prolonged submersion, though they excel at protecting gear inside backpacks during storms. Testing shows they maintain waterproof integrity for years with proper care and correct closure technique—the critical factor being the 3-4 roll minimum closure method. Environmental factors like temperature, age, and usage patterns can affect long-term performance, but OR's construction quality ensures reliable protection throughout their intended service life.

Which size Outdoor Research dry bag should I choose?

Size selection depends on your specific needs and gear requirements. For essential electronics and valuables, 5-10L bags provide adequate protection with minimal weight penalty. Clothing and insulation layers typically require 15-20L capacity, while sleeping systems benefit from 25-35L bags with compression features. Extended trips or group equipment may need 50L+ capacity or multiple bags for organization. The key is matching bag capacity to specific gear categories rather than trying to fit everything into one large bag. Multiple smaller bags often provide better organization, weight distribution, and redundancy than single large bags for most applications.

How do I properly close an Outdoor Research dry bag?

Proper closure technique is absolutely critical for waterproof performance and isn't optional. Start by expelling excess air while keeping contents away from the opening. Fold the top opening at least 3-4 times, ensuring no air bubbles or wrinkles remain in each fold. Each fold should be tight and even across the entire width. For roll-top models, make tight, consistent rolls and secure with the buckle system, pulling firmly to compress the fold. Test your seal by gently pressing the bag after closing—properly sealed bags should feel firm with minimal air movement. Practice this technique in controlled conditions before relying on it in the field, as improper closure leads to failures regardless of bag quality. Temperature affects closure flexibility, so allow extra time in cold conditions.

Can I repair a torn Outdoor Research dry bag?

Yes, small tears and punctures can be effectively repaired with proper techniques and materials. For minor damage, clean the area thoroughly and apply specialized fabric patches or seam sealer, extending the repair beyond the tear by at least half an inch on all sides. Allow adequate curing time before testing waterproofing. For larger damage or seam failures, contact OR's customer service as their excellent warranty program often covers replacement even for wear-related issues. Always carry a basic repair kit including seam sealer and patch material on extended trips for emergency repairs. Regular inspection and preventive maintenance like cleaning salt residue and proper storage significantly extend bag life and reduce the likelihood of field failures requiring emergency repair.
