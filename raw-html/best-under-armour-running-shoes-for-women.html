HOVR Technology Deep Dive

Under Armour's HOVR technology deserves detailed explanation as it's central to many of their best women's running shoes. HOVR is a proprietary foam that sits in a compression-molded Energy Web. This web contains the foam, providing structure while allowing it to expand and contract with each foot strike. The result is a firm, consistent feel that doesn't break down quickly.

During my testing, I found HOVR shoes maintain their cushioning properties remarkably well. While some competitive foams can feel noticeably different after 100-200 miles, HOVR maintains consistency throughout its lifespan. This makes it particularly appealing for runners who want predictable performance. The trade-off is that HOVR lacks the bouncy, energetic feel of some newer foam technologies, but many runners actually prefer this firmer, more controlled sensation.

The HOVR Infinite 5 represents the technology at its best. With a thick stack of HOVR foam and a durable rubber outsole, it's designed for high-mileage training. I used this shoe for my longest runs during marathon training, including several 20+ mile efforts. The consistent cushioning and stable platform made it ideal for maintaining pace and form over long distances.

Women typically have a narrower heel, higher instep, and different arch characteristics compared to men.
