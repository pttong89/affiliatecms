What's the difference between Outdoor Research booties and regular camp shoes?

Outdoor Research booties are specifically engineered for extreme cold weather conditions, unlike casual camp shoes. They feature technical insulation like PrimaLoft or down, water-resistant shells, and aggressive traction soles designed for snow and ice. Regular camp shoes prioritize packability and basic comfort but lack the thermal efficiency and weather protection needed for winter camping, mountaineering, or cold-weather activities. Can you walk long distances in Outdoor Research booties?

Outdoor Research booties are designed primarily for camp use and short-distance walking rather than extended hiking. While the Tundra Trax models have decent traction soles, they lack the ankle support, arch support, and durable construction needed for long-distance trekking. I've comfortably walked up to a mile in mine for glassing positions or camp setup, but wouldn't recommend them for serious hiking. For extended cold-weather hiking, pair quality insulated boots with OR gaiters instead.

How do I choose the right size for Outdoor Research footwear?

Outdoor Research booties typically run true to size, but I recommend going up half a size if you plan to wear thick socks or have wide feet. For gaiters, measure your boot circumference at the widest point and your calf circumference where the gaiter will sit. OR provides detailed sizing charts for each product. I've found their customer service extremely helpful for sizing questions, and their Infinite Guarantee means you can exchange if the fit isn't perfect. Always try them on with the socks and boots you'll actually use in the field.
