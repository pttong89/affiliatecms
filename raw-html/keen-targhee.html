Frequently Asked Questions

Which KEEN Targhee generation is best for beginners?

For beginners, I recommend the Targhee EXP or Targhee III Mid. These models offer the best balance of comfort, forgiveness, and value. The III is particularly forgiving during the break-in period and doesn't require the precise fitting that the IV demands. New hikers benefit from the generous toe box and immediate comfort, allowing them to focus on developing hiking skills rather than dealing with uncomfortable footwear. The proven track record of the III also means better availability of reviews and sizing guidance from other users.

How long do KEEN Targhee boots typically last?

With proper care, keen targhee boots typically last 300-500 trail miles for heavy users, or 2-3 years for weekend hikers. The Targhee IV, with its lifetime delamination warranty, should theoretically last longer, though it's too new for long-term data. Factors affecting lifespan include hiking frequency, terrain type, and maintenance habits. Regular leather conditioning, proper drying after use, and rotating between multiple pairs can extend life significantly. Many users report getting 400+ miles from their Targhee II and III boots before major wear issues. The most common failure point historically has been sole separation, which the IV's new construction method aims to eliminate.

Are KEEN Targhee boots good for wide feet?

Yes, particularly the Targhee women's and men's models in wide sizes. The Targhee II offers the most generous fit overall, while newer generations provide more targeted wide options. KEEN's naturally wider toe box accommodates most foot shapes better than European brands like Salomon or La Sportiva. However, the midfoot and heel remain standard width, so those with truly wide feet throughout should try them on first. The Targhee EXP Mid offers additional volume for high-arched or very wide feet. Always size up if you're between sizes, as the leather will stretch minimally over time.

Can you resole KEEN Targhee boots?

Traditional resoling isn't possible with most Targhee models due to their cemented construction method. However, the new Targhee IV's KEEN.FUSION technology might change this, though it's too early to tell. For older models, shoe repair shops can sometimes extend life through partial sole repairs and toe cap replacement, but full resoling isn't economically viable. This is why many hikers choose to buy multiple pairs during sales rather than investing in resoling. The inability to resole is offset by the boots' relatively affordable price point compared to resoleable options like Danner or Red Wing boots. Focus on proper care and maintenance to maximize your boots' natural lifespan instead.

What's the difference between Targhee Mid and Low versions?

The Mid version extends above the ankle for additional support and protection, while Low sits below the ankle like a hiking shoe. Mid boots provide better ankle stability on uneven terrain and offer more protection from debris and water. However, they're typically 4-6 oz heavier per pair and can feel restrictive to some hikers. Low versions are lighter, more breathable, and easier to break in, making them popular for day hiking and well-maintained trails. The Targhee Mid is generally recommended for backpacking and rough terrain, while Low versions work well for day hiking and casual use. Your choice should depend on your typical hiking terrain and personal preference for ankle mobility versus protection.

Do KEEN Targhee boots run true to size?

Sizing varies significantly between generations, which is crucial to understand before ordering. Targhee II and III generally run true to size, with some users preferring to size up a half-size for longer hikes or thicker socks. However, the Targhee IV runs approximately half-size smaller than its predecessors, so most people should order their normal size rather than sizing up. KEEN's toe box is naturally wider than most brands, so those coming from narrow boots might initially feel like they're too big. Always measure both feet and consider your typical sock thickness when choosing size. If possible, try them on in-store first, especially if you're switching between generations or have had sizing issues with previous Targhee models.
