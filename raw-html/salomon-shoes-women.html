Sizing & Fit Guide

Getting the right fit in Salomon shoes women models is crucial for comfort and performance. Through fitting dozens of women with various foot shapes and sizes, I've developed this comprehensive sizing guide to help you choose correctly the first time.

Critical Sizing Information

Salomon runs small and narrow compared to most athletic brands. I typically wear size 8.5 in Nike running shoes but require size 9 in most Salomon models, and 9.5 in their winter boots.

The European sizing system used by Salomon differs from US standards, making proper conversion essential for online purchases.

Trail Running Shoes Fit

For models like Speedcross and Ultra Flow, I recommend a snug but not tight fit. Your longest toe should have about 3-4mm of space to the front of the shoe.

  • Heel should not slip during movement
  • No pressure points on sides of foot
  • Toes should not hit front during downhills

Hiking Boots Fit

For best Salomon hiking shoes women's in boot format, allow more room for foot swelling and thick socks.

  • Thumbs width of space at toe
  • Comfortable with hiking socks
  • Secure heel with no lifting

Special Fit Considerations

Wide Feet Solutions

If you have wide feet, Salomon can be challenging. However, certain models like the X Ultra Pioneer accommodate wider feet better than others. Consider going up half a size and using thinner socks if needed.

The Ultra Flow model tends to run slightly wider than other Salomon shoes in my experience.

High Arches & Plantar Fasciitis

Salomon's anatomical footbeds work well for high arches, but those with plantar fasciitis may need additional arch support insoles. The Ortholite footbeds can be replaced with custom orthotics in most models.

Models with more volume like the Quest series accommodate orthotic inserts better.

My Fitting Recommendations

How should I care for my Salomon women's shoes?

Clean them after each use with a soft brush and mild soap, avoiding harsh detergents that can damage waterproof membranes. Air dry at room temperature, never near direct heat which can degrade materials and adhesives. For Gore-Tex models, periodically apply DWR treatment to maintain water repellency as it naturally wears off over time. Store in a cool, dry place with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape and absorb moisture. Remove insoles after wet conditions to allow complete drying. Check laces regularly for wear and replace QuickLace systems when they show fraying. For leather components, use appropriate leather conditioners to prevent cracking. Inspect the outsoles for excessive wear patterns which might indicate gait issues or the need for replacement. With proper care, quality Salomon shoes can provide years of reliable service across hundreds of miles of adventures.
