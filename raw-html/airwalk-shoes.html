The History and Legacy of Airwalk Shoes

Airwalk shoes burst onto the skateboarding scene in 1986, founded by George Yohn and Bill Mann in Altoona, Pennsylvania. What started as a small skate shoe company quickly evolved into a cultural phenomenon that would define an entire generation of skaters. The brand's early success came from understanding what skateboarders actually needed: durable shoes that could withstand the constant abuse of grip tape while providing the board feel necessary for technical tricks.

During the early 1990s, airwalk shoes became synonymous with skateboard culture. The company's marketing genius lay in their ability to capture the rebellious spirit of skating while making their products accessible to mainstream audiences. Their iconic advertisements featured professional skaters like Jason Lee, Tony Hawk, and Mike Vallely, cementing the brand's credibility within the skateboarding community. The pterodactyl logo became as recognizable as the Nike swoosh in skate shops across America.

The brand reached its peak in the mid-90s when it became the third-largest athletic shoe company in the world, with annual revenues exceeding $200 million. This success attracted the attention of larger corporations, and the brand changed hands multiple times. Unlike classic Chuck Taylor shoes, which maintained consistent quality, these ownership changes significantly impacted product quality and brand direction.

After being acquired by Collective Licensing International in 2004 and later by Authentic Brands Group in 2014, Airwalk shoes experienced a renaissance. The current iteration focuses on bringing back classic designs while incorporating modern comfort technologies. Today's Airwalk represents a perfect blend of nostalgic styling and contemporary performance, available exclusively through partnerships with major retailers like JCPenney and Walmart.

Sizing Guide and Fit Information

Understanding proper sizing for airwalk shoes is crucial for comfort and performance. Based on my testing across multiple models and customer feedback analysis, most Airwalk styles run true to size compared to other athletic brands. However, there are important nuances depending on the specific model and intended use that every buyer should understand.

Sizing Recommendations by Category

Casual/Lifestyle Models

Order your normal athletic shoe size. These models have standard width and length proportions.

Work/Safety Shoes

Go up 0.5 size to accommodate the composite toe and thicker construction.

Skate Models

True to size for board feel, but consider 0.5 size up if you prefer extra toe room.

Width considerations are particularly important for airwalk shoes. Most models are designed for medium width feet, though the soft materials typically accommodate slightly wider feet without discomfort. People with narrow feet may find the heel slips slightly, while those with wide feet should consider the work models which tend to run wider due to safety requirements.

International sizing can be confusing, so I recommend using the brand's official size chart rather than general conversion tables. European customers should note that Airwalk sizing aligns with US standards, not European. Unlike brands like Birdies that run small, Airwalk maintains consistent sizing across their entire lineup, making reordering straightforward.

Customer service reports indicate that sizing-related returns are relatively uncommon, suggesting the brand's sizing is reliable. When in doubt, I recommend ordering from retailers with generous return policies like Amazon or Walmart, both of which offer free returns on unworn shoes. This allows you to try multiple sizes risk-free and find your perfect fit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Airwalk shoes good for skateboarding?

Modern airwalk shoes can handle light to moderate skateboarding, especially models like the Anchor Low that are specifically designed for skating. While they may not match the durability of premium skate brands like Vans or Etnies for heavy use, they offer excellent value for casual skaters or beginners. The rubber compound provides decent grip, and the construction includes reinforcements in key wear areas. For dedicated skaters who session daily, I recommend them as backup shoes or for less technical skating.

How do Airwalk shoes fit compared to Nike or Adidas?

Airwalk sizing aligns closely with Nike and Adidas athletic shoes. If you wear a size 10 in Nike running shoes, you'll likely wear the same size in most Airwalk casual models. However, the work shoe line runs slightly larger due to safety toe construction, so consider going down half a size for those models. The width tends to be standard medium, similar to mainstream athletic brands. Unlike Air Force 1s which run large, Airwalks maintain consistent true-to-size fitting across most models.

What happened to the original Airwalk company?

The original Airwalk experienced rapid growth in the 1990s but struggled with quality control and corporate acquisitions. After several ownership changes, including purchases by Collective Licensing International in 2004 and later Authentic Brands Group in 2014, the brand has stabilized under professional management. ABG focuses on licensing partnerships with major retailers rather than operating their own manufacturing, which has improved availability while maintaining quality standards. Today's airwalk shoes represent a successful revival that honors the brand's skateboarding heritage while serving modern consumer needs.

Are Airwalk work shoes OSHA compliant?

Yes, Airwalk work shoe models meet OSHA safety standards and include features like composite toe protection, electrical hazard resistance, and slip-resistant outsoles. Models like the Camino and Venice carry proper safety certifications for workplace environments requiring protective footwear. These shoes successfully balance safety requirements with the brand's signature style, making them popular among workers who want protection without sacrificing appearance.
