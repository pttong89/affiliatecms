My 3-Year Experience with the Keen Targhee III

I purchased my first pair of Keen Targhee III boots in spring 2022, drawn by their reputation as KEEN's flagship hiking boot. After logging over 400 miles across diverse terrain—from the granite trails of Yosemite to the muddy paths of Olympic National Park—I have a comprehensive understanding of these boots' real-world performance.

First Impressions and Break-In Period

Out of the box, the Targhee III impressed me with its substantial build quality. The Nubuck leather upper felt premium, and the KEEN.DRY waterproof membrane promised reliable protection. However, the break-in period was longer than expected—about 30-40 miles before they felt truly comfortable.

The initial stiffness of the leather caused some pressure points along the sides of my feet, particularly during longer hikes. I learned to wear them for shorter walks around town before committing to full-day adventures.

Trail Performance Highlights

Where these boots truly shine is traction. The KEEN.ALL-TERRAIN outsole gripped confidently on wet rocks during a memorable hike to Mist Falls in Kings Canyon. The 4mm lugs provided excellent bite on loose gravel and maintained traction even after hundreds of miles.

Trail Tested: These boots have accompanied me through 15 national parks, from desert heat in Joshua Tree to snow-covered trails in Rocky Mountain National Park.

Long-Term Durability Observations

After three years of regular use, my Targhee III boots show expected wear but remain functional. The outsole lugs have worn down about 30%, and there's visible scuffing on the toe cap. More concerning is some separation beginning at the heel where the sole meets the upper—a common issue I've seen reported by other long-term users.

Design & Construction Analysis

Upper Construction

The Targhee III features a combination of Nubuck leather and performance mesh. The leather provides durability and weather resistance, while strategically placed mesh panels offer breathability. KEEN's signature toe protection extends well up the boot, safeguarding against rocks and roots.

The gusseted tongue effectively keeps debris out, though it can trap heat during summer hiking. Speed-lace webbing system allows for quick adjustments, but I found the metal eyelets sometimes catch on vegetation.

Sole and Midsole Technology

The dual-density EVA midsole provides moderate cushioning—not as plush as Hoka boots but more supportive than minimalist options. The external stability shank delivers lightweight support for heavier loads, though it sacrifices some flexibility.

KEEN's proprietary outsole rubber compound strikes a good balance between durability and grip. The multidirectional lug pattern works well on varied terrain, though it can collect mud on particularly sticky trails.

Waterproofing Technology

The KEEN.DRY membrane is KEEN's proprietary waterproof-breathable technology, positioned as an alternative to Gore-Tex. In my experience, it performs well in light rain and stream crossings but struggles with breathability compared to newer membrane technologies.

During a week-long backpacking trip in Olympic National Park's temperate rainforest, the boots kept my feet completely dry through constant drizzle and creek crossings. However, internal moisture buildup became noticeable during long, warm days.

Performance Analysis

Comfort and Fit Assessment

Comfort is where the Targhee III shows mixed results. The CMEVA midsole provides adequate cushioning for day hikes but lacks the plushness serious backpackers might want. The toe box offers generous room—perhaps too generous for some hikers—while the midfoot and heel run narrower.

During long days on the trail, I experienced some lateral foot pressure, particularly on the right foot. This seemed related to the somewhat stiff leather construction and may improve with extended break-in time.

Comfort Strengths

  • Generous toe box space
  • Good arch support
  • Adequate heel padding
  • Stable platform for heavy loads

Comfort Concerns

  • Extended break-in period
  • Limited breathability
  • Some pressure points
  • Heavy compared to modern boots

Traction and Stability Performance

The KEEN.ALL-TERRAIN outsole delivers excellent traction across diverse surfaces. I've tested these boots on wet granite in Yosemite, loose scree in the Rockies, and muddy trails in the Pacific Northwest. The multidirectional lugs provide reliable grip, though they can pack with clay-heavy mud.

Stability is generally good, though the somewhat flexible sole can feel less secure on technical terrain compared to stiffer hiking boots. The external stability shank helps with load-bearing but doesn't eliminate all flex.

Waterproofing Real-World Test Results

I've subjected these boots to numerous water tests, from intentional stream crossings to unexpected downpours. The KEEN.DRY membrane consistently kept water out during my testing, including a 10-minute submersion test in a mountain creek.

However, the trade-off in breathability becomes apparent during warm weather or high-exertion activities. Internal condensation can build up, leaving feet feeling damp even when external conditions are dry.

Pro Tip: For summer hiking, consider the non-waterproof Targhee III Mid version for better breathability.

Sizing & Fit Issues: The Hidden Problem

One of the most significant issues with the Targhee III isn't immediately apparent from marketing materials—inconsistent sizing between production runs. This problem became widely discussed in hiking communities throughout 2024 and continues into 2025.

Production Run Variations

Based on reports from multiple users and my own observations, KEEN appears to have modified the Targhee III fit sometime between 2022 and 2023. Newer production boots (often manufactured in India rather than Thailand) tend to run narrower, particularly in the midfoot area.

I experienced this firsthand when ordering a replacement pair in 2024. Despite ordering the same size and width specification, the new boots felt noticeably tighter across the midfoot and less accommodating in the toe box.

Sizing Warning: If you're ordering online, be prepared for potential fit variations. Many retailers offer generous return policies specifically because of this issue.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Keen Targhee III boots true to size?

The Keen Targhee III sizing can be inconsistent. While many users find them true to size, there have been reports of newer production runs being narrower than previous versions. I recommend trying them on in-store if possible, or ordering from retailers with good return policies. The toe box tends to be roomier while the midfoot and heel run more narrow. If you have wide feet, definitely consider the wide width options.

How long do Keen Targhee III boots last?

Based on real user experiences, Keen Targhee III boots typically last 300-500 hiking miles depending on terrain and usage. The Nubuck leather upper holds up well to abrasion, but some users report sole separation issues after 1-2 years of regular use. The outsole lugs maintain good traction for most of the boot's lifespan. For extended durability, consider rotating between the standard and waterproof versions based on conditions.

Are Keen Targhee III waterproof boots worth buying in 2025?

The Keen Targhee III offers good value for casual hikers, especially at current discounted prices around $90-120. However, with the release of the Targhee IV, the III shows its age in comfort and breathability. For serious hikers or those wanting the latest technology, consider the Targhee IV or alternatives like the Merrell Moab 3. The III remains solid for weekend warriors who prioritize value and don't mind the extra weight.

What's the difference between Keen Targhee III and IV?

The Targhee IV features improved breathability with integrated mesh panels, better comfort with Luftcell technology, enhanced durability with KEEN.FUSION construction, and updated styling. The IV also has better ankle padding and a more refined fit compared to the III. However, the III remains a solid budget option at significantly lower prices. After three years of testing and analysis, the Keen Targhee III remains a competent hiking boot with significant caveats. It excels in traction, waterproofing, and basic durability, making it suitable for casual hikers and those prioritizing value over cutting-edge features.
