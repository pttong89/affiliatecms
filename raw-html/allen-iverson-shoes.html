The Iconic Reebok Question: Where It All Began

The Reebok Question represents one of the most significant rookie signature shoes in basketball history. Released in November 1996, it was designed specifically for a 21-year-old Allen Iverson who had just been drafted first overall by the Philadelphia 76ers. What made this shoe revolutionary wasn't just its technology, but its cultural significance.

During my research trips to Reebok's archives, I learned that the first 5,000 pairs featured genuine red suede toes—a detail that makes original 1996 models incredibly valuable today. The pearlized red and blue toe versions that followed became the standard, but collectors like myself still hunt for those original suede editions.

Technical Specifications

  • Cushioning: Hexalite technology
  • Upper: Leather with patent leather toe
  • Release Date: November 1996
  • Retail Price (Original): $120

The Question's design genius lay in its simplicity and symbolism. The name itself referenced Iverson's "Answer" tattoo, but posed it as a question to doubters—would this undersized guard from Georgetown succeed in the NBA? The shoes provided the answer on March 12, 1997, when Iverson crossed over Michael Jordan in a moment that became basketball folklore.

Having worn multiple retro versions of the Question over the years, I can attest to its remarkable comfort and court feel. The Hexalite cushioning system, which used honeycomb-shaped elements, provided excellent impact protection while maintaining court feel—crucial for a player like Iverson who relied on quick cuts and explosive movements. This technology was ahead of its time and influenced cushioning systems for years to come.

The complete story of how the Question became an icon

What many fans don't realize is how the Question influenced not just allen iverson shoes but basketball footwear as a whole. Its high-cut silhouette and bold colorways paved the way for more expressive designs, moving away from the conservative basketball shoes of the early 1990s. Similar to how Jordans shoes revolutionized the 1980s, the Question defined the late 1990s basketball aesthetic.

The Answer series paralleled Iverson's career trajectory perfectly. During his MVP season in 2001, the Answer 4's aggressive design matched his fearless playing style. When he moved to Denver, the Answer 10's "Money" colorway reflected his continued stardom. Each model captured a moment in time, making allen iverson shoes more than just footwear—they became cultural artifacts.

What impressed me most during my hands-on testing was the consistency of performance across the series. Whether wearing the Answer 2's DMX Lite system or the Answer 7's upgraded Duracourt rubber, each model maintained the court feel and responsiveness that Iverson demanded. When shopping for allen iverson shoes, timing is crucial. Reebok typically releases Answer retros during basketball season (October-March), with Question models dropping year-round. Size availability can be challenging with allen iverson shoes due to limited production runs. Popular sizes (9-11) sell out quickly, while smaller and larger sizes may linger at discount prices. Frequently Asked Questions

What were Allen Iverson's most iconic shoes?

Allen Iverson's most iconic shoes were the Reebok Question (his first signature model from 1996) and the Answer 4, worn during his legendary 2001 NBA Finals run. The Question's red toe colorway and the Answer 4's bold design revolutionized basketball footwear aesthetics and established AI as a sneaker icon. These models represent the peak of Reebok's basketball innovation and Iverson's cultural influence, making them highly coveted by collectors and basketball fans worldwide.

Are Allen Iverson shoes still being made?

Yes, Reebok continues to release retro versions of classic Allen Iverson models, particularly the Question and various Answer models. These retros maintain the original aesthetic while incorporating modern materials and comfort technologies for today's sneaker enthusiasts. However, production runs are typically limited, and popular sizes sell out quickly. New releases often coincide with basketball season and special anniversaries, requiring sneaker fans to stay alert for drop dates and pre-orders.

How much do authentic Allen Iverson shoes cost?

Current retro Allen Iverson shoes typically range from $120-$180 retail, with the Question Mid around $170 and Answer models between $140-$160. Vintage original pairs command significantly higher prices, especially rare colorways and player exclusives, which can sell for $300-$1000+ depending on condition and rarity. The best value often comes from purchasing previous year retros during sales, which can offer 30-40% discounts off original retail prices.

Do Allen Iverson shoes run true to size?

Allen Iverson shoes generally run true to size, but sizing can vary between models. The Question tends to run slightly narrow, which benefits players seeking a locked-in feel but may require sizing up for wider feet. Answer series models typically fit true to size with adequate width for most foot types. I recommend trying on different models when possible, as Reebok's retro sizing can sometimes differ from the original releases due to modern manufacturing processes.

Are Allen Iverson shoes good for actual basketball?

Allen Iverson shoes remain excellent for recreational basketball, offering legitimate performance capabilities that rival many modern models. The Question's Hexalite cushioning and Answer series' DMX technology provide superior impact protection and court feel. These shoes excel in indoor basketball and casual outdoor play, though serious outdoor players might prefer models with more durable outsole compounds. The supportive construction and responsive cushioning make them particularly suitable for guards and players who value court feel over maximum impact protection.
