The Cioch Story: Handmade Excellence from Scotland

Nestled in the dramatic landscape of Scotland's Isle of Skye, cioch outdoor clothing has been quietly revolutionizing the outdoor gear industry for over two decades. Founded by Neil and Helen Urquhart, both experienced mountaineers and members of their local mountain rescue team, this family-run business represents everything that's been lost in today's mass-produced outdoor industry: attention to detail, personal service, and genuine expertise born from real-world experience.

What sets Cioch apart isn't just their location—though there's something poetic about waterproof gear being made in one of Scotland's rainiest regions—but their unwavering commitment to handmade quality. Every jacket, every pair of trousers, every piece of gear that leaves their Struan workshop has been individually crafted by skilled hands, not mass-produced on industrial assembly lines. This approach has earned them contracts with three separate mountain rescue teams, who've relied on the same Cioch gear for over eleven years—a testament to durability that would make any CFO nervous but perfectly demonstrates the company's philosophy.

The company's name itself reflects their Scottish heritage—"cioch" referring to the distinctive rocky peaks that dominate Skye's landscape. Just as these ancient formations have weathered centuries of Atlantic storms, cioch outdoor clothing is designed to withstand whatever nature throws at you. But unlike the static mountains, Cioch continues to evolve, incorporating the latest fabric technologies while maintaining their traditional craftsmanship approach.

During my recent visit to their workshop, I was struck by the contrast between their modest storefront and the sophisticated operation below. The manufacturing area buzzes with activity as skilled seamstresses work on individual orders, each piece receiving the kind of attention typically reserved for bespoke tailoring. It's here that you'll find garments being modified with reinforced knees for a Highland piper, or custom-fitted salopettes for a particularly challenging Alpine expedition. This level of personalization simply isn't possible with mass-market outdoor brands, making Cioch a unique proposition in today's standardized market.

Advanced Fabric Technology and Construction

The heart of any quality outdoor garment lies in its fabric technology, and this is where cioch outdoor clothing truly excels. Rather than developing proprietary systems that might compromise on performance, Cioch has wisely chosen to partner with proven fabric innovations, primarily utilizing the revolutionary Nikwax Analogy system that has made Paramo famous worldwide. This decision reflects their practical, no-nonsense approach to gear design—why reinvent the wheel when you can perfect the application?

The Nikwax Analogy system represents a fundamental shift in how we think about waterproof breathable fabrics. Unlike traditional membrane-based systems that rely on microscopic pores to manage moisture transfer, Analogy uses a unique pumping action created by directional fiber construction. When you move, the fabric actively transports moisture away from your body, creating a remarkably effective microclimate management system. I've tested this technology extensively during multi-day treks in the Scottish Highlands, and the difference is immediately noticeable—no more of that clammy, wet-from-within feeling that plagues many waterproof jackets.

What makes Cioch's application of this technology special is their understanding of how different weights and constructions affect performance in specific conditions. They work with multiple fabric weights, from lightweight options perfect for fast-moving activities to heavyweight constructions designed for prolonged exposure to harsh conditions. Helen Urquhart explained to me that they've developed detailed specifications for different use cases—a mountaineer heading to the Himalayas receives very different fabric recommendations than someone seeking everyday weather protection for coastal walks.

The construction quality at Cioch goes far beyond fabric selection. Their seam sealing process is meticulous—every seam is carefully taped using high-quality sealing tape, then inspected under magnification to ensure complete coverage. Stress points receive additional reinforcement, and critical areas like pocket attachments and zipper installations are backed with secondary fabric layers. This attention to detail extends to the smallest components: YKK Aquaguard zippers, reinforced stress points, and carefully engineered ventilation systems that actually work in real-world conditions.

Made-to-Measure Process and Benefits

The made-to-measure service offered by cioch outdoor clothing represents perhaps their greatest competitive advantage and the feature that most clearly differentiates them from mass-market alternatives. The process begins with a detailed consultation, either in person at their Skye workshop or via their comprehensive measurement forms and telephone discussions. Helen Urquhart personally handles many of these consultations, drawing on decades of mountaineering experience to understand not just your measurements, but how you move, what activities you'll be pursuing, and what specific challenges you face with off-the-shelf garments. This level of personal attention is increasingly rare in today's retail environment, but it's absolutely essential for creating truly functional outdoor gear.

During my own fitting process, I was impressed by the thoroughness of their approach. Beyond standard measurements, they consider factors like your typical layering system, whether you prefer a close-fitting or looser cut, your arm reach requirements, and even details like whether you typically wear gloves or mittens (which affects cuff design). They also discuss the specific conditions you'll face—gear designed for Scottish winter mountaineering requires different considerations than equipment for Alpine summer climbing or general hiking.

The benefits of this approach become immediately apparent when you wear the finished garment. The fit is simply superior to anything available off-the-shelf, not just in terms of comfort but in functional performance. A properly fitted waterproof jacket moves with you rather than against you, doesn't ride up when you raise your arms overhead, and doesn't gap at the waist when you lean forward. For those of us who spend serious time in challenging outdoor conditions, these details make the difference between gear that enhances your experience and gear that merely doesn't impede it.
