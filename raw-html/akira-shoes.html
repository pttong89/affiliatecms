The AKIRA Brand Story & Heritage

When Eric Hsueh, Erikka Wang, Sarah Hughes, and Gordon Liao opened the first AKIRA boutique in Chicago's trendy Bucktown/Wicker Park neighborhood in 2002, they had a revolutionary vision: to bring Chicago's bold, authentic style to the forefront of American fashion. The name "AKIRA" itself tells a story - it's "Erikka" spelled backward with a creative twist, embodying the brand's innovative approach to reimagining fashion norms.

What started as a single 800-square-foot boutique has evolved into a fashion empire spanning over 30 stores across 12 states, proving that Midwest fashion deserves its place on the national stage.

The Chicago Fashion Revolution

AKIRA shoes represent more than just footwear - they're a statement of Chicago's unique fashion identity. While East and West Coast brands dominated the fashion landscape for decades, AKIRA challenged this paradigm by proving that bold, trend-forward design could emerge from the heartland. Their philosophy centers on "original thinking and emphasis on strong design and innovation," which is evident in every pair of AKIRA shoes they produce.

The brand's commitment to Chicago runs deep. From their headquarters at 200 N Fairfield Ave to their rigorous selection process for employees (typically art school graduates or fashion program students), AKIRA maintains its authentic Chicago roots while expanding globally. This dedication to place and authenticity resonates in their shoe designs, which blend urban edge with accessible luxury.

The Azalea Wang Partnership

One of the most significant developments in AKIRA's footwear line is their collaboration with designer Azalea Wang. Founded by Erikka Wang (yes, one of AKIRA's co-founders), Azalea Wang represents the perfect synergy of AKIRA's bold aesthetic with specialized footwear expertise. This Asian-American, female-founded brand focuses on "designing for women by women," creating shoes that combine trend-forward designs with quality construction and extended sizing options.

Ultimate Buying Guide & Size Tips

Size Guide & Fit Recommendations

Based on my extensive experience with AKIRA shoes and conversations with other customers, sizing tends to run slightly small, particularly in their boot collections. I consistently recommend going up half a size from your normal shoe size, especially for ankle boots and platform styles that may have less stretch in the upper.

For their Azalea Wang collaborations, I've found the sizing to be more consistent and true to size. However, if you have wider feet, definitely size up as these styles tend to run narrow. Their thigh-high and knee-high boots offer the most accommodation for different calf sizes, particularly their "4-Way Stretch" collection which I've personally tested on various leg shapes.

Care & Maintenance Tips

Proper care significantly extends the life of AKIRA shoes, especially given their bold materials and embellishments. For metallic and patent leather styles, I use a soft microfiber cloth after each wear to prevent scuffing and maintain shine. Rhinestone and crystal embellishments require gentle cleaning with a soft brush to prevent stones from loosening.

Storage is crucial for maintaining the shape of platform shoes and boots. I use shoe trees for my more expensive pairs and stuff tissue paper in boots to prevent creasing. For suede styles, I always apply a protective spray before the first wear and reapply seasonally. This small investment in care has allowed me to keep AKIRA shoes looking new for years longer than expected.
