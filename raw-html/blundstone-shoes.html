The Complete Blundstone Sizing Guide

Understanding Australian Sizing

One of the most confusing aspects of buying Blundstone shoes is navigating their Australian sizing system. Unlike US brands, Blundstone uses Australian/UK sizing, which runs differently than American standards. After fitting hundreds of customers and testing multiple sizes personally, I've developed a reliable conversion approach.

For most people, I recommend going down half a size from your typical US athletic shoe size. For example, if you wear a US size 9 in Nike or Adidas, start with an Australian size 8.5 in Blundstone. However, this isn't universal ‚Äì people with narrow feet may need to go down a full size, while those with wide feet might match their US size exactly.

The Fitting Process I Recommend

The best time to measure your feet for Blundstone Chelsea boots is in the afternoon when your feet are naturally larger. I always measure both feet and size for the larger one. The boots should feel snug but not tight when you first try them on, with about a thumb's width of space in the toe box.

Pay attention to the elastic side panels ‚Äì they should feel secure without being restrictive. The boot should hug your foot around the heel and midfoot while allowing your toes to move freely. Remember that Blundstone leather will stretch and conform to your foot over the first few weeks of wear, so a slightly snug initial fit is ideal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do Blundstone shoes fit compared to regular sneakers?

Blundstone shoes typically run about half a size larger than regular sneakers. I recommend going down half a size from your usual athletic shoe size. The boots have a roomy toe box and generous width, which is perfect for all-day comfort but means sizing down is usually necessary. Always measure your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally larger, and consider the thickness of socks you'll wear most often.

Are Blundstone shoes waterproof?

Most standard Blundstone shoes are water-resistant but not fully waterproof. They can handle light rain and puddles well, but extended exposure to water or deep puddles will eventually seep through. However, Blundstone does make specific waterproof models with special treatments and sealing. For the best water protection, look for models specifically labeled as waterproof or consider applying a leather protectant treatment.

What's the difference between Blundstone 500 and 550 models?

The main differences are in comfort features and construction. The 550 series (Classics) have leather lining, additional comfort features like removable footbeds, and slightly better materials. The 500 series (Originals) have synthetic lining and fewer comfort features but are more affordable. Both share the same iconic silhouette and durability, but the 550s offer superior long-term comfort for daily wear.

How long do Blundstone shoes typically last?

With regular wear, Blundstone shoes typically last 2-5 years depending on usage and care. I've personally worn pairs for over 3 years with daily use. The leather uppers are very durable, and the soles can often be resoled by a cobbler when they wear down. Proper care including regular conditioning and rotation with other shoes can significantly extend their lifespan. Many users report getting 5+ years from their pairs.

Can you wear Blundstone shoes in snow and ice?

Standard Blundstone shoes have limited traction on snow and ice due to their smooth rubber soles. While they're great for dry winter conditions and light snow, I don't recommend them for icy conditions without additional traction aids. For serious winter conditions, consider the Thermal or All-Terrain models which have specialized tread patterns and insulation. Always exercise caution on slippery surfaces regardless of your footwear choice.
