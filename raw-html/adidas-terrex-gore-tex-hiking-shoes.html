How should I care for my Adidas Terrex Gore-Tex hiking shoes?

Proper care extends the life and performance of your Adidas Terrex Gore-Tex hiking shoes significantly. After each hike, remove dirt and debris with a soft brush and lukewarm water, avoiding harsh scrubbing that could damage the Gore-Tex membrane. Allow them to air dry naturally away from direct heat sources, which can compromise the waterproof membrane and cause materials to crack. Use a DWR (Durable Water Repellent) treatment periodically to restore water repellency on the outer fabric. Store them in a cool, dry place with good ventilation to prevent mold and material degradation. For deeper cleaning, use specialized footwear cleaners rather than harsh detergents. Replace the insoles when they show wear, and consider using quality hiking socks to reduce friction and moisture buildup inside the shoes.

What size should I choose for Adidas Terrex Gore-Tex hiking shoes?

Adidas Terrex Gore-Tex hiking shoes generally run true to size, but I recommend going up half a size from your regular athletic shoe size to accommodate hiking socks and foot swelling during long hikes. The Gore-Tex lining can make the interior feel slightly snugger initially, and proper hiking socks add bulk that requires extra room. Try them on in the afternoon when your feet are naturally more swollen, and wear the same type of socks you'll use for hiking. Ensure there's about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the shoe's front to prevent black toenails during descents. The Free Hiker models have a more sock-like fit and may feel tighter initially but adapt to your foot shape over time, while AX4 models offer a more traditional fit that's immediately comfortable.
