Sizing and Fit Guide: Getting It Right the First Time

Sizing the Blundstone 510 correctly is crucial for optimal comfort and performance, and I learned this lesson through trial and error during my first purchase. Blundstone uses Australian/UK sizing, which can confuse American buyers accustomed to US sizing standards. The general rule is to subtract one full size from your US men's size to get your Blundstone size. If you wear a US men's 10, you'd typically order a size 9 in Blundstone. However, this is where personal experience becomes invaluable – I actually found that going down only half a size provided the perfect fit for my slightly wider feet. When I first tried on the Blundstone 510, I made the mistake of ordering my exact converted size. The boots felt snug across the instep and toe box, creating pressure points that became uncomfortable during longer hikes. After consulting with a ranger in Glacier National Park who's worn Blundstones for over a decade, I learned that going up half a size for wider feet is not just acceptable – it's recommended. The proper fit should provide about a quarter-inch of space between your longest toe and the front of the boot when standing. There should also be slight give in the heel area – your heel shouldn't slip, but you should be able to slide your finger down behind it with some resistance. The boot should feel snug across the instep initially, as the premium leather will stretch and conform to your foot shape over time.
Important Note: Blundstone boots will stretch slightly over the instep but never lengthwise. If the boot feels too short initially, going up a half size is your only option – the length will not increase with wear.
Width considerations are particularly important for the Blundstone 510. While marketed as unisex, the boot has a relatively narrow profile that works well for average to slightly narrow feet. If you have wide feet or high arches, I strongly recommend trying them on in person if possible, or ordering from a retailer with generous return policies. For those with specific fit challenges, I've discovered several tricks during my extensive testing. If you have high arches, removing the standard footbed and using a thinner aftermarket insole can provide additional toe room. Conversely, if you find the boot slightly too roomy after break-in, the included extra footbed layers can fine-tune the fit. Temperature considerations also affect fit. I've noticed that the Blundstone 510 feels slightly tighter in cold conditions and more relaxed in warm weather. When hiking in varying climates, I adjust my sock thickness accordingly – thinner merino wool socks for summer desert hiking and thicker cushioned socks for winter mountain adventures. For women ordering the unisex Blundstone 510, the conversion from US women's sizing typically involves subtracting 2.5 sizes. A US women's 8.5 would translate to approximately a size 6 in Blundstone sizing. However, I always recommend checking the official sizing chart and considering foot width when making the final decision.
