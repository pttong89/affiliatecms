Are Magellan 800G boots truly waterproof?

How warm are 800G insulated boots in cold weather?

800G insulation provides excellent warmth for temperatures ranging from 20°F to -10°F when combined with proper wool socks and appropriate layering. The Cosmo Therm insulation used in Magellan boots offers superior heat retention while remaining breathable during moderate activity. However, individual cold tolerance varies significantly, and factors like circulation, sock selection, and activity level dramatically impact warmth perception. During my extensive field testing, I found these boots maintain comfortable foot temperature during stationary activities like tree stand hunting for up to six hours in single-digit temperatures. Active hunting in temperatures above 40°F often results in overheating and moisture buildup, reducing insulation effectiveness.

What's the difference between Magellan 400G and 800G boots?

The primary difference is insulation thickness and corresponding temperature ratings. 800G boots provide twice the insulation of 400G models, making them suitable for more extreme cold conditions. 800G boots excel for stationary activities like tree stand hunting in temperatures below 30°F, while 400G boots work better for active hunting in moderate temperatures ranging from 20°F to 50°F. The additional insulation in 800G models results in increased weight (approximately 0.5-1 pound per pair) and reduced breathability. Price differences are typically minimal ($10-20), making the choice primarily about intended use rather than budget. Most hunters find 400G sufficient for general use, while 800G becomes essential for late-season hunting in northern climates or extended stationary periods in cold conditions.
