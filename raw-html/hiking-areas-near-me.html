What should I do if I encounter wildlife while hiking?

Wildlife encounter protocols vary by species and region, but general principles include maintaining respectful distance, avoiding direct feeding, and understanding appropriate response behaviors. Make noise while hiking to avoid surprising animals, especially in areas with limited visibility. If you encounter large mammals, remain calm, avoid direct eye contact, back away slowly, and make yourself appear larger by raising your arms or jacket. Never run unless specifically advised for certain species in your area. Carry bear spray in regions with dangerous wildlife and understand proper usage techniques. Small animals like snakes typically flee when given space and escape routes. I research common wildlife in new hiking areas and understand specific safety protocols for potentially dangerous species. Report aggressive wildlife behavior to park authorities to protect other hikers and ensure proper management response.
