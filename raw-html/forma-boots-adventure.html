Leather Conditioning and Protection

Full-grain leather requires regular conditioning to maintain flexibility and weather resistance. I condition my boots every 2-3 months during riding season, or after particularly harsh conditions. Use leather conditioners specifically designed for performance footwear – avoid heavy oils that can clog pores and reduce breathability.

The conditioning process involves cleaning the boots thoroughly, allowing them to dry naturally (never use direct heat), and applying conditioner with a soft cloth. Work the conditioner into all leather surfaces, paying special attention to creases and flex points where cracking typically begins.

Storage and Seasonal Care

Proper storage prevents premature deterioration. Always store forma boots adventure in a cool, dry location with good air circulation. Avoid storage bags or confined spaces where moisture can accumulate. I use boot trees to maintain shape during extended storage periods.

For seasonal storage, clean and condition the boots thoroughly, then store them stuffed with newspaper to absorb residual moisture. Check them monthly and replace the newspaper if it becomes damp. This simple protocol prevents mold, mildew, and leather degradation.
