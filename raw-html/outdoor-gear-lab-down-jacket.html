What's the difference between down and synthetic insulated jackets according to Outdoor Gear Lab?

According to Outdoor Gear Lab's extensive testing, down offers superior warmth-to-weight ratio and compressibility, making it ideal for backpacking and activities where pack space and weight matter. However, down loses insulation properties when wet and takes longer to dry. Synthetic insulation retains warmth when wet, dries faster, and costs less, but is heavier and less compressible than down. Their testing shows synthetic jackets work better for high-output activities and wet climates, while down excels for dry conditions and situations where weight and packability are priorities. Many of their top picks use treated down to bridge this gap.

Are expensive down jackets worth the investment based on Outdoor Gear Lab's testing?

Outdoor Gear Lab's testing shows that while expensive jackets often offer premium materials, higher fill power down, and better construction details, mid-range options frequently provide excellent performance for most users. Their testing reveals that jackets like the REI Co-op Magma 850 ($279) and budget picks like the Decathlon MT100 ($90) can outperform jackets costing twice as much in specific categories. The investment in premium jackets becomes worthwhile for specialized use cases - extreme conditions, professional guiding, or users who prioritize specific features like ultralight weight or maximum warmth. For casual outdoor enthusiasts, their testing suggests mid-range options offer the best value.
