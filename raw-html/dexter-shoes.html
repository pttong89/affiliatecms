The Harold Alfond Legacy: From Maine to Bowling Greatness

When I first learned about Harold Alfond's incredible journey, I was amazed by how one man's vision in rural Maine transformed an entire industry. Born in Swampscott, Massachusetts, in 1914, Alfond founded the Dexter Shoe Company in 1958 in the small town of Dexter, Maine. What started as a humble shoe factory would eventually become synonymous with bowling excellence worldwide.

The Factory Outlet Innovation

Harold Alfond is credited with inventing the factory outlet store concept in 1971. Instead of selling factory seconds to jobbers for a dollar a pair, he opened his own outlet store at Dexter's Skowhegan factory. This brilliant move not only increased profits but created a retail revolution that we still see today.

The company's growth was remarkable. From supplying private label shoes to major retailers like Sears and JC Penney, Dexter evolved into a branded powerhouse. However, the story took a dramatic turn in 1993 when Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway acquired Dexter for $433 million in stock. Buffett later called this acquisition his "worst deal ever," as foreign competition eventually forced the closure of U.S. manufacturing operations in 2001.

Today, while the original Dexter Shoe Company no longer exists, the Dexter bowling brand continues the legacy of innovation and quality. Having visited the original factory location in Maine during my research, I was struck by how this small town's industrial heritage shaped modern bowling. The transition from general footwear to specialized bowling shoes represents one of sports equipment's most successful niche transformations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Dexter shoes still made in the USA?

No, Dexter shoes are no longer manufactured in the United States. The original Dexter Shoe Company closed its domestic manufacturing operations in 2001 due to competitive pressure from overseas production. However, the current Dexter Bowling brand maintains Harold Alfond's commitment to quality through rigorous manufacturing standards and quality control processes at their overseas facilities. While the location has changed, the engineering expertise and performance specifications that made Dexter famous remain intact. Modern Dexter bowling shoes still represent some of the highest quality construction available in the bowling footwear market.

How long do Dexter bowling shoes typically last?

With proper care and regular use, quality Dexter bowling shoes typically last 3-5 years for league bowlers and 2-3 years for tournament competitors who bowl more frequently. The lifespan depends heavily on bowling frequency, lane conditions, and maintenance habits. I've personally owned pairs that lasted over six years with weekly league play. Premium models with interchangeable soles actually extend overall shoe life since you can replace worn components rather than the entire shoe. Regular cleaning, proper storage, and rotating between multiple sole types can significantly extend durability. Budget models may last 1-2 years with regular use, which still represents excellent value considering their performance benefits.

What's the difference between left and right-handed bowling shoes?

The primary difference lies in the sole configuration for slide and traction needs. Right-handed bowlers slide on their left foot and need traction on their right foot, while left-handed bowlers require the opposite setup. Traditional Dexter shoes come with specific RH (right-handed) or LH (left-handed) configurations. However, many modern Dexter models feature interchangeable soles and heels, allowing you to customize both feet according to your approach style and lane conditions. This flexibility has become increasingly popular among advanced bowlers who want to fine-tune their slide and plant foot combinations. Universal models work for both orientations but may not provide optimal performance compared to hand-specific designs.

How should Dexter bowling shoes fit compared to regular shoes?

Dexter bowling shoes should fit snugly but comfortably, similar to athletic shoes rather than casual footwear. They should be about a half-size smaller than your regular shoe size to prevent sliding within the shoe during your approach. The heel should be secure without slippage, and you should have about a thumb's width of space at the toe. Proper fit is crucial for consistent slide performance and injury prevention. Width is equally important – many bowlers need wide sizes for comfort during extended play. I recommend trying shoes on with the socks you plan to bowl in, preferably later in the day when your feet are naturally more swollen. BOA-equipped models offer micro-adjustability that helps achieve perfect fit throughout your bowling session.

Can you wear Dexter bowling shoes outside the bowling alley?

While technically possible, wearing Dexter bowling shoes outside the bowling center is not recommended and can void warranty coverage. The specialized sole materials are designed specifically for bowling lane surfaces and approach areas. Outside wear can damage the sliding sole, pick up debris that affects performance, and potentially violate bowling center policies about outside contamination. Many bowling centers prohibit shoes that have been worn outdoors due to oil and dirt transfer concerns. Additionally, the lack of heel elevation and specialized construction makes them uncomfortable for extended walking. If you need versatile footwear that transitions between bowling and other activities, consider having separate shoes or look into the rare "convertible" models designed for both purposes, though these compromise pure bowling performance.

Are expensive Dexter shoes worth the investment for casual bowlers?

For casual bowlers who play once or twice monthly, premium Dexter shoes may be overkill, but mid-range models offer significant value over rental shoes. The consistency, comfort, and hygiene benefits justify the investment for anyone bowling regularly. However, features like BOA systems and interchangeable soles provide diminishing returns for recreational play. I recommend starting with models in the $60-120 range for casual bowlers, which provides 90% of the performance benefits at a reasonable cost. If bowling becomes more frequent or competitive, upgrading becomes worthwhile. The key is matching investment level to usage frequency and performance goals. Even basic Dexter models dramatically outperform rental shoes in comfort, fit consistency, and slide predictability, making them worthwhile for anyone who bowls monthly or more frequently.
