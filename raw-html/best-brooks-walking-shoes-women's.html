Should I size up for Brooks walking shoes?

Brooks shoes generally run true to size, but proper fitting requires considering several factors beyond simple length measurement. I recommend having your feet measured in the afternoon when they're naturally more swollen, simulating conditions during long walks. Most walkers need about a thumb's width of space between their longest toe and shoe front to prevent black toenails and hot spots during extended walking. Sock thickness also matters â€“ thicker hiking or cushioned walking socks require slightly more room. Brooks offers multiple width options, which is often more important than sizing up for proper fit. If you're between sizes, consider your intended use: size up slightly for long-distance walking or if you wear thick socks, but standard sizing works well for daily walking activities.

What's the difference between Ghost and Adrenaline models?

The primary difference between Ghost and Adrenaline models lies in their stability features and target users. Ghost models are neutral shoes designed for walkers with normal foot mechanics and gait patterns, offering balanced cushioning without motion control elements. Adrenaline models are stability shoes featuring Brooks' GuideRails technology, designed for walkers who overpronate or need additional support. The Adrenaline series includes firmer midsole materials and structured support elements to guide foot motion and prevent excessive inward rolling. From a comfort perspective, Ghost models typically feel softer and more flexible, while Adrenaline models provide a more structured, supportive feel. Both use similar cushioning technologies like DNA Loft, but the Adrenaline applies them within a stability framework. Choose Ghost for neutral gait patterns and maximum comfort, or Adrenaline if you need stability support or have been recommended motion control footwear.
