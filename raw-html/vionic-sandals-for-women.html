How long does it take to break in Vionic sandals, and will they feel comfortable immediately?

Most users, myself included, experience an adjustment period of 7-14 days. The arch support can feel pronounced initially, especially if you're accustomed to flat sandals. I recommend starting with 2-3 hours of wear for the first week, gradually increasing duration. By day 10, the support feels natural and comfortable. Some people with very flat feet may need up to three weeks to fully adapt. The key is patience – your feet are literally learning proper alignment. If you experience sharp pain rather than mild adjustment discomfort, consult the sizing guide or consider a different model.

Can I wear Vionic sandals if I already have custom orthotics from my podiatrist?

This requires careful consideration and ideally a conversation with your podiatrist. Vionic sandals have built-in arch support that may conflict with custom orthotics, potentially creating too much correction. In my experience, many people find Vionic's built-in support eliminates their need for separate orthotics for casual wear. However, those with severe foot deformities or specific medical conditions should stick with their prescribed custom orthotics. Some Vionic models have removable footbeds that might accommodate thin custom orthotics, but this varies by style. Always prioritize your podiatrist's recommendations for serious foot conditions.

Which Vionic sandal model is best for plantar fasciitis and heel pain relief?

The Walk Max Wanderer offers maximum support with its deeper heel cup and adjustable straps, making it ideal for active individuals. Do Vionic sandals run true to size, and how do I choose the right fit?

Generally yes, but there are model-specific variations. Toe-post styles like the Tide II may feel different initially due to the thong placement. I recommend ordering your normal size first, but keep Amazon's return policy in mind for adjustments. For wide feet, look for adjustable strap models like the Walk Max Wanderer. The arch support can make sandals feel snugger initially, so don't panic if they seem tight at first. Your feet will adapt to the proper positioning within the first week. If you're between sizes, I typically recommend sizing up, especially for hiking models where foot swelling is common. The deep heel cup should cradle your heel completely without overflow.

Are Vionic sandals suitable for hiking and outdoor activities?
