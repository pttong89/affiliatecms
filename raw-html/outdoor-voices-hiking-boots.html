The Outdoor Voices x Merrell Collaboration Story

The iconic Outdoor Voices x Merrell MOAB 2 in Taro colorway

In 2019, something revolutionary happened in the hiking world. Outdoor voices hiking boots weren't just another technical collaboration ‚Äì they represented a fundamental shift in how we think about outdoor footwear. The Austin-based lifestyle brand Outdoor Voices partnered with hiking giant Merrell to create what would become some of the most coveted boots on the trail.

The collaboration centered around Merrell's legendary MOAB 2 Mid Waterproof platform, but with Outdoor Voices' signature aesthetic twist. Instead of traditional earth tones, we got eye-catching colorways like soft lavender paired with deep forest green ‚Äì colors that made hikers do double-takes on the trail. This wasn't just about making pretty boots; it was about proving that outdoor boots could be both technically superior and Instagram-worthy.

What made this collaboration special was the attention to sustainability. The partnership incorporated recycled materials throughout the construction, from recycled wool and mesh to eco-friendly laces and rubber components. This environmental consciousness aligned perfectly with both brands' values and the growing demand for responsible outdoor gear.

Pro Tip from the Trail

During my research, I discovered that the collaboration came about when Outdoor Voices founder Tyler Haney was looking for hiking boots that didn't scream "serious mountaineer." She wanted something that could transition from trail to street, and Merrell's MOAB platform provided the perfect technical foundation for this vision.

The boots launched to immediate acclaim, with fashion publications like Glamour praising them as "hiking boots for women that are actually stylish." The limited release created a cult following, and even though the collaboration is no longer in production, these boots continue to command premium prices on secondhand markets. Understanding why these best outdoor boots were so successful helps us appreciate what makes great hiking footwear in today's market.

Features and Technical Specifications

Waterproof Protection

The outdoor voices hiking boots featured Merrell's M Select DRY waterproof membrane, providing reliable protection against wet conditions while maintaining breathability. This technology seals out water while allowing internal moisture to escape.

Lightweight Construction

Despite their mid-cut profile, these boots maintained a surprisingly light feel on foot. The combination of performance suede leather and mesh upper materials kept weight down without sacrificing durability or support.

Let me break down the key technical specifications that made these boots special. The foundation was Merrell's time-tested MOAB 2 Mid platform, which stands for "Mother Of All Boots" ‚Äì a testament to its popularity and versatility. But Outdoor Voices elevated this platform with thoughtful design choices and premium materials.

The midsole featured an EVA contoured footbed with zonal arch and heel support, providing comfort during long trail days. What set these apart from standard MOAB boots was the closed-cell foam tongue that prevented debris entry while adding comfort. The protective rubber toe cap extended durability in high-wear areas, crucial for rocky terrain encounters.

From a fit perspective, these outdoor hiking boots ran true to Merrell sizing, which tends to be slightly generous compared to athletic shoe sizing. The mid-cut design provided ankle support without restricting movement, making them ideal for day hikes and light backpacking trips. The lacing system allowed for customizable fit adjustment, accommodating different foot shapes and sock thickness preferences.

My Experience Testing These Boots

Testing the boots on varied terrain revealed their true capabilities

I had the opportunity to test a pair of outdoor voices hiking boots during a three-week hiking trip through Colorado's Front Range last fall. My friend Sarah, a dedicated Outdoor Voices fan, had managed to snag a pair during the original release and graciously let me borrow them for extended trail testing.

Right out of the box, I was struck by the quality of materials and attention to detail. The soft lavender and green colorway was even more striking in person than in photos, and I immediately understood why these boots had gained such a following among fashion-conscious hikers. But the real test would be how they performed on Colorado's challenging terrain.

Trail Performance Analysis

During my first day on the trail near Boulder, I covered about 8 miles on mixed terrain including rocky sections, muddy creek crossings, and loose gravel descents. The boots immediately impressed me with their comfort ‚Äì there was virtually no break-in period required. The EVA midsole provided excellent cushioning on rocky terrain, and the ankle support was substantial without feeling restrictive.

The Vibram outsole performed admirably on both wet rocks near stream crossings and loose scree fields. I felt confident in the traction, though I did notice some slippage on wet wooden bridge surfaces ‚Äì a common issue with most hiking boot soles. The waterproof membrane kept my feet dry during several creek crossings and an unexpected afternoon thunderstorm.

Trail Reality Check

After 15 miles of hiking, I discovered a potential issue: the stylish light-colored materials showed dirt and scuff marks much more readily than traditional earth-toned boots. While this didn't affect performance, it's worth considering if you prefer gear that maintains a clean appearance.

What really stood out was the versatility. After hiking, I wore these boots around downtown Boulder and received multiple compliments. They transitioned seamlessly from trail to street, something that's rare with traditional outdoor boots. This dual-purpose functionality justified the higher price point and explained the dedicated following these boots developed.

Durability Observations

After three weeks of regular use covering approximately 150 miles, the boots showed remarkable durability. The upper materials maintained their structure despite encounters with sharp rocks and thorny vegetation. The sole showed minimal wear, suggesting these boots would easily handle 500+ miles of hiking ‚Äì impressive for boots in this price range.

One area where I did notice some wear was the heel counter, which showed scuffing from rocky terrain contact during descents. However, this was purely cosmetic and didn't affect the boot's performance or structural integrity. The laces remained in excellent condition, and the eyelets showed no signs of wear or loosening.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Outdoor Voices hiking boots still available?

The original Outdoor Voices x Merrell collaboration is no longer in production, but you can still find them through secondary markets like eBay, Poshmark, and local consignment shops. Prices typically range from $100-200 depending on condition and size availability. For new alternatives, the Merrell Moab 3 Mid Waterproof offers similar performance with updated materials and construction. Many outdoor enthusiasts also recommend exploring outdoor voices hiking boots alternatives from brands like Columbia, Keen, and Salomon that capture the same spirit of stylish outdoor footwear.

How do these boots fit compared to regular Merrell sizing?

Most users report that Outdoor Voices hiking boots fit true to standard Merrell sizing, though some find them slightly more snug than regular MOAB boots. Merrell typically runs about a half-size larger than athletic shoes, so if you wear size 8 in Nike, you'd likely want 7.5 in Merrell. The mid-cut design accommodates most foot shapes well, but those with wide feet should consider going up a half-size. The lacing system allows for good adjustability, making them compatible with different sock thicknesses for various weather conditions.

What makes these boots better than regular MOAB boots?

The Outdoor Voices collaboration elevated the standard MOAB platform with premium materials, unique colorways, and sustainable construction elements. Key improvements included recycled materials throughout the build, enhanced upper materials with better weather resistance, and refined styling that transitions from trail to street. The collaboration also featured improved quality control and attention to detail in manufacturing. While the core MOAB comfort and performance remain, these boots offered a more refined aesthetic and environmental consciousness that appealed to style-conscious outdoor enthusiasts.

Can you wear these boots for serious hiking and backpacking?

Absolutely! Despite their fashion-forward appearance, outdoor voices hiking boots are built on Merrell's proven MOAB platform, which is used by serious hikers worldwide. They're excellent for day hiking, weekend backpacking trips, and moderate terrain adventures. The waterproof membrane, Vibram outsole, and supportive midsole provide all the technical performance needed for most hiking activities. However, for technical mountaineering, multi-week expeditions, or extreme conditions, you might want more specialized boots with additional ankle support and durability features.

How do you clean and maintain these boots?

The light colorways require more frequent cleaning than traditional dark hiking boots, but proper maintenance is straightforward. Remove loose dirt with a soft brush, then use a mixture of mild soap and water for deeper cleaning. Avoid harsh detergents that can damage the waterproof membrane. For the suede areas, use a suede-specific cleaner and brush. Allow boots to air dry completely before storage, and consider using a waterproofing spray treatment every 20-30 miles of use to maintain weather resistance. The premium materials respond well to proper care and will maintain their appearance with regular attention.

What are the best current alternatives to Outdoor Voices boots?

For similar style and performance, consider the Merrell Moab 3 Mid Waterproof as the direct successor, offering updated technology and materials. Columbia's Newton Ridge Plus provides excellent value with comparable performance and more colorway options. Other great alternatives include the Keen Targhee III Mid for wider feet, Salomon X Ultra 4 Mid GTX for technical performance, and Hoka Anacapa 2 Mid GTX for maximum comfort. When shopping alternatives, look for mid-cut designs, waterproof membranes, quality leather/mesh uppers, and versatile styling that works both on-trail and in urban environments. Many of these options are available through Zasepy outdoor boots retailers.
