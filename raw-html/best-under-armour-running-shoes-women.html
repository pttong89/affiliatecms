Key Features to Consider

Cushioning Technology

HOVR provides energy return and impact absorption, while Charged foam offers responsive cushioning at a lower cost. Flow technology eliminates the need for a traditional outsole, reducing weight.

Drop (Heel-to-Toe Offset)

Most UA shoes feature an 8mm drop, suitable for heel strikers. Lower drop shoes (4-6mm) benefit midfoot strikers, while zero-drop suits forefoot strikers.

Upper Construction

Engineered mesh provides breathability and structure. Clone technology in newer models adapts to foot shape for a personalized fit.

Outsole Durability

Carbon rubber in high-wear areas extends shoe life. Blown rubber in the midfoot provides flexibility and comfort.

Understanding Under Armour Running Technologies

HOVR Technology

HOVR foam provides a unique combination of energy return and impact absorption. Unlike traditional EVA foam, HOVR maintains its properties over hundreds of miles, ensuring consistent performance throughout the shoe's lifespan. The foam is encased in a mesh web that provides structure while allowing the foam to expand and contract naturally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Under Armour running shoes good for wide feet?

Under Armour offers several models that work well for wide feet, particularly the Charged Assert series and HOVR Infinite models. These shoes feature more accommodating toe boxes and softer upper materials. However, some performance models like the Flow Velociti Elite run narrower and may not be suitable for very wide feet. I recommend trying on shoes in-store or ordering from retailers with good return policies if you have wide feet.

How long do Under Armour running shoes typically last?

Based on my testing and user feedback, Under Armour running shoes typically last 300-500 miles depending on the model, running surface, and individual gait. Budget models like the Charged Assert 10 often exceed expectations, lasting 350+ miles, while premium models with HOVR technology can reach 450-500 miles. The Flow technology shoes show exceptional durability due to their unique construction, often lasting longer than traditional rubber-outsole shoes.

What's the difference between HOVR and Flow technology?

HOVR is Under Armour's signature cushioning foam that provides energy return and impact absorption, wrapped in a mesh web for structure. Flow technology, on the other hand, is a revolutionary approach that eliminates the traditional rubber outsole, creating a one-piece midsole/outsole that's lighter and provides better ground connection. HOVR focuses on cushioning comfort, while Flow prioritizes lightweight performance and durability. Many runners prefer HOVR for daily training and Flow for racing or speed work.

Can I use Under Armour running shoes for walking and everyday wear?

Absolutely! Under Armour running shoes work excellently for walking and casual wear. Models like the Charged Assert 10 and HOVR Sonic series provide all-day comfort for people who are on their feet frequently. The durable construction means they'll hold up well to daily wear, and the cushioning technologies provide comfort for extended periods. Many healthcare workers and retail employees specifically choose UA running shoes for their work shifts due to the superior comfort and support.

Are Under Armour running shoes suitable for overpronators?

Under Armour doesn't make traditional motion control shoes, but several models work well for mild to moderate overpronators. The HOVR Sonic series offers good stability through its structured design, and the Charged Assert models provide a stable platform. The firmer cushioning characteristic of UA shoes actually benefits many overpronators who need more structure. However, severe overpronators may need dedicated stability shoes from other brands. I recommend getting a gait analysis if you're unsure about your pronation pattern.

What's the best Under Armour shoe for marathon training?

For marathon training, I recommend the HOVR Infinite 5 or the newer Infinite Elite for long runs due to their maximum cushioning and comfort. For tempo runs and speed work, the HOVR Sonic 7 provides the right balance of responsiveness and protection. Race day options include the Flow Velociti Elite for those who prefer a firm, responsive ride, though some runners may find it too minimal for the full 26.2 miles. Consider rotating between 2-3 different models during training to prevent overuse injuries and find your race day preference.
