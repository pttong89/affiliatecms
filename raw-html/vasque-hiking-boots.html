The Heritage of Vasque Hiking Boots

Founded in 1964 by Italian cobbler Domenico Brustia, vasque hiking boots have been synonymous with outdoor adventure for six decades. The brand emerged from a vision to bring European-style craftsmanship and innovative design to the American hiking boot market. From their humble beginnings in Minnesota, Vasque quickly established themselves as the go-to choice for serious backpackers and mountaineers.

During their golden years, particularly the 1980s and 1990s, Vasque hiking boots were largely manufactured in Italy using traditional construction methods and premium materials. The Vasque boots from this era, especially models like the legendary Sundowner, became icons in the hiking community. These boots featured full-grain leather construction, Vibram soles, and were built to last decades with proper care and resoling.

The brand philosophy centered around the belief that when we spend more time outside, we feel better inside. This ethos drove continuous innovation in boot design, leading to breakthrough technologies in waterproofing, breathability, and comfort. Vasque hiking shoes evolved from traditional heavy leather boots to incorporate modern materials while maintaining their reputation for durability and performance.

Unfortunately, the hiking industry witnessed significant changes in recent years. In October 2024, Red Wing Shoe Company, which had acquired Vasque, announced the difficult decision to discontinue the brand after 60 years of operation. The company cited dramatic market changes in consumer preferences and shopping habits as primary factors. While this marks the end of an era, the legacy of Vasque hiking boots continues through the thousands of pairs still providing reliable service on trails worldwide.

Complete Buying Guide

Sizing and Fit Considerations

Proper fitting is crucial when selecting vasque hiking boots. Most models run true to size, but individual foot shape significantly impacts comfort. The Vasque hiking boots men's models typically feature a medium width profile, while women's versions offer slightly different last shapes to accommodate anatomical differences.

Always try boots on in the afternoon when your feet are naturally swollen, similar to how they'll feel after hours of hiking. Wear the same type and thickness of socks you plan to hike in. There should be roughly a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the boot's front, allowing for natural foot expansion during long hikes and downhill sections.

Waterproofing Technologies

Vasque utilized several waterproofing technologies throughout their history. Gore-Tex remained the gold standard for most premium models, offering excellent waterproofing while maintaining breathability. The UltraDry technology found in some models provided a more budget-friendly waterproof option, though with slightly reduced breathability compared to Gore-Tex.

Construction Quality and Materials

The quality of Vasque shoes womens and men's models varied significantly depending on the manufacturing era. Italian-made boots from the 1980s-2000s featured superior leather quality and construction techniques passed down through generations of European bootmakers. Later Chinese-manufactured models, while still functional, generally showed reduced durability and craftsmanship quality.

Key construction features to evaluate include: full-grain leather versus split leather uppers, stitching quality around high-stress areas, sole attachment method (cemented versus welted), and overall material thickness. Premium models featured Norwegian welt construction, allowing for professional resoling and extending boot lifespan significantly.

Are Vasque hiking boots still being made?

Unfortunately, vasque hiking boots are no longer in production as of late 2024. Red Wing Shoe Company announced the discontinuation of the Vasque brand after 60 years in the outdoor footwear market. The decision was driven by significant market changes in consumer preferences and retail patterns. However, remaining inventory is still available through various retailers, often at heavily discounted prices. This presents an excellent opportunity for hikers to acquire quality boots at reduced costs, though future warranty support and repair services will be limited. If you find a pair in your size, especially proven models like the Breeze or St. Elias, they're still worth purchasing for their inherent quality and performance capabilities.

What are the best Vasque hiking boot models?

The most popular and highly-rated vasque hiking boots models include the Breeze series (renowned for immediate comfort and excellent breathability), St. Elias (heavy-duty construction for serious backpacking), Sundowner (classic leather boots with legendary durability), and Skywalk (lightweight versatility for various terrains). The Breeze models consistently received top ratings from outdoor gear reviewers and hiking publications like Backpacker Magazine. The St. Elias excelled in technical conditions and heavy load carrying, while the vintage Italian-made Sundowners became collector items among serious hikers. Each model served different hiking styles and preferences, from day hiking to extended backcountry expeditions. The key is matching the boot characteristics to your specific hiking needs and foot shape.

How do Vasque hiking boots fit?

Vasque hiking boots generally fit true to size with a medium width profile, though individual models varied in their specific last shapes. The Breeze series tended to run slightly narrow, particularly in the midfoot, while the St. Elias and Sundowner models offered more room in the toe box area. Most experienced hikers recommend trying them on with the same thickness of hiking socks you plan to use on the trail. It's generally advisable to size up half a size from your street shoe size to accommodate foot swelling during long hikes and to provide adequate room for thick hiking socks. The boots should feel snug but not tight, with roughly a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the boot's front. Pay special attention to heel slip and arch support, as proper fit in these areas prevents blisters and provides essential support during extended hiking sessions.

Focus on proven models with established track records like the Breeze or St. Elias if you find them in your size.
