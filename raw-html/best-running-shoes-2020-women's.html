How to Choose the Perfect Running Shoes for Your Needs

Selecting from the best running shoes 2020 women's options requires understanding your unique needs, foot type, and running style. After years of testing and fitting hundreds of runners, I've developed a systematic approach to help you find your perfect match.

Understanding Your Foot Type and Gait

The first step in choosing the right shoe is understanding your foot mechanics. Women typically have different foot proportions than men, with narrower heels and wider forefoot areas. This is why the 2020 models on our list were specifically designed with these differences in mind.

  • Neutral Pronation: Your foot lands evenly and rolls naturally inward. Best options: Nike Pegasus 37, Brooks Ghost 13
  • Overpronation: Your foot rolls excessively inward after landing. Best options: ASICS Kayano 27, Brooks Adrenaline GTS 20
  • Supination: Your foot doesn't roll inward enough. Best options: Cushioned neutral shoes with flexible midsoles

Key Features to Consider

Weight and Drop

Lighter shoes (under 8.5 oz) feel faster but offer less protection. Higher drops (10-12mm) help heel strikers, while lower drops (4-8mm) encourage midfoot landing.

Cushioning Level

Maximum cushioning (like UltraBoost 20) protects joints on hard surfaces, while minimal cushioning provides better ground feel and natural motion.

When choosing from the best running shoes 2020 women's selection, also consider your typical running surfaces, weekly mileage, and any specific comfort preferences. The beauty of 2020's lineup was the variety of options available to match every runner's unique needs.

What's the best way to break in 2020 running shoes?

Most 2020 models required minimal break-in compared to previous generations, but proper introduction is still important for optimal performance and injury prevention. Start with 2-3 short runs (20-30 minutes) to allow the materials to adapt to your feet. Gradually increase distance by 10-15% weekly while monitoring for any hot spots or discomfort. The advanced foam technologies in the best running shoes 2020 women's models typically reach optimal performance after 20-30 miles. Avoid wearing new shoes for long runs or races until you've completed at least 50 miles in them. If persistent discomfort occurs after this break-in period, consider consulting a running specialty store for gait analysis and alternative recommendations.
