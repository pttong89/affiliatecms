Frequently Asked Questions

Are Wills vegan hiking boots truly waterproof?

Yes, based on my extensive testing, the Wills vegan hiking boots feature lab-tested waterproof construction with a Spotex™ breathable membrane, sealed seams, and treated uppers. I've successfully tested them in streams up to 8 inches deep and during 12+ hours of continuous rainfall without any water penetration. The waterproof protection extends to the top of the tongue, making them reliable for wet conditions and stream crossings.

How do Wills vegan boots compare to traditional leather hiking boots?

In my 18 months of testing, Wills vegan hiking boots match or exceed traditional leather boots in waterproofing and breathability while offering superior ethical credentials. The Vibram soles provide excellent traction, though I found them slightly less grippy on clay surfaces compared to some leather alternatives. The main advantages are zero break-in period, consistent performance in wet conditions, and complete cruelty-free construction with traceable materials.

What's the durability like after extended use?

After 500+ miles across diverse terrain, my Wills vegan hiking boots show impressive durability. The ballistic welded construction means no sole separation, and the Lyliane 3DMX/Cordura uppers resist scuffs and abrasions effectively. The waterproofing remains fully intact, though I do apply monthly vegan waterproofing treatments. The main wear areas are the heel and toe caps, which show normal usage patterns for this mileage.

Do these boots run true to size?

Wills vegan hiking boots are sized slightly larger to accommodate thick hiking socks and natural foot swelling during long hikes. Will's recommends sizing up if you're between sizes. In my experience, this sizing works perfectly for day hikes with medium-weight socks, though those with narrow feet might need additional insoles for optimal fit. The toe box provides ample room without feeling sloppy.

Are they suitable for winter hiking conditions?

The standard WVSport boots handle light winter conditions well, but for serious cold weather, consider the Insulated Alpine Trail model with Primaloft insulation. I've used the regular Wills vegan hiking boots down to 15°F during active hiking with appropriate socks. For icy conditions, you'll need microspikes, as the Vibram soles don't provide traction on ice despite excellent performance on snow and wet surfaces.

What's the weight compared to other hiking boots?

At 1.2kg per pair (size 40), Wills vegan hiking boots fall into the mid-weight category. They're heavier than ultralight trail runners but comparable to other waterproof hiking boots with similar construction. The weight includes the robust Vibram sole, waterproof membrane, and durable synthetic materials. For day hiking and weekend backpacking, the weight penalty is worth it for the waterproof protection and durability.
