How do HOKA shoes fit compared to other brands like Nike or Adidas?

HOKA generally runs true to size length-wise but tends to be narrower than brands like New Balance or Asics. If you typically wear Nike, stick with the same size. However, runners with wider feet should consider going up a half size or look for HOKA's wide-width options. The toe box is roomier than most Nike models but more constrained than Altra or Topo. I always recommend trying them on in-store or ordering from retailers with generous return policies, especially for your first HOKA purchase.

How often should I replace my HOKA running shoes?

HOKA shoes typically last 400-500 miles with proper care and rotation, though this varies by model and running style. Road-focused models like the Clifton series tend toward the higher end of this range, while trail models may wear faster on abrasive surfaces. Monitor the midsole compression and outsole wear patterns rather than relying solely on mileage. Signs for replacement include visible midsole compression, uneven wear patterns, or decreased comfort during runs. Rotating between two pairs extends individual shoe life and maintains consistent performance. I personally track mileage using running apps and replace shoes proactively rather than reactively.
