My Personal Testing Experience

My Testing Background

As someone who's tested running shoes professionally for over 8 years and logged more than 15,000 miles across various brands and terrains, I approach Under Armour shoes with the same rigorous testing protocol I use for all footwear reviews. My testing includes road running, trail running, treadmill sessions, and daily wear to assess durability and comfort.

Season-by-Season Testing Results

During my year-long testing period, I rotated through five different Under Armour models across all four seasons. Spring training in the Pacific Northwest's wet conditions tested the shoes' traction and water resistance. Summer runs in 90°F+ temperatures challenged their breathability and comfort. Fall trail running sessions evaluated durability and grip on varied terrain, while winter treadmill and indoor track sessions focused on long-term comfort and support.

Hot Weather Performance

During summer testing in temperatures exceeding 85°F, the HOVR Sonic 6 demonstrated excellent breathability. The engineered mesh upper effectively managed moisture, while the lighter colorways reflected heat better than darker options. However, I noticed the HOVR foam became slightly softer in extreme heat, which some runners might appreciate for additional cushioning.

Cold Weather Durability

Winter testing revealed one of Under Armour's strongest advantages: cold weather performance. Unlike some competing foams that become rigid in freezing temperatures, UA's midsole materials maintained consistent feel and flexibility down to 20°F. This makes them excellent choices for year-round outdoor runners in colder climates.

Long-Distance Testing Insights

My most revealing test came during a 20-mile training run in the HOVR Infinite Elite. Initially skeptical about UA's cushioning for ultra-distance efforts, I was pleasantly surprised by the shoe's performance. The firm midsole provided consistent energy return without the "sinking" feeling that can occur with overly soft foams during long efforts.

Mile markers revealed interesting patterns: comfort remained consistent through miles 1-10, with slight firmness increase during miles 11-15 as my feet swelled. However, the wide base design prevented the lateral instability I've experienced in narrower shoes during long runs. By mile 20, foot fatigue was minimal compared to similar efforts in other brands.

Key Discovery

Sizing and Fit Considerations

Under Armour shoes generally run true to size, but their construction differs from other brands in important ways. The wider toe box and more substantial heel counter create a different fit profile that can benefit certain foot shapes while feeling restrictive to others.

Sizing Recommendations:

  • Narrow Feet: Consider going down half a size, as UA's wider construction may cause heel slippage
  • Wide Feet: Perfect fit at true size, often more comfortable than competitors
  • High Arches: The firmer midsole provides good arch support without excessive pressure
  • Flat Feet: Wide base and stable platform work well for overpronators

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Under Armour running shoes good for beginners?

Absolutely! Under Armour running shoes are excellent for beginners due to their forgiving construction, durable materials, and reasonable pricing. Models like the Charged Assert 10 provide reliable performance without overwhelming new runners with complex technologies. The stable platform helps beginners develop proper running form while the consistent cushioning reduces injury risk. Additionally, UA shoes' durability means beginners can focus on building mileage without worrying about premature shoe breakdown. The wide toe box accommodates foot swelling during longer runs, which is especially beneficial as new runners build endurance.

How long do Under Armour running shoes typically last?

Under Armour running shoes consistently deliver exceptional longevity, often lasting 500-600 miles compared to the industry standard of 300-400 miles. This extended lifespan stems from UA's focus on durable construction materials and robust outsole compounds. In my testing, the Charged Assert series maintained structural integrity and cushioning properties well beyond 500 miles. HOVR models tend to show slightly faster midsole compression but still outperform many competitors. The key is rotating between multiple pairs and monitoring tread wear patterns. Heavier runners may see reduced lifespan, but even then, UA shoes typically exceed expectations for durability in their price range.

Do Under Armour shoes run true to size?

Yes, Under Armour running shoes generally run true to size, but their fit characteristics differ from other brands. UA employs a wider last construction, meaning they accommodate broader feet better than narrow-fitting brands like Nike. Most runners can order their standard running shoe size, but those with particularly narrow feet might consider going down a half size to prevent heel slippage. The toe box is generous, which benefits runners whose feet swell during long runs. I recommend trying them on if possible, as the fit feels different initially but becomes very comfortable once broken in. The secure midfoot and heel construction provides excellent lockdown without pressure points.

What's the difference between HOVR and Charged cushioning?

HOVR and Charged represent Under Armour's two primary cushioning technologies, each serving different runner preferences. HOVR foam provides more energy return and responsiveness, making it ideal for tempo runs and competitive training. It offers better bounce-back properties and maintains its feel across temperature variations. Charged cushioning focuses on impact absorption and durability, delivering consistent comfort for daily training and high-mileage running. Charged foam feels firmer initially but provides reliable shock absorption throughout the shoe's lifespan. HOVR models typically cost $20-40 more than Charged versions but offer enhanced performance characteristics. Both technologies significantly outperform UA's earlier foam formulations and compete effectively with industry-standard cushioning systems.

Are Under Armour running shoes good for flat feet?

Under Armour running shoes work exceptionally well for runners with flat feet due to their stable platform design and firm midsole construction. The wide base provides excellent ground contact and prevents excessive inward rolling (overpronation) that flat-footed runners often experience. Models like the HOVR Sonic and Charged Assert series offer structured support without requiring separate motion control features. The firmer foam density helps maintain arch support throughout the gait cycle, while the durable heel counter prevents excessive rear-foot movement. Many flat-footed runners find UA shoes more comfortable than heavily cushioned alternatives that can feel unstable. However, severe overpronators might still benefit from custom orthotics, which fit well in most UA models due to their removable insole design.

Can I use Under Armour running shoes for cross-training?

While Under Armour running shoes excel for their intended purpose, they're not optimal for cross-training activities. Running shoes are designed for forward motion and may lack lateral stability needed for gym workouts, aerobics, or sports involving side-to-side movement. The elevated heel-to-toe drop can also interfere with proper lifting mechanics during strength training. However, for light gym work and casual fitness activities, models like the Charged Assert provide adequate support. For serious cross-training, consider UA's dedicated training shoes like the Under Armour training shoes line, which offers lower drops, wider bases, and enhanced lateral support designed specifically for multi-directional movement and varied workout demands.
