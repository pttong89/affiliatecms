KEEN Targhee 2 Overview: What Makes It Special

The KEEN Targhee 2 stands as one of the most iconic hiking boots in outdoor footwear history, with over 8 million pairs sold worldwide since its introduction. This mid-height waterproof hiking boot has earned its reputation through a combination of immediate out-of-the-box comfort, reliable waterproofing, and KEEN's signature wide toe box design.

What sets the KEEN Targhee 2 apart from competitors is its unique approach to hiking boot construction. Unlike traditional boots that require extensive break-in periods, the Targhee 2 delivers comfort from day one, making it an excellent choice for both seasoned hikers and newcomers to the trail. The nubuck leather upper provides durability while maintaining breathability, and the compression-molded EVA midsole offers excellent cushioning without excessive weight. Detailed Features and Performance Analysis

The KEEN Targhee 2 incorporates several key technologies that contribute to its outstanding performance across diverse conditions. Understanding these features helps explain why this boot has maintained its popularity despite increased competition in the hiking footwear market.

Waterproofing and Breathability

The KEEN.DRY waterproof membrane represents the core of the KEEN Targhee 2's weather protection system. This proprietary technology creates a barrier against external moisture while allowing internal vapor to escape. During extensive testing, I found the waterproofing to be exceptionally reliable. Stream crossings, wet grass traverses, and prolonged exposure to rain never resulted in wet feet.

However, breathability presents a more nuanced picture. While the KEEN Targhee 2 performs better than many fully waterproof boots, it cannot match the ventilation of non-waterproof hiking shoes. On hot summer days or during strenuous climbs, expect some moisture buildup inside the boot. This trade-off is inherent to most waterproof footwear and shouldn't be considered a specific flaw of the Targhee 2.

Traction and Outsole Performance

The 4mm multi-directional lug pattern on the KEEN Targhee 2 delivers impressive grip across various surfaces. The wide spacing between lugs prevents mud accumulation, while the rubber compound provides excellent purchase on wet rocks and loose soil. I've tested these boots on everything from granite slabs to muddy forest floors, and the traction consistently inspires confidence.

The one limitation I've encountered with the KEEN Targhee 2 traction system occurs on smooth, wet quartzite – a specific rock type that becomes incredibly slippery when wet. This isn't unique to KEEN boots, but it's worth noting for hikers in areas with abundant quartzite formations.

Comfort and Fit Characteristics

The comfort profile of the KEEN Targhee 2 stems from several design elements working in harmony. The removable dual-density EVA footbed provides excellent arch support and heel cushioning. The compression-molded EVA midsole offers responsive cushioning that doesn't pack out quickly over time.

KEEN's signature wide toe box remains the standout feature of the KEEN Targhee 2. Unlike traditional boot shapes that taper toward the toes, the Targhee 2 allows natural toe splay, reducing pressure points and improving comfort during long hikes. This design particularly benefits hikers with wider feet or those prone to toe issues.

KEEN Targhee 2 vs Targhee 3 vs Targhee 4 Comparison

Understanding the differences between KEEN's Targhee generations helps hikers choose the model that best fits their needs. Each iteration brings improvements while sometimes sacrificing other qualities, creating distinct advantages for different use cases.

Targhee 2: The Classic Foundation

The original KEEN Targhee 2 established the template that made this line famous. It features a slightly stiffer sole than newer models, providing excellent support on technical terrain. The traditional construction methods used in the Targhee 2 have proven remarkably durable, with many users reporting years of reliable service. The weight of 16.6 ounces per boot represents the heaviest of the three generations.

For hikers prioritizing maximum support and proven durability, the KEEN Targhee 2 remains an excellent choice. The established manufacturing processes and time-tested materials reduce the risk of quality control issues that sometimes affect newer models.

Targhee 3: The Refined Evolution

KEEN designed the Targhee 3 to address some limitations of the original while introducing modern improvements. At 15.4 ounces per boot, it's noticeably lighter than the Targhee 2. The outsole features improved flexibility, creating a more athletic feel that some hikers prefer.

However, the Targhee 3 has faced criticism for durability issues, particularly sole delamination problems that typically surface within 6-18 months of use. While KEEN has made efforts to address these concerns, many long-time users still prefer the proven reliability of the KEEN Targhee 2.

Targhee 4: The Latest Innovation

The newest Targhee 4 incorporates KEEN.FUSION technology, promising improved durability through advanced construction methods. Early reports suggest better build quality than the Targhee 3, though long-term durability data remains limited.

For hikers considering the entire Targhee lineup, I recommend the KEEN Targhee 2 for maximum durability and traditional support, the Targhee 3 for lighter weight and athletic feel, and the Targhee 4 for the latest technology with unproven long-term durability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do KEEN Targhee 2 boots fit compared to other hiking boots?

The KEEN Targhee 2 runs approximately a half-size small in length but offers significantly more width than most hiking boots. KEEN recommends ordering a half-size up from your normal shoe size. The toe box is notably wider than traditional boot shapes, providing extra room for toe movement and swelling during long hikes. This makes them excellent for hikers with wide feet or those who experience toe crowding in conventional boots. If you have narrow feet, you may find the Targhee 2 too spacious even with thicker socks.

Are KEEN Targhee 2 boots suitable for backpacking with heavy loads?

Yes, the KEEN Targhee 2 provides adequate support for backpacking with moderate to heavy loads up to 40-50 pounds. The S3 heel support system and dual-density EVA midsole offer good stability and shock absorption. However, for extended backpacking trips with very heavy loads or challenging terrain, you might want to consider a more robust boot with additional ankle support. The Targhee 2 excels in day hiking and light backpacking scenarios where comfort and versatility are priorities over maximum support.

How long do KEEN Targhee 2 boots typically last?

Longevity varies significantly between production periods and individual use patterns. Earlier KEEN Targhee 2 models (pre-2018) frequently lasted 600-1000 miles or more with proper care. Recent production models show more variable durability, with some users reporting sole separation or other failures within 300-500 miles. Factors affecting lifespan include terrain type, frequency of use, storage conditions, and maintenance practices. For maximum durability, choose models manufactured before 2020 if available, and consider waterproofing treatments to extend leather life.

Can I use KEEN Targhee 2 boots in winter conditions?

The KEEN Targhee 2 performs well in mild to moderate winter conditions but has limitations in extreme cold or deep snow. The waterproof membrane and insulating properties of the boot construction provide reasonable warmth down to approximately 20°F when paired with appropriate socks. However, the boot lacks dedicated insulation, making it unsuitable for prolonged exposure to temperatures below 15°F. For serious winter hiking, consider the insulated versions or pair with gaiters and warm socks. The traction is adequate on packed snow but requires microspikes or crampons for icy conditions.

What's the difference between KEEN Targhee 2 and Targhee II?

There is no difference – KEEN Targhee 2 and KEEN Targhee II refer to the same boot model. KEEN uses both naming conventions in their marketing and product listings, which creates confusion among consumers. Whether you see "Targhee 2," "Targhee II," or variations like "Targhee II" or "Targhee II Mid", these all reference the same second-generation model. The Roman numeral version (II) appears more commonly on official KEEN websites and packaging, while the Arabic numeral (2) is often used in retail listings and casual references.
