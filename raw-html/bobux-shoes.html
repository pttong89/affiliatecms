The Five Stages of Bobux Development

Soft Sole (Newborn-Early Crawling)

The Soft Sole stage is where my journey with Bobux began when my youngest was just 3 months old. These shoes are designed like little socks with leather soles, providing protection while maintaining the barefoot experience crucial for sensory development. The elastic opening makes them easy to put on squirmy babies, and the soft leather sole provides just enough protection for outdoor exploration while allowing feet to feel different textures.

Xplorer (Crawling-Cruising)

As my children transitioned to crawling and pulling themselves up, the Xplorer stage provided the perfect balance of protection and flexibility. These shoes feature slightly more structure than Soft Soles but maintain the flexibility needed for natural movement. The reinforced toe area protects little feet during crawling adventures, while the grip sole helps prevent slipping on smooth surfaces.

Step Up (First Steps)

The Step Up stage holds a special place in my heart as it was what my daughter wore when she took her first independent steps. These shoes provide more ankle support than previous stages while maintaining flexibility in the forefoot. The slightly raised heel helps with the heel-to-toe walking motion that children are learning to master during this crucial developmental period.

I-Walk (Confident Toddlers)

Once my children became confident walkers who could run and climb, we moved to the I-Walk stage. These shoes offer more substantial support and protection while maintaining the flexibility that makes Bobux special. The durable construction held up remarkably well to playground adventures, puddle jumping, and the general rough-and-tumble life of active toddlers.

Kid+ (Active Children)

The Kid+ stage serves children who need more structured support for sports and intensive activities. While we haven't reached this stage yet with our younger children, I've observed other parents whose kids love these shoes for their combination of athletic performance and everyday comfort. Complete Sizing and Fitting Guide

One of the most challenging aspects of buying children's shoes online is getting the sizing right, especially with a brand like Bobux that includes built-in growth room. Through trial and error with my own children, I've developed a reliable system for ensuring proper fit that I'm excited to share with you.

Measuring Your Child's Feet

The key to proper Bobux sizing is accurate foot measurement. I recommend measuring both feet (children often have slightly different sized feet) in the afternoon when feet are at their largest. Place a piece of paper against a wall, have your child stand with their heel against the wall, and trace around their toes. Measure from the wall to the longest toe tip—this gives you the foot length in millimeters.

Bobux sizing includes approximately 14mm of total space: 7mm for growth and 7mm for wiggle room. This means if your child measures at the beginning of a size range, they'll have maximum growth room. If they measure at the top of a range, consider sizing up unless you're planning to replace the shoes soon.

Fitting Tips from Experience

After fitting numerous pairs of Bobux shoes on my children, I've learned several practical tips. First, always check for adequate toe room by pressing gently on the shoe front while your child is standing—you should feel about a thumb's width of space. Second, ensure the heel doesn't slip when your child walks; a secure heel fit is crucial for proper gait development.

For younger children in Soft Sole and Xplorer stages, the shoes should feel snug but not tight around the midfoot. The elastic openings should be easy to stretch but return to grip the ankle securely. For older children in Step Up and I-Walk stages, pay attention to the fastening system—straps should be adjustable enough to accommodate socks of different thicknesses.
