Should I trust weight specifications from manufacturers or Outdoor Gear Lab measurements?

Always prioritize Outdoor Gear Lab's measured weights over manufacturer specifications. Manufacturers often use "minimum weight" or "trail weight" that excludes essential components like stakes, guy lines, or stuff sacks. During my testing, I've found discrepancies of 8-16 ounces between advertised and actual weights when including all necessary components. Outdoor Gear Lab consistently provides "packaged weight" that includes everything in the box, giving you a realistic expectation for your pack weight. They also separate "trail weight" (tent body, fly, poles, stakes) from total weight, helping you understand what components you might leave behind in specific situations. For ultralight backpacking where every ounce matters, these accurate measurements are crucial for proper trip planning and gear selection decisions.
