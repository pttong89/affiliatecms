My Experience with the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX

When I first laced up the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX for a challenging day hike in Colorado's Front Range, I wasn't prepared for how dramatically they would change my hiking experience. After six months and over 200 miles of testing across various terrains, these boots have proven themselves as exceptional performers in the speed hiking category.

My initial impression was the remarkable balance between support and agility. Unlike traditional heavy hiking boots that can feel clunky on technical terrain, the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX felt more like a high-top trail runner with serious protection. The first major test came during a 12-mile loop in Rocky Mountain National Park, where I encountered everything from loose scree to muddy creek crossings.

What immediately stood out was the boot's performance on wet rocks. The Pomoca sole compound gripped confidently on surfaces where I'd typically be more cautious. During a particularly challenging section near Alberta Falls, where the trail becomes a series of wet granite steps, the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX provided the confidence I needed to maintain pace without compromising safety.

The Gore-Tex Extended Comfort membrane proved its worth during an unexpected thunderstorm on Mount Elbert. Despite hiking through puddles and stream crossings for over three hours in steady rain, my feet remained completely dry. However, I did notice some breathability limitations during hot summer days above 80°F, which is typical for waterproof boots. The key lesson I learned was choosing the right socks – merino wool blends work best for temperature regulation with the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX.

Pro Tip: After extensive testing, I found these boots perform best when you size up half a size and use quality hiking socks with moisture-wicking properties.

Technical Features and Design Analysis

The engineering behind the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX reflects decades of alpine expertise. At the heart of this boot lies the exclusive Pomoca performance sole package, developed specifically for Salewa's speed hiking applications. This isn't just marketing speak – the difference is immediately noticeable when transitioning from heel strike to toe-off on uneven terrain.

The 3F System deserves special attention as it fundamentally changes how the boot behaves during dynamic movement. This technology connects the instep area with the sole and heel, creating what Salewa calls "flexibility, firmness, and fit." In practical terms, this means the boot flexes naturally with your foot's movement while maintaining structural integrity for lateral stability on loose rock and steep terrain.

The Flex Collar technology addresses a common complaint with mid-height boots: restricted ankle movement during descents. Traditional boots often force your ankle into unnatural positions on steep downhills, leading to fatigue and discomfort. The Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX allows increased rear range of motion while maintaining medial and lateral support where you need it most.

Gore-Tex Extended Comfort represents the latest evolution in waterproof-breathable technology. Unlike standard Gore-Tex, this membrane prioritizes comfort and breathability for active use. During my testing, I found it notably more comfortable than older Gore-Tex implementations, though it still can't match the breathability of non-waterproof alternatives in hot conditions.

The upper construction combines breathable mesh with strategic TPU reinforcements and coated fabric panels. This hybrid approach reduces weight while providing protection where it matters most. The stretch gaiter integration keeps debris out without the bulk of separate gaiters – a feature I particularly appreciated during autumn hikes through fallen leaves and loose dirt.

Performance Testing Across Different Terrains

Real-world performance testing of the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX has taken me across diverse environments, from desert sandstone in Utah to muddy Pacific Northwest trails. Each terrain presented unique challenges that helped reveal both the strengths and limitations of these boots.

On rocky terrain, particularly the granite slabs common in California's Sierra Nevada, the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX excelled beyond my expectations. The Pomoca sole's compound rubber provides exceptional friction on both dry and slightly damp rock surfaces. During a traverse of Mount Whitney's famous 99 switchbacks, where many hikers struggle with loose talus and polished granite steps, these boots inspired confidence that allowed me to maintain rhythm rather than constantly adjusting my footing.

Muddy conditions proved equally impressive. During Washington's notoriously wet hiking season, I tested the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX on trails that resembled obstacle courses of slippery roots, clay-like mud, and standing water. The aggressive lug pattern cleared mud effectively, and the waterproof protection held up admirably even during several creek crossings where water briefly topped the collar.

Snow and ice performance, while not the primary design intent, proved surprisingly capable. Using microspikes for serious icy conditions, the boot's firm platform provided excellent crampon compatibility. The insulation properties, while minimal, proved adequate for shoulder-season conditions when paired with appropriate socks. However, I wouldn't recommend the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX for winter mountaineering or extended cold-weather hiking.

Desert hiking presented the biggest challenges, primarily due to breathability limitations. During summer hikes in Arizona's Superstition Mountains, the waterproof membrane became a liability in temperatures exceeding 95°F. Sand intrusion around the collar area also required frequent attention, though the stretch gaiter helped minimize this issue compared to completely open designs.

  • Wet/Muddy: Outstanding waterproofing and traction

Sizing, Fit, and Comfort Considerations

The most critical aspect of the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX experience is getting the sizing right. Based on my testing and feedback from fellow hikers, this boot consistently runs about half a size small compared to standard athletic shoe sizing. I normally wear a size 10.5 in running shoes, but found the size 11 Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX provided the ideal fit.

The toe box shape tends to run narrower than many American hiking boot brands, which can be problematic for hikers with wider feet or those who experience foot swelling during long hikes. I discovered that the break-in period is crucial – the synthetic materials do soften and accommodate foot shape over time, but this process takes approximately 20-30 miles of hiking rather than the immediate comfort some boots provide.

Lacing system performance deserves particular attention. The flat shoelaces included with the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX provide excellent lockdown without creating pressure points. The lacing geometry allows for precise adjustment of tension across different zones of the foot, which proves invaluable during long descents when foot swelling and forward sliding become concerns.

Ankle support strikes an ideal balance for speed hiking applications. Unlike rigid mountaineering boots that can feel restrictive, or low-cut trail runners that offer minimal protection, these boots provide confidence-inspiring lateral stability without limiting natural ankle movement. During technical scrambling sections, I never felt like the boots were fighting my natural movement patterns.

The OrthoLite footbed provides adequate cushioning for day hiking applications, though I found it beneficial to upgrade to custom insoles for hikes exceeding 15 miles. The footbed's moisture-wicking properties work well in conjunction with the Gore-Tex membrane, helping manage internal moisture during extended activity.

Sizing Recommendations

  • Order 0.5 size larger than your athletic shoe size
  • Consider width – these run narrow by American standards
  • Allow 20-30 miles for proper break-in
  • Use quality hiking socks to optimize fit and comfort

Common fit issues I've observed include initial heel slippage in properly sized boots (which resolves after break-in) and pressure points around the ankle collar for hikers with high-volume feet. The stretch gaiter, while functional for debris protection, can feel constrictive initially but becomes more comfortable as the materials relax.

Maintenance and Long-term Durability

After six months and approximately 200 miles of varied terrain hiking, the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX shows remarkably little wear considering the aggressive conditions I've subjected them to. However, proper maintenance has been crucial to preserving their performance and extending their lifespan.

The Gore-Tex membrane requires specific care to maintain its waterproof-breathable properties. I've found that cleaning after every few hikes with specialized boot cleaners preserves performance better than allowing dirt and oils to accumulate. The mesh upper panels, while durable, do show wear more quickly than full leather alternatives, particularly around high-flex areas.

Sole durability has exceeded my expectations. The Pomoca compound shows minimal wear on the primary contact points, and the lug pattern remains aggressive enough for confident traction after 200 miles. However, I did notice some minor separation beginning around the toe rand after extensive rocky terrain exposure – nothing that affects performance yet, but worth monitoring.

The waterproofing treatment requires periodic refreshing. I reapplied DWR (Durable Water Repellent) coating after approximately 100 miles, when I noticed water was no longer beading effectively on the upper materials. This simple maintenance step restored the boot's ability to shed moisture and improved breathability by preventing fabric saturation.

Internal components have held up well, though the OrthoLite footbed does compress over time. I replaced the insoles after 150 miles to maintain optimal comfort and support. The lacing hardware remains functional without any signs of wear or failure, and the flat laces have maintained their grip properties despite frequent adjustments.

Maintenance Schedule I Follow

  • After every hike: Remove debris, air dry thoroughly
  • Every 5-10 hikes: Deep clean with specialized boot cleaner
  • Every 100 miles: Reapply DWR treatment
  • Every 150 miles: Consider insole replacement

One durability concern that emerged involves the stretch gaiter integration. While functionally effective, the elastic materials show signs of wear faster than the main boot construction. This isn't affecting performance yet, but it's an area I'm monitoring for potential future issues. Overall, the Salewa Ultra Flex 2 Mid GTX appears built to handle several hundred miles of aggressive hiking with proper care.

How waterproof are these boots in real-world conditions?

What type of terrain are these boots best suited for?
