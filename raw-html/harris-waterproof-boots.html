History and Heritage

The story of Harris waterproof boots begins in 1953, when the original design first emerged from British engineering ingenuity. For over 70 years, these boots have maintained their fundamental design philosophy while quietly serving generations of workers, farmers, and outdoor enthusiasts who demand reliable waterproof protection.

Originally marketed under the Viking brand name, these boots gained popularity through word-of-mouth recommendations among agricultural workers and outdoor professionals. The Harris family, recognizing the exceptional quality and growing demand, eventually acquired the rights to manufacture and distribute these boots directly. This transition allowed them to maintain the original high standards while offering competitive pricing.

The manufacturing process remains unchanged from the original 1953 specifications, with each pair hand-welded in Malaysia using traditional techniques. This commitment to traditional craftsmanship ensures that modern harris waterproof boots maintain the same legendary durability that made them famous among British farmers and gamekeepers.

Throughout the decades, these boots have earned recognition from diverse user communities. Cavers rely on their waterproof integrity in underground environments, while farmers appreciate their chemical resistance when working with agricultural substances. The vegan-friendly construction has also attracted environmentally conscious consumers seeking animal-free alternatives to leather boots.

The Harris family's decision to establish direct distribution through The Dryboot Company represents a commitment to preserving this heritage while making these exceptional boots accessible to modern consumers. Their approach prioritizes function over flashy marketing, reflecting the boots' practical origins and continued focus on performance.
