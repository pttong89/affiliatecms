The Story Behind SJP Shoes: From Carrie Bradshaw to Real-World Fashion

The genesis of sjp shoes traces back to an unlikely partnership between Sarah Jessica Parker and George Malkemus III, the man who introduced Manolo Blahnik to America. It was Malkemus who first introduced Parker to Manolo Blahnik shoes long before she portrayed Carrie Bradshaw, creating a real-life connection that would later inspire millions of viewers. When Parker famously declared having a "religious experience" in a Manolo Blahnik store on Sex and the City, she wasn't just acting – she was sharing her genuine passion for exceptional footwear.

In February 2014, this passion materialized into SJP by Sarah Jessica Parker, a luxury footwear brand that promised to deliver the same quality and craftsmanship that had captivated the actress for decades. The collaboration with Malkemus was natural, combining his industry expertise with Parker's keen eye for design and understanding of what women truly wanted in their shoes. Every pair of sjp shoes was handcrafted in Italy by third and fourth-generation artisans, ensuring each shoe met the exacting standards that both Parker and Malkemus demanded.

What set SJP shoes apart from other celebrity fashion ventures was Parker's hands-on involvement in every aspect of the design process. She wasn't simply lending her name to the brand; she was actively designing, testing, and refining each style. I remember reading interviews where she described spending hours in the factory, working alongside Italian craftsmen to perfect the curve of a heel or the placement of a strap. This dedication to authenticity and quality became the hallmark of the brand.

The brand's philosophy centered on creating shoes that embodied New York sensibilities while honoring Italian shoemaking traditions. Parker often spoke about wanting to create footwear that could transition seamlessly from day to night, from the office to dinner, reflecting the reality of modern women's lives. This practical luxury approach resonated with customers who appreciated both the glamour and the functionality of high-quality shoes that could keep up with their busy lifestyles.

The Closure Announcement: End of an Era for SJP Shoes

In August 2024, the fashion world was stunned by the announcement that SJP shoes would be closing after a decade of success. The official statement was both graceful and bittersweet: "After 10 colorful years, SJP by Sarah Jessica Parker has made the difficult decision to close its doors this fall." This news sent shockwaves through the fashion community and left countless fans scrambling to purchase their final pairs of sjp shoes.

The timing of the closure seemed particularly poignant, coming on the heels of the successful "And Just Like That" series, which had renewed interest in Sarah Jessica Parker's fashion choices and by extension, her shoe brand. Many industry observers speculated about the reasons behind the closure, with most pointing to the challenging retail landscape that had emerged post-COVID, the death of business partner George Malkemus in 2021, and the increasing difficulty of maintaining a luxury brand in an increasingly competitive market.

How do SJP shoes fit and what should I know about sizing?

SJP shoes typically run narrow and small according to customer reviews. Many buyers recommend sizing up by half a size, especially for those with wider feet. The Italian sizing can also differ from US sizing, so it's crucial to check the specific size chart for each style. Since these were luxury shoes made with premium materials, they tend to stretch slightly with wear, but the initial fit should be snug rather than tight. I always recommend reading individual product reviews for specific styles, as some designs like the Lady flats fit differently than the Carrie pumps due to their construction variations.
