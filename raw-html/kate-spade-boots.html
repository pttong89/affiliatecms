Sizing and Fit: Getting It Right the First Time

After purchasing dozens of kate spade boots over the years and helping friends find their perfect fits, I've learned that sizing requires careful consideration. Kate Spade boots consistently run small and narrow compared to athletic footwear brands. My experience mirrors what I've read in countless customer reviews: always size up by at least half a size.

Understanding Kate Spade's Sizing Philosophy

Kate Spade follows European sizing conventions more closely than typical American brands. This means their size 8 fits more like a 7.5 in brands like Clark shoes or athletic sneakers. During my fitting sessions at the Kate Spade Madison Avenue store, the sales associate explained that this sizing approach ensures a sleeker, more refined silhouette that's characteristic of high-end European footwear.

Width Considerations and Comfort

The narrow width can be challenging for those with wider feet. I learned this lesson the hard way with my first pair of Dakota booties – they looked perfect but caused discomfort after a few hours of wear. Now I always recommend trying on kate spade boots later in the day when feet are naturally more swollen, ensuring a realistic fit assessment.
