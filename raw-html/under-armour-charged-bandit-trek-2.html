Frequently Asked Questions

Based on my extensive testing and analysis of user feedback, here are the most common questions about the Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2, along with detailed answers based on real-world experience.

Are the Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2 waterproof?

No, the Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2 is not waterproof. However, this design choice is intentional and offers significant advantages for most hiking conditions. The breathable mesh construction allows moisture to escape quickly, preventing the swampy foot condition common in waterproof shoes. During my testing in Colorado's afternoon thunderstorms, the shoes dried completely within 2-3 hours after the rain stopped. For hikers in predominantly dry climates or those who prioritize breathability over water resistance, this is actually a significant advantage. If you frequently hike in wet conditions, consider pairing these shoes with waterproof socks or look at Under Armour waterproof boots instead.

How do Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2 fit compared to other hiking shoes?

The under armour charged bandit trek 2 runs true to size for most users, though I strongly recommend going up half a size for optimal hiking comfort. The toe box is medium-width, making them suitable for most foot shapes but potentially narrow for those with wider feet. Compared to popular alternatives like Merrell Moab or Salomon shoes, the Under Armour model fits similarly to Salomon's narrower profile but with slightly more heel volume. The padded heel collar requires proper lacing technique to prevent slippage, and the overall fit becomes more personalized after about 20 miles as the sockliner molds to your foot. If you wear size 10 in most athletic shoes, order 10.5 for hiking to accommodate foot swelling and provide optimal downhill comfort.

What is the weight of the Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2?

The Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2 weighs approximately 11.5 ounces (326 grams) for a men's size 10, making it one of the lightest hiking shoes in its category. For comparison, this is nearly 4 ounces lighter than traditional hiking boots and about 2 ounces lighter than many competing trail shoes. During my extended testing, this weight advantage became increasingly apparent on longer hikes—the reduced weight significantly decreased leg fatigue and improved agility on technical sections. The lightweight construction comes from the engineered mesh upper, streamlined design, and optimized midsole foam. For hikers counting every ounce or those transitioning from heavy boots, this weight savings represents a game-changing improvement in hiking comfort and endurance.

How long do Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2 last?

With proper care and regular use, the Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2 typically lasts 400-600 miles of hiking, which translates to 6-12 months for most recreational hikers. The durability impressed me during testing—after 85 miles across varied terrain, the shoes show minimal wear on the outsole and upper. The mesh construction, while lightweight, proved more durable than expected, withstanding brushes with rocks and branches. The outsole rubber compound maintains good tread depth even after extensive use on abrasive surfaces. Factors affecting longevity include hiking frequency, terrain type, and maintenance habits. Regular cleaning and proper storage extend life significantly. Compared to budget hiking shoes that might last 200-300 miles, the Trek 2 offers excellent value for money when considering cost per mile of hiking enjoyment.

Are Under Armour Charged Bandit Trek 2 good for winter hiking?

The under armour charged bandit trek 2 can be used for light winter hiking in dry conditions, but they're not specifically designed for cold weather. The shoes lack insulation, making them unsuitable for temperatures below 40°F without additional measures like warm socks and gaiters. However, the higher ankle collar provides more protection than low-top trail runners, and the aggressive outsole offers decent traction on packed snow and frozen ground. During testing in early winter conditions in Colorado, they performed adequately on dry, frozen trails but became problematic on icy surfaces. For dedicated winter hiking, consider Under Armour boots with insulation and more aggressive traction. The Trek 2 is better suited as a three-season shoe for spring through fall conditions.
