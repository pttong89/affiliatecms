For most hiking scenarios, the waterproofing proves adequate and the quick-drying capability compensates for any minor moisture infiltration.

How does the sizing run for Vectiv Exploris Mid boots?

The Vectiv Exploris Mid boots generally run true to size for most hikers. I found the size 9 fit perfectly with my regular to wide feet and high arches. What's the difference between Exploris Mid and Exploris 2 Mid?

The main differences include improved waterproofing in the tongue area, updated VECTIV 2.0 propulsive plate technology for better stability, 2mm greater stack height for enhanced cushioning, and a broader platform design. The Exploris 2 also uses premium EVA foam and accommodates a wider range of foot shapes with its updated D-width last construction. For most hikers, the improvements in the Exploris 2 justify the slightly higher price point, especially regarding waterproofing and comfort.

How much do the Vectiv Exploris Mid boots weigh?

The Vectiv Exploris Mid boots weigh approximately 14.67 oz (416g) per boot, or 832g for a complete pair. This puts them firmly in the lightweight hiking boot category. The innovative design maintains this low weight while still providing excellent stability and protection, making them feel notably lighter on long trails compared to traditional heavy hiking boots. Can I use Vectiv Exploris Mid boots for winter hiking?

While the Vectiv Exploris Mid boots offer some cold weather protection, they're not ideal for serious winter hiking. For winter hiking, consider the leather version or pair them with warm merino wool socks and gaiters for light winter conditions. For serious cold weather or snow hiking, dedicated insulated winter boots would be more appropriate.

How long do Vectiv Exploris Mid boots last?

Based on extensive testing and user feedback, the Vectiv Exploris Mid boots typically last 500-800 miles of hiking depending on terrain and usage patterns.
