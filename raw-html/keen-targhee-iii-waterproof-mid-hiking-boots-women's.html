Key Features and Technology

KEEN.DRY Waterproof Technology

The waterproof membrane in these keen targhee iii waterproof mid hiking boots women's utilizes KEEN's proprietary KEEN.DRY technology. Unlike traditional Gore-Tex membranes, KEEN.DRY aims to balance waterproofing with enhanced breathability. During my testing in the Cascade Mountains, I encountered morning dew, light rain, and stream crossings – the boots maintained dry interiors throughout these conditions.

However, it's important to note that several long-term users report waterproofing failures along seam lines after 6-12 months of regular use. This seems to be a known issue with the KEEN Targhee III waterproof line, particularly affecting the area where the upper meets the midsole.

Construction and Materials

The upper construction combines nubuck leather with performance mesh panels. The leather is sourced from Leather Working Group certified tanneries, reflecting KEEN's commitment to more sustainable manufacturing. The mesh areas provide crucial ventilation, particularly important given the mid-height design that can trap heat around the ankle.

KEEN's signature rubber toe cap extends further up the boot than many competitors, providing excellent protection against rocks and roots. The EVA midsole incorporates dual-density construction – softer near the foot for comfort, firmer at the bottom for stability. This design approach works well for the KEEN Targhee III mid target audience of comfort-focused hikers.

Outsole and Traction

The KEEN All-Terrain rubber outsole features 4mm multi-directional lugs designed for varied terrain. In practice, these lugs perform adequately on dirt trails, loose gravel, and moderate rocky surfaces. However, they fall short on steep, technical terrain where more aggressive lug patterns excel. The outsole rubber compound offers reasonable durability, though I noticed some wear after approximately 200 miles of use.
