Complete Sizing and Fit Guide

Proper sizing is crucial for getting the most out of your emu boots, and I've learned this through both personal experience and helping others find their perfect fit. Unlike traditional shoes, sheepskin boots have unique sizing considerations that can make or break your comfort experience.

The most important principle to understand is that emu boots should fit snugly when new. The natural sheepskin will stretch and conform to your foot shape over the first few weeks of wear. I made the mistake with my first pair of ordering my usual size plus a half-size up, thinking I needed room for thick socks. The result was boots that became too loose and provided inadequate support.

Step-by-Step Measuring Guide

  1. Trace Your Foot: Place a sheet of paper against a wall, step onto it with your heel against the wall, and trace around your foot.
  2. Measure Length: Use a ruler to measure from heel to longest toe (usually the big toe).
  3. Measure Both Feet: Most people have slight size differences - use the larger measurement.
  4. Consider Width: EMU boots accommodate average to wide feet better than narrow feet.
  5. Time of Day Matters: Measure feet in the evening when they're at their largest.

For women's emu boots, I recommend ordering your true size if you typically wear sizes 6-10. If you're between sizes, size up only if you plan to wear thick winter socks regularly. Men's sizing follows similar principles, but EMU boots tend to run slightly more generous in men's sizes 9-12.

The break-in period is typically 3-5 days of regular wear. During this time, the sheepskin compresses and molds to your foot contours. I found that wearing them for short periods initially, gradually increasing wear time, provides the most comfortable break-in experience.

Fit Issues and Solutions

Too Tight Initially: If your emu boots feel uncomfortably tight when new, this is often normal. Focus on whether there are pressure points or if it's just overall snugness. The latter will resolve with wear.

Heel Slippage: Some initial heel movement is normal and will decrease as the boots break in. However, if you can lift your heel more than 1/2 inch, consider sizing down.

Toe Room: You should have about 1/4 to 1/2 inch of space beyond your longest toe. More than this, and the boots may feel sloppy after break-in.

For those considering Brunt boots or other work footwear alongside EMU boots, it's worth noting that EMU sizing doesn't directly correlate with work boot sizing due to the different construction methods and materials involved.

What's the proper way to clean and care for EMU boots?

EMU boots require minimal maintenance when cared for properly. For routine cleaning, allow mud or dirt to dry completely, then brush off with a soft-bristled brush. For deeper cleaning, use cold water mixed with wool wash and a clean cloth for spot treatment. Never machine wash or submerge EMU boots completely, as this can damage the sheepskin and waterproof treatment. Apply suede protector spray annually to maintain water resistance. Store boots in a cool, dry place with boot trees or newspaper to maintain shape. Professional cleaning is recommended for severe staining or damage.
