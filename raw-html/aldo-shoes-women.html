Sizing Guide and Fit Analysis

Accurate sizing represents one of the most critical factors when purchasing ALDO shoes women online or in-store, as fit variations between styles can significantly impact comfort and wearability. Through my extensive testing across multiple shoe types and seasonal collections, I've developed a comprehensive understanding of how ALDO's sizing translates across different foot shapes and style categories.

ALDO follows standard US women's sizing with B+ width as their default fitting standard, which accommodates most average-width feet effectively. However, important variations exist between different style categories that can affect purchasing decisions. Pointed-toe styles, particularly in their heel collections, tend to run approximately a half-size smaller than rounded or square-toe alternatives in the same numerical size. During my fitting sessions with various styles, I consistently needed to size up for pointed pumps while maintaining my regular size for sneakers and rounded flats.

Heels & Pumps

Size up 0.5 for pointed toes. Block heels fit true to size. Consider foot width for narrow styles.

Flats & Loafers

Generally true to size. Ballet flats may require sizing up if you have higher arches or prefer looser fit.

Sneakers & Boots

Sneakers run true to size. Boots may need sizing up for thick socks or wider calves.

International sizing conversions require particular attention when purchasing ALDO shoes women from different regional websites or during international travel. The brand primarily uses US sizing on their North American platforms, but European and Asian markets may display local sizing standards. I recommend always checking the specific size conversion chart on the purchasing platform rather than relying on general conversion formulas, as slight variations exist between ALDO's regional sizing implementations.

Width considerations become especially important for women with narrow or wide feet. ALDO's B+ standard width works well for average feet, but narrow-footed individuals may find certain styles, particularly sandals and slip-on designs, too loose for secure fitting. Conversely, women with wider feet should be cautious with pointed-toe styles and consider sizing up or choosing rounded-toe alternatives. The Blanchette Ballet Flat offers more generous width accommodation compared to their dressier pointed options.

Professional Fitting Tip

Always measure your feet later in the day when they're naturally more swollen, as this represents your feet at their largest daily size. This prevents purchasing shoes that feel perfect in the morning but become uncomfortable by afternoon.
