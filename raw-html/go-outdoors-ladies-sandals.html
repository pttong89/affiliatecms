Frequently Asked Questions

Are go outdoors ladies sandals suitable for serious hiking?

Absolutely! Quality outdoor sandals are designed specifically for hiking and outdoor adventures. During my extensive testing, I've successfully completed challenging trails wearing premium hiking sandals. They excel in warm weather conditions, provide excellent breathability, and offer sufficient protection for most hiking scenarios. However, for technical mountaineering or extreme cold conditions, traditional hiking boots remain the better choice. The key is selecting sandals with proper arch support, aggressive tread patterns, and durable construction materials.

How do I choose the right size for walking sandals?

Sizing for outdoor sandals differs significantly from regular shoes. I recommend measuring your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally swollen, then adding approximately half an inch to your longest toe measurement. Most outdoor sandals should be sized up by half a size from your regular shoe size to accommodate foot swelling during activities. Always ensure you can adjust the straps to achieve a secure fit without over-tightening. Remember that your feet should sit comfortably within the sandal footbed without hanging over the edges or sliding forward during movement.

What's the difference between men's and women's outdoor sandals?

The primary differences lie in fit, width, and strap positioning rather than fundamental construction. Women's outdoor sandals typically feature narrower heel cups, modified arch support positioning, and strap configurations designed for women's foot anatomy. However, many women with wider feet find better fits in men's models, which generally offer broader platforms and more generous toe boxes. Color options also tend to differ, with women's models offering more varied aesthetic choices. Functionally, both versions use identical materials and construction techniques, so cross-gender sizing is perfectly acceptable when it provides better fit.

Can I wear socks with outdoor sandals?

Yes, and I actually recommend it for certain conditions! Wearing thin hiking socks with outdoor sandals prevents blisters during long hikes, provides additional warmth in cooler conditions, and offers extra protection from debris. Choose moisture-wicking synthetic or merino wool socks designed for hiking. Avoid cotton socks, which retain moisture and increase blister risk. Many serious hikers and outdoor professionals regularly combine socks with sandals for optimal comfort and performance. While the aesthetic might not appeal to everyone, the practical benefits are undeniable for serious outdoor activities.

How long do quality outdoor sandals typically last?

Quality outdoor sandals should provide 2-5 years of regular use, depending on usage intensity and maintenance practices. Premium models from reputable manufacturers like Chaco, Teva, and Bedrock often last longer due to superior materials and construction methods. Some premium brands offer resoling services, potentially extending sandal life indefinitely. Signs that replacement is needed include significant sole wear, strap deterioration, or compromised footbed support. With proper care—regular cleaning, appropriate storage, and periodic maintenance—quality outdoor sandals represent an excellent long-term investment for outdoor enthusiasts seeking reliable, comfortable footwear for adventures.
