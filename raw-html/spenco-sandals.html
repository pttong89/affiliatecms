Sizing and Fit Guide

Proper fitting is crucial for maximizing the benefits of spenco sandals. Unlike fashion sandals where approximate sizing might suffice, orthotic sandals require precise fitting to deliver their full support potential. Through personal experience and extensive conversations with other users, I've developed a comprehensive approach to getting the perfect fit.

Most users find that Spenco sandals run true to size, but the orthotic footbed creates different fit dynamics than regular sandals. The arch support and heel cup need to align properly with your foot structure, which means paying attention to both length and width considerations. I recommend measuring your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally slightly swollen, mimicking the conditions you'll experience during long hiking days.

For the Yumi thong-style models, ensure your heel sits properly in the heel cup without hanging over the back edge. Your toes should have slight room at the front – about a thumb's width of space. The thong placement is crucial; it should sit comfortably between your toes without pressure or rubbing. If you're sensitive to thong-style footwear, consider starting with shorter wear periods to allow adaptation.

Kholo and other slide-style models offer more adjustability through strap systems, but proper heel placement remains critical. The arch support should feel like it's gently lifting your arch rather than creating pressure points. Some users experience initial discomfort as their feet adjust to proper alignment – this is normal and typically resolves within a week of regular wear.

Width considerations are particularly important for mens sandals and those with wider feet. Spenco offers many models in wide widths, and I strongly recommend investing in proper width sizing rather than sizing up in length. An oversized sandal compromises the orthotic positioning and reduces overall support effectiveness.

Break-in periods vary by individual, but most users need 3-5 days of gradually increasing wear time to fully adapt to the orthotic support. Start with short walks and gradually increase duration as your feet adjust to proper alignment. This break-in period is investment in long-term comfort and foot health, distinguishing quality waterproof sandals from cheap alternatives that offer no adaptation period because they provide no meaningful support.
