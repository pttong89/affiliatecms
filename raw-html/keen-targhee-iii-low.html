Waterproof Performance Testing: KEEN.DRY Technology

KEEN's proprietary KEEN.DRY waterproof membrane is the heart of the Targhee III Low's weather protection system. During my extensive testing across multiple seasons and conditions, I deliberately subjected these shoes to challenging water exposure scenarios to assess their real-world performance capabilities.

The waterproofing proved effective during stream crossings up to approximately 4 inches deep - the height of the low-cut collar. I regularly crossed rocky creek beds in Colorado's high country without experiencing any internal moisture. The KEEN.DRY membrane successfully blocked external water while allowing some degree of vapor transfer, though breathability remains limited compared to non-waterproof alternatives.

One limitation I discovered is the keen targhee iii low design's vulnerability to water entry from above. During heavy rainfall hikes, water can accumulate at the collar and eventually seep into the shoe's interior. This is inherent to the low-cut design and shouldn't be considered a defect, but hikers planning extended exposure to heavy precipitation might prefer the mid-height waterproof version.

The DWR (Durable Water Repellent) coating on the nubuck leather performed admirably during my testing period. Light rain and morning dew beaded effectively on the surface for the first 200 miles. However, I noticed the treatment beginning to diminish after extensive use, requiring periodic reapplication of aftermarket DWR treatments to maintain optimal performance.

Drying time after water exposure is reasonable but not exceptional. Following complete submersion tests, the shoes required 12-16 hours to fully dry in moderate humidity conditions. The mesh tongue area dries more quickly than the leather portions, creating some internal moisture variation during the drying process.
