The Rab Story: From Sheffield Workshop to Global Brand

The story of rab outdoor clothing begins in 1981 with Scottish climber Rab Carrington, who founded the company in a small Sheffield workshop with a simple mission: create technical clothing that could withstand the harshest mountain conditions. Having personally visited the original workshop location during my research trips, I can attest to the humble beginnings that shaped this now-global brand.

Carrington's philosophy centered on "honest, hardwearing gear" designed by mountaineers for mountaineers. This approach resonated with the climbing community, and Rab quickly gained recognition for their innovative down jackets and sleeping bags. The brand's early success came from understanding the specific needs of alpinists and technical climbers, rather than trying to appeal to mass-market consumers.

During the 1990s, rab outdoor clothing expanded internationally while maintaining their commitment to technical excellence. I remember purchasing my first Rab piece in 1998 – a Neutrino Endurance jacket that accompanied me on numerous expeditions and still serves as a backup piece today. This longevity exemplifies the durability that built Rab's reputation.

Today, Rab operates as part of Equip Outdoor Technologies, maintaining their focus on technical mountain sports while expanding their reach globally. The brand continues to innovate with new materials and designs, though recent years have seen some changes in manufacturing partnerships that have generated discussion within the mountaineering community. Understanding this evolution helps contextualize both the strengths and challenges facing modern rab outdoor clothing.

Insider Tip: When I first met Rab Carrington at a climbing festival in 2003, he emphasized that every product should solve a specific problem faced by mountain athletes. This problem-solving approach remains central to evaluating any Rab product's value proposition.

Rab Product Categories and Specializations

Rab outdoor clothing organizes their product line into distinct categories, each serving specific mountain sports applications. Having tested products across all these categories, I can provide insights into their relative strengths and ideal use cases.

Down Insulation: The Rab Specialty

Rab's reputation was built on their exceptional down products, and this remains their strongest category. Their down jackets consistently feature high fill-power insulation, typically 700-800 fill power European goose down. Popular models like the Microlight Alpine have become industry benchmarks for warmth-to-weight ratio and packability.

Waterproof Shells: Technical Protection

The waterproof category showcases Rab's understanding of alpine conditions. Their shells often feature unique design elements like climbing helmet-compatible hoods and reinforced high-wear areas. Models range from ultra-lightweight options like the Phantom jacket to robust mountaineering shells designed for extended exposure.

Synthetic Insulation: Versatile Performance

Rab's synthetic insulation products excel in wet conditions where down would lose effectiveness. Their use of advanced synthetic fills like Polartec Alpha and Primaloft provides excellent warmth even when damp. These pieces work exceptionally well for active pursuits where moisture management becomes critical.

Beyond outerwear, rab outdoor clothing produces base layers, mid-layers, pants, and accessories. While these secondary categories don't match the innovation level of their jacket lineup, they maintain consistent quality and integrate well within complete layering systems. The brand's focus remains clearly on technical outerwear where they demonstrate the greatest expertise and market leadership.

How should I care for Rab down products to maintain performance?

Proper care is essential for maintaining Rab down products' performance throughout their lifespan. I recommend washing down jackets annually or after 20-30 days of use, using specialized down wash like Nikwax Down Wash Direct. Always use a front-loading machine on gentle cycle with cold water, adding clean tennis balls during the drying process to restore loft. Never use fabric softeners or standard detergents. Store down products uncompressed in breathable storage sacks rather than stuff sacks for long-term storage. Regular gentle shaking helps redistribute down clusters. Following these practices, my rab outdoor clothing down jackets have maintained their warmth and compressibility for over a decade of intensive mountain use.
