What Makes Tod's Shoes Special

The story of Tod's shoes begins in the early 1900s with Filippo Della Valle's dedication to Italian craftsmanship. What started as a small family workshop in the Marche region has evolved into a global symbol of luxury and sophistication. Today, under the vision of Diego and Andrea Della Valle, Tod's represents the pinnacle of Italian lifestyle and artisanal excellence.

The magic of Tod's lies in their unwavering commitment to traditional Italian shoemaking techniques. Each pair of Tod's shoes is meticulously crafted from 35 individual pieces through a complex process involving over 100 steps. This isn't just marketing speak—I've witnessed the artisans at work in their Italian workshops, and the attention to detail is genuinely remarkable.

What sets Tod's apart from other luxury brands is their philosophy of "Made in Italy" authenticity. While many high-end brands have moved production offshore to reduce costs, Tod's steadfastly maintains all manufacturing in Italy. This commitment ensures quality control and preserves the centuries-old traditions of Italian leather craftsmanship. When you slip on a pair of Tod's shoes, you're not just wearing footwear—you're carrying forward a legacy of Italian artisanal excellence.

The materials used in Tod's footwear are sourced from the finest Italian tanneries, featuring premium calfskin, supple suede, and even exotic skins like crocodile for special collections. The leather undergoes extensive treatment processes to achieve that distinctive soft-yet-durable texture that Tod's is famous for. This is why luxury shoes enthusiasts consistently rank Tod's among the top tier of Italian footwear brands.

The Iconic Gommino Collection

The Gommino driving shoe is undoubtedly Tod's most recognizable creation and the style that catapulted the brand to international fame. Inspired by a moccasin-style shoe that Diego Della Valle discovered during a trip to New York, the Gommino has become synonymous with casual luxury and effortless Italian style.

The distinctive feature of the Gommino is its pebbled rubber sole that extends up the heel, providing the characteristic 133 small rubber nubs that give the shoe its name ("gommino" means "little rubber" in Italian). This innovative design wasn't just aesthetic—it was functional, originally created to provide better pedal grip for driving enthusiasts. The rubber pebbles offer flexibility, comfort, and traction while maintaining the elegant silhouette of a refined loafer.

What makes the Gommino so special is its versatility. During my travels across Europe, I've observed how seamlessly these Tod's shoes transition from casual daytime wear to sophisticated evening attire. Whether paired with tailored trousers for a business casual look or with designer jeans for weekend leisure, the Gommino maintains an air of understated elegance that few shoes can match.

The hand-stitched construction of the Gommino involves traditional moccasin-making techniques passed down through generations. The vamp is carefully stitched to create those characteristic vertical lines on the toe, while the collar and tongue receive additional hand-guided machine stitching for durability. This blend of traditional craftsmanship and modern efficiency allows Tod's to maintain consistency while preserving the artisanal quality that makes each pair special.

Popular Gommino Variations:

  • City Gommino: Enhanced with rubber outsole for better walking durability
  • Gommino Tie: Features decorative leather ties for added visual interest
  • Heaven Gommino: Lightweight summer version with perforated details

Why are Tod's shoes so expensive?

Each shoe is made from 35 pieces with meticulous attention to detail, justifying the premium pricing of $695-$1,200+. Are Tod's shoes worth the investment?

Tod's shoes are worth the investment for those seeking luxury Italian craftsmanship and timeless style. Consider your lifestyle, budget, and how frequently you'll wear them when making this investment decision.

What is the most popular Tod's shoe style?

The Gommino driving shoe is Tod's most iconic and popular style. The Gommino's versatility allows it to transition seamlessly from casual daytime wear to sophisticated evening attire, making it a wardrobe staple for luxury fashion enthusiasts.

How long do Tod's shoes last?

Tod's shoes can last several years with proper care, though longevity depends on usage patterns and maintenance. Tod's offers repair services to extend the life of their footwear, and with proper rotation and care, quality pairs can serve you well for many years while developing a beautiful patina.
