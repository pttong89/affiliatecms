Why Wide Fit Matters for Hikers

Having wide feet shouldn't limit your hiking adventures, yet many outdoor enthusiasts struggle with finding properly fitting footwear. The Merrell MOAB 2 wide fit addresses this critical need by providing additional room where it matters most - across the forefoot and toe box area.

Sizing and Fit Guide

Proper sizing for the Merrell MOAB 2 wide fit requires understanding both length and width measurements. Unlike regular width shoes where length is the primary concern, wide fit models demand careful attention to forefoot width, instep height, and heel cup accommodation.

Length Sizing Guidelines

  • Allow 1/2 inch thumb width between longest toe and shoe end
  • Measure feet in afternoon when naturally swollen
  • Try on with hiking socks you'll actually use
  • Test on inclined surfaces if possible

Width Fitting Tips

  • No pinching at widest part of foot
  • Toes should move freely without crowding
  • Upper material shouldn't bulge excessively
  • Heel should feel secure without slipping

The Merrell MOAB 2 wide fit generally runs true to size in length, but individual foot characteristics can influence the ideal fit. People with high insteps may need to size up half a size, while those with flat, wide feet often find their normal size perfect.

Common Sizing Scenarios

Normal Volume Feet: Order your regular shoe size in wide width

High Volume/Instep: Consider sizing up 1/2 size from regular shoe size

Low Volume: Your regular size should work, focus on width over length

Between Sizes: Size up for thick socks, size down for thin liners

Frequently Asked Questions

How much wider is the Merrell MOAB 2 wide fit compared to regular width?

The Merrell MOAB 2 wide fit typically adds about 1/4 to 3/8 inch of additional width across the forefoot compared to regular width models. This extra space is distributed across the toe box and midfoot areas, providing crucial room for those with wider feet or foot conditions that cause swelling. The wide fit designation corresponds to an E width in men's sizing and a D width in women's sizing, following standard footwear industry measurements. This additional width accommodates wider foot shapes without compromising the overall structure and support characteristics that make the MOAB 2 popular among hikers.

Should I size up or down when choosing Merrell MOAB 2 wide fit?

Most users find that Merrell MOAB 2 wide fit runs true to length, so stick with your normal shoe size. However, if you're between sizes, consider going up a half size for hiking with thick socks or if you experience foot swelling during long hikes. The wide fit provides the additional width needed without requiring length adjustments. I recommend trying them on in the afternoon when your feet are naturally swollen to simulate hiking conditions. Remember that proper hiking socks add volume, so always test fit with the actual socks you'll wear on the trail. If you have high-volume feet or prominent insteps, sizing up half a size often provides optimal comfort.

Choose the Speed 2 if you prefer lighter weight and faster hiking, or stick with the MOAB 2 for maximum durability and traditional hiking boot feel. How do I know if I need wide fit hiking shoes?

You likely need wide fit shoes if you experience pinching at the sides of regular shoes, develop blisters on your pinky toes or sides of feet, or feel like your toes are cramped together. Measuring your foot width with a Brannock device at a specialty store can provide definitive sizing guidance. Other indicators include: shoes feeling too tight even when properly sized for length, numbness or tingling during extended wear, or having to size up significantly in regular width shoes for width comfort. Many people with wide feet don't realize they need wide-fit footwear until they try properly fitted shoes.
