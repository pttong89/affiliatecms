Romika's Century of Excellence

When I first discovered Romika shoes during a visit to a specialty comfort shoe store in Portland, I was immediately struck by their understated elegance combined with obvious attention to comfort details. The story behind these remarkable shoes begins in 1921, when German shoemakers Hans Rollman, Karl Michael, and Karl Kaufman joined forces to establish what would become one of Europe's most respected footwear brands.

The name "Romika" itself cleverly combines the founders' surnames – ROllman, MIchael, and KAufman – creating a brand identity that has endured for over a century. What sets Romika apart in today's global marketplace is their unwavering commitment to maintaining production standards that honor their German heritage. Over 75% of Romika production still takes place in Europe, ensuring the "Made in Germany" quality that discerning customers expect.

Throughout the decades, Romika has weathered economic challenges and market changes by focusing on what they do best: creating footwear that prioritizes comfort without sacrificing style. Their philosophy centers on the belief that shoes should enhance, not hinder, your daily activities. This approach has led to innovations in footbed design, adjustable fitting systems, and the use of premium materials that improve with age rather than deteriorate.

Today, Romika operates as part of the Josef Seibel Group, another German comfort footwear specialist, allowing them to leverage shared expertise while maintaining their distinct identity. This partnership has enabled Romika to expand their global reach while preserving the artisanal techniques and quality standards that have defined the brand since its inception. When you invest in quality footwear, you're participating in a tradition that values craftsmanship over mass production.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Romika shoes good for plantar fasciitis?

Yes, Romika shoes are excellent for plantar fasciitis due to their built-in arch support, removable orthotic-friendly insoles, and contoured footbeds. Many customers with plantar fasciitis report significant comfort improvements when wearing Romika footwear, particularly the Ibiza and Loire collections. The anatomical design helps distribute pressure evenly across the foot while providing targeted support to the plantar fascia. I personally experienced relief from heel pain within weeks of switching to Romika shoes, and the removable insole feature allows for custom orthotics when additional support is needed.

Where are Romika shoes made?

Romika shoes are primarily made in Europe, with over 75% of production remaining in Germany to maintain their "Made in Germany" quality standards. Some styles are also manufactured in Asia and Indonesia, but the brand maintains strict quality control across all production facilities. This European manufacturing heritage ensures consistent craftsmanship and adherence to the comfort and durability standards that Romika has maintained since 1921. The company's commitment to preserving traditional shoemaking techniques while incorporating modern comfort technologies sets them apart from purely outsourced brands.

How do Romika shoes fit compared to American sizes?

Romika shoes use European sizing and tend to run true to size when converted, but they fit differently than American shoes due to their European last design. They're broader in the toe area and snugger around the heel and instep, allowing natural toe movement while providing secure heel support. This design accommodates foot swelling throughout the day and promotes better circulation. When converting from US to European sizes, I recommend measuring your foot length and consulting Romika's size chart rather than relying solely on general conversion formulas, as the width characteristics are distinctly European.

Can I replace the insoles in Romika shoes with custom orthotics?

Absolutely! Most Romika shoes feature removable insoles specifically designed to accommodate custom orthotics. This orthotic-friendly design makes them particularly popular among people with specific foot conditions who need personalized support while maintaining style and comfort. The removable footbeds are easy to take out and replace, and the shoe construction maintains its supportive characteristics even when using thicker custom insoles. This flexibility has been crucial for my own foot health, allowing me to switch between standard insoles for casual wear and specialized hiking inserts for outdoor activities.
