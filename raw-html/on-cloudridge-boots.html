What Makes On Cloudridge Boots Special

The On Cloudridge boots represent a paradigm shift in hiking footwear, born from Swiss precision engineering and inspired by the brand's professional trail-racing shoes. What immediately sets them apart is their revolutionary approach to weight reduction without compromising support—something I discovered firsthand during my recent hiking adventures in the Rocky Mountains.

These boots feature an innovative ripstop fabric upper that provides a sock-like fit, adapting to your foot's natural movement while maintaining structural integrity. The material choice isn't just about comfort—it's about creating a hiking boot that feels more like wearing premium trail running shoes while delivering the ankle support and protection traditional hikers expect.

The FlexLock speed-lacing system deserves special mention for its versatility. Unlike conventional boots that lock you into one lacing style, the Cloudridge allows you to switch between loose, comfortable fit for casual wear and tight, secure lacing for technical terrain—all without re-threading the entire boot. This adaptability makes them equally suitable for urban exploration and mountain trails.

Technical Specifications and Features

Key Technical Specifications

Weight: 410g (Men's UK 8)
Drop: 7mm
Upper Material: Ripstop Fabric
Lining: 3D Mesh
Midsole: Zero Gravity Foam
Outsole: Missiongrip Rubber
Closure: FlexLock Speed Lacing
Waterproof: No

The heart of the On Cloudridge boots lies in their Speedboard technology—a hiking-specific plate that supports your foot's natural rolling motion. Unlike traditional rigid shanks found in heavy boots, this flexible system encourages efficient energy transfer, making each step feel lighter and more responsive. The technology originates from On's professional racing shoes, adapted specifically for hiking applications.

The Missiongrip outsole represents Swiss precision engineering at its finest. Developed and tested in the Alps, it combines varied grip elements designed for ever-changing terrain. The rubber compound provides exceptional traction on dry rock and mixed surfaces, though I found it less effective on wet, flat rocks—a limitation worth considering if you frequently encounter such conditions.

What impressed me most was the breathability system. The 3D mesh tongue and lining create excellent airflow, preventing the swamp-foot syndrome common with traditional leather boots. This ventilation system works in harmony with the ripstop upper to regulate temperature, keeping feet comfortable during high-intensity hiking. Sizing Guide and Fit Recommendations

Proper sizing proves critical with On Cloudridge boots due to their unique ripstop upper construction and sock-like fit characteristics. After extensive testing across different foot types and consulting with other hikers, I've developed comprehensive sizing recommendations that account for the boots' specific design elements.

My Sizing Recommendations

  • Standard Fit: Order your normal running shoe size
  • Wide Feet: Consider half-size up for comfort
  • Thick Socks: Half-size up recommended
  • Long Distance: Account for foot swelling with larger size

The ripstop upper material provides more give than traditional leather, allowing the boot to conform to your foot shape over time. This adaptive quality means initial snugness often resolves with wear, but overly tight boots can cause pressure points that won't improve. I found the sweet spot with my normal size 10.5, providing adequate toe room without heel slippage.

Sizing Red Flags

  • Toes touching front of boot
  • Heel slipping when walking
  • Pressure on top of foot
  • Numbness after 30 minutes

Perfect Fit Signs

  • Thumb-width space at toe
  • Secure heel with no movement
  • Even pressure distribution
  • Comfortable after extended wear

The FlexLock lacing system allows fine-tuning of fit zones, compensating for minor sizing variations. I recommend experimenting with different lacing techniques—loose for casual wear, tight for technical terrain. This versatility makes the Cloudridge more forgiving of sizing choices compared to traditional boots with fixed lacing systems.

For reference, my foot measurements are 27cm length, medium width, and I wear size 10.5 in most athletic shoes including Jordan shoes and Travis Scott collaborations. The 10.5 Cloudridge provided perfect fit for day hiking with medium-thickness merino wool socks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are On Cloudridge boots waterproof?

No, the On Cloudridge boots are not waterproof. They feature breathable ripstop fabric and 3D mesh lining designed for warm weather hiking and excellent ventilation. The non-waterproof design keeps feet cooler and more comfortable in dry conditions, making them ideal for summer trail adventures and fast-paced hiking where breathability is prioritized over weather protection. For waterproof alternatives, consider checking out waterproof boot options in our gear collection.

How do On Cloudridge boots fit compared to regular running shoes?

On Cloudridge boots generally fit true to size compared to regular running shoes. However, many users recommend going half a size up if you have wider feet or plan to wear thick hiking socks. The ripstop upper provides a sock-like fit that adapts to your foot shape. I found them perfectly sized in my normal running shoe size, with adequate toe room and secure heel fit for technical terrain. The fit is noticeably different from traditional leather boots, offering more flexibility and comfort.

What is the weight of On Cloudridge boots?

The On Cloudridge boots weigh approximately 410g (14.46oz) per shoe for men's UK size 8, making them exceptionally lightweight for a hiking boot. Women's size 5 weighs around 340g per shoe. This ultralight construction, achieved through innovative ripstop fabric and Zero Gravity foam midsole, provides the agility of a trail running shoe with the support of a hiking boot. The weight advantage becomes immediately noticeable during long hiking days, reducing overall leg fatigue significantly.

How durable are On Cloudridge boots for regular hiking?

On Cloudridge boots offer moderate durability with an estimated 300-mile lifespan according to manufacturer specifications. The lightweight ripstop upper excels in comfort but may show wear faster on rocky terrain compared to traditional leather boots. The Missiongrip outsole provides excellent traction initially, though the shallow tread pattern may wear quicker than deeper-lugged competitors. They're best suited for regular day hikes rather than extended backpacking. For comparison, work boots like Brunt offer superior durability for heavy-duty applications.

What terrain are On Cloudridge boots best suited for?

On Cloudridge boots excel on well-maintained trails, rocky singletrack, and moderate terrain in dry conditions. The Missiongrip outsole provides reliable traction on mixed surfaces, while the lightweight design enables fast-paced hiking and trail running. They perform best in warm weather conditions where breathability is crucial. Less ideal for wet conditions, muddy trails, or extended multi-day backpacking where maximum durability is required. The Swiss-engineered design particularly shines on Alpine-style terrain with varied rock surfaces and technical sections.

Can I use On Cloudridge boots for trail running?

Yes, On Cloudridge boots are excellent for trail running and fast hiking. Their lightweight construction, flexible Speedboard technology, and CloudTec midsole provide responsive cushioning perfect for covering ground quickly. The mid-cut design offers ankle support while maintaining the agility needed for technical trail running. Many users specifically choose them as a crossover between trail running shoes and hiking boots for maximum versatility. They're particularly popular among ultralight hikers and those who prefer a more athletic approach to hiking. For dedicated trail running, you might also consider specialized athletic footwear options.
