Maintenance and Care Guide

Daily Field Care

Proper maintenance extends the life of lowa hunting boots significantly. After each hunt, I remove debris from the lugs and wipe down the leather with a damp cloth. The key is addressing mud and dirt immediately—once dried, it becomes much harder to remove and can damage the leather fibers over time.

Deep Cleaning Process

For thorough cleaning, I use a soft brush to remove stubborn dirt, followed by a leather-specific cleaner. The GTX membrane requires special attention—harsh chemicals can compromise waterproofing. I always rinse thoroughly and air dry away from direct heat sources.

Conditioning and Waterproofing

I condition my lowa hunting boots every 50-75 miles of use or monthly during hunting season. Quality leather conditioner maintains flexibility and prevents cracking. For waterproofing, I apply DWR treatment annually or when water no longer beads on the surface. This simple maintenance has kept my boots performing like new after thousands of hunting miles.

Storage and Off-Season Care

Proper storage prevents deterioration during off-season. I store my trekking boots in a cool, dry place with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape. The investment in quality storage pays dividends in boot longevity and performance consistency.
