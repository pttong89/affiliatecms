Understanding Plantar Fasciitis & Shoe Requirements

Plantar fasciitis affects the thick band of tissue connecting your heel to your toes, causing stabbing heel pain that's often worst in the morning. During my extensive hiking expeditions across national parks, I've encountered countless fellow outdoor enthusiasts struggling with this condition, which affects approximately 10% of the population.

As foot specialists explain, there are two primary types of plantar fasciitis pain patterns that directly influence shoe selection. Understanding your specific type is crucial for choosing the right On Cloud model.

Types of Plantar Fasciitis Pain

Compression-Type Pain

Pain occurs primarily at heel strike when your foot first contacts the ground. This type benefits from maximum cushioning and shock absorption, making shoes like the Cloudmonster 2 ideal.

Stretch-Type Pain

Pain worsens during push-off phase as the plantar fascia stretches. This type requires stability features and controlled flexibility, making the Cloudrunner 2 the preferred choice.

The key to successful plantar fasciitis management through footwear lies in matching the shoe's characteristics to your specific pain pattern and foot mechanics. Through my collaboration with podiatrists and extensive field testing, I've learned that the right shoe can dramatically reduce pain and improve mobility.

Essential Shoe Features for Plantar Fasciitis

  • Arch Support: Proper arch support reduces strain on the plantar fascia by supporting the foot's natural curve
  • Heel Cushioning: Adequate heel padding absorbs impact and reduces compression forces during heel strike
  • Heel-to-Toe Drop: A 6-12mm drop helps position the foot to reduce plantar fascia tension
  • Rigid Heel Counter: Provides stability and prevents excessive heel movement that can aggravate symptoms
  • Adequate Toe Room: Prevents pressure points and allows natural toe movement during gait
