The Disco 15 Endless Promise performs admirably in its rated temperature range, and I found the EN Comfort rating to be quite conservative. The 650-fill power hydrophobic down provides excellent loft and warmth retention, while the generous draft collar and full-length draft tube effectively seal out cold air. For those considering 2-person sleeping bags, the Disco can be zipped together with another Disco (men's and women's versions have opposing zippers) to create a spacious double bag perfect for couples who want to share warmth while maintaining individual comfort zones.

The NEMO Riff series takes the beloved Spoon shape concept and optimizes it for serious backpackers who count every ounce. With 800-fill power down and a more athletic cut, the Riff achieves remarkable weight savings while maintaining the comfort advantages that make nemo sleeping bags special.

Ultralight Spoon Shape Design

The Riff's Athletic Spoon shape strikes an impressive balance between weight savings and comfort. During my Colorado high country testing, the Riff's more tailored fit actually felt warmer in cold conditions due to less dead air space to heat.

The 800-fill power down is noticeably superior to the Disco's 650-fill, compressing smaller and providing better warmth-to-weight ratio. For thru-hikers and ultralight enthusiasts exploring options like hiking bags, the Riff represents an excellent compromise between comfort and packability.

Performance in Extreme Conditions

What impressed me most about the Riff was its performance in challenging conditions. The higher fill power down maintains loft better in humid conditions, and the 20D ripstop fabric, while lighter, proved surprisingly durable during rugged use. The same Thermo Gills ventilation system as the Disco allows precise temperature regulation, crucial for maintaining comfort during variable mountain weather.

However, the Riff does require more careful consideration of sizing. The athletic cut means less forgiveness for movement, and side sleepers who are particularly active during sleep might find the Disco's roomier design more suitable. For dedicated backpackers seeking the ultimate in winter camping sleeping bags, the Riff's efficiency makes it compelling despite the higher price point.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are NEMO sleeping bags worth the higher price?

Absolutely, especially for side sleepers and those who prioritize comfort. The innovative spoon shape design, quality materials, and thoughtful features like Thermo Gills ventilation justify the premium pricing. After testing numerous brands, the comfort improvement is immediately noticeable and translates to better sleep quality outdoors. For occasional campers on tight budgets, consider budget-friendly alternatives, but serious outdoor enthusiasts will find NEMO bags provide excellent long-term value through improved sleep and durability.

How do NEMO sleeping bags compare to Sea to Summit?

Both brands offer high-quality sleeping bags, but with different strengths. NEMO excels in comfort innovation with their spoon shape design, making them superior for side sleepers and restless sleepers. Sea to Summit sleeping bags often provide better packability and weight savings, particularly in their ultralight models. Sea to Summit tends to offer more technical features and precise temperature ratings, while NEMO focuses on comfort and livability. For pure performance metrics, Sea to Summit might edge ahead, but for actual sleep comfort, NEMO typically wins. Your choice depends on whether you prioritize technical specifications or real-world comfort.

Can you zip two NEMO sleeping bags together?

Yes, but with specific requirements. NEMO designs their men's bags with left-hand zippers and women's bags with right-hand zippers, allowing you to zip a men's and women's bag together to create a double sleeping bag. However, you cannot zip two men's or two women's bags together due to matching zipper orientations. This system works across the Disco, Riff, and Forte lines, creating spacious double sleeping systems perfect for couples. The combined spoon shape provides exceptional room for two people while maintaining warmth efficiency. Some users have successfully modified zippers, but this voids warranties and isn't recommended.

What's the difference between regular and long sizes?

NEMO offers regular and long sizes across their sleeping bag lines, with long versions adding approximately 6 inches in length. Regular bags typically fit people up to 6 feet tall comfortably, while long versions accommodate users up to 6'6". The long versions add roughly 4-6 ounces in weight and increase packed volume slightly. Beyond height accommodation, taller users often benefit from the additional shoulder and hip room in long versions. If you're between 5'10" and 6'2", consider your sleeping style – side sleepers and those who move frequently often prefer the extra room of long versions, while back sleepers can usually manage with regular sizes.

How should I care for my NEMO sleeping bag?

Proper care extends your NEMO sleeping bag's lifespan significantly. Store it uncompressed in the large storage sack, never in the compression sack long-term. Wash only when necessary using down-specific detergent in a front-loading machine on gentle cycle with extra rinse. Dry thoroughly on low heat with tennis balls or dryer balls to restore loft. For field care, air out daily, use a liner to reduce washing frequency, and keep it dry. The Endless Promise models are designed for easier recycling, but proper care still maximizes their useful life. Spot clean minor dirt and stains rather than washing the entire bag when possible.

Are NEMO bags suitable for children?

NEMO doesn't manufacture dedicated children's sleeping bags, but their adult sizes can work for larger children and teenagers. The spoon shape actually benefits active young sleepers who move frequently during sleep. However, proper fit is crucial for warmth, so oversized adult bags may not provide adequate temperature performance for smaller children. For dedicated children's camping sleeping bags, consider specialized youth models from other manufacturers. Teenagers approaching adult size often transition well to NEMO bags, particularly the Disco series, which accommodates various sleep positions comfortably. Always prioritize proper fit over brand preference for children's safety and comfort.
