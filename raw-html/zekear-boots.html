Frequently Asked Questions

Based on hundreds of customer inquiries and my extensive testing, here are the most frequently asked questions about zekear boots.

Are Zekear boots true to size?

Based on customer feedback and my personal experience, zekear boots tend to run slightly small. Many users recommend ordering a half size up, especially if you plan to wear thick socks or have wider feet. The slip-on design means there's less adjustability compared to laced boots, making proper sizing crucial for comfort. I recommend measuring your feet in the evening when they're slightly swollen for the most accurate fit. If you're between sizes, always choose the larger option with zekear boots to avoid discomfort and circulation issues.

How long do Zekear boots last?

With proper care and moderate use, zekear boots typically last 1-2 seasons depending on usage frequency and conditions. They're designed for casual wear rather than heavy-duty use, so longevity varies significantly based on application. For daily urban commuting, expect 6-12 months of good performance. If used only occasionally for light outdoor activities, they may last 18-24 months. The fleece lining tends to maintain its warmth properties throughout the boot's lifespan, while the outer material may show wear first. Regular cleaning and proper storage can extend their usable life.

Are Zekear boots waterproof?

Most zekear boots offer water-resistant properties rather than being fully waterproof. They can handle light rain, snow, and wet sidewalks for short periods but aren't suitable for prolonged exposure to standing water or heavy precipitation. The synthetic suede upper provides some water resistance initially, but this protection diminishes over time with wear and washing. For better water protection, consider applying a water-repellent spray designed for suede materials. If you need truly waterproof footwear, look into specialized outdoor boots like Aquatalia boots which are designed specifically for wet conditions.
