What's the difference between hiking boots and hiking shoes?

Hiking boots extend above the ankle providing superior support, protection, and stability on rough terrain. They're ideal for backpacking, rocky trails, and carrying heavy loads. Hiking shoes sit below the ankle, offering more flexibility and lighter weight but less protection. Top rated men's hiking boots are better for serious hiking, while shoes work well for day hikes on established trails. Choose boots for technical terrain, heavy packs, or unstable surfaces where ankle support prevents injury.

How long do top rated men's hiking boots last?

Top rated men's hiking boots typically last 500-1000 miles depending on usage, terrain, and construction quality. Premium leather boots like the Lowa Renegade can exceed 800 miles with proper care. Lightweight synthetic boots may last 500-700 miles. Factors affecting lifespan include hiking frequency, terrain type, maintenance, and storage conditions. Regular cleaning and waterproofing treatments extend boot life significantly. Quality boots amortize their cost over years of reliable service, making them a smart long-term investment for regular hikers.
