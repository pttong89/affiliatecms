Care and Maintenance Tips

Proper maintenance extends the life of your viking waterproof boots and ensures consistent performance. During my 13 years of professional use, I've developed a maintenance routine that has kept my boots in excellent condition despite extreme use conditions.

Daily Maintenance

  • Post-Use Cleaning: Rinse off mud and debris immediately after use to prevent material degradation
  • Proper Drying: Remove insoles and allow boots to air dry away from direct heat sources
  • Inspection: Quick check for cuts, punctures, or worn areas before next use

Weekly Care

  • Deep Cleaning: Use mild soap and soft brush to clean all surfaces thoroughly
  • Conditioning: Apply appropriate conditioner to leather components (avoid membrane areas)
  • Hardware Check: Inspect laces, eyelets, and buckles for wear or damage

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Problem: Reduced Waterproof Performance

Solution: Clean thoroughly and apply DWR (Durable Water Repellent) treatment to fabric areas. For Gore-Tex models, use Nikwax TX.Direct as recommended by Viking.

My experience: This solution restored full waterproofing in 90% of cases during field testing.

Problem: Sole Separation

Solution: Many Viking boots can be professionally resoled. Contact Viking customer service for authorized repair centers. Temporary field repairs possible with marine-grade adhesive.

Cost comparison: Professional resoling ($60-80) vs. replacement ($150-300)

Problem: Insulation Degradation

Solution: Replace removable insoles with aftermarket options like Superfeet or custom orthotics. For built-in insulation, consider specialized insulated waterproof boots for extreme conditions.

Upgrade tip: High-quality aftermarket insoles often outperform original equipment
