What Are HOKA Flip Flops?

What's the difference between HOKA Ora Recovery slides and flip flops?

The main difference lies in the strap configuration and fit. HOKA Ora Recovery flip flops feature a traditional thong-style toe post design that secures the foot between the big toe and second toe, providing a more locked-in feel during movement. The slides have a single wide strap across the forefoot without the toe post, making them easier to slip on and off. Both use identical midsole and outsole technology, offering the same cushioning and support benefits. Flip flops tend to be better for active use and walking, while slides excel for quick wear around the house or post-workout recovery. Some users with wider feet prefer slides for comfort.

How should HOKA flip flops fit?

HOKA flip flops should fit with your heel positioned properly in the heel cup and toes not hanging over the front edge. Most users recommend going down half a size from your regular running shoe size, as sandals don't require the extra toe room needed in closed shoes. The toe post should feel comfortable without causing irritation â€“ some new pairs may need a brief break-in period. Your foot should sit securely in the footbed without sliding forward or backward during walking. HOKA only makes these in whole sizes, so if you're between sizes, size down rather than up. The strap should feel snug but not tight, allowing natural foot movement.
