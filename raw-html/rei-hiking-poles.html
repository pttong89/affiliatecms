How do I choose the right length for REI hiking poles?

Proper REI hiking pole length creates a 90-degree elbow angle when tips touch the ground near your feet. For general hiking, use this standard position. Shorten poles by 5-10cm for steep uphill sections to improve leverage, and lengthen them by 5-10cm for downhill terrain to maintain better balance and reduce knee impact. Most REI poles adjust from 24-55 inches, accommodating virtually all adult heights. During my field testing, I discovered that proper length dramatically affects hiking efficiency and comfort. Incorrectly sized poles cause shoulder strain, reduced stability, and decreased hiking enjoyment. Always test adjustments on varied terrain to find your optimal settings for different conditions and trail types.

What's the difference between REI's aluminum and carbon fiber hiking poles?

REI's aluminum poles offer superior durability and bend resistance at lower cost, typically weighing 18-22 ounces per pair. Choose aluminum for rugged conditions and budget-consciousness, carbon fiber for weight savings and frequent use. My extensive testing revealed aluminum poles bend rather than break under severe side loads, while carbon poles may fail catastrophically.
