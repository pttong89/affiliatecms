Frequently Asked Questions

Are Merrell Moab GTX boots truly waterproof?

Yes, the Merrell Moab GTX boots feature genuine Gore-Tex waterproof membrane technology that provides excellent water protection. In my testing, I've walked through puddles, creek crossings, and wet morning grass without any water seepage. The Gore-Tex membrane is backed by a gusseted tongue design that prevents water entry from the top. However, like all waterproof boots, breathability is reduced compared to non-waterproof versions, and the waterproofing can degrade over time with heavy use.

How does the sizing run for Merrell Moab GTX?

Based on extensive user feedback and my personal experience, the Merrell Moab GTX runs approximately half a size small. I typically wear size 10 in most brands but needed a 10.5 in the Moab GTX for proper fit. The toe box can feel narrow initially, especially around the 2nd and 3rd toes. I recommend trying them on with hiking socks or ordering a half size up from your normal shoe size. Merrell also offers wide width options for those who need extra room.

What's the difference between Moab 3 GTX and regular Moab 3?

The main differences are waterproofing and breathability. The Moab 3 GTX features Gore-Tex waterproof membrane for complete water protection, while the regular Moab 3 uses Merrell's proprietary waterproof membrane or comes in non-waterproof versions. GTX versions are typically $20-40 more expensive but offer superior waterproofing. However, the regular Moab 3 waterproof version provides better breathability. The GTX also weighs slightly more due to the Gore-Tex lining. Choose GTX for maximum water protection in wet conditions.

How long do Merrell Moab GTX boots typically last?

With proper care, Merrell Moab GTX boots typically last 300-500 miles of hiking, or 1-2 years of regular use. The Vibram TC5+ outsole is quite durable, and I've seen minimal wear after 200+ miles of varied terrain. The upper materials (leather and mesh) hold up well to abrasion. However, the Gore-Tex membrane may begin to lose effectiveness after 12-18 months of regular use. Factors affecting longevity include hiking frequency, terrain difficulty, care and maintenance, and individual gait patterns.

Can you use Merrell Moab GTX for backpacking?

Yes, the Merrell Moab GTX works well for light to moderate backpacking with packs up to 30-35 pounds. The supportive midsole, ankle stability, and durable construction handle multi-day trips effectively. I've used mine on 3-day backpacking trips without issues. However, for heavy loads over 40 pounds or very technical terrain, a proper backpacking boot with higher ankle support would be better. The low-cut design limits ankle protection compared to mid or high-cut boots, so consider your typical terrain and pack weight when deciding.
