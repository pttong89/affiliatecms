Complete Buying Guide: What to Look For

Choosing the best Skechers for walking womens requires understanding your specific needs, foot type, and walking patterns. After three years of testing and hundreds of conversations with fellow walkers, I've identified the key factors that separate good walking shoes from great ones.

1. Understand Your Foot Type

High Arches: Need extra cushioning and flexibility. The GO WALK Joy or Summits work best with their soft midsoles and adaptable design.

Flat Feet/Overpronation: Require motion control and arch support. The Arch Fit 2.0 or Max Cushioning Arch Fit provide the stability needed to prevent ankle rolling and reduce knee stress.

Normal Arches: Can wear most Skechers models comfortably. Focus on your specific comfort preferences and intended use rather than corrective features.

Weight Considerations

Lighter shoes (under 8oz) reduce fatigue during long walks but may sacrifice durability. Heavier models (8-10oz) typically offer better support and cushioning for extended wear.

Seasonal Factors

Summer walking requires maximum breathability - choose mesh uppers. For year-round use, consider waterproof options for wet conditions.

2. Fit and Sizing Guidelines

Most Skechers run true to size, but individual models vary slightly. During my testing, I found that slip-on models like the GO WALK Flex tend to run slightly large initially but conform to your feet after several wears. Lace-up models generally provide the most adjustable fit.

Important: Always measure your feet in the afternoon when they're slightly swollen from daily activities. This ensures a comfortable fit during actual walking conditions.

For women needing wider fits, several models offer wide width options. The GO WALK series typically accommodates wider feet better than the more structured Arch Fit models.

Which Skechers model is best for long-distance walking?

The Skechers Max Cushioning Arch Fit is considered the best for long-distance walking. It provides maximum cushioning with a 36mm heel height, excellent arch support, and superior durability. How long do Skechers walking shoes typically last?

Skechers walking shoes typically last 300-500 miles or 6-12 months with regular use. The durability depends on your walking frequency, surface types, and shoe model. Higher-end models like the Max Cushioning series tend to last longer, while lighter models may show wear sooner but offer superior comfort. Do Skechers walking shoes run true to size?

Most Skechers walking shoes run true to size, though some users report they run slightly large. The GO WALK collection tends to be true to size, while slip-on models might feel looser initially but conform to your feet over time. I recommend trying them on or ordering your regular size first, with the option to exchange if needed. During my testing, I found that lace-up models offered the most adjustable fit, while slip-ons required more consideration of foot shape and preferred snugness level.

Can you wash Skechers walking shoes?

Many Skechers walking shoes are machine washable, particularly the GO WALK series. Check the shoe tongue or product description for washing instructions. Generally, remove insoles, use cold water, gentle cycle, and air dry. Avoid putting them in the dryer as heat can damage the materials and affect the fit. I've successfully machine washed my GO WALK Joy shoes multiple times, and they maintained their shape and comfort. However, always verify the specific model's care instructions as some premium materials may require hand washing only.

What's the difference between GO WALK and Arch Fit series?

GO WALK focuses on lightweight comfort with responsive cushioning and flexible design, ideal for casual walking. Arch Fit series emphasizes support with podiatrist-designed insoles, firmer midsoles, and targeted arch support, better for people needing stability or dealing with foot issues. Arch Fit is recommended for longer walks and those with overpronation. During my comparison testing, I found GO WALK models offered superior initial comfort and easier walking motion, while Arch Fit models provided better long-term foot health benefits and reduced fatigue during extended use.
