Complete Sizing and Fitting Guide

Understanding SAS shoe sizing was crucial for my successful experience with SAS shoes near me. Unlike standard shoe brands that focus primarily on length, SAS emphasizes both length and width fitting, which explains why their shoes feel so comfortable for extended wear. The sizing process becomes especially important when you realize that 85% of people wear shoes that don't fit properly, leading to foot problems and discomfort.

SAS offers more width options than virtually any other comfort shoe brand. Women's widths range from slim (AAA) through triple wide (EEE), while men's widths span from narrow (C) to triple wide (EEEE). During my fitting experience, I discovered I needed a wide width in SAS shoes despite wearing medium width in other brands. This difference occurs because SAS shoes are constructed on lasts designed for optimal foot health rather than fashion aesthetics.

Professional measurement at a SAS retailer involves more than just standing on a Brannock device. The fitter will measure both feet (as they're often different sizes), assess your arch height, and observe your gait pattern. They'll also ask about any foot problems, daily activity levels, and intended use. This comprehensive approach ensures you get shoes that support your specific needs, whether you're looking for everyday comfort or specialized footwear for backpacking adventures.

When trying on SAS shoes, proper fitting involves more than just ensuring your toes don't touch the front. The heel should feel secure without slipping, the arch support should align with your foot's natural arch, and there should be minimal pressure points across the top of your foot. I spent considerable time learning these fitting principles, which proved invaluable when helping family members find their proper SAS sizes.

For those shopping online, SAS provides detailed sizing guides for each style, as different models can fit differently even in the same size. Pro Sizing Tips

Always shop for SAS shoes in the afternoon when your feet are naturally slightly swollen, mimicking how they'll feel during extended wear. Bring the type of socks you plan to wear regularly, and if you use custom orthotics, bring those as well for proper fitting.

Remember that SAS shoes may feel different initially as they support your feet in ways you're not used to. Allow 1-2 weeks for adjustment, but the shoes should never cause pain or pressure points from day one.
