How to Choose the Right Size and Fit

Proper sizing and fit are absolutely crucial for supportive walking sandalsâ€”perhaps even more so than with regular shoes. Unlike enclosed footwear that can accommodate minor sizing discrepancies, sandals require precise fit to deliver their intended support and prevent issues like sliding, pressure points, and reduced effectiveness of arch support features.

Start by measuring both feet in the evening when they're at their largest due to natural swelling from daily activity. Many people discover they have different-sized feet during this processâ€”always size for the larger foot. When measuring, stand on a piece of paper and trace around both feet, then measure from heel to longest toe. This measurement should be compared against each brand's specific sizing chart, as sizes can vary significantly between manufacturers of supportive sandals.

Width considerations are equally important for supportive sandals. If you have wide feet, look for brands that offer wide width options or adjustable strap systems that can accommodate broader feet without compromising support. Conversely, those with narrow feet should seek sandals with multiple adjustment points to achieve a secure fit that prevents sliding within the sandal. During my testing, I found that many users incorrectly size up to accommodate width issues, which actually reduces the effectiveness of arch support features.

How do I know if my walking sandals have adequate arch support?

Adequate arch support in walking sandals should feel immediately noticeable when you step into them. Your arch should feel cradled and supported without any gaps between your foot and the footbed. Quality arch support should feel firm yet comfortable, providing a sense of stability and proper foot positioning.
