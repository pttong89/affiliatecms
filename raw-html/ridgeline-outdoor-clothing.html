In my field testing, the Monsoon maintains better breathability during extended wear periods and offers superior durability for rough use. The Torrent series sacrifices some durability and breathability for reduced weight and pack size, making it ideal for backpacking and fast-and-light applications. For general waterproof outdoor clothing needs, either series will exceed expectations, but your specific use patterns should guide the choice between maximum protection and minimum weight.

How do I care for and maintain Ridgeline waterproof clothing?

Proper care extends the life and performance of Ridgeline outdoor clothing significantly. Machine wash in cool water with technical fabric detergent, avoiding fabric softeners that can compromise waterproof treatments. I recommend Nikwax Tech Wash for cleaning and TX.Direct for reproofing treatments every 10-15 washes or when water no longer beads on the surface. Tumble dry on low heat to reactivate DWR treatments, or use a warm iron over parchment paper for the same effect. Store garments uncompressed in cool, dry conditions. For field maintenance, brush off dirt and debris rather than frequent washing, as the mechanical action of cleaning gradually degrades waterproof treatments. With proper care, Ridgeline garments maintain peak performance for many years of intensive use.
