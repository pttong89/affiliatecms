Sizing, Fit, and Comfort: What You Need to Know

Proper fit is crucial for tennis shoes, as inadequate sizing can lead to blisters, discomfort, and reduced performance. Through my testing experience with multiple Under Armour models and consultation with podiatrists, I've developed a comprehensive understanding of how these shoes fit different foot types. The sizing consistency across under armour tennis shoes has impressed me, though individual models may have specific considerations.

Under Armour generally runs true to size, but I recommend measuring your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally slightly swollen from daily activity. This measurement more accurately represents your foot size during tennis play. The brand's width options accommodate various foot shapes, with regular, wide, and extra-wide options available in most models. For players with narrow feet, I suggest trying the regular width first, as Under Armour's construction tends to run slightly narrow compared to some competitors.

The break-in period for Under Armour tennis shoes is typically minimal, usually requiring only 2-3 playing sessions to achieve optimal comfort. During this period, I recommend gradually increasing playing time to allow the materials to conform to your foot shape. The synthetic and mesh uppers used in most models are designed to stretch and adapt while maintaining structural support.

For players with specific foot conditions such as plantar fasciitis or overpronation, Under Armour offers models with enhanced arch support and motion control features. The best Under Armour shoes for these conditions typically feature firmer midsole construction and additional heel counter support. I always recommend consulting with a sports medicine professional for specific medical concerns.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Under Armour tennis shoes good for wide feet?

Under Armour offers wide and extra-wide options in most tennis shoe models, making them suitable for wider feet. However, the standard width tends to run slightly narrow compared to some competitors. I recommend trying wide sizes if you typically wear regular width in other brands. The materials do stretch slightly during the break-in period, but proper initial fit is crucial for comfort. Many users with wide feet report excellent experiences once they find the correct width sizing.

How long do Under Armour tennis shoes typically last?

Based on my testing and user feedback, Under Armour tennis shoes typically last 300-500 hours of court time, depending on playing style and court surface. Aggressive baseline players may see shorter lifespans due to increased lateral movement and toe dragging. The outsole durability is particularly impressive, often outlasting the upper materials. Regular rotation between multiple pairs can extend overall shoe life. Proper care, including cleaning after use and allowing shoes to air dry, significantly impacts longevity. Most recreational players can expect 6-12 months of regular use from a single pair.

What's the difference between Under Armour running shoes and tennis shoes?

Under Armour tennis shoes feature enhanced lateral support and reinforced toe areas specifically designed for the side-to-side movements in tennis. The outsole patterns are optimized for court traction rather than forward motion. Tennis shoes typically have lower heel-to-toe drop ratios for better court feel and stability. Running shoes prioritize forward motion cushioning and flexibility in the toe box. While you can use running shoes for casual tennis, dedicated tennis shoes provide superior performance and injury prevention for serious play. The construction methods and materials also differ to accommodate the specific demands of each sport.

Can I use Under Armour tennis shoes for other activities?

Under Armour tennis shoes work well for various court sports including pickleball, racquetball, and casual basketball. The lateral support makes them suitable for gym workouts and cross-training activities. However, they're not optimal for running due to different cushioning and support patterns. The durable construction handles walking and casual wear well, though dedicated walking shoes might be more comfortable for extended periods. I've successfully used tennis shoes for light hiking on well-maintained trails, though proper hiking footwear is recommended for serious outdoor activities. The versatility makes them excellent for active individuals who participate in multiple sports.

Do Under Armour tennis shoes run true to size?

Under Armour tennis shoes generally run true to size in length but tend toward a narrower fit compared to brands like New Balance or ASICS. Most users find their normal size appropriate, though those with wider feet may need to size up by half a size or choose wide width options. The sizing consistency across different Under Armour models is excellent, so once you determine your size in one model, it typically translates to others. I recommend measuring feet in the afternoon when they're naturally slightly swollen for the most accurate sizing. When in doubt, ordering two sizes and returning the one that doesn't fit perfectly is often the best approach for online purchases.
