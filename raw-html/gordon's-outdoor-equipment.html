Gear Testing and Evaluation

Proper gear evaluation goes beyond reading specifications. Physical testing reveals how equipment performs in real conditions and whether it suits your specific needs and preferences.

In-Store Testing Checklist

  • • Try on boots with your typical hiking socks
  • • Test backpack fit with realistic load weights
  • • Operate all zippers, buckles, and adjustment systems
  • • Check fabric hand-feel and construction quality
  • • Verify size charts against your actual measurements

Pro Tip: Gordon's staff encourages thorough in-store testing. Don't feel rushed – take time to properly evaluate expensive purchases, and ask questions about return policies for items that don't perform as expected in the field.

Understanding Gear Specifications

Marketing specifications can be misleading without proper context. Learning to interpret technical specifications helps identify gear that will actually meet your needs.

Key Metrics to Understand

  • • Waterproof Ratings: 10,000mm+ for serious weather protection
  • • Fill Power (Down): 650+ for good warmth-to-weight ratio
  • • Denier (Fabric Weight): Higher numbers = more durable, heavier fabric
  • • Temperature Ratings: Consider comfort vs. survival ratings for sleeping bags

Common Purchasing Mistakes to Avoid

  • • Buying gear for aspirational activities before mastering basics
  • • Choosing ultra-lightweight gear without considering durability needs
  • • Ignoring proper fit in favor of brand loyalty or price
  • • Purchasing complete gear systems without testing individual components
  • • Overlooking local climate conditions in favor of gear designed for other environments
  • • Buying cheap safety equipment as a false economy
