Frequently Asked Questions

Are Inkkas hiking boots waterproof?

Inkkas hiking boots are not fully waterproof in the technical sense. While the leather construction provides some natural water resistance, they lack specialized waterproof membranes like Gore-Tex found in dedicated hiking boots. The boots can handle light moisture and brief puddle exposure but will eventually allow water penetration during extended wet conditions. For consistently wet environments, I recommend treating them with leather waterproofing products or choosing technical waterproof alternatives. However, for dry climate hiking and occasional moisture exposure, their natural water resistance proves adequate.

How do Inkkas hiking boots fit compared to regular shoes?

Based on my extensive testing and user feedback analysis, Inkkas hiking boots typically run narrow and about a half-size small compared to standard athletic shoes. The toe box is more tapered than conventional hiking boots, which can cause discomfort for those with wider feet. I strongly recommend ordering a half-size up from your normal shoe size, especially if you plan to wear thick hiking socks. Many users report needing to exchange their initial purchase for proper sizing. The ankle area fits snugly, providing good support once properly sized, but the narrow construction may not suit all foot shapes.

Third, the artisan craftsmanship and attention to detail exceed typical mass-produced hiking boots. However, they sacrifice some technical features like advanced waterproofing and aggressive tread patterns in favor of versatile styling that transitions from trails to urban environments.

How long do Inkkas hiking boots last with regular use?

The durability of Inkkas hiking boots varies significantly based on usage patterns, care practices, and individual foot mechanics. For maximum longevity, rotate between multiple pairs, use appropriate leather care products, and avoid extreme conditions that exceed their design limitations. The handcrafted construction typically outlasts mass-produced alternatives when properly maintained.

However, they excel for casual hiking, travel adventures, and situations where you need versatile footwear that performs on trails while looking appropriate in social settings. For serious backpacking, consider them as camp shoes or supplement with technical hiking boots for demanding terrain.
