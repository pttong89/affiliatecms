Size and Fit Considerations

Proper fit is crucial for any mountain equipment backpack. I've seen too many climbers struggle with poorly fitted packs, especially during long approaches or technical sections. Mountain Equipment's sizing runs slightly smaller than mainstream brands, reflecting their alpine heritage.

My Fitting Protocol

  1. Measure torso length: From C7 vertebra to iliac crest (top of hip bones)
  2. Load the pack: Always test with 10-15kg of gear to assess true fit
  3. Check shoulder alignment: Straps should curve over shoulders without gaps
  4. Hip belt position: Should sit on hip bones, not waist
  5. Movement test: Simulate climbing movements and stride patterns
