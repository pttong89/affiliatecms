Fit and Sizing Considerations

Proper fit can make or break your hiking experience. Through years of testing different brands and models, I've discovered that Go Outdoors men's walking shoes often run slightly different than athletic shoes. Most hiking footwear should feel snug around the heel and midfoot while allowing approximately one thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the shoe's front.

Always try on go outdoors mens walking shoes in the afternoon when your feet have naturally swelled to their largest daily size. Wear the same thickness of socks you'll use for hikingâ€”typically merino wool or synthetic hiking socks rather than thin cotton varieties. This ensures the most accurate fit assessment.
