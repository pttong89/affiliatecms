Complete Buying Guide

Sizing and Fit Considerations

Proper sizing is crucial for under armour waterproof boots performance. Based on extensive testing with different foot types, I recommend ordering a half-size up from your normal athletic shoe size. The waterproof membrane and thicker construction require additional room for comfort during long wear periods.

Important Sizing Tip: Under Armour boots run slightly narrow compared to other brands. Those with wide feet should consider the 4E width options when available.

  • Measure feet in the afternoon when they're naturally swollen
  • Account for hiking sock thickness (typically add 0.5 size)
  • Allow thumb-width space between longest toe and boot front

Care & Maintenance Tips

Proper care extends the life of your under armour waterproof boots significantly. Through my testing and conversations with Under Armour customer service, I've developed a maintenance routine that preserves both waterproofing and overall boot integrity.

Cleaning Protocol

  1. 1 Remove laces and insoles before cleaning
  2. 2 Use lukewarm water and mild soap (avoid harsh detergents)
  3. 3 Scrub gently with soft brush for stubborn dirt
  4. 4 Air dry away from direct heat sources

Waterproofing Maintenance

  • Reapply DWR treatment every 3-4 months
  • Test waterproofing with water droplets regularly
  • Use products specifically designed for technical fabrics
  • Avoid fabric softeners which can damage membranes

What NOT to Do

  • • Never put boots in washing machine or dryer
  • • Avoid petroleum-based products that can damage membranes
  • • Don't use excessive heat for drying (damages waterproof coating)
  • • Avoid storing in damp areas (promotes mold growth)

Storage and Seasonal Care

When storing under armour waterproof boots seasonally, ensure they're completely clean and dry. I recommend stuffing them with newspaper to maintain shape and storing in a breathable bag. Cedar shoe trees work excellently for maintaining form, especially important given the technical construction of these boots.

For those using boots professionally or frequently, rotation is key. Having two pairs allows each to completely dry between uses, significantly extending overall lifespan. This approach has proven especially valuable for the athletic footwear variants that see daily use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Under Armour waterproof boots truly waterproof?

Yes, under armour waterproof boots feature genuine waterproof membranes that block liquid water while allowing moisture vapor to escape. Through my testing in various wet conditions—from creek crossings to prolonged rain exposure—they've maintained complete water protection. However, like all waterproof footwear, the seal depends on proper fit and membrane integrity. I've never experienced water intrusion through the membrane itself, though poor sealing around the ankle opening can occur with improper sizing or extreme conditions.

How do Under Armour boots compare to traditional hiking brands?

Under Armour brings athletic performance technology to outdoor footwear, resulting in lighter, more comfortable boots compared to traditional hiking brands like Merrell or Salomon. The trade-off is typically in extreme durability—while perfectly adequate for most users, they may not withstand the abuse that heavy-duty mountaineering boots can handle. However, for general hiking, tactical use, and outdoor work, they often provide superior comfort and comparable weather protection at competitive pricing.

What's the break-in period for Under Armour waterproof boots?

Most under armour waterproof boots have minimal break-in requirements due to their athletic-inspired design and materials. I typically recommend 2-3 short walks (1-2 miles each) before committing to longer adventures. The synthetic materials and engineered fit reduce hot spots common with leather boots. However, models with more leather components or tactical designs may require up to a week of regular wear. The key is gradual increase in wear time rather than jumping into full-day use immediately.

Can these boots handle extreme weather conditions?

Under Armour waterproof boots excel in moderate to challenging weather but have limitations in extreme conditions. They're excellent for rain, snow, mud, and wet terrain from spring through fall. However, for extreme cold (below 10°F), mountaineering conditions, or desert environments, specialized footwear is recommended. The waterproof membrane performs best in temperatures between 20-80°F, and the breathability that makes them comfortable can become a liability in very cold conditions without proper insulation layers.

How long do Under Armour waterproof boots typically last?

Lifespan varies significantly based on usage patterns. For casual hiking and outdoor activities, expect 2-3 years or 500+ miles of use. Professional or daily wear applications typically see 12-18 months before replacement. The waterproof membrane generally outlasts other boot components, with sole wear and upper material breakdown being primary failure points. Regular maintenance, proper cleaning, and rotating between multiple pairs can extend lifespan considerably. Heavy industrial use may reduce this to 6-12 months, making them better suited for moderate work applications.

Are there specific care requirements for the waterproof membrane?

The waterproof membrane in under armour waterproof boots requires specific care to maintain performance. Avoid harsh detergents, fabric softeners, and excessive heat that can damage the membrane structure. Use technical fabric cleaners and reapply DWR (Durable Water Repellent) treatments every 3-4 months for optimal performance. The membrane itself is durable, but the exterior DWR coating needs periodic renewal. I've found that boots maintained properly retain their waterproof properties throughout their useful life, while neglected pairs may lose water resistance in as little as 6 months.
