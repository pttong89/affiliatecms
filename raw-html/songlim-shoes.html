The Historic Legacy of Songlim Shoes

The story of Songlim shoes begins in 1936, during the Japanese colonial period, when Lee Gyu-seok founded what was originally called Songrim Hwajeom in a small workshop on Euljiro 3-ga in Seoul. What started as a modest shoemaking venture has evolved into one of Korea's most prestigious and enduring handmade footwear institutions, now spanning four generations of dedicated craftsmen.

As someone who has extensively researched Korean hiking culture, I find the continuity of Songlim shoes remarkable. The business has remained in the same location since its founding, weathering the Korean War, rapid industrialization, and countless economic changes. Around 1950-1953, during the Korean War era, Songlim shoes began specializing in hiking boots, basing their designs on British military boots and adapting them for civilian mountaineering use.

The legacy of excellence is demonstrated through the notable figures who have worn Songlim shoes. In 1977, renowned mountaineer Go Sang-don wore Songlim boots while climbing Annapurna, one of the world's most challenging peaks. Even more impressively, in 1995, explorer Heo Yeong-ho traversed both the South and North poles wearing Songlim shoes. Former South Korean President Kim Young-sam was reportedly a regular customer, testament to the brand's reputation for quality and prestige.

Today, the company operates under Im Myeong-hyeong, representing the third generation of family ownership. Im has been working at the store since 1988 and brings decades of experience to maintaining the traditional craftsmanship methods that make Songlim shoes legendary. The business holds the prestigious designation of Seoul Future Heritage, recognizing its cultural and historical significance to Korea's industrial heritage.

Materials and Construction Techniques

The construction of Songlim shoes follows traditional Goodyear welting techniques, ensuring that soles can be replaced multiple times throughout the boot's lifetime. This approach means that a well-maintained pair can last decades with proper care and periodic resoling.

Each boot features hand-stitched construction throughout, from the upper assembly to the sole attachment. The welt stitching alone requires exceptional skill, as it must be both durable and waterproof. The craftsmen use heavy-duty thread and reinforce all stress points to ensure the boots can withstand extreme conditions.
