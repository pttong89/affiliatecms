Frequently Asked Questions

How long do Skechers last when worn daily for standing jobs?

Based on extensive user feedback and my personal experience, Skechers designed for standing typically last 6-12 months with daily 8+ hour use. The memory foam maintains its support properties for approximately 300-400 miles of walking, equivalent to about 4-6 months of regular workplace standing and walking. Premium models like the Max Cushioning Arch Fit tend to last longer due to superior construction materials. To maximize lifespan, I recommend rotating between two pairs and allowing 24 hours between wears for foam recovery. Proper care, including regular cleaning and avoiding extreme temperatures, can extend their useful life significantly.

Are Skechers suitable for people with plantar fasciitis or flat feet?

Many Skechers models, particularly the Arch Fit series, are specifically designed to address common foot conditions like plantar fasciitis and flat feet. The Arch Fit technology features a podiatrist-certified insole system that provides targeted arch support and helps maintain proper foot alignment during extended standing. However, individual needs vary significantly, and I strongly recommend consulting with a podiatrist before making a purchase. Many users with mild to moderate plantar fasciitis report significant improvement when transitioning to supportive Skechers models. For severe conditions, custom orthotic insoles may be necessary and can often be accommodated in Skechers with removable footbeds.

What's the difference between Go Walk and Max Cushioning series?

The primary differences lie in construction complexity and intended use intensity. Go Walk series emphasizes lightweight comfort and everyday versatility, featuring moderate cushioning (typically 23-27mm) and flexible construction ideal for general walking and light standing duties. The Max Cushioning series provides enhanced support with thicker midsoles (30-36mm), firmer stability features, and advanced technologies like Arch Fit insoles, making them better suited for extended standing periods and users requiring additional foot support. Max Cushioning models are heavier but offer superior durability and support for demanding workplace environments. Choose Go Walk for comfort and mobility; select Max Cushioning for maximum support and extended standing performance.

Can I wear Skechers walking shoes for exercise and running?

While Skechers walking shoes excel at their intended purpose, they're not optimized for high-impact exercise or serious running. The memory foam and flexible construction prioritize comfort over the stability and energy return needed for athletic activities. For occasional light jogging or gym walking, models like the Go Walk 7 can suffice, but dedicated running shoes would be more appropriate for regular exercise routines. The walking-specific design features that make these shoes excellent for standing—soft midsoles, flexible construction, and comfort-focused geometry—actually work against efficient running biomechanics. If you need versatility for both standing work and exercise, consider cross-training alternatives or maintain separate shoes for different activities.

How do I choose the right size in Skechers walking shoes?

Skechers sizing can vary between models, but generally runs true to size with some models running slightly large. For the best fit, measure your feet in the afternoon when they're naturally expanded, as this mimics the swelling that occurs during long standing periods. Most users find their normal shoe size works well, but those with narrow feet may benefit from going down a half-size, while those with wide feet might need to size up. The stretchy mesh uppers in models like Go Walk accommodate various foot shapes, but ensure adequate toe room for expansion during extended wear. Many Skechers offer wide-width options for better accommodation. I recommend ordering from retailers with easy return policies to test the fit, as comfort is highly individual and what works for one person may not work for another.
