Brand Overview and Philosophy

The Thursday Boot Company Story

Founded in 2014 by Connor Wilson and Nolan Walsh, Thursday Boot Company emerged from a simple frustration: quality boots were either prohibitively expensive or poorly made. The founders believed there was a gap in the market for well-constructed, stylish boots at an accessible price point. Their mission was to create boots that combined the durability of work boots with the sophistication of dress boots, all while maintaining affordability.

Based in New York City, thursdayboots has grown from a startup to one of America's most recognizable heritage boot brands. Their direct-to-consumer model allows them to offer Goodyear welted boots at prices typically reserved for cemented construction footwear. This approach has democratized quality footwear, making premium boot construction accessible to a broader audience.

Construction and Materials Philosophy

Thursday boots utilize Goodyear welt construction, a time-tested method that creates a durable, resoleable boot. This construction technique, traditionally found only in boots costing $400 or more, involves stitching the upper leather to a leather midsole through a leather strip called a welt. The outsole is then stitched to the welt, creating a strong, flexible bond that can be resoled multiple times.

The brand sources leather from reputable tanneries, focusing on full-grain leather that develops character over time. Their signature leather offerings include their proprietary Thursday Chrome leather, known for its durability and low-maintenance properties, and various pull-up leathers that develop rich patinas with wear.

Sizing Guide and Fit Analysis

Understanding Thursday Boot Sizing

One of the most critical aspects of purchasing thursdayboots online is getting the sizing right. Based on my experience and extensive research within the Thursday boots community, I've discovered several key insights that can help ensure a perfect fit on your first order.

Thursday boots consistently run approximately half a size larger than standard athletic shoes. If you typically wear a size 10 in Nike or Adidas sneakers, you'll likely need a size 9.5 in Thursday boots. This sizing characteristic is consistent across most of their models, though individual foot shape and personal preference for fit snugness can influence the ideal size choice.

Personal Sizing Experience

I wear a 10.5 in most athletic shoes and a 10D on the Brannock device (the standard foot measuring tool). After trying multiple sizes, I found that a size 10 in Thursday boots provides the ideal fit—snug enough to prevent heel slippage while allowing adequate toe room for all-day comfort. The boots felt slightly tight initially but stretched perfectly during the break-in period.

The 2023 Last Update Impact

Thursday updated their boot last (the foot-shaped form used to construct the boot) in 2023, transitioning to what they call the "Kingmaker" last. This update addressed some previous fit issues, particularly for people with wider feet or high insteps. The new last provides more room in the toe box while maintaining the sleek profile that defines the Thursday aesthetic.

If you're ordering boots manufactured after mid-2023, you'll benefit from this improved fit. However, if you previously owned Thursday boots and found them tight, you might find the updated models more accommodating. Some long-time Thursday enthusiasts actually prefer the original last for its more snug, precise fit.

Sizing Best Practices

  1. Measure in the afternoon: Feet swell throughout the day, so afternoon measurements are more accurate
  2. Consider sock thickness: Account for the type of socks you'll typically wear with boots
  3. Read recent reviews: Look for sizing feedback from customers with similar foot measurements
  4. Understand the return policy: Thursday offers exchanges, so take advantage if needed
  5. Consider width: Thursday boots are built on a D-width last, which may feel narrow for some

Width Considerations and Solutions

Thursday boots are constructed on a standard D-width last, which works well for most foot types but can feel restrictive for those with wider feet. If you have wide feet (E or EE width), you have several options to achieve a comfortable fit.

First, consider sizing up half a size to accommodate width. While this may create slight length excess, quality boot socks can help fill the extra space. Alternatively, professional boot stretching can widen the leather upper by up to half a width size, though this should be done by an experienced cobbler to avoid damaging the boot's structure.

For those with high insteps, the lacing technique can significantly impact comfort. I recommend using a parallel lacing pattern rather than crossing laces, as this provides more even pressure distribution and accommodates higher foot volumes more comfortably.

Essential Care and Maintenance Guide

Daily Care Routines

Proper care is essential for maximizing the lifespan and appearance of your thursdayboots. Based on three years of hands-on experience, I've developed a care routine that keeps my boots looking great while preserving their structure and comfort.

After each wear, I remove debris with a horsehair brush and allow the boots to air dry completely if they've gotten wet. Using cedar shoe trees immediately after removal helps maintain shape and accelerates drying by wicking moisture from the interior. This simple routine prevents most common issues like leather cracking and shape deterioration.

Weekly Maintenance Checklist

  • Brush with horsehair brush to remove surface dirt and restore leather luster
  • Inspect for new scuffs, scratches, or wear areas requiring attention
  • Check lacing for fraying or stretching that might affect fit
  • Rotate with other footwear to allow 24-48 hours rest between wears
  • Apply spot treatment for any stains or discoloration immediately

Seasonal Deep Cleaning and Conditioning

Every 3-4 months, I perform a thorough conditioning treatment using high-quality leather conditioner. I prefer products like Venetian Shoe Cream or Lexol for their balance of nourishment and protection without over-softening the leather structure.

The conditioning process involves removing laces, cleaning with a damp cloth, applying conditioner with a soft cloth in circular motions, and allowing 24-hour absorption time. This treatment keeps the leather supple and prevents cracking while maintaining the boot's structural integrity.

Weatherproofing and Protection Strategies

Thursday boots aren't waterproof out of the box, so weatherproofing is crucial for wet climate wear. I apply a leather waterproofing spray every 2-3 months, focusing on the leather upper while avoiding the stitched areas which can absorb too much product.

Addressing Common Issues

The most common issue I've encountered is insole compression after 12-18 months. Replacing the original Poron insoles with high-quality aftermarket options like Superfeet or Dr. Scholl's can restore comfort and extend the boot's usable life.

Heel wear is another common concern. Regular inspection allows for early intervention through heel pads or professional repair before the wear becomes extensive. I've found that addressing heel wear early costs $20-30 versus $60-80 for complete heel replacement.

For scuffs and scratches, I keep a small tin of matching shoe cream for immediate touch-ups. This prevents small issues from becoming major cosmetic problems and maintains the boots' professional appearance.
