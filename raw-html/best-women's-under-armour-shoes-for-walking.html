Complete Buying Guide

Choosing the best women's Under Armour shoes for walking requires understanding your specific needs, foot type, and walking habits. After years of helping clients select appropriate footwear, I've developed a systematic approach that ensures you get the perfect match for your lifestyle and biomechanics.

Understanding Your Foot Type

The first step in selecting proper walking shoes is understanding your foot mechanics. Under Armour designs their shoes with different arch support levels and heel-to-toe drops to accommodate various foot types. If you have flat feet or low arches, look for models with enhanced arch support like the Charged Assert series. Those with high arches typically benefit from the softer, more accommodating cushioning found in HOVR models.

Sizing and Fit Considerations

Under Armour women's shoes typically run true to size, but I recommend shopping for shoes in the afternoon when your feet are naturally slightly swollen, mimicking how they'll feel during longer walks. Allow for about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the shoe's end. The heel should feel secure without slipping, and there should be no pressure points across the top of your foot. For reference, if you wear size 8 in Under Armour tennis shoes, you'll likely wear the same size in their walking shoes.

Technology Features to Consider

Under Armour's key technologies each serve different purposes. Charged Cushioning provides firmer, responsive support ideal for power walking and longer distances. HOVR technology offers softer, more plush cushioning perfect for casual walking and all-day comfort. The External Heel Counter enhances stability for overpronators, while their UA Storm technology provides weather resistance for outdoor walkers. Consider your primary walking environment and choose accordingly.

Urban Walking

Choose models with durable outsoles and enhanced cushioning for concrete surfaces

Trail Walking

Look for shoes with aggressive tread patterns and ankle support features

Gym Walking

Prioritize breathability and versatility for indoor/outdoor use

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Under Armour shoes good for walking long distances?

Yes, Under Armour shoes are excellent for long-distance walking. Their Charged Cushioning technology provides superior energy return and impact absorption, while the engineered mesh uppers offer breathability for extended wear. Models like the Charged Assert 10 and HOVR Sonic series are specifically designed for all-day comfort with strategic cushioning placement and durable construction that maintains support throughout long walks. I've personally tested these shoes on walks exceeding 15 miles and found them to maintain comfort and performance consistently.

What's the difference between Under Armour HOVR and Charged cushioning?

Under Armour's HOVR technology uses a foam compound that provides zero gravity feel and energy return, ideal for longer distances and recovery. It offers a softer, more responsive ride that reduces fatigue during extended walking sessions. Charged Cushioning, on the other hand, is firmer and more supportive, converting impact into energy return for a springy feel that propels you forward. HOVR is better for comfort-focused walking and those who prioritize plush cushioning, while Charged is ideal for more active walking, power walking, and light jogging activities where responsiveness matters most.

How do Under Armour women's shoes fit compared to Nike or Adidas?

Under Armour women's shoes typically run true to size but with a slightly more athletic fit compared to Nike and Adidas. They tend to have a more secure heel lock and narrower midfoot, which many women find supportive for walking activities. The toe box is generally roomier than Nike but comparable to Adidas models. If you wear a size 8 in Nike, you'll likely wear the same size in Under Armour, but consider going up a half size if you prefer a looser fit or plan to wear thicker socks. The women-specific last design means better accommodation of female foot anatomy compared to unisex alternatives.

Can I use Under Armour running shoes for everyday walking?

Absolutely! Under Armour running shoes often make excellent walking shoes because they're designed for forward motion and impact absorption. Models like the Charged Rogue and Surge series provide the cushioning and support needed for daily walking while offering the durability for occasional runs. The key is choosing models with adequate arch support and cushioning for your specific walking needs and foot type. In fact, many of the shoes in our best women's Under Armour shoes for walking list are versatile enough to handle both activities effectively, making them excellent value for multi-purpose use.

How often should I replace my Under Armour walking shoes?

Replace your Under Armour walking shoes every 300-500 miles of use, or approximately every 6-8 months with regular daily walking. Signs it's time for replacement include: worn tread patterns on the outsole, compressed midsole cushioning that doesn't bounce back, uneven wear patterns indicating gait issues, or general discomfort during walks. The high-quality construction of Under Armour shoes means they often last toward the higher end of this range, especially models with durable rubber outsoles and reinforced heel counters. Track your mileage and monitor the shoe's condition to ensure optimal support and injury prevention.
