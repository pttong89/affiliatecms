The Athletic Propulsion Labs Story

Athletic Propulsion Labs emerged in 2009 when identical twin brothers Adam and Ryan Goldston, fresh USC graduates and former college athletes, revolutionized athletic footwear from their Los Angeles dorm room. At just 22 years old, these entrepreneurs created something unprecedented in the sneaker world – shoes so effective they were banned by the NBA.

The breakthrough came with their Concept 1 basketball shoe, featuring patented Load 'N Launch technology that increased vertical leap by up to 3.5 inches. This innovation made APL the first brand in NBA's 64-year history to have a shoe banned for providing "undue competitive advantage." The controversy catapulted the brand into mainstream attention, attracting celebrities and athletes worldwide.

Today, apl shoes represent the intersection of luxury and performance, with flagship stores in Los Angeles and New York's SoHo district. The brand has evolved beyond basketball to include running, training, and lifestyle sneakers, all incorporating their signature innovative technologies. Unlike mass-market competitors such as Jordan shoes, APL maintains an exclusive, luxury positioning.

APL Technology Breakdown

Load 'N Launch Technology

The revolutionary Load 'N Launch system uses compression springs strategically placed in the forefoot area, unlike traditional shoes that focus on heel cushioning. This patented technology stores and releases energy during each step, providing measurable performance enhancement that led to the NBA ban.

During my testing on steep inclines, the energy return was particularly noticeable during push-off phases, making climbs feel less taxing compared to standard athletic shoes or even specialized hiking footwear like Tecovas boots.

Propelium Midsole

APL's proprietary Propelium technology delivers superior cushioning while maintaining structural integrity longer than conventional EVA foam. This lightweight material maximizes energy generation and provides exceptional comfort during extended wear.

The difference becomes apparent during long hiking sessions – where traditional sneakers lose responsiveness, APL's Propelium maintains consistent energy return. This technology sets them apart from conventional athletic brands and even premium options like Allbirds shoes.

TechLoom Upper Construction

The signature TechLoom upper uses advanced knitting techniques to create a seamless, sock-like fit. This innovative construction provides targeted support zones while maintaining breathability and flexibility. The material adapts to foot movement, offering both comfort and performance benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are APL shoes worth the high price?

From my experience testing multiple models across various activities, APL shoes justify their premium pricing through innovative technology, exceptional comfort, and versatile performance. However, the value depends on your priorities – if you need specialized athletic footwear for competitive sports, traditional brands might offer better performance per dollar. For lifestyle use with performance benefits, they're worthwhile investments, especially when compared to other luxury sneaker options like Allen Edmonds shoes or designer collaborations.

How do APL shoes fit compared to other brands?

APL shoes consistently run narrow and about half a size small. After trying multiple models, I recommend sizing up 0.5 sizes for most people. This sizing characteristic differs from more accommodating brands but aligns with other premium sneakers that prioritize sleek aesthetics. The TechLoom upper does stretch slightly over time, but initial fit should feel snug without being restrictive. If you have wide feet, consider trying them in-store first or ordering from retailers with generous return policies.

Which APL model is best for beginners?

The TechLoom Phantom offers the best entry point into APL footwear at $205, combining their core technologies with proven versatility. Can you run marathons in APL shoes?

My experience suggests APL shoes excel for moderate distances and mixed activities, but competitive runners seeking maximum performance should stick with established running brands. The comfort and energy return make them excellent for casual runners, fitness enthusiasts, and anyone combining running with other activities during outdoor adventures.
