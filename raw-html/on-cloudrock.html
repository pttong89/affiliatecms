Frequently Asked Questions

Are On Cloudrock boots good for hiking?

Yes, On Cloudrock boots are excellent for light to moderate hiking. They excel on well-maintained trails and day hikes, offering superior comfort through their CloudTec cushioning system. However, they're better suited for casual hikers rather than technical mountaineering. The boots provide good ankle support and waterproof protection, making them ideal for trail walking, urban hiking, and light backpacking. Their lightweight design and running shoe inspiration make them comfortable for long distances on packed trails.

What's the difference between Cloudrock and Cloudrock 2?

The Cloudrock 2 represents a significant improvement over the original. Key differences include reduced weight (about 3-4 ounces lighter), improved durability with better outsole protection, enhanced waterproofing membrane, upgraded FlexLock lacing system, and better heel-to-toe drop ratio. The Cloudrock 2 also features 95% recycled polyester content and improved Missiongrip outsole. The newer model addresses many durability concerns from the original while maintaining the comfort that made Cloudrock popular.

How long do On Cloudrock boots last?

On Cloudrock boots typically last 300-500 miles of hiking, depending on terrain and usage. The Cloudrock 2 shows improved durability over the original model. Factors affecting longevity include hiking frequency, terrain type (rocky surfaces wear faster), maintenance, and individual gait patterns. The waterproofing may degrade after 150-200 miles in harsh conditions. With proper care, casual hikers can expect 1-2 years of regular use, while frequent hikers might need replacement after 8-12 months of heavy trail use.

Are On Cloudrock boots waterproof?

Yes, On Cloudrock boots feature a proprietary waterproof membrane that effectively keeps feet dry in wet conditions. The waterproof barrier works well for stream crossings, puddles, and light rain. However, the membrane's effectiveness can diminish over time, particularly after 150-200 miles of use. The boots also offer decent breathability compared to many waterproof hiking boots, though they can still feel warm in hot weather. For best results, apply periodic waterproofing treatments to maintain protection.

What size should I get in On Cloudrock?

Most hikers should size up 0.5 sizes from their regular shoe size for On Cloudrock boots. The boots generally run true to size, but hiking requires extra toe room for downhill descents and foot swelling. If you have wider feet, consider going up a full size, as the boots tend to run slightly narrow. Always try them on with hiking socks, and ensure you have about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the boot's end. The fit should be snug around the heel and midfoot without being tight.
