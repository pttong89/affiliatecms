Fall and Winter Hunting Seasons

Fall and winter represent peak seasons for Miller Outdoor Adventures, with waterfowl hunting reaching its prime from November through February. Early season teal hunting in September provides excellent opportunities for hunters to prepare for the main duck season while enjoying warm weather conditions. The main duck season typically begins in late November, coinciding with major waterfowl migrations through the Mississippi Flyway. December and January offer the most consistent hunting, with cold weather pushing birds south and concentrating them in Arkansas waters.

Weather monitoring becomes critical during peak hunting season, as Arkansas weather can change rapidly and dramatically affect bird movement patterns. Cold fronts from the north push waterfowl south, creating exceptional hunting opportunities in the days following weather systems. Local knowledge from Miller Outdoor Adventures guides helps predict these movements and position hunters for optimal success. Snow goose seasons extend into March, providing additional hunting opportunities when regular duck seasons close.
