Key Features and Technology

KEEN.DRY Waterproof Technology

The cornerstone of the KEEN Women's Targhee III is its waterproof-breathable membrane. KEEN's proprietary KEEN.DRY technology creates an effective barrier against moisture while allowing perspiration to escape. During my creek crossings in the Cascade Range, where I've deliberately submerged these boots in icy mountain streams, my feet remained completely dry.

What sets KEEN.DRY apart from competitors is its integration with the boot's overall construction. The membrane doesn't feel like an afterthought – it's seamlessly woven into the boot's architecture, maintaining breathability even during strenuous summer hikes. I've worn these boots during 10-hour hiking days in 85-degree weather, and while my feet worked up a sweat, they never felt uncomfortably damp or clammy.

Superior Traction System

The KEEN.RUGGED outsole with its 4mm multi-directional lugs provides exceptional grip across diverse terrain types. From the slick wooden boardwalks of Olympic's temperate rainforest to the granite slabs of Yosemite's approach trails, I've consistently felt confident in my footing. The lug pattern features a strategic combination of deep channels for mud clearance and varied angles for optimal bite on different surfaces.

Pro Tip: The outsole compound performs exceptionally well in temperatures ranging from 20°F to 90°F. I've found it maintains flexibility and grip even during early season hikes when morning frost transitions to afternoon heat.

Roomy Toe Box Design

One of the most celebrated features of the KEEN Women's Targhee III is its generous toe box. KEEN's "Original Fit" philosophy provides ample space for natural toe splay, which becomes crucial during long descents when feet naturally swell. This design particularly benefits hikers with wider feet or those who experience foot swelling during extended activities.

The spacious forefoot doesn't compromise the boot's performance. The speed-lace webbing system allows for precise adjustment, ensuring the midfoot and heel remain securely locked while giving toes freedom to move naturally. This balance prevents the sloppy feel that sometimes accompanies overly roomy boots while eliminating the pinched sensation of narrow designs.

Sizing and Fit Guide

True-to-Size Performance

Based on extensive testing and feedback from fellow hikers, the KEEN Women's Targhee III runs true to size for most feet. However, KEEN's generous toe box means the boots may feel slightly roomier than other brands' equivalent sizes. I typically wear a size 8.5 in athletic shoes and find the same size perfect in these boots, providing adequate toe space without excess volume.

The critical consideration is sock thickness and intended use. For day hiking with standard hiking socks, your regular size should work perfectly. If you plan on winter hiking with thick wool socks or have feet that swell significantly during long hikes, consider going up half a size. The wide width option provides additional accommodation for broader feet.

Sizing Recommendations

Narrow to Average Feet:

Order your normal athletic shoe size. The roomy toe box will provide comfortable space without feeling loose.

Wide Feet:

Start with regular width in your normal size. If needed, the wide width option offers additional room.

Break-in Period

Unlike many hiking boots that require extensive break-in periods, the KEEN Women's Targhee III offers remarkable out-of-the-box comfort. The supple leather upper and well-designed interior minimize hot spots and pressure points during initial wear. Frequently Asked Questions

Are KEEN Women's Targhee III boots true to size?

Yes, the KEEN Women's Targhee III generally runs true to size, though the generous toe box may feel roomier than other brands. Most hikers find their regular athletic shoe size works perfectly. However, if you have narrow feet or prefer a snugger fit, you might consider going down half a size. For wide feet, the regular size typically provides excellent accommodation, with a wide width option available if needed. I recommend trying them on with your preferred hiking socks to ensure optimal fit before committing to longer hikes.

How waterproof are the KEEN Women's Targhee III boots?

The waterproof performance of the KEEN Women's Targhee III is excellent, utilizing KEEN.DRY technology that creates a reliable barrier against moisture. In my testing, including deliberate stream crossings and prolonged exposure to wet conditions, the boots kept feet completely dry. The waterproof membrane is durable and maintains effectiveness even after hundreds of miles of use. However, like all waterproof boots, they're less breathable than non-waterproof alternatives, which can lead to moisture buildup during intense summer hiking. The waterproofing works best in cool to moderate temperatures where breathability isn't as critical.

What's the difference between Targhee II and Targhee III?

The Targhee III represents a refined evolution of the Targhee II design. Key improvements include a sleeker profile that reduces bulk while maintaining protection, updated materials for better durability, and enhanced traction patterns on the outsole. The Targhee III feels more like a hiking shoe than a traditional boot compared to its predecessor, with a slightly lower heel differential and improved flexibility. The toe box remains generously sized in both versions, but the III offers better overall comfort and a more contemporary aesthetic. Performance-wise, the III provides similar waterproofing and protection with improved agility on technical terrain.

How long do KEEN Targhee III boots typically last?

With proper care and moderate use, the KEEN Women's Targhee III can last 300-500 miles of hiking, depending on terrain and hiking style. The durable leather upper and robust construction contribute to longevity, while the KEEN.RUGGED outsole maintains traction even as it wears. Factors affecting lifespan include frequency of use, terrain type, and maintenance practices. Regular cleaning, proper drying between uses, and occasional waterproofing treatment can extend boot life significantly. Many users report getting 12-24 months of regular hiking use from a single pair. The replaceable footbed allows for extended comfort even as the boot ages, making them a good long-term investment for serious hikers.

Are these boots suitable for winter hiking?

The KEEN Women's Targhee III works well for light winter conditions and three-season hiking, but isn't designed for serious winter mountaineering. They provide excellent waterproofing and decent insulation for temperatures down to around 20°F with appropriate socks. The traction is adequate for light snow and ice, but not sufficient for technical winter conditions requiring crampons. For winter day hiking on maintained trails with minimal snow, they perform admirably. However, for serious winter backpacking or mountaineering, consider dedicated winter boots with enhanced insulation and more aggressive traction systems. The boots excel in spring and fall conditions where temperatures fluctuate around freezing.
