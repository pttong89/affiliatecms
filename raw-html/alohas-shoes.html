What Are Alohas Shoes?

Alohas shoes represent a revolutionary approach to sustainable fashion, combining Spanish craftsmanship with innovative on-demand production. Founded in 2015 by Alejandro Porras in Hawaii, the brand was inspired by traditional Spanish espadrilles but has evolved into a comprehensive footwear collection that challenges fast fashion norms.

What sets Alohas shoes apart from competitors like Allbirds or Clark shoes is their commitment to eliminating overproduction. Unlike traditional brands that manufacture thousands of pairs hoping to sell them, Alohas produces only what customers have already ordered, reducing waste by up to 30%.

The Barcelona-based company operates from Spain's coastal Alicante region, where skilled artisans handcraft each pair using locally-sourced materials. This geographical concentration ensures quality control while supporting local economies—a stark contrast to mass-produced footwear from global sneaker brands.

Alohas shoes range from €120-180 ($130-200), positioning them in the premium sustainable footwear market alongside brands featured in comprehensive footwear reviews at Famous Footwear. While the price point may seem steep compared to fast fashion alternatives, the cost reflects ethical labor practices, premium materials, and the true cost of sustainable production.

Pro Sizing Tips From My Experience
