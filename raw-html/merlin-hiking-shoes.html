Sizing and Fit Considerations

Proper fit represents the most critical factor in hiking shoe selection. Merlin shoes typically run true to size, though individual foot characteristics may require adjustments. I recommend trying shoes later in the day when feet have naturally swollen, simulating conditions experienced during extended hiking. Allow approximately half-inch space between longest toe and shoe front to accommodate foot expansion and prevent black toenails during descents.

Professional Fitting Tips

  • Measure feet at end of day for accurate sizing
  • Wear hiking socks during fitting process
  • Walk on inclined surfaces to test downhill fit
  • Check for pressure points around ankle and forefoot areas
  • Ensure heel stays secure without excessive movement

Can Merlin hiking shoes be resoled?

Most Merlin hiking shoes use bonded construction methods that make traditional resoling impractical and cost-prohibitive. The outsoles are permanently attached to the midsole assembly using adhesives and molding techniques that cannot be easily reversed by standard cobbling methods. While some specialized repair services might attempt outsole replacement, the cost typically exceeds purchasing new shoes, making replacement the more economical option. This construction approach allows for lighter weight and better waterproof integration but sacrifices repairability. For hikers prioritizing long-term sustainability, this limitation should be considered against the shoes' other benefits including lower initial cost and proven performance capabilities throughout their designed lifespan.
