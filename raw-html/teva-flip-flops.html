Sizing and Fit Considerations

Proper sizing is crucial for flip flop comfort and safety. Teva flip flops generally run true to size, but individual models may vary slightly. The Olowahu tends to run slightly large, so consider sizing down half a size if you're between sizes. The Mush II typically fits true to size for most foot shapes.

When trying on flip flops, ensure your heel doesn't hang over the back edge and your toes don't extend beyond the front. The straps should feel snug but not tight, allowing natural foot movement without requiring constant gripping. Remember that some models will stretch slightly with wear, particularly those with fabric or synthetic straps.

Pro Sizing Tips

  • Shop for flip flops in the afternoon when your feet are naturally slightly swollen
  • If between sizes, size up for comfort during extended wear
  • Consider foot width – some Teva models accommodate wider feet better than others
  • Account for seasonal swelling if you'll primarily wear them in hot weather

Care and Maintenance Tips

Proper care extends the life of your Teva flip flops significantly. After beach or pool use, rinse them with fresh water to remove salt and chlorine that can degrade materials over time. For routine cleaning, use mild soap and a soft brush to remove dirt and debris from the footbed texture.

Store your flip flops away from direct sunlight, which can cause materials to become brittle and fade. If they develop an odor, create a paste with baking soda and water, apply it to the footbed, let it sit for 30 minutes, then rinse thoroughly. This natural approach effectively eliminates bacteria without damaging the materials.

When comparing care requirements to other brands like best flip flops from premium manufacturers, Tevas are remarkably low-maintenance. Unlike leather options that require conditioning or suede models that need special cleaning, Teva's synthetic materials handle neglect well while still responding positively to basic care.
