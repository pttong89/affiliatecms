What is the Danner Combat Hiker?

The Danner Combat Hiker represents a unique chapter in military footwear history. Born from urgent battlefield requirements during the early years of the Afghanistan conflict, these boots were specifically engineered to address the shortcomings of standard military footwear when faced with Afghanistan's unforgiving mountainous terrain.

Unlike traditional combat boots designed for desert warfare, the Danner Combat Hiker incorporated civilian hiking boot technology with military durability standards, creating a hybrid that excelled in both combat operations and long-distance trekking.

The development story of the Danner Combat Hiker begins with a military crisis. Standard desert boots issued to troops were causing an alarming number of ankle and foot injuries due to Afghanistan's rocky, mountainous terrain. The Army urgently sought a solution, leading to an off-the-shelf procurement that would result in one of the most beloved military boots ever issued. What made these boots special wasn't just their construction, but their integration of civilian hiking technology with military durability requirements.

Today, while the original military contract has ended, the Combat Hiker's legacy lives on through its civilian counterpart, the Crater Rim, and through a thriving surplus market where military personnel and outdoor enthusiasts alike seek out these legendary boots. The design philosophy behind combat hiking boots has influenced modern outdoor footwear development significantly.

While excellent overall, the Combat Hiker has limitations. The Gore-Tex lining reduces breathability in hot conditions, making them less suitable for summer desert hiking. The weight penalty affects performance on long-distance, fast-and-light adventures where every ounce matters.

The boot's design optimizes for durability and protection over ultralight performance, making them ideal for technical terrain and heavy loads but potentially overkill for casual day hiking on established trails.

Condition Check

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes the Danner Combat Hiker different from regular hiking boots?

The Danner Combat Hiker was specifically engineered for military operations in Afghanistan, featuring a more aggressive Vibram sole with heel brake technology, extra toe room for steep descents, Gore-Tex waterproofing, and a reinforced construction designed for extreme terrain and heavy loads. Unlike civilian hiking boots optimized for weight savings, the Combat Hiker prioritizes durability and protection for mission-critical applications. The 360-degree rubber rand and military-specification materials create a boot capable of withstanding conditions that would destroy conventional hiking footwear. Additionally, the unique lacing system and internal construction reflect military ergonomic requirements rather than casual hiking preferences.

Are Danner Combat Hikers still available for purchase?

Original Danner Combat Hikers are no longer in production but can be found on the surplus market through eBay and military surplus stores. The civilian equivalent is the Danner Crater Rim, which shares nearly identical construction with minor differences in lacing hardware. Military surplus Combat Hikers often offer exceptional value, frequently selling for $80-150 versus $250-300 for new Crater Rims. When purchasing surplus, inspect for sole wear, leather condition, and overall integrity. Many surplus pairs are unused or lightly used, making them an excellent option for budget-conscious buyers seeking authentic military-specification footwear. For those preferring new boots with warranty coverage, Nike combat boots or the Crater Rim provide modern alternatives.

How do Danner Combat Hikers fit compared to other boots?

Combat Hikers tend to run slightly long and narrow compared to modern hiking boots. Most users recommend ordering a half-size down from your regular shoe size or considering wide sizes if you have broader feet. The extra toe room is intentional for downhill hiking safety, preventing toe jamming on steep descents while carrying heavy loads. This generous toe box initially feels unusual but proves invaluable during extended downhill hiking. The boot's military last differs significantly from civilian hiking boots, designed for functionality over fashion. If transitioning from Bates combat boots or other military footwear, expect a similar fit philosophy but with hiking-specific ergonomics. Always test with thick hiking socks and consider the boot's intended use when determining proper sizing.

What is the difference between Combat Hiker and Crater Rim models?

The primary difference lies in the upper lacing system - Combat Hikers use traditional eyelets throughout while Crater Rims feature speed-lace hooks at the top portion. Both share the identical Vibram Bifida sole, Gore-Tex lining, nubuck leather construction, and internal support systems. The Crater Rim represents Danner's civilian market adaptation of the Combat Hiker, with minor cosmetic changes but identical performance characteristics. Combat Hikers often command lower prices on the surplus market, making them attractive to budget-conscious buyers. From a performance standpoint, differences are negligible - both excel in challenging terrain and adverse conditions. The choice typically comes down to availability, price, and personal preference for traditional versus speed-lacing systems. Both models reflect the same design philosophy and construction standards that made the Combat Hiker legendary among military personnel and outdoor enthusiasts.
