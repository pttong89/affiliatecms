How often should I replace shoes used for standing all day?

Shoes worn for standing all day should be replaced every 300-500 miles or 6-8 months of regular use, whichever comes first. Signs it's time to replace include worn tread, compressed midsole cushioning, uneven wear patterns, or experiencing new foot discomfort. Professional-grade shoes from Hoka, Brooks, or New Balance typically last longer than budget options. Consider rotating between two pairs to extend their lifespan—this allows cushioning materials to decompress between wears and reduces overall wear. Monitor the midsole compression by pressing on the heel area; if it doesn't spring back, the cushioning is compromised. Quality shoes maintain their support longer, making them more economical over time. Keep a replacement schedule and don't wait until shoes are completely worn out, as worn shoes can contribute to foot problems and reduced work performance.
