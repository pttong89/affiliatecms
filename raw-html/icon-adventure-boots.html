Frequently Asked Questions

Are Icon adventure boots waterproof?

Yes, many Icon adventure boots feature waterproof technology. Models like the Stormhawk and Patrol 3 WP include eVent waterproof/breathable membranes or similar technology that I've tested extensively in Pacific Northwest rain. The waterproofing holds up excellently over time, maintaining breathability while keeping feet completely dry. However, not all Icon boots are waterproof, so check the specific model specifications before purchasing. Models without "WP" designation typically use DWR coatings for water resistance rather than full waterproofing.

How do Icon adventure boots compare to other brands?

Icon adventure boots offer excellent value for money, typically priced between $145-$265 compared to $350-$500 for premium alternatives. Through extensive testing, I've found they provide solid protection and comfort comparable to more expensive brands like Alpinestars or SIDI, but may not have the same premium materials or long-term durability as top-tier options. The BOA lacing system and CE certification put them ahead of many competitors in their price range. For most adventure riders, Icon boots deliver 90% of premium boot performance at 60% of the cost.

What sizes do Icon adventure boots come in?

Icon adventure boots typically come in US men's sizes 7-15, including half sizes for better fit accuracy. They generally run true to size based on my experience testing multiple models, but I recommend checking the specific size chart for each model as some may fit slightly different due to varying construction methods. The Stormhawk runs slightly narrow while the Patrol 3 offers a roomier fit. Women should typically order their normal shoe size, though those with narrow heels might prefer a half-size smaller for optimal fit.
