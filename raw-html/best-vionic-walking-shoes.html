Frequently Asked Questions

Are Vionic shoes good for plantar fasciitis?

Absolutely. Vionic shoes are specifically designed by podiatrists to address plantar fasciitis through their Vio Motion technology, which provides proper arch support and heel alignment. Multiple models have earned the APMA Seal of Acceptance, meaning they're clinically proven to promote foot health. The contoured footbed helps reduce strain on the plantar fascia ligament, while the deep heel cup provides stability. Many users report significant pain reduction within the first week of wearing Vionic shoes. For severe cases, consider pairing with Vionic slippers for complete at-home support.

How long do Vionic walking shoes typically last?

With regular use, quality Vionic walking shoes typically last 400-600 miles or 6-12 months of daily wear. The durability largely depends on your walking patterns, weight, and terrain. The Walk Strider and 23Walk 2.0 models show excellent longevity in my testing, with the footbed maintaining support well beyond what you'd expect from conventional athletic shoes. Signs it's time to replace include visible wear on the outsole, flattening of the arch support, or loss of heel cushioning. Many users report getting 18+ months from their Vionic shoes with proper care and rotation between pairs.

Do Vionic shoes run large or small?

Vionic sizing can be inconsistent across models, but generally runs true to size with some exceptions. The Walk Strider tends to run slightly large, especially in width, making it ideal for those with wide feet or high insteps. The 23Walk 2.0 runs more true to size but has a roomier toe box. Recent manufacturing changes have introduced some variability, with Vietnam-made models running narrower than China-made versions. I recommend ordering your normal size first, but have a return plan ready. If you're between sizes, size up for the Walk Strider and stick with your normal size for the 23Walk 2.0. Consider that the arch support will feel different initially and may affect perceived fit.

Can you wear Vionic walking shoes for running?

While Vionic walking shoes provide excellent support and cushioning, they're specifically engineered for walking biomechanics, not running. The firmer heel counter and arch support that make them exceptional for walking can feel restrictive during running's higher-impact movements. The outsole pattern and midsole flex points are optimized for the heel-to-toe roll of walking rather than running's midfoot strike patterns. If you need supportive footwear for both activities, consider having dedicated walking and running shoes. However, for light jogging or walk-run intervals, many users find them acceptable. For serious runners with foot issues, consult a podiatrist about proper running shoe selection that complements your Vionic walking shoes.
