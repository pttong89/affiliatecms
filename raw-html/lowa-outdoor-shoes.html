Always try on hiking boots in the afternoon when your feet are naturally more swollen, mimicking the conditions you'll experience during actual hiking. Wear the same sock thickness you plan to use on the trail, and walk around the store for at least 10-15 minutes to assess comfort.

Can LOWA outdoor shoes be resoled when they wear out?

Resoling options vary by model, with higher-end LOWA boots typically offering professional resoling services through authorized repair centers. Many popular hiking models like the Renegade use cemented construction that doesn't allow for traditional resoling, but the durable construction means they typically last long enough to justify replacement rather than repair. Some users have successfully had local cobblers perform emergency repairs, but this approach may compromise waterproofing and isn't recommended for critical applications.

What's the best way to care for and maintain LOWA boots?

Proper maintenance significantly extends LOWA boot lifespan and performance. Clean boots after each use with a soft brush and mild soap, paying special attention to removing dirt from seams and hardware. Allow boots to dry naturally at room temperature, never near direct heat sources that can damage leather and adhesives. Apply leather conditioner every 3-4 months or when the leather appears dry, using products recommended by LOWA like Nikwax or their proprietary care products. Reapply waterproofing treatment annually or when water no longer beads on the surface. Store boots in a cool, dry location with cedar shoe trees to maintain shape. Replace insoles when they show wear, and address minor repairs promptly to prevent major problems.
