Brand History and Jamie Thomas Legacy

The story of Straye shoes begins in 2016 when skateboarding legend Jamie Thomas decided to revolutionize the skate footwear industry once again. After his success with Fallen Footwear, Thomas partnered with Angel Cabada, the former founder of Supra, and skateboarding icon Chad Muska to create a brand that would bridge the gap between vintage aesthetics and modern performance needs.

Having personally witnessed the evolution of skate shoes over the years, I can attest that Straye entered the market at a perfect time. The skateboarding community was craving authentic, durable options that didn't break the bank, and Thomas's vision of creating "the most durable, value-priced skate shoe" resonated immediately with skaters worldwide. The brand's philosophy centers on being "forward, fluid and unexpected," which is evident in every model they produce.

What sets Straye apart from other skateboard shoe brands is their commitment to supporting the skateboarding community through their impressive team roster. Professional skaters like Boo Johnson, Antwuan Dixon, Dane Vaughn, Windsor James, Ben Norberg, and Lem West represent the brand, ensuring that every design meets the demands of serious skateboarding while maintaining street-ready style. This connection to skateboarding's roots, similar to what you'd find in other authentic performance shoe collections, ensures that Straye maintains credibility within the skateboarding community.

The brand's Hollywood, California roots run deep, with Thomas drawing from nearly three decades of design and brand-building expertise. Unlike some corporate-backed shoe companies, Straye operates with the authenticity that comes from being skater-owned and skater-operated, making decisions based on what actually works on the streets and in skateparks rather than what looks good in boardrooms.
