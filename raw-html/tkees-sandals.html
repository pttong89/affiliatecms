Frequently Asked Questions

Are TKEES sandals worth the high price point?

The value proposition depends on your priorities and foot type. If you have narrow feet, appreciate minimalist design, and wear sandals frequently, TKEES can be a worthwhile investment. The premium materials and craftsmanship do justify some of the cost, and they'll last multiple seasons with proper care. However, if you prioritize immediate comfort, need arch support, or have wide feet, the high price may not be justified given the potential fit issues. Consider them a style investment rather than purely functional footwear.

How long does the break-in period typically last?

Most users report a break-in period of 1-3 weeks with regular wear. The timeline varies based on the specific style (The Boyfriend tends to be more comfortable initially), your foot shape, and how often you wear them. The leather straps need time to soften and conform to your feet, while the toe post requires adjustment. To speed up the process, some users recommend wearing them for short periods initially and gradually increasing wear time. If they're still uncomfortable after a month of regular use, they may not be the right fit for your feet.

What's the best way to care for and maintain TKEES sandals?

Proper care will extend the life of your TKEES significantly. Clean them regularly with a soft, damp cloth to remove dirt and salt. For deeper cleaning, use a mild leather cleaner and allow them to air dry completely. Avoid exposing them to extreme heat or direct sunlight for extended periods. Store them in a cool, dry place when not in use. Some users apply a leather conditioner occasionally to keep the material supple, but this isn't always necessary. With proper care, many owners report their TKEES lasting 2-3 years of regular summer wear.

Can TKEES sandals be worn in water or at the beach?

While TKEES can handle occasional water exposure, they're not designed as dedicated water shoes. The leather construction means they'll take time to dry and may show water marks or staining if frequently submerged. For beach use, they work well for walking on sand and casual beach activities, but remove them before swimming. If they do get wet, stuff them with newspaper and let them air dry completely to maintain their shape. For frequent water activities, consider keeping a separate pair of rubber flip-flops specifically for water use.
