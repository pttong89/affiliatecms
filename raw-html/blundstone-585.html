Detailed Features and Construction

The construction of the Blundstone 585 reflects over 150 years of Australian bootmaking expertise. Every component is carefully engineered to provide maximum comfort while maintaining the durability that has made Blundstone a trusted name among workers, adventurers, and outdoor enthusiasts worldwide.

Premium Leather Upper

The rustic brown leather used in the 585 is a full-grain premium hide that's been specially treated to achieve its distinctive aged appearance. Unlike corrected grain leathers, this full-grain construction allows the boot to breathe naturally while developing a unique patina over time. The leather is water-resistant out of the box, though regular conditioning enhances this property significantly.

The multi-piece construction, with each seam double-stitched for durability, sets these apart from dressier Chelsea boots. This approach prioritizes longevity over aesthetics, ensuring the boots can handle whatever outdoor adventures you throw at them.

The elastic side panels deserve special mention – they're manufactured from a high-quality elastic that maintains its stretch and rebound even after years of use. The brown color perfectly complements the rustic leather, and the fabric pull tabs at front and back make the slip-on design incredibly convenient. I've compared these to Blundstone 500 boots, and the 585's elastic is noticeably superior in both feel and longevity.

Advanced Comfort Technology

The SPS Max Comfort system is where the 585 truly shines. The removable footbed features XRD® Technology in the heel strike zone, which provides exceptional shock absorption – crucial for long days on hard surfaces. The polyurethane midsole adds another layer of cushioning while maintaining durability.

The leather lining extends throughout the shaft, providing better breathability than synthetic alternatives. This natural material helps regulate temperature and moisture, making the boots comfortable in a wide range of conditions.

The injection-molded TPU outsole represents modern bootmaking at its finest. This process creates a lightweight, flexible sole that's bonded directly to the boot upper, eliminating the need for traditional stitching methods. While this means the boots aren't resoleable, the trade-off is immediate comfort and a significantly lighter boot that doesn't require the break-in period of traditional welted construction.

Sizing Guide and Fit

Getting the right fit with Blundstone 585 boots is crucial for comfort, and there are some important considerations that differ from typical American sizing. After helping dozens of fellow outdoor enthusiasts find their perfect fit, I've developed a comprehensive understanding of how these boots should fit and what to expect during the sizing process.

Understanding Blundstone Sizing

Blundstone uses Australian/UK sizing, which typically runs about one size smaller than US sizing. However, the 585 has some unique fit characteristics that set it apart from other models in the line:

  • True to size fit: Most people can order their normal US size in the 585
  • Wider fit option: If you prefer a roomier feel, go up half a size
  • Narrow feet: The 585's wider last may require sizing down for narrow-footed individuals
  • Thick sock accommodation: Consider your typical sock thickness when sizing

Unlike Blundstone 550 boots, which run narrower, the 585 has a more generous fit across the midfoot and toe box.

When trying on the 585s, you should feel a slight give in the heel and about a quarter-inch of space from your longest toe to the front of the boot. The boots will stretch slightly over the instep with wear but will never stretch lengthwise. This is crucial to remember – if your toes are hitting the front when new, the boots are too small and will never become comfortable.

My Personal Sizing Experience

I wear a size 10D on a Brannock device and typically take a 10 in most boots. With the 585, I found that a size 10 US (9 AU) fit perfectly with medium-weight hiking socks. The initial fit was snug but not tight, and after about a week of wear, they molded to my feet beautifully.

For comparison, I need a 10.5 in men's Blundstone boots from the Original 500 series, demonstrating how the 585's wider last accommodates feet more generously.

The included comfort insoles are a game-changer for fine-tuning fit. If you find your boots slightly too roomy, these additional insoles can be placed under the regular footbed to take up excess volume. This feature makes the 585 more forgiving than many boots in terms of achieving the perfect fit.

For those with specific foot issues, the removable footbed system allows for custom orthotics. I've successfully used the boots with both over-the-counter arch supports and custom orthotics, making them suitable for people with plantar fasciitis or other foot conditions that require additional support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Blundstone 585 boots waterproof?

The Blundstone 585 boots are water-resistant, not fully waterproof. They feature premium water-resistant leather that handles light rain, puddles, and wet conditions very well. However, in heavy downpours or when submerged, water can eventually penetrate through the leather or ankle opening. For enhanced water protection, I recommend treating them regularly with a quality leather waterproofing product. This treatment significantly improves their water resistance and helps maintain the leather's condition over time.

How do Blundstone 585 boots fit compared to US sizes?

Blundstone uses Australian/UK sizing, which typically runs about one size smaller than US sizing. However, the 585 model fits relatively true to US size for most people due to its wider last design. If you wear a size 10 in most US boots, you'll likely be comfortable in a US 10 (AU 9) in the 585. For those preferring a roomier fit or who wear thick socks regularly, sizing up half a size is recommended. The included extra comfort insoles help fine-tune the fit if needed.

What's the difference between Blundstone 585 and 500?

The main differences are in comfort and construction. The 585 (Classics series) features superior shock absorption with XRD technology, softer leather requiring minimal break-in, full leather lining, and a slightly wider fit. The 500 (Original series) has the traditional Blundstone feel with a firmer sole, requires more break-in time, and costs slightly less. Both are excellent boots, but the 585 prioritizes comfort while the 500 maintains the classic experience. Most users find the small price difference worth it for the 585's enhanced comfort features.

How long do Blundstone 585 boots typically last?

With regular use, Blundstone 585 boots typically last 3-5 years, depending on usage intensity and care. The injection-molded sole construction means they can't be resoled, so lifespan depends on sole wear. However, the high-quality leather upper often outlasts the sole. With proper care including regular conditioning and rotation with other footwear, many users report 4+ years of daily wear. The investment in quality materials and construction makes them cost-effective despite the premium initial price. For occasional outdoor use, they can last significantly longer.

Can you wear Blundstone 585 boots for hiking?

Yes, the Blundstone 585 is excellent for light to moderate hiking, day hikes, and trail walking. The comfortable sole, good traction pattern, and ankle mobility make them suitable for most hiking scenarios. However, they're not designed for technical mountaineering, multi-day backpacking, or extreme terrain requiring rigid ankle support. For hiking on well-maintained trails, shorter distances, and casual outdoor exploration, they perform admirably. Many national park visitors find them ideal for sightseeing hikes and nature walks. Consider dedicated hiking boots for more demanding adventures.
